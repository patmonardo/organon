# VAK_4.97

## Sanskrit

त्रिषु द्वीपेष्वानन्तर्यं षण्ढादीनां तु नेष्यते ।
अल्पोपकारालज्जित्वाच्छेषे गतिषु पञ्चसु ॥

## IAST

triṣu dvīpeṣv ānantaryaṃ śaṇḍhādīnāṃ tu neṣyate |
alpopakārālajjitvāc cheṣe gatiṣu pañcasu ||

## Padaccheda

triṣu | dvīpeṣu | ānantaryam | śaṇḍha-ādīnām | tu | na | iṣyate | alpa-upakārāt | alajjitvāt | śeṣe | gatiṣu | pañcasu

## Literal Translation

An act of immediate consequence is possible in the three continents, but it is not accepted for eunuchs and the like, because of little benefit and shamelessness. The remaining acts occur throughout the five destinies.

## Philosophical Translation

The five immediate-consequence karmas occur only among human beings of the three continents; they are not constituted as such in śaṇḍha and comparable persons. Other grave karmic activities, however, may occur throughout all five destinies.

## Technical Vocabulary

- triṣu dvīpeṣu — in Jambūdvīpa, Pūrvavideha, and Aparagodānīya
- ānantarya — karma whose result follows without an intervening birth
- śaṇḍhādīnām — of eunuchs and related embodied categories
- upakāra — benefit or service rendered
- alajjitva — absence of moral inhibition
- gati — destiny

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse specifies the conditions under which karmic obstruction is technically instantiated. Ānantarya karma requires a qualified locus, a qualified agent, and a qualified relation to an exceptional field. The visible deed alone does not determine its complete karmic status.

The gravity of matricide and patricide depends partly upon the extraordinary benefit conferred by one’s parents. Karma is therefore determined through an agent–field–relation, not merely through an external event-description.

## Logical Determination

Karma is determinate relation:

activity
    as determined through
agent–field–relation

The karmic character of an act depends upon where it occurs, who performs it, against whom it is performed, and what relation already exists between them.

## Interpretive Note

The same intensified human form that supports Path-entry also supports the most radical obstruction to the Path. Capacity for liberation and capacity for ānantarya belong to the same developed form of moral agency.

The external judgment “X killed Y” is insufficient. Complete karmic determination requires the agent, field, prior benefit, intention, locus, and completion of the act.
