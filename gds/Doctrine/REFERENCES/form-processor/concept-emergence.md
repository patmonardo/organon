# Concept Emergence

Concept is the named scientific object that emerges after Principle has been satisfied.

---

## Position in the Arc

```text
Reflection -> Principle -> Concept -> Judgment
```

Concept is not merely a label assigned to appearance. It is the post-Principle identity of an entity that has passed the nomological gate.

---

## What Emerges

A Concept record fixes:

- identity: the stable reference or key
- marks: quality, measure, ground, determinacy
- principle_ref: the Principle evaluation that permitted emergence
- provenance: source and program lineage

---

## Artifact Shape

```text
concept_id | entity_id | identity_field | marks | principle_ref | source_id | program_name
```

A Concept without `principle_ref` is not doctrinally complete.

---

## Relationship to Judgment

Judgment determines what the Concept is: singular, stable, grounded, lawful, possible, necessary. Judgment presupposes Concept. It does not create Concept.

---

## Doctrine Rule

Do not collapse Concept into an extracted entity. Named entity recognition can provide candidate material, but Concept is a scientific object only after Reflection and Principle.
