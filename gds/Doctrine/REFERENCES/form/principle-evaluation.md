# Principle Evaluation

Principle is the nomological gate before Concept. It is not a validation rule attached after the fact. It determines whether an entity may become a scientific object at all.

---

## Position in the Arc

```text
Reflection (7 moments) -> Principle -> Concept
```

Principle is evaluated at or just after Absolute Reflection. The entity is fully articulated by Reflection but is not yet Concept.

---

## What Principle Asks

Principle asks:

1. Does the entity satisfy the program's lawful conditions?
2. Can the entity be unified with the required concept, judgment, and syllogism forms?
3. Is there enough ground for scientific objecthood?
4. Does the result preserve provenance and traceability?

If yes, Concept emerges. If no, the entity remains at the Absolute Reflection threshold.

---

## Artifact Shape

A principle table should record:

```text
entity_id | principle_name | condition | satisfied | evidence_ref | source_id | program_name
```

The table must be readable. A user should see why an entity was admitted or rejected.

---

## ZeroCopy Rule

Principle evaluation touches the Absolute Reflection boundary. The agent should inspect shape data without marshaling. The entity's shape at Moment 7 is the shared kernel-agent object.

---

## Doctrine Rule

Never treat Principle as a post-Concept filter. Principle comes first. Concept is the result of satisfying Principle.
