# Hegelian Objectivity: Procedure as Process

In this Doctrine, Procedure is the entry into Objectivity. It emits Process, not dead output.

---

## Position in the Arc

```text
Concept -> Judgment -> Syllogism -> Procedure
```

After Concept is determined by Judgment and mediated through Syllogism, Procedure emits the realized record of the process.

---

## What Procedure Emits

Procedure emits artifact tables that together record how knowledge came to be:

- source artifacts: what was given
- observation artifacts: what appeared
- reflection artifacts: how essence unfolded
- principle artifacts: what lawful conditions held
- concept artifacts: what identities emerged
- judgment artifacts: what determinations were made
- syllogism artifacts: what conclusions followed
- manifest artifacts: what the program declares as its realized image

Each table is a slice of Process.

---

## Not Static Output

A table is not the doctrine. A table is the material record of a stage in the process. Objectivity is the movement of these stages into realized form.

---

## Review Rule

When reviewing a Procedure example, ask:

1. Does it emit all necessary stage artifacts?
2. Does each artifact preserve provenance?
3. Can a reader trace from output back to Principle?
4. Does the procedure expose serviceable outputs without leaking internal algorithms?

If yes, the Procedure has entered Objectivity properly.
