# Kant: Complete Knowledge of Principles, Not Wholes

## The Insight

**That is exactly Kant's point. There are a complete knowledge of Principles but only of Principles. Never of Wholes of Cognition applied to Appearances. People misunderstand Gödel.**

- **Complete knowledge of Principles** = Yes, possible (Pure Form, Kernel)
- **Complete knowledge of Wholes of Cognition applied to Appearances** = No, never possible (infinite)
- **Gödel** = People misunderstand - it's not about Principles, it's about Wholes of Cognition applied to Appearances

## Kant's Point

**There can be complete knowledge of Principles, but never of Wholes of Cognition applied to Appearances.**

- **Principles** = Can have complete knowledge (discovered by Kernel, Pure Form)
- **Wholes of Cognition** = The Set of Morphs (all trig identities, etc.)
- **Applied to Appearances** = Infinite applications, cannot be complete
- **Kant's distinction** = Principles are complete, applications are infinite
