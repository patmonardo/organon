## The Three Pillars

### Kant: Principle of Consciousness

**"The Principle of Consciousness"** - the unity of apperception that makes experience possible.

- **Pure Form** = The transcendental logic layer
- **Possibility of Content** = The conditions that make content possible
- **Given Form** = The discursive logic that operates on given content

Kant provides the **architectural distinction** between:
- What makes experience possible (Pure Form)
- What operates on given experience (Given Form)

### Fichte: Principle Provider with Recursive Descent

**"Principle Provision with Recursive Descent"** - the principle is provided and then recursively applied.

- **Pure Form** = Principle Provider (strictly)
- **Recursive Descent** = The principle enters into Given Form and descends
- **Shape:Context:Morph** = The triadic unity that provides the principle

Fichte provides the **pattern**:
- Principle is provided from above
- Principle enters into the discursive layer
- Principle recursively descends through nested structures

### Hegel: Dialectic as Immanent

**"Dialectic runs through everything"** - the middle (madhya) that is immanent in all structure.

- **Dialectic** = The middle that runs through everything
- **Immanent** = Not external, but internal to the structure
- **Essence** = The minimal pass that materializes dialectic

Hegel provides the **process**:
- Dialectic is not external to form
- Dialectic is immanent in the structure
- Dialectic prepares and materializes
