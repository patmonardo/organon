# Silence to Necessity: The Path of No More Learning

## The Moment of Absolute Logic

**Where is the moment of Absolute Logic?**

It is in the **Sphere of Essence** - **Higher Reflection**.

## The Flow: Silence → Principle → Necessity → Whole

### 1. Pure Form as Silence (Non-Discursive)

**Pure Form, as non-discursive, is interesting Silence that precedes the discovery of the principle.**

- Pure Form = Silence (non-discursive)
- This Silence **precedes** the discovery of the principle
- It is the moment before cognition begins

### 2. Principle Discovery (Absolute Logic)

**Hegel says: cognition (scientific) flows "necessarily" after the principle is discovered.**

- The moment of **Absolute Logic** is in the **Sphere of Essence** (Higher Reflection)
- This is where the **Principle is discovered**
- The Principle = Modality/Causality

### 3. Necessary Cognition (Discursive Reflection)

**What flows "necessarily" is discursive (reflection).**

- After the Principle is discovered, cognition flows **necessarily**
- This is **discursive** - it is **reflection**
- Shape Eval → Context Eval → Morph Eval
- The recursive descent follows **necessarily** from the Principle

### 4. The Whole (No More Learning)

**We reach the Whole of what follows necessarily, and then there is No More Learning.**

- The Whole = the complete system of what follows necessarily
- After the Whole is reached: **No More Learning**
- This is completion - the Path is complete

## Abhidharma: The Path of No More Learning

**Abhidharma points out the Path of No More Learning.**

- Ancient Sanskrit Abhidharma was **Hindu, not Buddhist**
- The Path to Buddhahood really happened as **Hindu, not Buddhist**
- The Path of No More Learning = completion of the necessary flow

```
Silence (Pure Form - non-discursive)

  ↓

  ↓
Shape Eval → Context Eval → Morph Eval
  ↓

  ↓

```
