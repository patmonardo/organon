## Chunk Details

### Chunk 1: Chapter 2 introduction — Foundation, essentialities
**Lines**: 2-19
**Title**: Chapter 2 introduction — Foundation, essentialities
**Key Points**:
- Foundation: The essentialities or the determinations of reflection
- Reflection is determined reflection; essence is determined essence, essentiality
- Reflection is shining of essence within itself
- Essence as infinite immanent turning back
- Not immediate simplicity, but negative simplicity
- Movement across moments that are distinct, absolute mediation with itself
- In these moments it shines; moments are determinations reflected into themselves

### Chunk 2: Three moments: identity, difference, contradiction
**Lines**: 21-30
**Title**: Three moments: identity, difference, contradiction
**Key Points**:
- First: essence is simple self-reference, pure identity
- This determination is absence of determination
- Second: specifying determination is difference
- Difference: external or indefinite, diversity in general, or opposed diversity or opposition
- Third: as contradiction, opposition reflected into itself and returns to foundation

### Chunk 3: A. IDENTITY — essence as simple self-identity
**Lines**: 32-39
**Title**: A. IDENTITY — essence as simple self-identity
**Key Points**:
- Essence is simple immediacy as sublated immediacy
- Its negativity is its being
- Equal to itself in absolute negativity
- By virtue of which otherness and reference to other have disappeared into pure self-equality
- Essence is therefore simple self-identity

### Chunk 4: Self-identity as immediacy of reflection — essential identity
**Lines**: 41-46
**Title**: Self-identity as immediacy of reflection — essential identity
**Key Points**:
- This self-identity is immediacy of reflection
- Not self-equality which being is, or also nothing
- Self-equality which, in producing itself as unity, does not produce itself over again, as from another
- Pure production, from itself and in itself
- Essential identity

### Chunk 5: Not abstract identity — relative negation
**Lines**: 48-54
**Title**: Not abstract identity — relative negation
**Key Points**:
- Not abstract identity
- Not identity which is result of relative negation preceding it
- Relative negation separates what it distinguishes but leaves it existing outside it
- Same after as before

### Chunk 6: Being's negativity is identity itself
**Lines**: 56-63
**Title**: Being's negativity is identity itself
**Key Points**:
- Being, and every determinateness of being, has sublated itself not relatively, but in itself
- This simple negativity, negativity of being in itself, is the identity itself
- In general, therefore, it is still the same as essence
