## Overview

This is the third subspecies of B. FINITUDE, developing **finitude itself**. It shows how the finite contains the contradiction that propels it beyond itself into the infinite. The analysis moves from the definition of finitude through the understanding's persistence in sorrow to the resolution: the finite's transition into the infinite.

## Structure

### Introduction (lines 4-37)
**Definition of finitude**: Opposition of existence and negation (limit immanent) = in-itselfness. This negation = finitude. Finite things: non-being constitutes their nature. In self-reference, refer to themselves negatively; propel beyond themselves. Truth of being = finis, end. Finite perishes (not mere possibility). Being of finite things = to have germ of transgression in in-itselfness. **Hour of birth = hour of death**.

### (a) The immediacy of finitude (lines 39-137)

#### Mournful note; understanding's persistence (lines 41-79)
- Finitude = qualitative negation driven to extreme. No affirmative being distinct from determination. Things destined to ruin.
- Finitude = most obstinate category of understanding. Negation fixed in itself, stark contrast to affirmative.
- Understanding persists in sorrow: makes non-being determination, non-being imperishable/absolute. Finitude unalterable quality, does not pass over into affirmative. **Finitude is eternal**.

#### Finite as absolute? Contradiction with infinite (lines 81-137)
- Finite as absolute not standpoint any philosophy would endorse. All depends on view: whether finitude's being insisted on OR transitoriness and perishing perish.
- Official claim: finite incompatible with infinite, absolutely opposed. Union declared impossible.
- Development will show: finite as contradiction collapses internally, but in collapse actually resolves contradiction. **Perishing rather perishes**.

### (b) Restriction and the ought (lines 139-266)

#### Contradiction abstractly present; limit as finitude (lines 141-155)
- Contradiction abstractly present by fact that something is finite. Something reflected into itself, developed as being-in-itself with determination and constitution. Limit within it = finitude.

#### Determination and constitution; limit as connection; restriction and ought (lines 157-189)
- Determination and constitution: otherness immanent = connection of two sides. Unity of something = reference turned back upon itself.
- Self-identical in-itself refers to itself as own non-being (negation of negation). Something's own limit posited as negative which is essential = **restriction**.
- In-itself as negative reference to limit, as negative reference to itself as restriction = **ought**.

#### Ought: double determination; finite = ought + restriction (lines 191-266)
- For limit to be restriction: something must transcend it in itself, refer to it as non-existent.
- Ought contains double determination: (1) Determination with in-itselfness over against negation; (2) Non-being as restriction.
- Finite = connecting determination and limit. Determination = ought, limit = restriction. Both moments of finite, both finite.
- What ought to be is, and at same time is not. Restriction not alien; finite's own determination also its restriction.
- As "ought" finite transcends restriction; but has restriction only as ought. Two indivisible.

### (c) Transition of the finite into the infinite (lines 268-310)

#### Ought and restriction mutually connected; finite as contradiction (lines 270-277)
- Ought contains restriction; restriction contains ought. Mutual connection = finite itself.
- Restriction = negative of ought; ought = negative of restriction. **Finite in itself = contradiction of itself**. Sublates itself, goes away, ceases to be.

#### Result (a): other finite (infinite progression) (lines 279-286)
- Result = negative of negative (its very determination). Finite has not ceased; only become momentarily other finite. Going-over into another finite, **and so forth to infinity**.

#### Result (b): identity with itself = infinite (lines 288-310)
- In going-away and ceasing-to-be, finite attained its being-in-itself; rejoined itself.
- Ought transcends restriction (transcends itself); but beyond only restriction itself. Restriction points beyond to ought; but ought same diremption as restriction. In going beyond, restriction rejoins itself.
- **Identity with itself, negation of negation = affirmative being = other of finite = infinite**.
