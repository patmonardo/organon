## Chunk Details

### Chunk 1: Introduction I: infinite as definition of absolute; not free from restrictedness

**Lines**: 4-24
**Title**: Introduction I: infinite as definition of absolute; not free from restrictedness
**Key Points**:

- Infinite in simple concept = fresh definition of absolute
- Self-reference devoid of determination; posited as being and becoming
- Forms of existence have no place in definitions of absolute (only determinacies, finite)
- Infinite accepted unqualifiedly as absolute (explicitly determined as negation of finite)
- Restrictedness explicitly referred to and denied
- But by just this negation, infinite not already free from restrictedness and finitude

### Chunk 2: Introduction II: true vs bad infinity; outline

**Lines**: 25-44
**Title**: Introduction II: true vs bad infinity; outline
**Key Points**:

- Essential to distinguish true concept of infinity from bad infinity
- Infinite of reason vs infinite of understanding
- Bad infinite = finitized infinite
- Wanting to maintain infinite pure and distant from finite: by that very fact only made finite
- Outline:
  - (a) In simple determination: affirmative as negation of finite
  - (b) But thereby in alternating determination with infinite; abstract, one-sided infinite
  - (c) Self-sublation of this infinite and finite in one process = true infinite

### Chunk 3: Infinite in general I: negation of negation; spirit at home

**Title**: Infinite in general I: negation of negation; spirit at home

### Chunk 4: Infinite in general II: finite transcends itself to become infinite

**Title**: Infinite in general II: finite transcends itself to become infinite

### Chunk 5: Infinite in general III: not external elevation; finite's own nature

**Lines**: 66-84
**Title**: Infinite in general III: not external elevation; finite's own nature
**Key Points**:

- Infinite does not stand above finite as ready-made (finite fixed outside/below)
- Not we only (subjective reason) who transcend finite into infinite
- Not external elevation (remains external to finite, doesn't affect it)
- Finite itself being elevated to infinity
- Not alien force; rather its nature to refer itself to itself as restriction (restriction as such and as ought)
- Transcend restriction; or rather, in self-reference, have negated restriction and gone above/beyond
- Not in sublation of finite in general that infinity comes to be
- Finite is just this: through its nature comes to be itself the infinite
- Infinity = affirmative determination, vocation, what it truly is in itself
