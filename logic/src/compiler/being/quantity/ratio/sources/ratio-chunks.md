## Source Analysis: Line-by-Line Verification

### Chunk 1: Ratio: quantum qualitatively determined
**ID**: `ratio-intro-qualitative-moment`
**Source Lines**: 6-50
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "The infinity of quantum has been determined up to"
- Ends: "and is thus infinite within."

**Analysis**: Ratio: Infinite quantum is unity of quantitative/qualitative determinateness - ratio. Quantum qualitatively determined, referring to beyond. Each possesses determinateness in reference to other. In otherness, return into themselves. Quantum is itself posited as relation - self-enclosed totality, infinite within.

### Chunk 2: Direct ratio: qualitative moment not yet explicit
**ID**: `ratio-direct-intro`
**Source Lines**: 52-74
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "Ratio in general is:"
- Ends: "and as identical with the quantum, becomes measure."

**Analysis**: Direct ratio: Qualitative moment not yet explicit. Quantum posited as having determinateness in externality, still as quantum. Quantitative relation is contradiction of externality/self-reference. Contradiction sublates: (2) inverse ratio (negation co-posited), (3) ratio of powers (unity self-production) → measure.

### Chunk 4: Exponent: one determinateness of both quanta
**ID**: `ratio-direct-exponent`
**Source Lines**: 85-90
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "1. In the ratio which, as immediate, is direct,"
- Ends: "namely the exponent of the ratio."

**Analysis**: Direct ratio: Determinateness of each quantum lies in reciprocal determinateness of other. One determinateness/limit of both (itself a quantum) - the exponent.

### Chunk 5: Exponent as qualitatively determined quantum
**ID**: `ratio-direct-exponent-qualitative`
**Source Lines**: 92-106
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "2. The exponent is some quantum or other;"
- Ends: "external, indifferent determinateness of magnitude."

**Analysis**: Exponent: Some quantum, but qualitatively determined (difference/beyond/otherness in it). Difference is unit and amount. Unit is being-determined-for-itself; amount is indifferent fluctuation. In ratio, each moment appears as quantum on its own - delimitations.

### Chunk 6: Exponent: meaning of both determinations
**ID**: `ratio-direct-exponent-meaning`
**Source Lines**: 108-132
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "The exponent is this difference as simple determinateness,"
- Ends: "must remain the same amount of that unit."

**Analysis**: Exponent: Difference as simple determinateness - meaning of both determinations. (1) Quantum/amount: if unit is one, amount is quantum of exponent. (2) Simple determinateness as qualitative moment: one side determined, other determined by exponent. Ratio rests solely on exponent - unit remains unit, other remains same amount.

### Chunk 7: Incompleteness: each side only one moment
**ID**: `ratio-direct-incompleteness`
**Source Lines**: 134-155
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "3. Accordingly, the two truly constitute only one quantum;"
- Ends: "posited as negative with respect to each other."

**Analysis**: Incompleteness: Two constitute one quantum. One side is unit (not amount), other is amount. They are not complete quanta. Incompleteness is negation - not general variability but determined: as one altered, other increases/decreases. Only unit altered; amount remains same quantum of units. Each side only one moment - self-subsistence negated, negative with respect to each other.

### Chunk 8: Transition to inverse ratio: exponent as product
**ID**: `ratio-direct-transition-inverse`
**Source Lines**: 157-191
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "The exponent ought to be the complete quantum,"
- Ends: "it is the inverse ratio."

**Analysis**: Transition to inverse: Exponent ought to be complete quantum (determinations come together). But as quotient, only value of amount or unit. Not posited as determinant/qualitative unity. Sides are incomplete quanta - negation qualifying them. More real ratio arises: exponent as product of sides - inverse ratio.
