## Chunk 1.1: Purpose Rediscovered, Exempt from Transition
**Lines**: 2-42
**Key Points**:
- In centrality of objective sphere (indifference to determinateness), subjective concept rediscovered negative point of unity
- In chemism rediscovered objectivity of determinations of concept
- First posited as concrete objective concept
- Determinateness has determinateness of externality within it
- Simple unity is unity that repels itself from itself, maintains itself in repelling
- Purpose is subjective concept as essential striving and impulse to posit itself externally
- Exempt from transition
- Neither force expressing itself, nor substance/cause manifesting in accidents/effects
- Force has not expressed itself is abstract inner, first has existence in externalization
- Same applies to cause and substance
- Have actuality only in accidents and effects, activity is transition
- Do not maintain themselves in freedom
- Purpose can be defined as force or cause, but expressions cover incomplete side
- If said according to truth, only in way that sublates their concept
- Cause that solicits itself to expression, cause of itself, effect immediately cause
- When purposiveness attributed to intelligence, done with specific reference to content
- Purpose as such is rational in concrete existence
- Manifests rationality by being concrete concept holding objective difference in absolute unity

**Keywords**: purpose, negative unity, externality, striving, impulse, transition, force, cause, substance, rational

## Chunk 1.2: Purpose as Syllogism Within, Self-Equal Universal
**Lines**: 51-73
**Key Points**:
- Within purpose essentially syllogism
- Self-equal universal
- Contains self-repelling negativity
- Universal though at first still indeterminate activity
- Since activity is negative self-reference, determines itself immediately
- Gives itself moment of particularity
- Particularity as totality of form reflected into itself
- Is content as against posited differences of form
- Same negativity through self-reference immediately reflection of form into itself and singularity
- From one side, reflection is inner universality of subject
- From other side, outwards reflection
- To this extent purpose still something subjective
- Activity still directed to external objectivity

**Keywords**: syllogism, self-equal universal, self-repelling negativity, particularity, singularity, reflection, subjective

## Chunk 1.3: Purpose as Concept Come to Itself, Finitude
**Lines**: 75-119
**Key Points**:
- Purpose is concept that has come to itself in objectivity
- Determinateness given itself is that of objective indifference and externality
- Self-repelling negativity one whose moments are only determinations of concept itself
- Also have form of objective indifference to one another
- In formal judgment subject and predicate determined as self-subsistent over against each other
- Self-subsistence still only abstract universality
- Now attained determination of objectivity
- As moment of concept, complete difference enclosed within simple unity of concept
- Purpose is total reflection of objectivity into itself, such immediately
- Self-determination or particularity as simple reflection into itself distinguished from concrete form
- Is determinate content
- Purpose is finite, even though according to form equally infinite subjectivity
- Determinateness has form of objective indifference, shape of presupposition
- Finitude consists in having before it objective, mechanical and chemical world
- Activity directed as to something already there
- Self-determining activity in identity immediately external to itself
- Reflection into itself just as much as reflection outwards
- Purpose has truly extra-mundane concrete existence
- Objectivity stands opposed to it
- Mechanical and chemical whole still not determined and not pervaded by purpose
- Stands on its side opposed to it

**Keywords**: concept come to itself, finitude, presupposition, mechanical, chemical, extra-mundane, opposed

## Chunk 1.4: Movement of Purpose - Realization
**Lines**: 121-164
**Key Points**:
- Movement of purpose directed at sublating presupposition
- Immediacy of object
- Positing it as determined by concept
- Negative relating to object equally negative attitude towards itself
- Sublating subjectivity of purpose
- Positively, realization of purpose
- Unification of objective being with it
- Being as moment of purpose immediately determinateness identical with it
- Shall be as external determinateness
- Objective as presupposition shall be posited as determined by concept
- Purpose is impulse to its realization
- Determinateness of moments of concept is externality
- Simplicity of moments within unity incommensurable with what unity is
- Concept repels itself from itself
- Repulsion is resolution of self-reference of negative unity
- By virtue of which latter is exclusive singularity
- By excluding unity resolves itself, discloses itself
- Is self-determination, positing of itself
- In determining itself, subjectivity makes itself into particularity
- Gives itself content enclosed within unity of concept
- Still inner content
- Positing, simple reflection into itself, at same time immediately presupposing
- At same moment subject of purpose determines itself
- Referred to indifferent, external objectivity
- To be made equal by it with determinateness of inner content
- Posited as something determined by concept, first of all as means

**Keywords**: movement, realization, presupposition, impulse, repulsion, self-determination, particularity, means
