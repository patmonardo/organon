## Chunk 2: Reflection of Concept into Itself - Self-Mediation (lines 10-41)

**Text**: "1. At first, therefore, singularity appears" through "where it is posited as concept"

**Key Points**:
- Singularity appears as reflection of concept out of its determinateness into itself
- = concept's self-mediation
- Since otherness made into other, restores itself as self-equal (in determination of absolute negativity)
- Negative in universal (by which universal is particular) = doubly reflective shine
- Inward reflective shining → particular remains universal
- Outward shining → determinate particular
- Turning back into universal is twofold:
  - Abstraction (lets particular fall away, climbs to higher/highest genus) - FALSE START
  - Singularity (universality in determinateness descends) - TRUE WAY
- False start: abstraction strays from way of concept, abandons truth
- Higher/highest universal = surface progressively more void of content
- Singularity (which abstraction scorns) = depth in which concept grasps itself, posited as concept

**Keywords**: reflection into itself, self-mediation, otherness, absolute negativity, doubly reflective shine, inward, outward, abstraction, false start, depth, posited as concept

## Chunk 3: Universality and Particularity as Total Concept (lines 43-51)

**Text**: "Universality and particularity appeared, on the one hand" through "what they are in and for themselves"

**Key Points**:
- Universality and particularity appeared as moments of becoming of singularity
- But the two are in themselves the total concept
- In singularity they do not pass over into other
- What is posited in singularity = what they are in and for themselves

**Keywords**: moments of becoming, total concept, pass over, in and for themselves

## Chunk 4: Abstract Universal Void of Concept (lines 52-73)

**Text**: "The universal is for itself because it" through "void of spirit, color, and content"

**Key Points**:
- Universal is for itself because absolute mediation in itself (self-reference only as absolute negativity)
- Abstract universal = sublating is external act (dropping off determinateness)
- Negativity attaches to abstract universal but remains outside it (mere condition)
- Abstraction holds its universal opposite it
- Universal does not have singularity in itself, remains void of concept
- Life, spirit, God, pure concept = beyond grasp of abstraction
- Abstraction keeps singularity away from products
- Singularity = principle of individuality and personality
- Abstraction = lifeless universalities, void of spirit, color, content

**Keywords**: absolute mediation, absolute negativity, abstract universal, external act, dropping off, mere condition, void of concept, individuality, personality, lifeless

## Chunk 5: Unity Indissoluble - Products of Abstraction are Singulars (lines 75-101)

**Text**: "But the unity of the concept is so indissoluble" through "the opposite of what it is supposed to be"

**Key Points**:
- Unity of concept so indissoluble that products of abstraction (supposed to drop singularity) are themselves singulars
- In elevating concrete to universality, abstraction grasps universal as only determinate universality
- This = precisely singularity (self-referring determinateness)
- Abstraction = partitioning of concrete, isolating determinations
- Only singular properties/moments picked out (product must contain what it itself is)
- Difference: singularity of abstraction's products vs singularity of concept
- In former: singular and universal differ as content and form (content not absolute form, not concept itself, form not totality of form)
- Closer consideration: product of abstraction = unity of singular content and abstract universality = something concrete (opposite of what supposed to be)

**Keywords**: indissoluble, products of abstraction, determinate universality, partitioning, isolating, content and form, absolute form, totality of form, concrete

## Chunk 6: Particular and Singular as Determinate Universal (lines 103-121)

**Text**: "The particular, for the same reason that" through "and be counted with them"

**Key Points**:
- Particular (because determinate universal) = also singular
- Conversely: singular (because determinate universal) = equally particular
- If stay at abstract determinateness: concept has three particular determinations (universal, particular, singular)
- Earlier: only universal and particular as species of particular
- Singularity = turning of concept as negative back to itself
- Turning back from abstraction (truly sublated in turning) can be placed as indifferent moment alongside others, counted with them

**Keywords**: determinate universal, three particular determinations, species, turning back, negative, sublated, indifferent moment

## Chunk 7: Particularity as Totality - Middle Term of Syllogism (lines 123-137)

**Text**: "If singularity is listed as one of the" through "the middle term of the formal syllogism"

**Key Points**:
- If singularity listed as one particular determination: particularity = totality embracing them all
- As totality = concretion of determinations = singularity itself
- Also concrete as determinate universality
- = immediate unity in which none of moments posited as distinct or determinant
- In this form = middle term of formal syllogism

**Keywords**: totality, embracing, concretion, immediate unity, middle term, formal syllogism

## Chunk 8: Dissolution of Determinations - Inseparability Posited (lines 139-177)

**Text**: "It follows that each of the determinations" through "in its determinateness the whole concept"

**Key Points**:
- Each determination immediately dissolved itself, lost itself in its other
- Each distinction confounded in course of reflection that should isolate and hold it fixed
- Only representational thinking (abstraction isolated them) capable of holding universal, particular, singular rigidly apart
- Then can be counted (relies on quantity - external to being, inappropriate here)
- In singularity: earlier true relation (inseparability of determinations) posited
- As negation of negation, singularity contains opposition of determinations and opposition itself at its ground
- = unity where determinations come together, each in the other
- In this reflection, universality is in and for itself
- Singularity = essentially negativity of determinations of concept
- Not as third something distinct from them
- What posited: positedness = being-in-and-for-itself
- Each distinct determination = the totality
- Turning back of determinate concept into itself = its determination is to be in its determinateness the whole concept

**Keywords**: dissolved, lost itself, confounded, representational thinking, inseparability, negation of negation, opposition, unity, negativity, being-in-and-for-itself, totality, whole concept

## Chunk 9: Singularity as Immediate Loss - Steps into Actuality (lines 179-198)

**Text**: "2. Singularity is not, however, only the turning back" through "is now posited abstraction"

**Key Points**:
- Singularity = not only turning back of concept into itself, but immediate loss of it
- Through singularity (where internal to itself), concept becomes external to itself and steps into actuality
- Abstraction = soul of singularity = self-reference of negative
- Abstraction nothing external to universal and particular but immanent in them
- These concreted through it, become content, singular
- As negativity, singularity = determinate determinateness, differentiation as such
- Through reflection of difference into itself, difference becomes fixed
- Determining of particular occurs only by virtue of singularity
- Singularity = abstraction which, precisely as singularity, is now posited abstraction

**Keywords**: immediate loss, external to itself, steps into actuality, soul of singularity, immanent, concreted, determinate determinateness, differentiation, fixed, posited abstraction

## Chunk 10: Singular as Qualitative One - This (lines 200-227)

**Text**: "The singular, therefore, is as self-referring negativity" through "as a mere commonality"

**Key Points**:
- Singular = self-referring negativity = immediate identity of negative with itself
- Exists for itself
- = abstraction determining concept as immediate (according to ideal moment of being)
- Singular = one which is qualitative, or a this
- According to qualitative character:
  - First: repulsion of itself from itself (many other ones presupposed)
  - Second: negative reference to presupposed others (singular is exclusive)
- Universality referred to singulars as indifferent ones = only their commonality
- If universal = what common to several singulars: indifferent subsistence taken as starting point
- Mixes immediacy of being into determination of concept
- Lowest conception of universal connected with singular = external relation as mere commonality

**Keywords**: self-referring negativity, immediate identity, exists for itself, qualitative, this, repulsion, exclusive, commonality, immediacy of being, external relation

## Chunk 11: This as Posited Immediacy - Reflective Mediation (lines 229-248)

**Text**: "The singular, which in the reflective sphere" through "yet is, precisely in its separation, a positive connection"

**Key Points**:
- Singular in reflective sphere of concrete existence = as a this
- Does not have excluding reference to other (characteristic of qualitative being-for-itself)
- This = one reflected into itself, without repulsion
- Or repulsion in reflection one with abstraction (reflective mediation in this)
- Makes it posited immediacy pointed at by someone external to it
- This is; immediate; a this only in so far as pointed at
- "Pointing at" = reflective movement taking hold of itself, positing immediacy (as external to itself)
- Singular also a this (immediate result of mediation) but does not have mediation outside it
- Itself repelling separation, posited abstraction
- Yet, precisely in separation, positive connection

**Keywords**: reflective sphere, concrete existence, this, excluding reference, reflected into itself, reflective mediation, posited immediacy, pointing at, positive connection

## Chunk 12: Singular as Self-Subsistent Differences - Essential Relation (lines 250-266)

**Text**: "This act of abstraction by the singular is" through "it refers to it just as essentially"

**Key Points**:
- Act of abstraction by singular = immanent reflection of difference
- = first positing of differences as self-subsisting, reflected into themselves
- They exist immediately
- But separating = reflection in general (reflective shining of one in other)
- Differences stand in essential relation
- Not singulars just existing next to each other (plurality belongs to being)
- Singularity positing itself as determinate does not posit itself in external difference but in difference of concept
- Singularity excludes universal from itself, but since universal is moment of it, refers to it essentially

**Keywords**: immanent reflection, self-subsisting, reflected into themselves, essential relation, difference of concept, excludes, refers essentially

## Chunk 13: Concept Posited as Judgment (lines 268-283)

**Text**: "The concept, as this connection of" through "it is posited as judgment"

**Key Points**:
- Concept as connection of self-subsistent determinations has lost itself
- Concept itself no longer posited unity of these determinations
- These no longer moments (reflective shining of concept) but subsist in and for themselves
- As singularity, concept returns in determinateness into itself
- Therewith determinate has itself become totality
- Concept's turning back into itself = absolute, originative partition of itself
- As singularity it is posited as judgment

**Keywords**: lost itself, self-subsistent, moments, returns into itself, totality, absolute originative partition, posited as judgment
