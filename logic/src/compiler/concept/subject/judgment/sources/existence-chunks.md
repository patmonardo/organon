## Chunk 1: Introduction - Judgment as Truth (lines 3-24)

**Text**: Opening paragraph through "likewise has the form of immediacy"

**Key Points**:
- In subjective judgment: same object double (singular actuality + essential identity/concept)
- Singular raised into universality = universal made singular into actuality
- Judgment = truth (agreement of concept and reality)
- But at first judgment is immediate (no reflection, no movement of determinations)
- Immediacy renders first judgment = judgment of immediate existence
- Also called qualitative judgment (quality extends to universality, which has form of immediacy)

**Keywords**: subjective judgment, singular actuality, essential identity, concept, truth, agreement, concept and reality, immediate, qualitative judgment, immediacy

## Chunk 2: Judgment of Inherence (lines 26-35)

**Key Points**:
- Judgment of existence = judgment of inherence
- Subject = immediate, first and essential term
- Predicate = does not subsist on its own, has foundation in subject

**Keywords**: judgment of inherence, subject, immediate, essential, predicate, foundation

## Chunk 3: Subject and Predicate Determinations (lines 39-87)

**Text**: "1. The subject and predicate" through "that this judgment is called 'positive'"

**Key Points**:
- Subject and predicate = names at first, receive determination as judgment runs course
- As sides of judgment (posited determinate concept), have determination of moments of concept
- But on account of immediacy, determination simple, not enriched by mediation
- Caught up in abstract opposition of abstract singularity and abstract universality
- Predicate = abstract universal (conditioned by mediation, sublation of singularity/particularity, but mediation only presupposition)
- In sphere of concept: immediacy = one that contains mediation in and for itself, arisen through sublation (immediacy of universal)
- Qualitative being in concept = universal; as being, immediacy not yet posited as such
- Only as universality = concept determination in which negativity essentially belongs
- Subject = abstract singular, immediate supposed to be such, singular as something in general
- Subject = abstract side (concept passed over into externality)
- Copula ("is") = immediate, abstract being
- Connection does not contain mediation or negation = "positive"

**Keywords**: names, moments of concept, immediacy, abstract singularity, abstract universality, abstract universal, mediation, presupposition, immediacy of universal, negativity, abstract singular, externality, copula, positive

## Chunk 4: First Pure Expression - "The Singular is Universal" (lines 89-127)

**Text**: "2. The first pure expression" through "not to divert attention from the form to itself"

**Key Points**:
- First pure expression: "the singular is universal"
- Must not be "A is B" (formless, meaningless names)
- Judgment has determinations of concept for extremes
- "A is B" can stand for any mere proposition
- What asserted in every judgment = "the singular is universal" (every judgment in principle also abstract judgment)
- If no thought given to this, either:
  - No attention to determinate form differentiating subject/object
  - Further content drifts in before mind (busy with picture, fails to reflect on form)
- Content like "Gaius" chosen because uninteresting (not to divert from form)

**Keywords**: first pure expression, singular is universal, A is B, formless, determinations of concept, abstract judgment, form, content

## Chunk 5: Objective Meaning - Perishableness and Resolution (lines 129-191)

**Text**: "The objective meaning of the proposition" through "the universal is singular"

**Key Points**:
- Objective meaning: perishableness of singular things + positive subsistence in concept
- Concept = imperishable; what emerges in division = subjected to alteration, falling back into universal nature
- Universal gives itself determinate existence
- As essence → reflective shine; ground → concrete existence; substance → manifestation
- So universal resolves itself into singular
- Judgment = resolution of universal, development of negativity (implicitly already is)
- Converse proposition: "the universal is singular" (also spoken in positive judgment)
- Subject (immediate singular) referred to other (universal), posited as concrete (something of many qualities, thing of manifold properties, actual of manifold possibilities, substance of accidents)
- Subject reflected into itself, continues across them, maintaining itself in them
- Subject inherently = universal
- Predicate = universality not as real/concrete but abstract = determinateness (contains only one moment, excludes others)
- On account of negativity (extreme, self-referring), predicate = abstract singular
- Example: "rose is fragrant" - predicate expresses one property, isolates it
- From this side: "the universal is singular"

**Keywords**: perishableness, positive subsistence, imperishable, alteration, resolution, development of negativity, concrete, many qualities, manifold properties, reflected into itself, abstract singular, isolates

## Chunk 6: Twofold Result - Form and Content (lines 192-272)

**Text**: "By juxtaposing this reciprocal determination" through "it converts itself into content"

**Key Points**:
- Twofold result:
  - (1) Subject = existent/singular, predicate = universal. But judgment connects them, subject determined as universal by predicate → subject = universal
  - (2) Predicate determined in subject (determination of subject, not indeterminate). Predicate = singular
- Subject and predicate should retain opposition of concept determinations
- When subject determined as universal, predicate should not also be universal (no judgment) → predicate = singular
- If subject = singular, predicate = universal
- Mere identity: "singular is singular," "universal is universal" (sides fallen apart, judgment sublated)
- First proposition ("universal is singular") = judgment according to content (isolated determination in predicate, totality in subject)
- Other ("singular is universal") = according to form (immediately given through judgment)
- In immediate positive judgment, extremes still simple: form and content united
- Does not consist of two propositions; twofold connection = one positive judgment
- Extremes: (a) self-subsisting abstract determinations, (b) each side determined through other by copula
- Implicitly, difference of form/content present
- First proposition ("singular is universal") = form (expresses immediate determinateness)
- Other ("universal is singular") = content (result of immanent reflection, form converted into identity withdrawn into itself, persists over against distinction)

**Keywords**: twofold result, reciprocal determination, opposition, form, content, immediate determinateness, immanent reflection, identity

## Chunk 7: Two Propositions Cannot Be United (lines 274-309)

**Text**: "3. If now the two propositions" through "and their reciprocal determination or mediation"

**Key Points**:
- If two propositions united (both subject and predicate = unity of singularity and universality) → both = particular
- This = implicitly their inner determination
- But combination arrived at only through external reflection
- Result: "particular is particular" = no longer judgment but empty identical proposition
- Singularity and universality cannot yet be united into particularity (in positive judgment still posited as immediate)
- Judgment must still be distinguished according to form and content
- Subject and predicate distinguished as immediacy and mediated
- Judgment = both self-subsistence of connected terms and their reciprocal determination/mediation

**Keywords**: united, particular, external reflection, empty identical proposition, immediacy, mediated, self-subsistence, reciprocal determination

## Chunk 8: Positive Judgment Not True - Form and Content Fail (lines 310-335)

**Text**: "In first place, then" through "must be posited as negative instead"

**Key Points**:
- First place (form): "singular is universal" - but immediate singular definitely not universal (predicate wider extension, does not correspond)
- Subject = being existing immediately for itself (opposite of abstraction, universality posited through mediation)
- Second place (content): "universal is singular" - subject = universe of qualities, infinitely determined concrete universe
- Totality = bad infinite plurality
- Subject not one single property predicate declares
- Both propositions must be united, positive judgment must be posited as negative

**Keywords**: wider extension, does not correspond, immediately for itself, universe of qualities, bad infinite plurality, posited as negative

## Chunk 9: Truth of Positive Judgment in Negative (lines 338-411)

**Text**: "1. We spoke earlier of the common notion" through "the singular is a particular"

**Key Points**:
- Common notion: truth/falsity depends on content, logical truth only form (no contradiction)
- But two concepts relate as singular and universal (truly logical content)
- Positive judgment not true, has truth in negative judgment
- Content ought not contradict itself, but does in positive judgment
- Positive judgment has no truth through its form as positive
- Truth of reason vs correctness of intuition/perception
- Positive judgment first attains truth in negative judgment
- "Singular is not abstractly universal" - predicate (because predicate, abstract universal) = determinate → singular = particular
- "Universal is not abstractly singular" - predicate (because predicate, refers to universal subject) = more than mere singularity → universal = particular
- Both reduce to: "the singular is a particular"

**Keywords**: common notion, logical truth, form, contradiction, truly logical content, truth of reason, negative judgment, abstractly universal, abstractly singular, particular

## Chunk 10: Particularity Arises Through Negative Connection (lines 413-441)

- But first negation cannot yet be determination (positing of singular = second moment, negative of negative)
- "Singular is particular" = positive expression of negative judgment
- Not positive judgment itself (latter has abstraction for extremes)
- Particular = first mediated determination (through positing of judgment connection)
- Determination not only of extremes but of connection

**Keywords**: external reflection, negative connection, underlying basis, first negation, second moment, positive expression, mediated determination

## Chunk 11: Transition Founded on Relation of Extremes (lines 443-478)

**Text**: "This transition is founded on" through "the connection also has negativity in it"

**Key Points**:
- Transition founded on relation of extremes and connection in judgment

- Connection = essentially separation, negative → positive judgment to be posited as negative
- No need to make fuss about "not" attached to copula
- In judgment, determination of extremes = determinate connection
- Judgment determination (extreme) not purely qualitative (immediate being) nor determination of reflection (exclusive, only implicitly identical)
- Judgment determination = universal within, posited as extending continuously in its other
- Judgment connection = same determination as extremes (universality, continuous extension)

**Keywords**: transition, relation of extremes, essentially separation, copula, determinate connection, universal within, extending continuously, negativity

## Chunk 12: Not-Universal is Particular (lines 480-557)

**Text**: "The just stated transition from" through "directly the particular"

**Key Points**:
- Transition from form of connection to form of determination
- "Not" of copula must be attached to predicate → predicate = not-universal
- Not-universal = particular
- If focus on negative according to abstract determination of immediate non-being → predicate = totally indeterminate not-universal
- Normally treated in logic with contradictory concepts (not-white = red, yellow, blue, black)
- But white = unconceptualized determination of intuition
- Not-white = unconceptualized not-being (abstraction from beginning of Logic, becoming closest truth)
- To use unconceptualized content in judgment determinations = uncritical (like Kant applying understanding concepts to thing-in-itself)
- Concept = true thing-in-itself, rational
- Other determinations belong to being/essence (not yet forms developed into truth in concept)
- In sphere of immediate existence: non-being = limit (something refers to other)
- In sphere of reflection: negative refers essentially to positive, determined (ground comes as third)
- In absolutely fluid continuity of concept: not immediately positive, negation taken up into universality, posited as identical
- Non-universal = directly particular

**Keywords**: not-universal, particular, contradictory concepts, unconceptualized, thing-in-itself, limit, ground, fluid continuity, identical

## Chunk 13: Negative Judgment Still a Judgment (lines 559-609)

**Key Points**:
- Negative judgment still a judgment (relation of subject/predicate, singularity/universality, form of judgment)
- Subject (immediate underlying basis) remains untouched by negation
- Retains determination of having predicate, reference to universality
- What negated in predicate = not universality as such but abstraction/determinateness of predicate (appeared as content)
- Negative judgment = not total negation
- Universal sphere containing predicate remains standing
- Connection still essentially positive
- Example: "rose is not red" - only determinateness denied, separated from universality (color retained)
- From side of universal sphere, judgment still positive
- "Singular is particular" expresses: particular contains universality; predicate not just universal but determinate
- Negative form: rose not red but supposed to have some other determinate color
- Singularity of determinateness only sublated
- Universal sphere left standing, determinateness retained (transformed into indeterminate determinateness = particularity)

**Keywords**: still a judgment, untouched, universal sphere, essentially positive, determinate, indeterminate determinateness, particularity

## Chunk 16: Infinite Judgment - Negative Infinite (lines 699-744)

**Text**: "In these two judgments" through "it is nonsensical"

**Key Points**:
- Two judgments (earlier obtained through external reflection) express predicate in positivity
- But negation of negative judgment must appear in form of negative judgment
- Negative judgment still contains positive connection and universal sphere
- Negative judgment contains universality more purified of limitation than positive
- All the more to be negated of subject as singular
- Whole extent of predicate negated, no longer positive connection = infinite judgment
- Negative infinite = judgment in which even form of judgment sublated (nonsensical judgment)
- Ought to be judgment (connection) but connection ought not be there
- Examples: spirit not red/yellow, not acid/alkali; rose not elephant; understanding not table
- Correct/true yet nonsensical, fatuous (not judgments at all)
- More realistic: evil action (crime)
- In civil litigation: thing negated as property still conceded if party had right (universal sphere "right" acknowledged)
- Crime = infinite judgment negating not only particular right but universal sphere (right as right)
- Correct (effective action) but thoroughly negative with respect to morality = nonsensical

**Keywords**: infinite judgment, negative infinite, nonsensical, sublated, evil action, crime, universal sphere

## Chunk 17: Positive Infinite - "The Singular is Singular" (lines 746-782)

**Text**: "The positive element of the infinite judgment" through "there is no longer a judgment"

**Key Points**:
- Positive element = negation of negation = reflection of singularity into itself
- Singularity first posited as determinate determinate
- "The singular is singular" = what infinite judgment said
- In judgment of existence, subject = immediate singular (more of just something in general)
- Through mediation of negative and infinite judgment, posited as singular for first time
- Singular posited as expanding into predicate (identical with it)
- Universality no longer immediate but summing of distincts
- Positively infinite: "the universal is universal" (universal posited as turning back into itself)
- Through reflection of judgment determinations into themselves, judgment sublated
- Negatively infinite: difference too great, no positive connection
- Positively infinite: only identity present, total lack of difference, no longer judgment

**Keywords**: positive element, negation of negation, determinate determinate, expanding, summing of distincts, turning back, sublated, total lack of difference

**Key Points**:

- Terms no longer immediately determined but reflected into themselves
- Judgment of existence passed over into judgment of reflection

**Keywords**: sublated itself, copula, qualitative extremes, concept, torn apart, reflected into themselves, judgment of reflection
