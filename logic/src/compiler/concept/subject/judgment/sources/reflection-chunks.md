## Chunk 1: Introduction - Universal Collected into Unity (lines 3-57)

**Text**: Opening paragraph through "that exists in and for itself"

**Key Points**:
- Subject = singular as such
- Universal no longer abstract universality or singular property
- Posited as universal that collected itself into unity through connection of different terms
- Or: coalescing of manifold properties and concrete existences
- First have determinate content strictly speaking (content as such)
- Content = form determination reflected into identity (distinct from form)
- In judgment of existence: content merely immediate, abstract, indeterminate
- Examples: human is mortal, things perishable, useful/harmful, hardness/elasticity, happiness
- Express essentiality which is relational determination or comprehensive universality
- Universality still distinct from universality of concept as such
- No longer abstract universality of qualitative judgment
- Still has connection to immediate (at basis of negativity)
- Concept determines immediate existence to relational determinations extending across diverse multiplicity
- True universal = inner essence of multiplicity (in sphere of appearance)
- Relative nature/mark not yet element of multiplicity existing in and for itself

**Keywords**: singular as such, collected into unity, coalescing, determinate content, form determination, reflected into identity, relational determination, comprehensive universality, inner essence, sphere of appearance

## Chunk 2: Not Merely Quantity (lines 59-72)

**Text**: "It may seem fitting to define" through "belonging to mediation"

**Key Points**:
- May seem fitting to define as judgment of quantity (as existence = qualitative)
- But immediacy in existence not just there (also essentially mediated and abstract)
- Here: immediacy sublated not just sublated quality, not merely quantity
- Quality = most external immediacy
- Quantity = most external determination belonging to mediation

**Keywords**: judgment of quantity, qualitative judgment, immediacy, mediated, abstract, most external, mediation

## Chunk 3: Movement in Subject, Not Predicate (lines 74-95)

**Text**: "Also to be noted concerning" through "as a direct advance in determination"

**Key Points**:
- In judgment of existence: movement showed itself in predicate (subject = underlying basis)
- In judgment of reflection: movement runs course in subject (reflected in-itselfness)
- Essential = universal/predicate (constitutes basis against which subject measured)
- Predicate receives further determination indirectly (through development of subject form)
- Progression of subject = direct advance in determination

**Keywords**: movement in predicate, movement in subject, underlying basis, reflected in-itselfness, essential, measured, indirectly, direct advance

## Chunk 4: Objective Signification - Judgments of Subsumption (lines 97-113)

**Text**: "As regards the objective signification" through "judgments of subsumption"

**Key Points**:
- Singular enters into existence by virtue of universality (in essential determination which is relational)
- Essentiality maintains itself across manifold of appearance
- Subject = determined in and for itself (determinateness in predicate)
- Singular reflected into predicate (universal essence)
- Subject = concrete existence, phenomenal something
- Predicate no longer inheres in subject
- Rather: implicit being under which singular subject subsumed as accidental
- Judgments of existence = judgments of inherence
- Judgments of reflection = judgments of subsumption

**Keywords**: objective signification, relational, essentiality, manifold of appearance, determined in and for itself, reflected, concrete existence, phenomenal, inheres, subsumed, accidental, inherence, subsumption

## Chunk 5: Singular Judgment - "This is Essential Universal" (lines 117-142)

**Text**: "Now the immediate judgment of reflection" through "proximate truth in the particular judgment"

**Key Points**:
- Immediate judgment of reflection again: "the singular is universal" (but with new signification)
- More accurately: "this is an essential universal"
- But "this" is not essential universal
- Positive judgment (positive according to form) must as judgment be taken negatively
- But negation does not directly affect predicate (predicate = implicit being)
- Subject = alterable, needs determination
- Negative judgment: "'not a this' is a universal of reflection"
- Such in-itself has more universal concrete existence than "this"
- Singular judgment has proximate truth in particular judgment

**Keywords**: essential universal, alterable, needs determination, not a this, universal of reflection, proximate truth, particular judgment

## Chunk 6: Particular Judgment - Extension of Singular (lines 146-183)

**Text**: "The non-singularity of the subject" through "therefore indeterminate"

**Key Points**:
- Non-singularity posited instead of singularity = particularity
- Particularity determined as essential singularity (not simple abstract determination)
- Not singular sublated, concrete existent dissolved
- Rather: extension of singular in external reflection
- Subject: "these ones" or "particular number of singulars"
- "Some singulars are universal" appears positive but also negative
- "Some" contains universality (comprehensive) but also disproportionate (particularity)
- Negative determination of subject = also determination of connection (copula)
- Example: "some humans are happy" → "some humans are not happy"
- Positive and negative no longer fall outside one another
- Particular immediately contains both (because judgment of reflection)
- Particular judgment = indeterminate

**Keywords**: non-singularity, particularity, essential singularity, extension, external reflection, these ones, some, comprehensive, disproportionate, copula, indeterminate

## Chunk 7: Subject Contains Universal Nature (lines 185-211)

**Text**: "If, in the example of such a judgment" through "result of the judgment of existence"

**Key Points**:
- Subject ("some humans") contains: particular form determination ("some") + content determination ("humans")
- Singular judgment subject: "this human" (Gaius - external pointing)
- Particular judgment subject cannot be "some Gaiuses" (Gaius = singular as singular)
- To "some" added more universal content ("humans," "animals")
- Not mere empirical content but determined by form of judgment
- Universal because "some" contains universality (must be separated from singulars)
- Universality = universal nature or species (result of judgment of reflection, but anticipated)
- Just as positive judgment anticipates determination (result of judgment of existence)

**Keywords**: form determination, content determination, external pointing, universal content, empirical content, universal nature, species, anticipated

## Chunk 8: Subject as Totality - Extension to Universality (lines 213-239)

**Text**: "Thus the subject that contains" through "passed over into the universal"

**Key Points**:
- Subject contains: singulars, connection to particularity, universal nature
- Already posited as totality of determinations of concept (but external consideration)
- What posited in subject by form: extension of "this" to particularity
- But generalization not commensurate to "this" ("this" perfectly determinate, "some" indeterminate)
- Extension ought to be appropriate to "this" (completely determined)
- Such extension = totality or universality in general
- Universality has "this" for basis (singular reflected into itself)
- Further determinations run course outside it
- As particularity determined as "some," so universality = "allness"
- Particular judgment passed over into universal

**Keywords**: totality, determinations of concept, external consideration, extension, commensurate, perfectly determinate, indeterminate, allness, passed over

## Chunk 9: Universal Judgment - "Allness" as External Universality (lines 243-295)

**Text**: "The universality of the subject" through "category of the in-and-for-itself"

**Key Points**:
- Universality = external universality of reflection, "allness"
- "All" = all of all singulars (singular remains unchanged)
- Only commonality of self-subsisting singulars (association by comparison)
- First comes to mind at subjective level of representation
- Most obvious reason: fits many
- In analysis: polynomial has greater universal value than binomial (more terms)
- Demand for full universality = pantonomial (exhausted infinity)
- But binomial already pantonomial (where method/rule concerns dependence, underlying function same)
- Method/rule = true universal (repeated, gains nothing through increased terms)
- Bad infinity: universality of concept = achieved beyond; bad infinity = unattainable beyond
- If allness = universality exhausted in singulars → relapse into bad infinity
- Or: mere plurality taken for allness (but plurality = particularity, not allness)
- Obscure intimation: universality of concept in and for itself
- Concept strives beyond stubborn singularity, externality of reflection
- Passing off allness as totality (category of in-and-for-itself)

**Keywords**: allness, external universality, commonality, self-subsisting, association, comparison, polynomial, binomial, pantonomial, method, rule, bad infinity, achieved beyond, plurality, totality, in-and-for-itself

## Chunk 10: Empirical Allness - Task and Ought (lines 306-326)

**Text**: "This is apparent in other ways" through "objective allness"

**Key Points**:
- Allness = above all empirical universality
- Singular presupposed as immediate (pre-given, externally picked)
- Reflection collecting into allness = external to it
- Singular ("this") absolutely indifferent to reflection
- Universality and collected singularity cannot combine to unity
- Empirical allness = task (ought, cannot be represented in form of being)
- Empirically universal proposition rests on tacit agreement:
  - If no contrary instance: plurality counts for allness
  - Subjective allness (known cases) = objective allness

**Keywords**: empirical universality, immediate, pre-given, externally picked, external, indifferent, cannot combine, task, ought, tacit agreement, subjective allness, objective allness

## Chunk 11: Subject Contains Achieved Universality (lines 328-340)

**Text**: "Now a closer examination" through "equal to what was presupposed"

**Key Points**:
- Subject contains achieved universality as presupposed (even posited in it)
- "All humans" expresses: (1) species "human", (2) species in singularization (singulars expanded to universality of species)
- Conversely: through conjunction with singularity, universality perfectly determined as singularity
- Posited universality = equal to presupposed

**Keywords**: achieved universality, presupposed, posited, species, singularization, expanded, perfectly determined, equal

## Chunk 12: Singularity Expanded to Allness - Objective Universality (lines 342-370)

**Text**: "But, strictly speaking" through "we now have to say 'the human being'"

**Key Points**:
- Should not anticipate presupposed but consider result in form determination
- Singularity expanded to allness = posited as negativity (identical self-reference)
- Has not remained first singularity (Gaius) but determination identical with universality
- = absolute determinateness of universal
- First singularity of singular judgment not immediate (came through dialectical movement of judgment of existence)
- Determined to be negative identity of determinations
- True presupposition in judgment of reflection (in-itself vs positing)
- What singularity is in itself now posited (identical self-reference of determinate)
- Reflection expanding to allness not external (makes explicit what was implicit)
- Result = objective universality
- Subject shed form determination ("this" → "allness" through "some")
- Instead of "all humans" → "the human being"

**Keywords**: form determination, expanded to allness, negativity, identical self-reference, absolute determinateness, dialectical movement, negative identity, presupposition, in-itself, positing, implicit, explicit, objective universality, shed form determination

## Chunk 13: Genus - Concrete Universality (lines 372-401)

**Text**: "The universality that has thereby arisen" through "at this point sublated itself"

**Key Points**:
- Universality = genus (concrete in its universality)
- Genus does not inhere in subject (not property at all)
- Contains all singular determinacies dissolved into substantial purity
- Posited as negative self-identity = essentially subject (no longer subsumed under predicate)
- Nature of judgment of reflection altogether altered
- Judgment was essentially subsumption (predicate = implicit universal, relational determination or mark)
- Subject = appearance
- But when determined to objective universality: subject ceases to be subsumed
- Predicate of that sort = particular (with respect to objective universality)
- Relation reversed, judgment sublated itself

**Keywords**: genus, concrete universality, inheres, property, substantial purity, negative self-identity, essentially subject, subsumption, implicit universal, relational determination, mark, appearance, objective universality, reversed, sublated

## Chunk 14: Transition to Judgment of Necessity (lines 403-432)

**Text**: "This sublation of the judgment" through "the judgment of necessity"

**Key Points**:
- Sublation of judgment coincides with determination of copula
- Sublation of determinations and transition into copula = one and same
- Subject raised to universality = equal to predicate (reflected universality contains particularity)
- Subject and predicate identical (in copula they coincide)
- Identity = genus or nature of thing in and for itself
- As identity again divides: inner nature connecting subject and predicate
- Connection of necessity (two terms = unessential distinctions)
- What belongs to all singulars of genus belongs to genus by nature (immediate consequence)
- Subject ("all humans") sheds form determination → "the human being"
- Combination (implicit and explicit) = basis of new judgment (judgment of necessity)

**Keywords**: sublation, copula, transition, equal, identical, coincide, genus, nature in and for itself, inner nature, connection of necessity, unessential distinctions, implicit and explicit, judgment of necessity
