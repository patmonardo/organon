## Overview

The one is the simple reference of being-for-itself to itself in which its moments have fallen together. The ideality of being-for-itself thus turns into reality—a reality of the most fixed and abstract kind, as a one. The one is the posited unity of being and existence, as the absolute union of the reference to another and the reference to itself. But the determinateness of being enters into opposition to the determination of the infinite negation, so that the negative is an other distinct from it. The one's own self-determining shows itself as distinct. The one repels itself from itself, positing many ones. This is repulsion—the becoming of many ones.
