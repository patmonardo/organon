## Overview

Pure Being is the absolute beginning of Hegel's Logic—the indeterminate immediate that is free of all determinateness. It is the most abstract category, containing nothing but itself. Yet in its very purity and emptiness, Being reveals itself to be identical with Nothing. This is the foundational moment that initiates the entire dialectical movement of the Logic.

## Structure

### Pure being without determination (lines 32-39)
**Indeterminate immediacy**: Being, pure being, without further determination. In its indeterminate immediacy it is equal only to itself and also not unequal with respect to another. It has no difference within it, nor any outwardly. If any determination or content were posited in it as distinct, or if it were posited by this determination or content as distinct from an other, it would thereby fail to hold fast to its purity.

### Pure being as emptiness (lines 40-47)
**Nothing to intuit or think**: It is pure indeterminateness and emptiness. There is nothing to be intuited in it, if one can speak here of intuiting; or, it is only this pure empty intuiting itself. Just as little is anything to be thought in it, or, it is equally only this empty thinking. **Being, the indeterminate immediate is in fact nothing, and neither more nor less than nothing**.
