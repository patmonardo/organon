## Chunk Details

### Chunk 1: Introduction — truth already present; need to take it up; unity uneven expression
**Lines**: 4-18
**Title**: Introduction: truth already present; need to take it up; unity uneven expression
**Key Points**:
- In reciprocal determination alternating back and forth, truth of finite and infinite already implicitly present
- All needed is to take up what is there
- Back and forth movement = external realization of concept (content posited externally, as falling out of two)
- Need comparing of two different moments in which unity is given (concept itself gives)
- "Unity of finite and infinite" = uneven expression for unity as it is in truth
- Removal of uneven determination must be found in externalization of concept

### Chunk 2: First immediate determination I: each contains the other; two finites
**Lines**: 20-50
**Title**: First immediate determination I: each contains the other; two finites
**Key Points**:
- In first immediate determination: infinite = transcending of finite, negation of finite
- Finite = that which must be transcended, negation in it of itself, and this is infinite
- In each, determinateness of other
- According to infinite progression: two should be mutually excluded, follow alternately
- Neither can be posited/grasped without other
- In saying infinite is negation of finite, finite itself said also
- Finite readily conceded as null; this nothingness is infinite from which inseparable
- Taken without connecting reference (joined only through "and"): subsist independently, each existent over against other
- Infinite, thus positioned, is one of two; as only one, itself finite
- Not whole but only one side; has limit in that which stands over against it
- = finite infinite
- We have before us only two finites

### Chunk 3: First immediate determination II: finitude of infinite; finite as self-subsistence
**Lines**: 52-60
**Title**: First immediate determination II: finitude of infinite; finite as self-subsistence
**Key Points**:
- Finitude of infinite (and unity with finite) lies in fact that separated from finite, placed on one side
- Finite, removed from infinite and positioned for itself, is self-reference
- In which relativity, dependence, transitoriness removed
- Same self-subsistence and self-affirmation which infinite presumed to be

### Chunk 4: Two pathways yield same result
**Lines**: 62-78
**Title**: Two pathways yield same result
**Key Points**:
- Two pathways of consideration seem to have different determinateness for point of departure
- Former: assumes only reference of infinite and finite to each other
- Latter: their complete separation from each other
- Yield one and same result
- Infinite and finite, taken together as referring to each other (connection presumed external but essential)
- Each contains its other in own determination
- Just as, when each taken for itself, each has other present in it as own moment

### Chunk 5: Scandalous unity; two unities; falsification
**Lines**: 80-103
**Title**: Scandalous unity; two unities; falsification
**Key Points**:
- Yields scandalous unity of finite and infinite
- Unity which is itself infinite that embraces both itself and finite
- Infinite understood in sense other than when finite separated and placed on other side
- Since they must be distinguished, each within it itself unity of both
- There are thus two such unities
- Common element, unity of both determinacies, as such unity posits them at first as negated
- Each to be what it is in being distinguished; in unity lose qualitative nature
- Important reflection: counter incorrigible habit of representing infinite and finite in unity as still holding quality they would have when taken apart
- Seeing in unity nothing except contradiction, not resolution by negation of qualitative determinateness
- Unity of infinite and finite, at first simple and universal, falsified

### Chunk 6: Double unity falsified; finitized infinite and infinitized finite
**Lines**: 105-138
**Title**: Double unity falsified; finitized infinite and infinitized finite
**Key Points**:
- Since two to be taken as distinguished, unity of infinite (itself both moments) determined differently in each
- Infinite, determined as such, has in it finitude distinct from it
- In unity, infinite = in-itself; finite = only determinateness, limit in infinite
- But such limit = absolute other of infinite, its opposite
- Infinite's determination (in-itself as such) corrupted by being saddled with quality of this sort
- Infinite = finitized infinite
- Finite as such only non-in-itself but equally has opposite in it by virtue of unity
- Elevated above worth, infinitely elevated
- = infinitized finite
- Just as simple unity falsified, so too double unity
- Infinite taken in one of two unities not as negated but as in-itself (determinateness/restriction should not be posited)
- Finite held fixed as not negated (null in itself)
- In combination with infinite, elevated to what it is not, infinitized (determination not vanished but perpetuated)

### Chunk 7: Understanding's falsification; each is itself the unity
**Lines**: 140-165
**Title**: Understanding's falsification; each is itself the unity
**Key Points**:
- Falsification: holding reciprocal reference fixed as qualitative differentiation
- Maintaining determination separate, absolutely separate
- Comes from forgetting what for understanding itself is concept of these moments
- According to concept, unity not external bringing together
- Not incongruous combination going against nature
- Rather, each is itself this unity
- This only as sublating of itself in which neither has advantage over other in in-itselfness and affirmative existence
- Finitude only as transcending of itself; within it infinite (other of itself) contained
- Infinite only as transcending of finite; contains its other essentially
- Within it is other of itself
- Finite not sublated by infinite as power present outside it
- Its infinity consists rather in sublating itself

### Chunk 8: Sublating is negation of negation
**Lines**: 167-183
**Title**: Sublating is negation of negation
**Key Points**:
- Sublating not alteration or otherness in general, not sublating of something
- That into which finite sublated = infinite as negating of finitude
- But latter long since been only existence, determined as non-being
- It is only negation that in negation sublates itself
- Infinity determined as negative of finite and determinateness in general = empty beyond
- Its sublating of itself into finite = return from empty flight
- Negation of beyond which is inherently negative

### Chunk 11: Twofold sublation: external and connection; finite restored
**Lines**: 211-242
**Title**: Twofold sublation: external and connection; finite restored
**Key Points**:
- Negation of finite and infinite can be taken as simple, mutual externality, only following of one upon other
- Starting from finite: limit transcended, finite negated
- Beyond = infinite; but in this limit rises up again
- Transcending of infinite
- Twofold sublation: partly only external event and alternating of moments; partly still not posited as one unity
- Each move beyond = independent starting point, fresh act; two fall apart
- But connection also present
- Finite comes first; transcending of it; negative/beyond = infinite
- Third: negation transcended, new limit, finite again
- Complete, self-closing movement arrived at beginning
- What emerges = same as departure: finite restored
- Finite rejoined itself, in beyond only found itself again

### Chunk 12: Infinite also returns to itself
**Lines**: 244-255
**Title**: Infinite also returns to itself
**Key Points**:
- Same case regarding infinite
- In infinite, in beyond of limit, new limit arises (same fate: as finite must be negated)
- What again at hand = same infinite that just disappeared in new limit
- By being sublated, traversing new limit, infinite not advanced one jot further
- Distanced itself neither from finite (finite just this: pass over into infinite) nor from itself (arrived at itself)

### Chunk 13: Both are movement returning to itself; result
**Lines**: 257-282
**Title**: Both are movement returning to itself; result
**Key Points**:
- Finite and infinite both = movement of each returning to itself through its negation
- Only as implicit mediation
- Affirmative of each contains negative of each = negation of negation
- They are result
- As such, not in determination they had at beginning
- Neither finite existence on its side nor infinite existence/being-in-itself beyond that existence (beyond existence in determination of finitude)
- Understanding resists unity only because presupposes restriction and finitude to remain, like being-in-itself, constants
- Overlooks negation of both present in progression
- Overlooks two occur only as moments of whole
- Each emerges only through mediation of opposite but essentially by sublation of opposite

### Chunk 14: Error of one-sidedness; double meaning; true infinite
**Lines**: 284-333
**Title**: Error of one-sidedness; double meaning; true infinite
**Key Points**:
- If immanent turning back reckoned as turning back of finite to itself and infinite to itself
- Error: finite and infinite each taken as starting point; two results
- Matter of total indifference which taken as starting point
- Distinction caused by duality of results dissolves
- In infinite progression (open-ended on both sides), each moment recurs in equal alternation
- Totally extraneous at which position progression arrested and taken as beginning
- Moments distinguished but each equally only moment of other
- Since both moments of progress, they are jointly finite
- Since equally jointly negated in it and in result, result as negation of their joint finitude = infinite
- Their distinction = double meaning
- Finite: (1) finite over against infinite; (2) finite and infinite over against infinite
- Infinite: (1) one of two moments (bad infinite); (2) infinite in which two moments (itself and other) only moments
- Nature of infinite = process in which lowers itself to be only one of its determinations over against finite (therefore one of finites)
- And elevates this distinction and itself to be self-affirmation
- Through this mediation = true infinite

### Chunk 15: True infinite as becoming
**Lines**: 335-347
**Title**: True infinite as becoming
**Key Points**:
- Determination of true infinite cannot be captured in formula of unity of finite and infinite
- Unity = abstract, motionless self-sameness; moments unmoved beings
- But infinite (like both its moments) essentially only as becoming
- Becoming further determined in its moments
- Becoming: first, abstract being and nothing; as alteration, existence, something and other
- Now as infinite, finite and infinite (these two themselves as in becoming)

### Chunk 16: True infinite as being; circle vs straight line
**Lines**: 349-382
**Title**: True infinite as being; circle vs straight line
**Key Points**:
- Infinite as being-turned-back-unto-itself, reference of itself to itself = being
- Not indeterminate, abstract being (posited as negating negation)
- Consequently also existence or "thereness" (contains negation, determinateness)
- It is, and is there, present, before us
- Only bad infinite = beyond (negation of finite posited as real, abstract first negation)
- Determined only as negative, does not have affirmation of existence
- Ought not be there, unattainable
- To be unattainable not grandeur but defect (result of holding fast to finite as existent)
- Untrue = unattainable; such infinite = untrue
- Image of progression in infinity = straight line
- Infinite only at two limits of line, always only where existence is not but transcends itself (in non-existence, indeterminate)
- As true infinite, bent back upon itself, image = circle
- Line that has reached itself, closed and wholly present, without beginning and end

### Chunk 17: True infinity as reality; ideality
**Lines**: 384-427
**Title**: True infinity as reality; ideality
**Key Points**:
- True infinity as existence posited as affirmative (contrast to abstract negation) = reality in higher sense
- Has now obtained concrete content
- Not finite which is real, but infinite
- Reality further determined as essence, concept, idea, etc.
- More immediate occasion for recalling reality: negation (against which reality is affirmative) = negation of negation
- Consequently itself posited over against reality which finite existence is
- Negation determined as ideality
- Idealized = finite as it is in true infinite (determination, content, distinct but not subsistent existent, moment)
- Ideality has more concrete signification (not fully expressed through negation of finite existence)
- As regards reality and ideality: opposition of finite and infinite grasped so finite = "the real," infinite = "the idealized"
- Concept regarded as idealization (mere idealization) in contrast to existence (regarded as "the real")
- When contrasted this way: back to one-sidedness of abstract negative characteristic of bad infinite
- Still fixed in affirmative existence of finite
