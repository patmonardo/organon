## Source Analysis: Line-by-Line Verification

### Chunk 1: Quantum as inherently self-contradictory
**ID**: `infinity-concept-self-contradictory`
**Source Lines**: 6-22
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "Quantum alters and becomes another quantum;"
- Ends: "is indifferent subsisting-for-itself."

**Analysis**: Quantum as self-contradictory: Quantum alters to another quantum, goes to infinity because inherently self-contradictory. Other is other of quantum as such - negative of itself as limited, hence unlimitedness/infinity. Quantum is ought: being-determined-for-itself is being determined in other.

### Chunk 2: Finitude and infinity: each possesses moment of other
**ID**: `infinity-concept-finitude-infinity`
**Source Lines**: 24-49
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "In this way, finitude and infinity each at once acquires"
- Ends: "each already possesses within it the moment of the other."

**Analysis**: Finitude/infinity: Each has double meaning. Finite: (1) limited, (2) sending beyond itself. Infinite: (1) unlimitedness, (2) being-turned-back-into-itself. Sending beyond is determination of infinite. In quantum, each possesses moment of other.

### Chunk 3: Qualitative vs quantitative infinite
**ID**: `infinity-concept-qualitative-vs-quantitative`
**Source Lines**: 51-74
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "The qualitative and quantitative infinite are distinguished"
- Ends: "in the quantitatively infinite process."

**Analysis**: Qualitative vs quantitative infinite: Qualitative opposition is abstract (finite/infinite opposed absolutely). Quantitative: magnitude is sublated determinateness, alterable. In quantitative infinite, finite has absolute determinateness and refers to itself - mutual reference displayed in infinite process.

### Chunk 4: Infinite process: expression of contradiction
**ID**: `infinity-process-contradiction`
**Source Lines**: 78-89
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "The process to infinity is in general the expression of contradiction,"
- Ends: "its externality belongs to its determination."

**Analysis**: Infinite process: Expression of contradiction in quantitative finite/quantum. Reciprocal determination of finite/infinite. In quantity, limit sends itself beyond and continues there. Quantitative infinite has quantum in it - in externality quantum is itself.

### Chunk 5: Bad infinity: perpetual generation, not attainment
**ID**: `infinity-process-bad-infinity`
**Source Lines**: 91-129
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "The infinite progress is now"
- Ends: "so does the beyond perpetually become a quantum."

**Analysis**: Bad infinity: Expression of contradiction, not resolution. Perpetual generation without attainment. Beyond is non-being of quantum, but quantum is continuous with beyond (other of itself). Beyond becomes quantum, which again flees to beyond - perpetual cycle.

### Chunk 6: Infinitely great and small: unsubstantial shadows
**ID**: `infinity-process-infinitely-great-small`
**Source Lines**: 131-157
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "The continuity of quantum with its other brings about"
- Ends: "which in them should have reached its goal."

**Analysis**: Infinitely great/small: Conjunction in expression of infinitely great/small. Both remain alterable - absolute determinateness not attained. Great enlarged shrinks to insignificance (opposition qualitative). Increase not approximation (distinction non-quantitative). Contradiction: quantum ought to be great (quantum) and non-finite (not quantum).

### Chunk 7: Bad quantitative infinity: perpetual movement
**ID**: `infinity-process-bad-infinity-characterization`
**Source Lines**: 159-180
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "This infinity, which persists in the determination"
- Ends: "but rather become bonded in their very flight from each other."

**Analysis**: Bad quantitative infinity: Perpetual movement back and forth (limit to non-being, back to limit). Progress is repetition - positing, sublating, positing again. Impotence of negative - what it sublates comes back. Positing and sublation flee from each other yet bonded in flight.

### Chunk 8: Concept of quantum posited in infinite progress
**ID**: `infinity-quantum-concept-posited`
**Source Lines**: 184-222
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "The infinite quantum as infinitely great or infinitely small is"
- Ends: "the concept of quantum is posited."

**Analysis**: Concept posited: Infinitely great/small are unsubstantial shadows. In infinite progress, contradiction explicit - quantum's nature posited. Quantum as degree has determinateness outside (bad infinity). But non-being is magnitude - quantum continues in non-being. Beyond sublated, determined as quantum. Through externality quantum is itself - concept posited.

### Chunk 9: Restoration of concept: negation of negation
**ID**: `infinity-quantum-restoration`
**Source Lines**: 225-244
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "If we now first look at this progress"
- Ends: "the bad infinite itself, also disappears."

**Analysis**: Restoration: Progress shows sublating of quantum and its beyond - negation of quantum and negation of negation. Truth is unity of two negations (as moments). This resolves contradiction - restoration of concept of magnitude (indifferent/external limit). Bad infinite itself disappears.

### Chunk 10: Quantum returns to quality: qualitatively determined
**ID**: `infinity-quantum-return-quality`
**Source Lines**: 246-296
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "Even the first sublating, the negation of quality as such"
- Ends: "outside it, but in it."

**Analysis**: Return to quality: Negation of negation restores concept. Quantum determined according to concept - externality posited as moment of magnitude. Quantum has determinateness in another quantum through infinity - qualitatively what it is. Quantum returns to quality - qualitatively determined. Externality (its quality) is being itself in externality, referring to itself, simple unity. Being-for-itself - infinity is in it, not outside.

### Chunk 11: Transition to ratio: quantum repelled from itself
**ID**: `infinity-quantum-transition-ratio`
**Source Lines**: 298-339
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "The infinite, which in the infinite progress only has"
- Ends: "it is in it what it is."

**Analysis**: Transition to ratio: Infinite is quality. Quantum surpasses itself seeking being-determined-for-itself. Surpassing negated - quantum finds absolute determinateness in infinity. Externality (beyond) determined as quantum's own moment. Quantum repelled from itself - two quanta as moments of one unity. This unity is determinateness - quantitative relation/ratio. In ratio, externality turned back into itself - qualitative determination.
