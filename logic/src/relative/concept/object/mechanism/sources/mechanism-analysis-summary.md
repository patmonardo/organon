## Overview

Absolute Mechanism completes the first moment of Objectivity. It shows how the mechanical process resolves into free mechanism with center and law. The center-law structure reveals the syllogistic composition underlying apparent externality. Three syllogisms emerge: absolute center, relative centers, and formal objects - each is the middle term of the others. This systematic composition prepares the transition to Chemism as the second moment of Objectivity.

## Structure

### a. The center (lines 4-165)
- **Empty manifoldness gathered into objective singularity**: The empty manifoldness of the object is gathered first into objective singularity, into the simple self-determining middle point. Essential determinateness constitutes the real middle term between interacting objects; it unites them in and for themselves and is their objective universality. In the material world, the central body is the genus or individualized universality of single objects and their mechanical process.
- **Central body as individual, syllogistic structure**: The central body has ceased to be a mere object; it has the being-for-itself of objective totality and can be regarded as an individual. The central individual dirempts itself into extremes. Three syllogisms emerge: absolute center, relative centers, and formal objects - each is the middle term of the other two. This constitutes free mechanism.

### b. The law (lines 167-218)
- **Idealized reality vs external reality**: In law, the more specific difference of idealized reality vs external reality comes into view. The object as immediate totality does not yet possess externality differentiated from concept. The moment of identity or idealization is ought. Individuality is the concrete principle of negative unity. Central point expanded inside pure ideality by difference. Reality corresponding to concept is idealized reality. The determinateness of the animating principle, the difference of concept itself, is the law. Only free mechanism has law. Law is the inexhaustible source of self-igniting fire. In ideality of difference refers only to itself - free necessity.

### c. Transition of mechanism to chemism (lines 236-278)
- **Free mechanism determines itself to chemism**: The soul is still immersed in the body. The inner concept of objective totality is free necessity, in the sense that law has not yet stepped opposite its object. Concrete centrality as universality immediately diffused in objectivity. Law is immanent in objects, constitutes their nature and power, but difference shut up in ideality. Objects not themselves differentiated in idealized non-indifference of law. The lack of self-subsistence is no longer just striving towards middle point, but striving towards object determinedly opposed to it. Center has itself for that reason fallen apart. Negativity passed over into objectified opposition. Centrality is now reciprocally negative and tense connection of objectivities. **Free mechanism determines itself to chemism**.
