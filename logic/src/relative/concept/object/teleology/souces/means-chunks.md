## Chunk 1.1: First Positing and Presupposing, Second Determining
**Lines**: 4-25
**Key Points**:
- First immediate positing in purpose equally positing of something internally determined
- Determined as posited, at same time presupposing objective world
- Indifferent to determination of purpose
- Subjectivity of purpose is absolutely negative unity
- Second determining is sublation of presupposition as such
- Sublation is immanent turning back
- Moment of first negation which is positing of negative over against subject
- External object sublated by it
- As against presupposition or immediacy of determining
- As against objective world, as yet only first
- Itself immediate and hence external negation
- Positing not yet realized purpose itself
- Only beginning of realization
- Object so determined is now the means

**Keywords**: positing, presupposing, sublation, immanent turning back, external negation, means

## Chunk 1.2: Means as Middle Term, Finitude of Purpose
**Lines**: 27-54
**Key Points**:
- Through means purpose unites with objectivity and in objectivity unites with itself
- Means is middle term of syllogism
- Purpose in need of means for realization because finite
- Need of middle term that has shape of external existence
- Indifferent towards purpose itself and its realization
- Absolute concept has mediation within itself
- First positing not presupposition in whose object fundamental determination would be indifferent externality
- World as creation has only form of such externality
- Negativity and positedness constitute fundamental determination
- Finitude of purpose consists in determining as such external to itself
- First determining falls apart into supposing and presupposing
- Negation of determining only according to one side already immanent reflection
- According to other side rather only first negation
- Immanent reflection itself also self-external and reflection outwards

**Keywords**: middle term, finitude, mediation, externality, immanent reflection, reflection outwards

## Chunk 1.3: Means as Formal Middle Term, External Linkage
**Lines**: 56-78
**Key Points**:
- Means is formal middle term of formal syllogism
- Something external to extreme of subjective purpose
- Also to extreme of objective purpose
- Particularity in formal syllogism indifferent medius terminus replaceable by others
- Particularity is middle term by virtue of being determinateness with reference to one extreme
- Universality with reference to other extreme
- Obtains mediating determination by being related to other
- Means is mediating middle term only because
- First: immediate object
- Second: means by virtue of reference connecting it with extreme of purpose external to it
- Reference which is for it form to which it is indifferent

**Keywords**: formal middle term, external, particularity, universality, mediating determination, reference

## Chunk 1.4: Means as Mechanical Object, Concept and Objectivity Externally Linked
**Lines**: 79-123
**Key Points**:
- Concept and objectivity in means only externally linked
- Means only merely mechanical object
- Reference of object to purpose is premise or immediate reference
- With respect to purpose immanent reflection
- Means is inhering predicate
- Objectivity subsumed under determination of purpose
- On account of concreteness, purpose is universality
- Through purposive determination present in it, means also subsumptive with respect to other extreme
- At moment still indeterminate objectivity
- Conversely, as contrasted with subjective purpose
- Means has as immediate objectivity universality of existence
- Subjective singularity of purpose still misses
- Since purpose in means as only external determinateness at first
- Itself as negative unity outside means
- Means for its part is mechanical object
- Possesses purpose only as determinateness, not as simple concretion of totality
- As unifying means, middle term must itself be totality of purpose
- Determination of purpose in middle term at same time immanent reflection
- As reflection, formal self-reference
- Determinateness posited as real indifference, objectivity of middle term
- Precisely for this reason subjectivity in one respect pure subjectivity
- At same time also activity
- In subjective purpose negative self-reference still identical with determinateness as such
- With content and externality
- In initial objectification of purpose which is becoming-other of simple concept
- Moments come apart, each outside other
- Becoming-other or externality itself consists in coming apart

**Keywords**: mechanical object, externally linked, subsumption, totality, immanent reflection, activity, becoming-other

## Chunk 1.5: Means as Totality, Penetrable by Purpose
**Lines**: 125-162
**Key Points**:
- Whole middle term is totality of syllogism
- Abstract activity and external means constitute extremes
- Determinateness of object through purpose constitutes middle term
- Universality is connection of purposiveness and means
- Means is object, in itself totality of concept
- Does not have with respect to purpose power of resistance
- Initially has against another immediate object
- To purpose, which is posited concept, utterly penetrable
- Receptive to communication because in itself identical with it
- Posited that penetrable by concept
- In centrality object striving towards negative unity
- In chemism, whether neutral or non-indifferent, no longer self-subsistent
- Non-self-subsistence consists in being totality of concept only implicitly
- Concept is being-for-itself
- With respect to purpose object has character of powerless and serving
- Purpose is subjectivity or soul of object
- Has in latter external side

**Keywords**: totality, penetrable, powerless, serving, subjectivity, soul, external side

## Chunk 1.6: Activity Through Means Still Directed Against Presupposition
**Lines**: 163-181
**Key Points**:
- Object immediately subjected to purpose not extreme of syllogism
- Connection between two constitutes premise
- Means has one side from which still has self-subsistence as against purpose
- Objectivity in means bound with purpose still external to it
- Only immediately so connected, presupposition still persists
- Activity of purpose through means still directed against presupposition
- Purpose is activity, no longer mere impulse and striving
- In means moment of objectivity posited in determinateness as something external
- Simple unity of concept now has objectivity as such within it

**Keywords**: premise, self-subsistence, presupposition, activity, impulse, striving, objectivity
