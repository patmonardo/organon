## Source Analysis: Line-by-Line Verification

### Chunk 1: Formal necessity — real actuality
**ID**: `act-b-1-formal-necessity-real-actuality`
**Source Lines**: 3-18
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "1. The necessity which has resulted is formal"
- Ends: "This actuality is real actuality."

**Analysis**: Complete logical thought about necessity = formal (moments formal, simple determinations, totality as immediate unity/conversion, lack self-subsistence). Unity = simple, indifferent to differences. Necessity = actuality (unity indifferent to form determinations = has content). Content = indifferent identity, contains form as indifferent (variety of determinations) = manifold content. This actuality = real actuality.

### Chunk 2: Real actuality can act — manifestation
**ID**: `act-b-2-real-actuality-can-act`
**Source Lines**: 20-38
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "Real actuality is as such at first"
- Ends: "in another self-subsistent."

**Analysis**: Complete logical thought about real actuality = thing of many properties, concretely existing world. Not concrete existence dissolving into appearance, but actuality = in-itself and immanent reflection, preserves itself in manifoldness, externality = inner relating to itself. What is actual can act, announces actuality by what it produces. Relating to other = manifestation (not transition, not appearing). Self-subsistent with immanent reflection/determinate essentiality in another self-subsistent.

### Chunk 5: Real possibility as circumstances — totality of conditions
**ID**: `act-b-5-real-possibility-circumstances`
**Source Lines**: 57-117
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "This real possibility is itself immediate concrete existence,"
- Ends: "itself the possibility."

**Analysis**: Complete logical thought about real possibility = immediate concrete existence (not because formal moment opposite, but because determination of being real possibility). Real possibility of fact = immediately existent manifoldness of circumstances. Manifoldness = both possibility and actuality, identity = content indifferent to form determinations, constitute form. Immediate real actuality determined against possibility = real possibility. Real possibility = posited whole of form (actuality as formal/immediate, possibility as abstract in-itself). Actuality constituting possibility = in-itself of other actual, ought to be sublated, only possibility. Real possibility = totality of conditions, dispersed actuality, in-itself of other, intended to return to itself.

### Chunk 6: Real possibility — contradiction, completeness of conditions
**ID**: `act-b-6-real-possibility-contradiction`
**Source Lines**: 88-159
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "What is really possible is, therefore,"
- Ends: "itself the possibility."

**Analysis**: Complete logical thought about really possible = formally identical according to in-itself, free of contradiction (simple content). But self-identical must not contradict developed circumstances. But manifold in itself, manifold connection, variety passes into opposition = contradictory. To demonstrate contradiction: fasten on multiplicity. Contradiction not function of comparing; manifold sublates itself, founders to ground, determination = only possibility. When all conditions present = fact actually there. Completeness = totality in content, fact = content equally actual/possible. In conditioned ground: conditions have form outside. Here: immediate actuality = itself possibility (not determined by presupposing reflection).

**Note**: This chunk overlaps with chunk 5 (lines 88-117), but represents a distinct logical development about contradiction and completeness.

### Chunk 7: Self-sublating real possibility — self-rejoining
**ID**: `act-b-7-self-sublating-real-possibility`
**Source Lines**: 126-199
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "In self-sublating real possibility,"
- Ends: "which is not that of which it is the possibility."

**Analysis**: Complete logical thought about self-sublating real possibility = twofold sublated (actuality and possibility). (1) Actuality formal/concrete existence, through sublating = reflected being, moment of other, in-itself. (2) Concrete existence as possibility/in-itself of other, sublates itself, passes into actuality. Movement produces same moments, each from other; not transition but self-rejoining. Formal possibility: other also possible. Real possibility: no other over against it (itself also actuality). Circle of conditions sublates itself, makes itself into in-itselfness (in-itself of other). Moment of in-itselfness sublates itself, becomes actuality. Disappears: actuality as possibility of other, possibility as actuality not of which it is possibility.

**Note**: This chunk overlaps with chunk 6 (lines 126-159), but represents a distinct logical development about self-sublation and self-rejoining.

### Chunk 8: Real necessity — cannot be otherwise
**ID**: `act-b-8-real-necessity`
**Source Lines**: 162-228
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "3. The negation of real possibility is thus its self-identity;"
- Ends: "which is indifferent to form."

**Analysis**: Complete logical thought about negation of real possibility = self-identity. In sublating, recoiling within itself = real necessity. Necessary cannot be otherwise; only possible can be (possibility = in-itself, positedness, otherness). Formal possibility = transition into other. Real possibility has actuality within = already necessity. Really possible cannot be otherwise; under conditions, nothing else can follow. Real possibility and necessity apparently distinguished; identity already presupposed. Real possibility = connection full of content (identity existing in itself, indifferent to form).

**Note**: This chunk overlaps with chunk 7 (lines 162-199) and extends into chunk 9 (lines 187-228), representing the development of real necessity.

### Chunk 9: Relative necessity — begins from contingent
**ID**: `act-b-9-relative-necessity`
**Source Lines**: 187-260
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "But this necessity is at the same time relative."
- Ends: "out of itself into contingency."

**Analysis**: Complete logical thought about necessity = relative (has presupposition, begins from contingent). Real actual = determinate actual, determinateness as immediate being = multiplicity of circumstances. Immediate being as determinateness = negative of itself = in-itself/possibility = real possibility. Unity of two moments = totality of form (external to itself). Unity: (1) manifold concrete existence = possibility immediately/positively (possible because actual), (2) possibility posited = only possibility, conversion into opposite = contingency. Possibility in condition = in-itself of other. In-itself sublates itself, positedness posited = real possibility becomes necessity. But begins from unity not reflected into itself; presupposing and movement separate; necessity not determined out of itself into contingency.

**Note**: This chunk overlaps with chunk 8 (lines 187-228), representing the development of relative necessity from real necessity.

### Chunk 11: Real necessity contains contingency — absolute actuality
**ID**: `act-b-11-real-necessity-contains-contingency`
**Source Lines**: 224-241
**Status**: ✓ VERIFIED

**Text Boundaries**:
- Starts: "In actual fact, therefore, real necessity is"
- Ends: "this unity is to be called absolute actuality."

**Analysis**: Complete logical thought about real necessity in itself = also contingency. Apparent because: necessary according to form, limited according to content, derives contingency through content. Contingency also in form: real possibility necessary only in itself, as posited = mutual otherness of actuality/possibility. Real necessity contains contingency; turning back from restless being-other, but not from itself to itself. Unity of necessity and contingency = absolute actuality.

**Note**: This chunk overlaps with chunk 9 (lines 224-241), representing the conclusion that real necessity contains contingency and leads to absolute actuality.
