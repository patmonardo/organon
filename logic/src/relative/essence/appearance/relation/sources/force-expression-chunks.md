## Chunk Details

### Chunk 1: Force — truth of whole and parts
**Lines**: 4-17
**Title**: Force — truth of whole and parts
**Key Points**:
- Force is negative unity into which contradiction of whole and parts resolved itself
- Truth of that first relation
- Whole and parts is thoughtless relation understanding first comes up with
- Dead mechanical aggregate with form determinations
- Brings manifoldness of self-subsisting matter together into one unity
- But unity external to manifoldness
- Relation of force is higher immanent turning back
- Unity of whole ceases to be external and indifferent to manifoldness

### Chunk 2: Essential relation — moments
**Lines**: 19-23
**Title**: Essential relation — moments
**Key Points**:
- In essential relation as now determined
- Immediate and reflected self-subsistence now posited in manifoldness as sublated or as moments
- Whereas in preceding relation were self-subsisting sides or extremes

### Chunk 3: Three moments — transition, translation, mediation
**Lines**: 25-55
**Title**: Three moments — transition, translation, mediation
**Key Points**:
- First, reflected unity and immediate existence, in so far as both first and immediate
- Sublate themselves and pass over into other
- Force passes over into expression
- What expressed is disappearing something that returns into force as ground
- Only exists as supported and posited by it
- Second, transition not only becoming and disappearing
- But negative reference to itself
- That which alters determination in altering reflected-into-itself and preserves itself
- Movement of force not transition as translation
- In alteration posited through itself remains what it is
- Third, reflected self-referring unity itself also sublated and moment
- Mediated through other, has this as condition
- Negative self-reference, which is first and begins movement of transition out of itself
- Has equally presupposition by which solicited, and other from which begins

### Chunk 4: a. The conditionedness of force — immediate subsistence
**Lines**: 59-87
**Title**: a. The conditionedness of force — immediate subsistence
**Key Points**:
- Force contains moment of existing immediacy
- Determined over against immediacy as negative unity
- Unity, in determination of immediate being, is existing something
- Something appears as first, since immediate is negative unity
- Force, since reflected something, appears as positedness
- Pertaining to existing thing or matter
- Not that force is form of thing and thing determined by it
- Thing is immediate indifferent to it
- No ground in thing for having force
- Force, side of positedness, presupposes thing essentially
- If asked how thing or matter happens to have force
- Appears externally connected and impressed by alien power

### Chunk 5: Force as matter — quiescent
**Lines**: 88-124
**Title**: Force as matter — quiescent
**Key Points**:
- As immediate subsistence, force is quiescent determinateness of thing in general
- Not anything that expresses itself but immediately external
- Force also designated as matter
- Instead of magnetic force, electric force, magnetic matter, electric matter assumed
- Instead of force of attraction, fine ether assumed
- Matters into which inert, powerless negative unity of thing dissolved itself
- But force contains immediate concrete existence as moment
- Though condition, transient and self-sublating
- Not as concretely existing thing
- Not negation as determinateness, but negative unity reflected into itself
- Thing where force supposed to be no longer has significance
- Force itself is positing of externality that appears as concrete existence
- No longer merely determinate matter
- Self-subsistence passed over into positedness and appearance

### Chunk 6: Force — unity and activity
**Lines**: 126-157
**Title**: Force — unity and activity
**Key Points**:
- Force is unity of reflected and immediate subsistence
- Form-unity and external self-subsistence
- Both in one
- Contact of sides of which one is in so far as other is not
- Self-identical positive reflection and negated reflection
- Force is self-repelling contradiction
- Active
- Self-referring negative unity
- Reflected immediacy or essential in-itselfness posited as only sublated or moment
- In so far as distinguishes itself from immediate concrete existence, passing over into it
- Force, as determination of reflected unity of whole
- Posited as becoming concretely existent external manifoldness from out of itself
- But force is activity at first only in principle and immediately
- Reflected unity, and essentially negation of it
- Inasmuch as differs from unity, but only identity of itself and negation
- Essentially refers to identity as immediacy external to it
- Has as presupposition and condition

### Chunk 7: Force conditioned through force
**Lines**: 159-187
**Title**: Force conditioned through force
**Key Points**:
- Presupposition is not thing standing over against it
- In force any indifferent self-subsistence sublated
- As condition of force, thing is self-subsistent other to it
- But because not thing, and self-subsistent immediacy attained determination of self-referring negative unity
- Self-subsistent other is itself force
- Activity of force is conditioned through itself as through other to itself, through force
- Force is relation in which each side same as other
- Forces that stand in relation, refer to each other essentially
- Different at first only in general
- Unity of relation at first internal and exists only implicitly
- Conditionedness of force through another force is doing of force itself in itself
- Force is positing act as pre-supposing
- Act that only negatively refers to itself
- Other force lies beyond positing activity
- Reflection that in determining immediately returns into itself

### Chunk 8: b. The solicitation of force — presupposing
**Lines**: 191-209
**Title**: b. The solicitation of force — presupposing
**Key Points**:
- Force is conditioned because moment of immediate concrete existence it contains is only posited
- But because at same time immediate, posited as presupposed in which force negates itself
- Externality present to force is own activity of presupposing posited at first as another force
- Presupposing is reciprocal
- Each of two forces contains unity reflected into itself as sublated
- Therefore presupposing
- Posits itself as external
- Moment of externality is own
- But since equally unity reflected into itself
- Posits externality not within itself but as another force

### Chunk 9: Solicitation — stimulus
**Lines**: 211-237
**Title**: Solicitation — stimulus
**Key Points**:
- External as such is self-sublating
- Activity that reflects itself into itself essentially refers to externality as to other
- Equally to it as to something null in itself and identical with it
- Since presupposing activity equally immanent reflection
- Sublates external negation, posits it as externality
- Force, as conditioning, reciprocally stimulus for other force
- Attitude of each force not passive determination
- Stimulus rather only solicits it
- Force within it negativity of itself
- Repelling of itself from itself is own positing
- Act consists in sublating externality of stimulus
- Reducing it to stimulus and positing it as own repelling of itself from itself
- As own expression

### Chunk 10: Force expresses itself — reciprocal
**Lines**: 239-307
**Title**: Force expresses itself — reciprocal
**Key Points**:
- Force that expresses itself same as presupposing activity
- Makes itself external
- But as self-expressive, negates externality and posits it as own activity
- Force in concept at first determined as self-sublating identity
- In reality one force determined as soliciting, other as solicited
- Concept of force is identity of positing and presupposing reflection
- Reflected and immediate unity
- Each determination moment, in unity, mediated through other
- Nothing in two forces determines which soliciting and which solicited
- Both form determinations belong to each in equal manner
- Identity not external comparison but essential unity
- One force determined first as soliciting, other as solicited
- Determinations appear as two differences present immediately
- But essentially mediated
- One force solicited, stimulus posited from outside
- But force itself presupposing, reflects into itself, sublates externality
- That solicited is own doing
- Through own determining, other force is other force and soliciting
- Soliciting force refers to other negatively, sublates externality, is positing
- But positing only on presupposition has other over against it
- Soliciting only to extent has externality in it, to extent solicited
- Soliciting only to extent solicited to be soliciting
- First solicited only to extent solicits other to solicit it
- Each receives stimulus from other
- Stimulus each delivers as active consists in receiving stimulus from other
- Stimulus received solicited by itself
- Both given and received stimulus, active expression and passive externality
- Nothing immediate but mediated
- Each force is determinateness other has over against it
- Mediated through other, mediating other is own determining positing

### Chunk 11: Turning back — infinity
**Lines**: 309-340
**Title**: Turning back — infinity
**Key Points**:
- Force happens to incur stimulus through another force
- Behaves passively but passes over from passivity into activity
- Turning back of force into itself
- Force expresses itself
- External expression is reaction
- Posits externality as own moment
- Sublates having been solicited through other force
- Two are one: expression of force by virtue of which latter, through negative activity directed at itself
- Imparts determinate being-for-other to itself
- Infinite turning in externality back to itself
- Only refers to itself
- Presupposing reflection, to which belong conditionedness and stimulus
- Immediately also reflection that returns into itself
- Activity essentially reactive, against itself
- Positing of stimulus or external is itself sublation of it
- Conversely, sublation of stimulus is positing of externality

### Chunk 12: c. The infinity of force — externality identical with inwardness
**Lines**: 344-369
**Title**: c. The infinity of force — externality identical with inwardness
**Key Points**:
- Force is finite inasmuch as moments still have form of immediacy
- Presupposing and self-referring reflection different
- One appears as external self-subsisting force, other as passively referring to it
- Force still conditioned according to form and content
- Determinateness of form entails restriction of content
- But activity of force consists in expressing itself
- Sublating externality and determining it as that in which identical with itself
- What force truly expresses: reference to other is reference to itself
- Passivity consists in activity
- Stimulus by virtue of which solicited to activity is own soliciting
- Externality that comes to it nothing immediate but mediated by it
- Own essential self-identity not immediate but mediated by virtue of negation
- Force expresses: externality is identical with inwardness
