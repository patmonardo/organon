## Chunk Details

### Chunk 1: Expression of force — reflective shine
**Lines**: 4-31
**Title**: Expression of force — reflective shine
**Key Points**:
- Relation of whole and parts is immediate relation
- Reflected and existent immediacy have self-subsistence of own
- But now, since stand in essential relation, self-subsistence is negative unity
- Posited in expression of force
- Reflected unity essentially becoming-other, translation into externality
- Externality immediately taken back into unity
- Difference of self-subsisting forces sublates itself
- Expression of force only mediation of reflected unity with itself
- Present is empty and transparent difference, reflective shine
- Shine is mediation which is independent subsistence
- Not just opposite determinations openly sublating themselves
- Movement not only transition
- Immediacy from which start and transition into otherness were made itself only posited
- Each determination already in immediacy unity with other
- Transition equally self-positing turning back into itself

### Chunk 2: Inner and outer — one identity
**Lines**: 33-66
**Title**: Inner and outer — one identity
**Key Points**:
- Inner determined as form of reflected immediacy or essence
- Over against outer as form of being
- Two only one identity
- Identity is, first, sustaining unity of two as substrate replete of content
- Absolute fact with respect to which two determinations indifferent, external moments
- Content and totality, inner that has become outer
- In outer, not something-that-has-become but self-equal
- Outer not only equal to inner according to content
- Two rather only one fact
- Fact, as simple identity with itself, different from form determinations
- Determinations external to it
- Itself inner different from externality
- Externality consists in two determinations, inner and outer, both constituting it
- Fact itself nothing other than unity of two
- Two sides same according to content
- In fact, self-penetrating identity, substrate full of content
- In externality, as forms of fact, indifferent to identity
- Each indifferent to other

### Chunk 3: Form determinations — immediate conversion
**Lines**: 68-155
**Title**: Form determinations — immediate conversion
**Key Points**:
- Different form determinations that have identical substrate, not in them but in other
- Determinations of reflection each for itself
- Inner as form of immanent reflection, form of essentiality
- Outer as form of immediacy reflected into other, form of unessentiality
- Nature of relation has shown determinations constitute just one identity
- In expression force is determining one and same as presupposing and returning into itself
- Inner and outer as determinations of form
- First, only simple form itself
- Second, because determined as opposite, unity is pure abstract determination
- One immediately other, is other because is one that it is
- Inner immediately only outer, determinateness of externality for reason that inner
- Outer only inner because only outer
- Unity of form holds two determinations as opposites
- Identity only transition, only other of both, not identity replete with content
- Holding fast to form is side of determinateness
- Determined not real totality but totality only in determinacy of form
- Unity coincidence of two opposed determinations
- When one taken first, substrate for this reason essentially in other determinateness
- But also only in other, just as only in first
- Something only inner, for that reason only outer
- Something only outer, for that reason only inner
- If inner essence, outer being, fact only in essence, for that reason only immediate being
- Fact which only is, for that reason only in essence
- Outer and inner determinateness so posited each presupposes other
- Passes over into it as truth, in being truth remains posited as determinateness
- Points to totality of both
- Inner completion of essence according to form
- Essence implies deficient, only with reference to other, outer
- Other not just being or concrete existence but reference to essence or inner
- Not just reference of two to each other
- Determining element of absolute form: each term immediately opposite
- Each common reference to third or unity
- Mediation misses identical substrate
- Reference immediate conversion of one into other
- Negative unity tying them together is simple point empty of content

### Chunk 4: Two identities — totality
**Lines**: 157-181
**Title**: Two identities — totality
**Key Points**:
- First identity: identity of inner and outer is substrate indifferent to difference
- As form external to it, identity as content
- Second: unmediated identity of difference, immediate conversion of each into opposite
- Inner and outer as pure form
- Both identities only sides of one totality
- Totality itself only conversion of one identity into other
- Totality, as substrate and content, immediacy reflected into itself
- Only through presupposing reflection of form that sublates difference
- Posits itself as indifferent identity, reflected unity over against it
- Content is form itself in so far as latter determines itself as difference
- Makes itself into one side as externality, other side as immediacy reflected into itself
- Or into inner

### Chunk 5: Each as totality — actuality
**Lines**: 183-231
**Title**: Each as totality — actuality
**Key Points**:
- Differences of form, inner and outer, each posited as totality
- Within it of itself and its other
- Inner, as simple identity reflected into itself, is immediacy
- No less than essence, being and externality
- External, as manifold and determined being, only external
- Posited as unessential, having returned into ground, therefore inner
- Transition of each into other is immediate identity as substrate
- Also mediated identity: each is what it is in itself, totality of relation
- Precisely through its other
- Determinateness of either side mediated through determinateness of other
- Because each in itself totality
- Totality mediates itself with itself through form or determinateness
- Determinateness mediates itself with itself through simple identity
- What something is, that it is entirely in externality
- Externality is totality and unity reflected into itself
- Appearance not only reflection-into-other but immanent reflection
- Externality expression of what it is in itself
- Content and form absolutely identical
- In and for itself, nothing but to express itself
- Revealing of essence, essence consists simply in being self-revealing
- Essential relation, in identity of appearance with inner or essence
- Has determined itself as actuality
