## Purpose

This workbook records the Part-A introduction framing for Foundation:

- chapter-level orientation,
- explicit first movement into Identity,
- transition intent toward Difference and Contradiction.

It also keeps the architectural presupposition explicit:

- a determination of reflection presupposes a determining reflective consciousness.
- in Foundation, this operates as a section-principle (not an axiom block).
- axiomatic fixation is deferred to Entity/Spectral theory stages.
- dialectic here advances from given concepts, not from mathematically constructed intuition.
- therefore Reflection theory in this section is methodological-principled, not formal-axiomatic.

It does **not** do detailed entry analysis for all parts; those are handled in dedicated part workbooks.
