## Chunk Details

### Chunk 1: Introduction — difference as negativity of reflection
**Lines**: 6-12
**Title**: Introduction — difference as negativity of reflection
**Key Points**:
- Difference is negativity that reflection possesses in itself
- Nothing which is said in identity discourse
- Essential moment of identity itself
- As negativity of itself, determines itself and is differentiated from difference

### Chunk 2: Absolute difference as simple
**Lines**: 14-47
**Title**: Absolute difference as simple
**Key Points**:
- Difference in and for itself, absolute difference, difference of essence
- Not difference through something external but self-referring, simple difference
- Essential to grasp absolute difference as simple
- In absolute difference of A and not-A, simple "not" constitutes difference
- Difference itself is simple concept
- Difference of reflection, not otherness of existence
- Other of essence is other in and for itself, simple determinateness in itself
- In sphere of reflection, difference comes in as reflected, posited as it is in itself

### Chunk 3: Difference in itself — unity with identity
**Lines**: 49-82
**Title**: Difference in itself — unity with identity
**Key Points**:
- Difference in itself refers itself to itself
- Negativity of itself, difference not from another but of itself from itself
- Not itself but its other
- What is different from difference is identity
- Difference is itself and identity
- Two together constitute difference; difference is whole and its moment
- As simple difference, is no difference; only with reference to identity
- Difference entails itself and reference to identity equally
- Essential nature of reflection, determined primordial origin of all activity and self-movement
- Both difference and identity make themselves into moment or positedness
- Difference as unity of itself and identity is internally determined difference
- Has its other, identity, within

### Chunk 4: Difference has both moments — diversity
**Lines**: 84-94
**Title**: Difference has both moments — diversity
**Key Points**:
- Difference has both moments, identity and difference
- Two are both positedness, determinateness
- In this positedness each refers to itself
- Identity is moment of immanent reflection
- Difference is difference in itself, reflected difference
- Difference, having two moments which are reflections into themselves, is diversity

### Chunk 5: Diversity — identity breaks apart
**Lines**: 98-121
**Title**: Diversity — identity breaks apart
**Key Points**:
- Identity internally breaks apart into diversity
- As absolute difference in itself, posits itself as negative of itself
- Two moments (itself and negative of itself) are reflections into themselves
- Or because it immediately sublates its negating and is reflected into itself
- Different subsists as diverse, indifferent to any other
- Because identical with itself, identity constitutes base and element
- Diverse remains what it is even in its opposite, identity
- Diversity constitutes otherness as such of reflection
- In reflection, self-identity, reflected immediacy, constitutes subsistence of negative and its indifference

### Chunk 6: Diversity — moments not determined with respect to each other
**Lines**: 123-140
**Title**: Diversity — moments not determined with respect to each other
**Key Points**:
- Moments of difference are identity and difference itself
- These moments are diverse when reflected into themselves
- In determination of identity, only self-referring
- Identity not referred to difference, difference not referred to identity
- Each referred only to itself, two not determined with respect to each other
- Because two not differentiated within, difference is external to them
- Diverse moments conduct themselves not as identity and difference but as moments different in general
- Indifferent to each other and to their determinateness

### Chunk 7: Diversity — reflection becomes external, likeness and unlikeness
**Lines**: 142-208
**Title**: Diversity — reflection becomes external, likeness and unlikeness
**Key Points**:
- In diversity, as indifference of difference, reflection has become external
- Difference only positedness or as sublated, but is whole reflection
- Both identity and difference are reflections, each is whole
- Determinateness to be only identity or only difference is sublated something
- Not qualities, determinateness only as negation
- Duplicity: immanent reflection as such and determinateness as negation or positedness
- Positedness is reflection external to itself
- Reflection in itself and external reflection are two determinations
- Immanent reflection is identity, indifferent to difference, is diversity
- External reflection is determinate difference
- External identity is likeness, external difference is unlikeness
- Likeness is identity but only as positedness, not in and for itself
- Unlikeness is external difference, not difference of unlike itself
- Whether like or unlike depends on point of view of third external to them

### Chunk 8: External reflection connects diversity — comparing
**Lines**: 210-231
**Title**: External reflection connects diversity — comparing
**Key Points**:
- External reflection connects diversity by referring it to likeness and unlikeness
- Reference is comparing, moves back and forth from likeness to unlikeness
- Back and forth referring external to determinations themselves
- Each, for itself, referred to third
- External reflection external to itself
- Determinate difference is negated absolute difference
- Not simple difference, not immanent reflection, has reflection outside it
- Moments come apart, both refer to immanent reflection confronting them

### Chunk 9: Likeness and unlikeness — separation and self-sublation
**Lines**: 233-274
**Title**: Likeness and unlikeness — separation and self-sublation
**Key Points**:
- In reflection alienated from itself, likeness and unlikeness present as unconnected
- Reflection keeps them apart by "in so far," "from this side or that"
- Diverse things from one side like, from another unlike
- In so far as alike, to that extent not unlike
- Likeness refers only to itself, unlikeness only unlikeness
- Because of separation, they sublate themselves
- Keeping likeness and unlikeness apart is their destruction
- Both are determinations of difference, references to each other
- Each intended to be what other is not
- Both have connecting reference essentially, no meaning outside it
- But because of indifference, each referred to itself
- Each is like itself; difference has vanished, each is only likeness

### Chunk 10: Negative unity — transition to opposition
**Lines**: 275-347
**Title**: Negative unity — transition to opposition
**Key Points**:
- Indifferent viewpoint or external difference sublates itself
- Negativity which in comparing belongs to that which does comparing
- Oscillates from likeness to unlikeness, lets one disappear into other
- Negative unity of both
- Unity transcends what is compared and moments of comparing
- But unity is nature of likeness and unlikeness themselves
- Independent viewpoint is self-reference that sublates distinctness
- Likeness and unlikeness disappear together into their likeness
- But negative unity also posited in them
- They are likeness and unlikeness of third, of another than themselves
- Like is not like of itself, unlike is like
- Each is unlike of itself
- Each is itself and its other (likeness/unlikeness)
- Positedness has lost determinateness as against reflection
- Likeness and unlikeness are implicitly existent reflection
- Merely diverse passes over through positedness into negative reflection
- Diverse is difference merely posited, difference which is no difference
- Negation that negates itself within
- Likeness and unlikeness return through indifference into negative unity
- Diversity, indifferent sides of which are moments of negative unity, is opposition

### Chunk 11: Opposition — introduction
**Lines**: 351-370
**Title**: Opposition — introduction
**Key Points**:
- In opposition, determinate reflection, difference, brought to completion
- Opposition is unity of identity and diversity
- Moments are diverse in one identity, so they are opposites
- Identity and difference are moments of difference held inside difference itself
- Reflected moments of its unity
- Likeness and unlikeness are externalized reflection
- Their self-identity contrasts with identity reflected into itself
- Immediacy which is not reflected into itself
- Positedness of sides of external reflection is being, non-positedness is non-being

### Chunk 12: Moments of opposition — positedness reflected into itself
**Lines**: 372-392
**Title**: Moments of opposition — positedness reflected into itself
**Key Points**:
- Moments of opposition are positedness reflected into itself or determination in general
- Positedness is likeness and unlikeness
- These two, reflected into themselves, constitute determinations of opposition
- Their immanent reflection: each is within it unity of likeness and unlikeness
- Likeness only in reflection which compares according to unlikeness
- Mediated by other indifferent moment
- Similarly unlikeness only in same reflective reference in which likeness is
- Each, in its determinateness, is whole
- Whole because contains its other moment
- But other is indifferent existent
- Each contains reference to its non-being
- Reflection-into-itself, or whole, only as essentially referring to its non-being

### Chunk 13: Positive and negative — determinations of opposition
**Lines**: 394-420
**Title**: Positive and negative — determinations of opposition
**Key Points**:
- Self-likeness, reflected into itself, containing reference to unlikeness within it, is positive
- Unlikeness containing reference to its non-being, to likeness, is negative
- Both are positedness
- Opposition: positedness reflected into likeness with itself, and into inequality with itself
- Positive and negative
- Positive is positedness as reflected into self-likeness
- But reflected is positedness, negation as negation
- Immanent reflection has reference to other for its determination
- Negative is positedness as reflected into unlikeness
- Positedness is unlikeness itself
- Reflection is identity of unlikeness with itself and absolute self-reference
- Each equally has other in it: positive has unlikeness, negative has likeness

### Chunk 14: Positive and negative as self-subsisting
**Lines**: 422-448
**Title**: Positive and negative as self-subsisting
**Key Points**:
- Positive and negative are sides of opposition that have become self-subsisting
- Self-subsisting because reflection of whole into itself
- Belong to opposition as determinateness reflected into itself
- Because of self-subsistence, opposition implicitly determinate
- Each is itself and its other
- Each has determinateness not in other but within
- Each refers itself to itself only as referring itself to its other
- Twofold aspect: reference to non-being as sublating otherness in itself
- Non-being only moment in it
- But positedness has become being, indifferent subsistence
- Other of itself which each contains is non-being of that in which it should be contained only as moment
- Each only to extent that its non-being is, two in identical reference

### Chunk 15: Three aspects: moments, diverse, in and for themselves
**Lines**: 450-594
**Title**: Three aspects: moments, diverse, in and for themselves
**Key Points**:
- First: positive and negative are absolute moments of opposition
- Subsistence indivisibly one reflection, one mediation
- Each by virtue of non-being of its other, hence by virtue of its other or its own non-being
- Simply opposites, each only opposite of other
- Not yet positive and negative, both negative with respect to each other
- Each simply is, first, to extent that other is; by virtue of other, its own non-being; only positedness
- Second, to extent that other is not; by virtue of non-being of other; reflection into itself
- Both one mediation of opposition in which they simply are only posited moments
- Second: mere positedness reflected into itself
- Positive and negative indifferent towards first identity where only moments
- Two sides merely diverse, can be interchanged, each can be taken equally as positive or negative
- Third: positive and negative not only posited being, nor merely indifferent
- Their positedness, reference to other in unity, taken back into each
- Each itself positive and negative within
- Positive and negative are determination of reflection in and for itself
- Only in reflection of opposite into itself is opposite either positive or negative
- Each is self-subsistent unity existing for itself
- Positive: positedness for it posited being as sublated, non-opposed, sublated opposition
- Immanent reflection that negates otherness
- Negative: absolute reflection, negative as sublated positedness, negative in and for itself
- Positively rests upon itself, negates reference to other, excludes positive from itself
- Independently existing opposite
- Positive and negative in themselves and in and for themselves
- In themselves: abstracted from excluding reference, taken only in accordance with determination
- Taken not as positedness, each immediate, being and non-being
- But moments of opposition: in-itself constitutes only form of immanent reflectedness
- To say positive and negative exist in themselves essentially implies to be opposed is determination of sides themselves
- Sides, as positive or negative in themselves, not outside reference to other
- Reference, precisely as exclusive, constitutes their determination or in-itselfness
- In this, they are at same time in and for themselves
