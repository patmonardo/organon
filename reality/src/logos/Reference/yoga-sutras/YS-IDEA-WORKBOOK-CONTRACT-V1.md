# YS IDEA Workbook Contract (V1)

Status: active
Applies to: all YS IDEA workbooks (SANSKRIT, FICHTE, HEGEL)

## Canonical Authority

- The Markdown files in the IDEA workbooks are the **authoritative artifacts** for the translation and philosophical analysis of the Yoga Sutras.
- They supersede any prior `COMPILER` or TypeScript representations.
- They are designed for long-form reading, novel synthesis, and philosophical rigor.

## Structure and Tone

- **Focus**: The focus is on writing **highly polished, original essays**.
- **Voice**: The writing should be scholarly, rigorous, and deeply engaged with both the original Sanskrit nuances and the structural mechanics of German Idealism (Fichte and Hegel).
- **Prose over Data**: Avoid pseudo-code, Cypher notation, or excessive structural bullet points. Prefer fluid, well-argued paragraphs.

## Core Layout

Every IDEA workbook section detailing a Sutra (or group of Sutras) should follow a three-part structure:

### I. Text and Translation (Sanskrit WB only)

- Devanāgarī text.
- IAST transliteration.
- A highly polished, original English translation that captures the philosophic weight of the Sanskrit.

### II. Conceptual Alignment / Philological Commentary

- **Sanskrit WB**: Analyze the precise meaning of key terms (e.g., _viveka-khyāti_, _pratiprasava_).
- **Fichte/Hegel WB**: Map the Sutra to specific movements in the Science of Knowing (e.g., _hiatum_, _qualitative oneness_) or the Logic (e.g., _Absolute Actuality_).

### III. Philosophical Essay

- This is the core deliverable. A dedicated, multi-paragraph essay that unpacks the mechanics, the systemic implications, and the profound depth of the alignment between the Yoga Sutras and the logical/idealist framework.

## Cross-Referencing

- The Fichte and Hegel IDEA workbooks must build upon the translation established in the SANSKRIT IDEA workbook. Ensure terminological consistency across workbooks.
