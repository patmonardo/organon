Span:

- sourceFile: `src/logos/ys/ys_iv_29.ts`
- lineStart: 24
- lineEnd: 118

Summary:
The achievement of _dharma-megha_ corresponds to Fichte's Lectures 23 and 24, where pure inner certainty grounds itself. The Absolute Light becomes a "principle-providing" immanent intuition—the self-enclosed, living "We."

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. Certainty is not grounded on anything external; it is grounded absolutely in itself as the "persisting oneness of the 'what'".

- k3. Fichte explicitly alters Kant here: Modality is read at the level of the Idea (a posteriori analytica, in-act certainty), not at the level of the Concept/Understanding.
- k4. The "Truth of Māyā" is revealed: the appearance mechanism (the Gap) operates as an absolute form of self-projection, but once understood, it becomes entirely "non-productive" and ceases to deceive.
