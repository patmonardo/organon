# Actuality/Actuality - HEGEL IDEA WORKBOOK

## Purpose

Mapping the final sequences of the Yoga Sūtras (IV.27 through IV.33) to the apex of Hegel's _Science of Logic_: the exhaustion of Actuality, the transition to the Concept, and the establishment of Absolute Necessity as the logical circle closes upon itself.

---

## Sūtra IV.27: Actuality and its Remainders

### I. Conceptual Alignment

- **Chidreṣu (Gaps) + Pratyayāntarāṇi (Intrusions) $\leftrightarrow$ Contingent Remainders**: Actuality, even as it transitions to the Concept, retains the residue of external conditioning until absolutely sublated.
- **Saṁskārebhyaḥ (Latencies) $\leftrightarrow$ Mechanical Habit**: The physical sedimentation of past relative necessity operating blindly.

### II. Philosophical Essay: The Stubbornness of Contingency

In Hegel’s Logic, the transition from the Doctrine of Essence to the Doctrine of the Concept is a leap into Freedom. But Hegel is notoriously clear-eyed about empirical reality: logic subdues, but it does not instantly vaporize, the blind mechanism of the world. "Actuality" still drags behind it the contingent remainders of its history.

Patañjali maps this exactly in IV.27. The mind is tipped toward _kaivalya_, but in the gaps (_chidreṣu_) of its concentration, the old mechanical habits (_saṁskāras_) fire off non-discriminative intrusions (_pratyayāntarāṇi_). For both thinkers, these intrusions are not refutations of the Absolute Truth; they are merely the rattling of a dying engine. Contingency remains, but meaning has been irrevocably withdrawn from it.

## Sūtra IV.29: The Absolute Relation (Dharma-megha)

### I. Conceptual Alignment

- **Dharma-Megha Samādhi $\leftrightarrow$ Absolute Relation (at the level of the Idea)**: Not a mediated, categorical relation, but the active, living logical structure.
- **Sarvathā Viveka-khyāteḥ $\leftrightarrow$ The Absolute Discriminator**: The Universal cleanly separating itself from its particular instantiations.

### II. Philosophical Essay: The Universal Discriminator

Hegel criticizes Kant for placing the category of "Relation" down in the Understanding. For Hegel, true "Absolute Relation"—the relationship between the Universal and the Particular—belongs at the absolute peak of the system: the Idea in act.

_Dharma-megha samādhi_ (IV.29) is Patañjali's Absolute Relation. It is not a blurry, mystical unification where everything melts into one. It is an algebraic discriminator. The "Cloud of Dharmas" perfectly sorts the logic of reality, delineating the pure, invariant Universal (the Seer) from the infinite, mutating particulars (the seen). It is the Idea operating in full self-consciousness, untainted by attachment (_akusīda_).

## Sūtra IV.31: Meaning over Substance

### I. Conceptual Alignment

- **Jñānasyānantyāt (Infinity of Knowledge) $\leftrightarrow$ The Unconditioned Concept**: The Concept realizes itself as infinite self-determination, free from external limits.
- **Jñeyam Alpam (Knowables are little) $\leftrightarrow$ The Truth of Substance:** The dense, heavy "Substance" of empirical objects dissolves into the transparent "Meaning" of the Concept.

### II. Philosophical Essay: The Dissolution of the Object

The climax of Hegel's Doctrine of Essence is the realization that "the Truth of Substance is the Concept." The seemingly solid, independent objects of the empirical world are revealed to be merely posited moments within the infinite, self-determining thought of the Subject. The heaviness of material "Substance" vanishes into the clarity of logical "Meaning."

Patañjali describes this phenomenal shift in IV.31. When the coverings of ignorance are vaporized (_malāpetasya_), the infinite nature of knowledge (_jñānasyānantyāt_) stands revealed. Against this infinity of pure Meaning, the catalog of finite empirical objects (_jñeyam_—the knowables/substances) shrinks to virtually nothing (_alpam_). The Concept has wholly digested Substance.

## Sūtras IV.32 & 33: The Circle Closes (Absolute Necessity)

### I. Conceptual Alignment

- **Pariṇāma-krama-samāptiḥ (Halt of sequence) $\leftrightarrow$ The End of Relative Necessity**: The external compulsion of cause-and-effect exhausts its teleological mandate.
- **Aparānta-nirgrāhyaḥ kramaḥ (Sequence grasped at terminal limit) $\leftrightarrow$ Absolute Necessity**: The end returns to the beginning; the teleological circle closes, demonstrating that the Absolute Idea governed the entire sequence.

### II. Philosophical Essay: The Law of Lawfulness

In Hegel's system, "Relative Necessity" is the realm of external causes pushing things forward in time. "Absolute Necessity," however, occurs only when the teleological process completes itself. At the end of the _Science of Logic_, the Idea reaches its terminal limit, loops back, and realizes that its end was present in its beginning. The sequence is grasped as a completed circle, self-determining and free.

This is the exact logical architecture of Patañjali's conclusion. In IV.32, the _guṇas_ fulfill their purpose; their sequence of transformations halts. Relative necessity dies. Then, in IV.33, the sequence of time itself (_krama_) is finally apprehended (_nirgrāhya_) from its absolute terminal limit (_aparānta_). The Yogi "sees" time not from within the river, but from the shore. The sequence is recognized not as a random chain of accidents, but as the unfolding logic of the Absolute Concept. The circle is sealed.
