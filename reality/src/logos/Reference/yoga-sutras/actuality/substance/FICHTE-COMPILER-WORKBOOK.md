### Entry fic-iv-034 — The Science of Knowing in Specie

Span:

- sourceFile: `src/logos/ys/ys_iv_34.ts`
- lineStart: 30
- lineEnd: 475

Summary:
The absolute closure of _kaivalya_ corresponds to Fichte's final Lecture 25. Having proven absolute knowing, the science must explain its own genesis (the descent into multiplicity) and affirm that the "science in specie" is purely instrumental.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. Fichte asks: if Absolute Knowing is unconditionally One (devoid of quality/quantity), how does it ever descend into space/time multiplicity?
- k2. The answer is Teleological: simple existence has its ground in an absolute purpose—"that absolute knowing should be."
- k3. The _guṇas_ mutating "for the purpose of the Puruṣa" maps directly to Fichte's assertion that life/existence only has value and reality insofar as it generates the conditions for Absolute Knowing to appear.
- k4. Once this is achieved, the philosophical "science of knowing" (the method, the path, the _guṇas_) is revealed to be strictly instrumental. It is a ladder. "Whoever has arrived no longer worries about the ladder."
- k5. The return of the _guṇas_ (_pratiprasava_) is the discarding of the ladder; the establishment in _svarūpa_ is the Absolute Identity where "Science of Knowing = Absolute Knowing."
