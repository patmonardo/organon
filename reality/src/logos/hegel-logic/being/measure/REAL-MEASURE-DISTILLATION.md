### RM1. Real Measure Gives Independent Measure-Relations

**Distillation:**
Real measure begins when measure belongs to self-subsistent things that confront one another. Their internal measures are no longer merely inward thresholds but real relations among bodies, materials, or other concrete somethings.

**Structural Moves:**

- Measures belong to independent things.
- Their relation is now real.
- Measure becomes a series of relations.

---

### RM2. Nodal Lines Interrupt Gradualness

**Distillation:**
Because the measure-relations remain quantitative, they admit more and less; yet because they are also qualitative, the gradual progression is broken by nodal points. A new quality appears through a leap, not through smooth conceptual continuity.

**Structural Moves:**

- Measure admits quantitative variation.
- Gradual variation reaches a nodal point.
- The new quality appears as a leap.

---

### RM3. The Measureless Reveals the Substrate

**Distillation:**
The measureless is not just the loss of order. It is the point where specific measure-relations are shown to be states of one underlying substrate. Measure thereby demotes itself to a moment and prepares the becoming of essence.

**Structural Moves:**

- Measure collapses under its own variability.
- The substrate persists through changing states.
- Real measure passes into becoming essence.
