### CG1. Cognition Is the Idea in Judgment

**Distillation:**
Cognition begins when the immediate Idea of life is judged into subjective and objective sides. The concept now confronts itself as subject matter. The Idea is no longer immediate life, but not yet fully reconciled with itself.

**Structural Moves:**

- Cognition is the Idea in judgment.
- The concept confronts itself as object.
- Reconciliation is not yet complete.

---

### CG2. The Idea of the True Carries the Theoretical Side

**Distillation:**
The idea of the true develops the theoretical impulse of cognition. It moves through analytic and synthetic forms and seeks to comprehend objectivity as it is. Yet theoretical cognition alone still leaves the unity of concept and object incomplete.

**Structural Moves:**

- Truth first appears as theoretical cognition.
- Analytic and synthetic cognition are both necessary.
- Theoretical cognition remains incomplete.

---

### CG3. The Idea of the Good Carries the Practical Side and Opens the Absolute Idea

**Distillation:**
The idea of the good gives cognition practical self-determination. But as long as good remains an ought over against actuality, contradiction persists. The practical Idea therefore drives itself beyond this split into the Absolute Idea.

**Structural Moves:**

- The good is practical self-determination.
- The ought reveals the finitude of this form.
- Cognition passes into Absolute Idea.
