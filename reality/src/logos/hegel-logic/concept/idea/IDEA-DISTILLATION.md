### ID1. The Idea Is the Adequate Concept

**Distillation:**
The Idea is the adequate concept, the true as such, where concept and objectivity are no longer merely related but correspond. Whatever has truth has it only by virtue of this unity. The Idea is therefore not a merely regulative beyond but the actual measure of truth.

**Structural Moves:**

- The Idea is the adequate concept.
- Truth belongs only through this unity.
- The Idea is not a mere beyond.

---

### ID2. Life Is the Idea in Immediate Form

**Distillation:**
In `life/`, the Idea first appears immediately. Concept and objectivity correspond, but only in the living individual's process of self-maintenance, reproduction, and genus. The Idea is present, but it has not yet fully liberated its concept into explicit self-relation.

**Structural Moves:**

- Life is the immediate Idea.
- The Idea first exists as living process.
- The chapter turns toward cognition.

---

### ID3. Cognition Is the Idea in Judgment and Doubling

**Distillation:**
In `cognition/`, the Idea doubles itself into subjective concept and objective concept. It first pursues truth theoretically, then the good practically. Cognition is therefore the Idea no longer immediate, but still divided between concept and objectivity.

**Structural Moves:**

- The Idea is doubled in judgment.
- Theoretical and practical forms are distinguished.
- Cognition turns toward absolute self-reconciliation.

---

### ID4. Absolute Idea Is the Truth of Life and Cognition

**Distillation:**
In the `speculation/` local stack, the third chapter appears as the Absolute Idea. Here theoretical and practical Idea are reconciled, and method is grasped as the self-movement of the concept itself. The Idea is now free, self-knowing, and at rest in its own activity.

**Structural Moves:**

- Theoretical and practical Idea are unified.
- Method is the concept's own activity.
- The Idea knows itself as absolute truth.

---

### ID5. The Idea Freely Releases Itself Into Nature

**Distillation:**
The Absolute Idea does not terminate as inert completion. In the freedom of method, it determines itself as external idea, that is, nature. The Idea's completion is therefore also a discharge into a new sphere.

**Structural Moves:**

- Completion is not inert repose.
- The Idea determines itself as external idea.
- The chapter passes into nature.
