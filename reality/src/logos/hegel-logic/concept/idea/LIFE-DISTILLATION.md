### LF1. Life Is the Immediate Idea

**Distillation:**
Life is the immediate Idea because concept and objectivity correspond in one living unity. Yet this unity is not static identity. It exists only as negative self-relation, as living individuality that maintains itself through difference.

**Structural Moves:**

- Life is the immediate Idea.
- Concept and objectivity correspond.
- This unity exists only through negativity.

---

### LF2. The Living Individual Must Mediate Itself Through Process

**Distillation:**
The living individual cannot remain in immediate self-identity. It must enter into life-process, where contradiction, need, and pain become the form of self-maintenance. Externality is taken up and sublated in living activity.

**Structural Moves:**

- Individuality is unstable as immediacy.
- Life-process is necessary mediation.
- Externality is sublated in self-maintenance.

---

### LF3. Genus Carries Life Beyond Immediate Singularity

**Distillation:**
In genus, life becomes concrete universality through relation to otherness like itself. Life thereby overcomes the closure of singular individuality. The Idea in life thus withdraws from immediacy and passes into cognition.

**Structural Moves:**

- Genus is concrete universality.
- Singularity is overcome through relation.
- Life turns into cognition.
