### CH1. Chemism Makes Determinateness Belong to the Object Itself

**Distillation:**
Chemism departs from mechanism because the object's reference to another now belongs to its own nature. The object is no longer indifferent to determinateness. Its concept includes a tension that makes it seek completion in relation.

**Structural Moves:**

- Determinateness is no longer merely external.
- Relation to another belongs to the object's nature.
- The object is driven into process.

---

### CH2. Chemical Process Moves Through Affinity, Neutrality, and Re-Excitation

**Distillation:**
Chemical process begins in affinity, passes through a medium of communication, and reaches a neutral product in which prior tension is resolved. Yet this neutrality is only formal, so the process reopens from within and releases elemental moments.

**Structural Moves:**

- Affinity begins the process.
- Neutral product resolves the first contradiction.
- Neutrality is only formal.
- Process reopens from within.

---

### CH3. Chemism Sublates Itself Into Teleology

**Distillation:**
Because the chemical object is driven by its own contradiction, chemism cannot remain a closed sphere of reciprocal tension. Its return to elemental determination already points beyond itself. The higher truth is a unity that is no longer merely sought in process but explicitly posited as purpose.

**Structural Moves:**

- Chemism cannot rest in tension and neutrality alone.
- Elemental return is not final.
- Teleology is the higher truth of chemism.
