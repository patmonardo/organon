### MC1. Mechanism Begins from External Self-Subsistent Objects

**Distillation:**
Mechanism is the first form of objectivity because objects stand as complete totalities outside one another. Their unity is real, but it does not yet belong to them as self-determining concept. Connection remains external arrangement, composition, or law.

**Structural Moves:**

- Objects stand as self-subsistent totals.
- Their connection is external.
- The concept is present only implicitly.

---

### MC2. The Mechanical Object Is Indifferent to Its Determinateness

**Distillation:**
The mechanical object contains determinateness, but not as its own negative unity. Its parts and connections can be rearranged without touching its inward concept. Determination therefore lies outside it, in other objects or in an external order.

**Structural Moves:**

- Determinateness belongs to the object only externally.
- Object and order remain indifferent to each other.
- Determinism stretches into an empty infinite regress.

---

### MC3. The Mechanical Process Communicates, Reacts, and Returns Into Law

**Distillation:**
Mechanical process first communicates a universal determinateness, then particularizes it in reaction, and finally returns into a product that is only externally determined. Yet this process does not leave things where they began: it yields center and law as the immanent truth of mechanical interaction.

**Structural Moves:**

- Communication posits shared universality.
- Reaction restores singularity and resistance.
- Product yields center and law.
- Mechanism points beyond itself.
