### CN1. The Concept as Such Is Not a Dead Determination

**Distillation:**
The chapter begins by refusing the ordinary doctrine that treats the concept as a fixed determination handled by the understanding. What is at stake is not the dead concept but the concept as such: the living totality that determines itself from within.

**Structural Moves:**

- The ordinary faculty doctrine is refused.
- The concept is treated as self-determining totality.
- The chapter begins from living universality.

---

### CN2. Universality, Particularity, and Singularity Are the Concept's Own Moments

**Distillation:**
The concept contains universality, particularity, and singularity as its own triadic moments. These are not externally attached categories. Because positedness is identical here with being-in-and-for-itself, each moment is both a determination and the whole concept within that determination.

**Structural Moves:**

- The concept is triadic.
- The moments are internally generated.
- Each moment is also the whole.

---

### CN3. Universality Determines Itself Into Particularity

**Distillation:**
Universality is not empty sameness. Because the concept is totality, universality determines itself and immediately becomes only one moment over against the distinctness of the others. This self-limitation is precisely particularity.

**Structural Moves:**

- Universality is active self-reference.
- It immediately limits itself.
- Particularity is generated from universality itself.

---

### CN4. Singularity Recollects the Concept and Opens Judgment

**Distillation:**
Singularity is the concept reflecting itself out of difference into absolute negativity. In this return, the concept also steps into its otherness. Singularity is therefore not a terminal point but the hinge by which the concept becomes judgment.

**Structural Moves:**

- Singularity gathers the concept from difference.
- This gathering is absolute negativity.
- The chapter turns into judgment.
