### JD1. Judgment Is the Concept's Own Self-Division

**Distillation:**
Judgment is not an external act performed on ready-made concepts. It is the concept itself positing its own determinations over against one another. In that sense, judgment is the concept in the form of self-diremption.

**Structural Moves:**

- Judgment belongs to the concept itself.
- The concept posits its determinations as opposed.
- The chapter begins from self-diremption.

---

### JD2. Judgment Is the First Reality of the Concept

**Distillation:**
Judgment is the concept's first realization because here the concept enters determinate existence. Subject and predicate stand as self-subsistent totalities, yet their unity persists within them. This gives judgment its characteristic tension: the concept has become real, but only in a divided and unstable form.

**Structural Moves:**

- Judgment is the first reality of the concept.
- Subject and predicate stand as self-subsisting sides.
- Their unity is implicit rather than fulfilled.

---

### JD3. Subject and Predicate Receive Their Truth Only In the Judgment

**Distillation:**
Subject and predicate are not ready-made contents simply glued together. Before the judgment, they are only names or indeterminate poles. The predicate first gives the subject its conceptual truth, and the copula states more than grammar: it says that the predicate belongs to the being of the subject.

**Structural Moves:**

- Subject and predicate are first indeterminate.
- The predicate gives the subject conceptual truth.
- The copula states ontological belonging.

---

### JD4. Judgment Passes Beyond Itself Into Syllogism

**Distillation:**
Judgment asserts identity while still preserving subject and predicate as distinct extremes. This contradiction is the chapter's motor. The unity it says is precisely what it has not yet fully posited. Judgment is therefore driven beyond itself into syllogism, where mediation becomes explicit.

**Structural Moves:**

- Judgment says identity while preserving separation.
- This contradiction drives the chapter forward.
- Mediation is not yet explicit.
- Judgment turns into syllogism.
