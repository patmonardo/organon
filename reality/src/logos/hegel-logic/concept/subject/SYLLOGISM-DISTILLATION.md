### SY1. Syllogism Restores the Concept Out of Judgment

**Distillation:**
Syllogism is the restoration of the concept out of the diremption of judgment. What judgment held only as an internal unity is now explicitly posited as mediation. The extremes remain, but their unity is no longer missing.

**Structural Moves:**

- Syllogism restores the concept out of judgment.
- The unity of the extremes is explicitly posited.
- Mediation becomes the chapter's central form.

---

### SY2. The Rational Is This Mediated Unity

**Distillation:**
The syllogism is the fully posited concept and therefore the rational. Rationality is not an empty formal procedure, nor an immediate possession of content without mediation. It is the concept determining itself through the mediated unity of its extremes.

**Structural Moves:**

- The syllogism is the fully posited concept.
- The rational is mediated unity.
- Rationality is replete with content.

---

### SY3. The Middle Term Is the Essence of the Syllogism

**Distillation:**
What matters in the syllogism is the middle term. The middle is not a merely external connector but the unity and ground of the extremes. The understanding distorts the syllogism by spatializing the middle and treating mediation as an external link.

**Structural Moves:**

- The middle term is the true center.
- It is the unity and ground of the extremes.
- Externalized mediation is the defect of understanding.

---

### SY4. Existence, Reflection, and Necessity Carry the Syllogism to Objectivity

**Distillation:**
The syllogism unfolds through existence, reflection, and necessity. In existence, immediate mediation proves unstable. In reflection, mediation becomes gathered and explicit. In necessity, the middle becomes the objective nature of the fact. At that point the syllogism reaches its truth and passes into objectivity.

**Structural Moves:**

- Existence gives immediate but unstable mediation.
- Reflection deepens mediation.
- Necessity gives objective universality.
- The chapter passes into objectivity.
