### §1. Particularity Is the Universal's Own Immanent Determinateness

**Lines:** 4–47

**Distillation:**
As concept-determinateness, particularity is not a limit set over against an external other. It is the universal's own immanent moment. The particular therefore contains universality as its substance and differs from other particulars only within that common universality. Because all particulars share one universal, their diversity is itself gathered into a totality. Particularity is thus both totality and simple determinateness, the essential principle of articulated difference.

**Structural Moves:**

- **No external limit:** Particularity belongs within the universal.
- **Shared substance:** Particulars differ only within one universality.
- **Totality of difference:** Diversity is itself conceptually gathered.

### §2. The Universal Determines Itself as Particular and Divides Itself Truly

**Lines:** 52–95

**Distillation:**
The particular is the universal itself in the form of its difference or outwardly reflective shine. Since there is no other outside the universal from which it could be distinguished, the universal determines itself and is thus itself the particular. This yields the only true logical division: the concept sets itself on one side as immediate universality and thereby makes itself particular. The opposed sides are not merely externally coordinated. Their opposition is one determinateness, the simple negativity within the universal itself.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Self-differentiation:** The universal itself becomes the particular.
- **True division:** Logical division is generated from within the concept.
- **One negativity:** Opposed sides are rooted in one determinateness.

### §3. Particularity Is the Truth of Earlier Differences

**Lines:** 97–131

**Distillation:**
What earlier logical forms treated as separate differences now attain their truth in the concept. Immediate being, reflection-determinations, whole and parts, cause and effect all already carried conceptual unity implicitly, but they did not yet have the form of universality. In particularity, difference is no longer merely a relation between externally given terms. It is grasped as one determinate concept. This is why even cause and effect are not two truly separate concepts, but one determinate concept under the form of causality.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Earlier forms reinterpreted:** Prior differences are shown to have conceptual truth.
- **From relation to concept:** Difference is gathered into one determinate universal.
- **Result:** Particularity is the first truthful form of articulated difference.

### §4. Completeness and Natural Multiplicity Do Not Refute the Concept

**Lines:** 133–169

**Distillation:**
The determinateness of particularity is complete in the difference between universal and particular. Nature's many species do not overturn this logical truth; they show instead nature's impotence to exhibit the rigor of the concept. Natural multiplicity and pictorial imagination both let the concept's differences run loose in external manifoldness. They may bear traces of the concept, but they do not display it in its strict truth. The concept remains the power that can freely release its differences without losing itself in them.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Logical completeness:** Particularity is complete in its own internal division.
- **Nature's impotence:** External multiplicity is not conceptual rigor.
- **Freedom of the concept:** The concept can let difference go free without ceasing to be concept.

### §5. The Determinate Concept Appears as Abstract Universality

**Lines:** 171–236

**Distillation:**
When the concept determines itself, it gives one of its moments the form of immediate existence. In that form the universality that clothes the determinate becomes abstract universality. The particular has this universality as its essence, but once the difference is posited as being, universality and determinateness fall apart into form and content. The result is the determinate concept as ordinarily grasped: not false in itself, but an unconceptualized concept, because its mediation is present only implicitly and not yet posited.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Posited determinateness:** The concept gives difference the form of immediacy.
- **Abstract universality:** Universality appears as form over against content.
- **Unconceptualized concept:** Mediation remains unposited.

### §6. Ordinary Understanding Mistakes This Abstract Universal for the Concept Itself

**Lines:** 238–291

**Distillation:**
What is commonly called a determinate concept is usually just this abstract universal. Understanding proceeds by such fixed determinations and for that reason does not move beyond finitude and necessity. Even the highest abstraction remains determinate through its opposition to determinateness and is therefore still conceptually deficient. The charge that the concept is empty misses the point entirely. The true issue is not emptiness as such, but whether the determinateness is the principle of the concept's own differentiation and realization.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Ordinary misuse:** The abstract universal is mistaken for the concept as such.
- **Limit of understanding:** Fixed determination cannot reach living conceptuality.
- **True issue:** The concept requires self-differentiating determinateness.

### §7. The Infinite Force of Understanding Both Fixes and Sets Finite Determinations in Motion

**Lines:** 293–426

**Distillation:**
Understanding is held in low regard because it fixes finite determinacies and gives them the rigid form of abstract universality. Yet this fixity is also its greatness. By splitting the concrete into sharply defined determinations, understanding gives them the hardness through which they become capable of dialectical transition. Reason's task is not to despise this work but to bring those fixed determinations back into conceptual unity. The very universality that stabilizes the finite also exposes its disproportion to the concept and kindles its dissolution into the opposite. Understanding is therefore both necessary and self-overcoming.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Fixity of understanding:** Finite determinacies receive stable form.
- **Positive force:** This sharpness is the condition of dialectic.
- **Transition to reason:** Fixed determinations are driven back into unity.

---

### §8. Particularity Ends by Positing Determinate Universality as Singularity

**Lines:** 428–452

**Distillation:**
When difference is fully granted its due within the concept, determinateness united with universality becomes self-referring determinateness, determinate determinateness. This is singularity. Thus particularity does not remain merely the second moment of the concept. It turns back into the concept's absolute self-return and, in doing so, passes over into singularity as the posited loss of its merely outward distinction.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Difference fully posited:** Determinateness is gathered into universality.
- **Determinate determinateness:** Particularity becomes self-referring.
- **Transition:** Particularity passes into singularity.
