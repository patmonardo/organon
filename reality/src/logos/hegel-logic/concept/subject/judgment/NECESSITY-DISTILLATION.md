### §1. Objective Universality Appears as Genus and Species

**Lines:** 1–25

**Distillation:**
The judgment of necessity begins when universality is no longer merely reflective but objective and existing in and for itself. This objective universality is distinguished from substance by belonging to the concept and containing its own immanent differentiation. In judgment it first appears as genus and species: universality as substantial basis, and particularity as the differentiated determination grounded within it.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Objective universality:** Universality is now in-and-for-itself.
- **Immanent difference:** Distinction belongs to the universal itself.
- **Genus and species:** Universality takes the form of grounded articulation.

---

### §2. The Categorical Judgment Grounds Predication in Essence

**Lines:** 26–110

**Distillation:**
In the categorical judgment, the predicate expresses the subject's immanent nature rather than an accidental property. Yet this necessity is still immediate. The subject remains externally singular with respect to its universal basis, and the connection between subject and predicate is still inward rather than fully posited. Categorical judgment therefore overcomes accidentality but does not yet bring its necessity fully to the surface.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Essential predication:** The predicate belongs to the subject's nature.
- **Necessity still inward:** The connection is not yet explicit in form.
- **Limit:** Immediate singularity remains contingent.

---

### §3. The Hypothetical Judgment Makes Necessary Connection Explicit

**Lines:** 111–207

**Distillation:**
The hypothetical judgment explicitly posits what the categorical judgment only contained inwardly: that the being of one term is the being of another. Immediate existence is treated as conditioned existence. The two sides no longer stand simply as subject and predicate but as moments of one necessary relation. Yet the form still appears propositionally rather than fully conceptually, because the identity of the connected terms is not yet gathered into totalized universality.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Condition and conditioned:** One term's being lies in another.
- **Necessity made explicit:** Inner connection becomes posited relation.
- **Residual defect:** The unity is not yet the explicit totality of the concept.

---

### §4. The Disjunctive Judgment Posits the Concept Itself

**Lines:** 208–451

**Distillation:**
The disjunctive judgment is the truth of necessity-judgment because here objective universality is united with its complete form. The subject is genus as concrete universality, and the predicate is the totality of its differentiated species. The negative unity of the concept is now explicit as the either-or that differentiates and binds the species at once. In this way subject and predicate are shown to have the same content. Their copula is no longer an external link but the concept itself re-emerging as posited unity. The judgment of necessity therefore rises into the judgment of the concept.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Concrete universality:** Genus contains its differentiated totality.
- **Negative unity:** Species exclude one another within one conceptual sphere.
- **Identity of extremes:** Subject and predicate coincide in content.
- **Transition:** The judgment of necessity becomes judgment of the concept.
