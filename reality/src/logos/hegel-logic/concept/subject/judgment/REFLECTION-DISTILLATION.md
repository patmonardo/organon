### §1. Reflection Replaces Inherence with Subsumption

**Lines:** 1–115

**Distillation:**
In the judgment of reflection, the predicate is no longer an immediate quality inhering in the subject but a reflected universality that extends across a multiplicity of appearances. The subject is now taken as a phenomenal singular or concrete existence, while the predicate expresses an essential relational determination. For that reason this judgment is no longer one of inherence but of subsumption: the subject falls under a universality that is supposed to be its inner essence, though that universality is still tied to reflection and appearance.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Reflected predicate:** Universality now gathers manifold appearances.
- **Phenomenal subject:** The subject is a singular concrete existence.
- **Subsumption:** The universal stands over against the subject as its essential determination.

### §2. Singular and Particular Reflection-Judgments Expand the Subject Beyond the This

**Lines:** 116–243

**Distillation:**
The singular judgment of reflection still begins from a this, but it immediately points beyond bare singularity toward an essential universal. The particular judgment expands that this to some singulars and reveals both positive and negative value within the same form. Through this movement the immediate singular is widened, yet the widening remains indeterminate and externally reflective. The subject is being generalized, but not yet in a way adequate to the concept.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Singular reflection:** The this is referred to essential universality.
- **Particular widening:** Some singulars replace the isolated this.
- **Indeterminacy remains:** Reflection still works through external collection.

---

### §3. Universal Judgment Produces Objective Universality and Reverses the Relation

**Lines:** 244–434

**Distillation:**
The universal judgment first appears as allness, the external universality of all singulars taken together. But this allness cannot remain a mere aggregate, because the movement of reflection shows that what is really reached is not empirical completeness but objective universality. The subject ceases to be merely subsumed under a universal predicate and is raised into genus or nature itself. With this, the relation of subject and predicate reverses: what appeared as reflected universality in the predicate now becomes the substantial essence of the subject. The judgment of reflection therefore culminates in necessity.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Allness criticized:** Mere aggregate universality is insufficient.
- **Objective universality:** Reflection yields genus or nature.
- **Reversal of relation:** Subject and predicate exchange their weight.
- **Transition:** Reflection passes into necessity.
