### §1. Necessity as Objective Universality

**Lines:** 4–38

**Distillation:**
The syllogism of necessity arises when the middle term becomes objective universality: no longer an adventitious immediate content, but the immanent reflection of the determinateness of the extremes. The genus now contains the whole determinateness of the extremes in simple identity. The extremes therefore differ only by an external and unessential form, while their inner identity is already grounded in the middle. Yet this necessity is still at first only substantial content; the task of the section is to make this inner necessity fully explicit in the posited form of the syllogism.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Objective middle:** The middle is the universal nature of the fact.
- **Inner identity of the extremes:** Their content is already one in the middle.
- **Externality reduced to form:** Difference now belongs only to form, not essence.
- **Program of Part C:** Substantial necessity must become explicit formal necessity.

### §2. The Categorical Syllogism: Substance Elevated into Concept

**Lines:** 42–89

**Distillation:**
The categorical syllogism is the first syllogism of necessity. A subject is conjoined with a predicate through its substance, but substance is now taken at the level of the concept: as universal rather than accidental substrate. Its differences become the extremes of universality and singularity, and the middle term is the genus whose specific difference determines the relation between them. The connection is therefore no longer through arbitrary qualities, but through the subject's essential nature.

**Structural Moves:**

- **First necessity-form:** The categorical syllogism begins Part C.
- **Substance reconceived:** Substance becomes conceptual universality.
- **Specific difference:** The genus mediates through its essential determinateness.
- **Essential predication:** The predicate belongs through substance, not accident.

---

### §3. The Categorical Syllogism Defeats Contingency but Initiates Objectivity

**Lines:** 91–138

**Distillation:**
Although the categorical syllogism initially falls under the schema $S-P-U$, its middle is now the essential nature of the singular, and its universal extreme is the specific difference of the genus. For that reason, neither the arbitrariness of the syllogism of existence nor the circular presupposition of the syllogism of reflection reappears here. One essence runs through all three terms, and their differentiations are only formal moments. In that identity, objectivity begins: the middle is now the contentful identity of the extremes.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Old schema, new content:** The external form remains, but the middle is transformed.
- **No arbitrary mediation:** Essential nature replaces contingent qualities.
- **No regress or reflective circularity:** Necessity stabilizes the inference.
- **Beginning of objectivity:** One essence runs through all three terms.

### §4. The Residual Subjectivity of the Categorical Form

**Lines:** 140–199

**Distillation:**
Yet the categorical syllogism still retains a subjective element. Its identity is substantial and inner, but not yet identity of form. The singular remains immediate and contingent: it is only one among many singulars subsumed under the genus, and it also contains determinations not included in the middle term's universal nature. The same contingency affects the other extreme as well. Thus the extremes are both objectively grounded and immediately self-subsistent. Because their identity is still only inner, the categorical syllogism determines itself as the hypothetical syllogism.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Inner but not formal identity:** Necessity has not yet penetrated the whole form.
- **Contingent singularity:** The actual singular still bears indifferent immediacy.
- **Residual externality:** Both extremes retain an existence not fully contained in the middle.
- **Transition:** Categorical necessity gives way to hypothetical necessity.

---

### §5. The Hypothetical Syllogism: Condition and Conditioned

**Lines:** 203–225

**Distillation:**
The hypothetical judgment expresses necessary connection without asserting the immediate being of its terms: “If A is, so is B.” The syllogism adds immediacy to this conditional structure with the minor premise “But A is,” thereby producing the conclusion “Therefore B is.” The conclusion is not merely an abstract copula but an accomplished mediating unity. The being of A must therefore be understood, not as bare immediacy, but as a middle term.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Pure condition:** The major premise states connection without actuality.
- **Added immediacy:** The minor inserts the immediate being of A.
- **Mediated result:** B's being is now concluded through mediation.
- **New middle-term role:** The actuality of A becomes the operative middle.

---

### §6. Negative Unity as the Active Middle of the Hypothetical Syllogism

**Lines:** 227–329

**Distillation:**
The two sides of the hypothetical judgment are not immediate beings but beings held in necessity. Their relation is best understood as condition and conditioned. What translates the dispersed conditions into actuality is a self-referring negative unity: singularity as active mediation. This is the free unity of the concept, gathering the dispersed material and determining itself as activity. The conclusion therefore posits the identity of mediator and mediated: the necessary and its necessity are no longer different in content, but only superficially distinguished.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Conditioned being:** Both terms are appearances held in necessity.
- **Negative middle:** Self-referring negativity acts as the mediating power.
- **Activity of the concept:** The middle is no longer static content but active unification.
- **Identity of mediator and mediated:** The conclusion makes their content one.

### §7. The Empty Difference of A and B and the Rise of the Disjunctive Syllogism

**Lines:** 330–364

**Distillation:**
In the hypothetical syllogism, the form of connection and the necessary content become identical. The difference between A and B turns out to be only an empty name: the being of the one is the being of the other. Their externality is therefore sublated, and what remains is a unity reflected into itself, an identical content that now stands as self-identical existent universality. This differentiating identity that retrieves itself out of its differentiation is the disjunctive syllogism.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Form and content converge:** Necessity and the necessary become one.
- **Empty nominal difference:** A and B no longer differ in content.
- **Externality sublated:** Their separation becomes merely apparent.
- **Transition:** The syllogism becomes disjunctive.

---

### §8. The Disjunctive Syllogism as Totalized Universality

**Lines:** 365–427

**Distillation:**
The disjunctive syllogism has for its middle term a universality completely replete with form: the universal sphere that contains the whole of its particularizations. The middle is genus, but genus fully sorted into its species, and these species are held together as mutually excluding determinations. The “either-or” is therefore not external but immanent to the universal itself. What is posited here is the truth of the hypothetical syllogism: the unity of mediator and mediated. For that reason, the disjunctive syllogism is already on the verge of ceasing to be a syllogism at all.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Totalized middle:** The universal contains all its species within itself.
- **Either-or as immanent negativity:** Particularizations exclude each other from within the genus.
- **A through its stages:** The same subject is universal, determinate, and excluding singular.
- **Truth of the hypothetical:** The distinction between mediator and mediated collapses.

---

### §9. The Sublation of Formalism in the Disjunctive Syllogism

**Lines:** 428–499

**Distillation:**
Because the middle term already contains both extremes in their full determinateness, the difference between middle and extremes loses independent significance. The form of the syllogism had consisted in the middle's distinctness from the extremes; that distinction is now sublated. The disjunctive syllogism therefore abolishes the formalism and subjectivity of the syllogism as such. What had appeared as mediation through a third now reveals itself as the concept's own totality already present in all the terms.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Middle contains the extremes:** No independent determinateness remains outside it.
- **Formal difference collapses:** Mediator and mediated are no longer opposed.
- **End of syllogistic subjectivity:** The concept is no longer merely inward over against its terms.
- **Repletion of the concept:** Totality stands explicit in form.

---

### §10. The Syllogism Sublates Itself into Objectivity

**Lines:** 500–537

**Distillation:**
The movement of the syllogism is the sublation of mediation itself. At first, nothing is in and for itself; each term exists only through another. But once this mediation is fully completed and then sublated, what emerges is an immediacy that is identical with mediation itself. This is the concept restored out of its otherness: a being that is in and for itself, an objective fact. The syllogism of necessity therefore culminates in objectivity.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Mediation completed:** Every term has existed only through another.
- **Sublation of mediation:** The need for an external mediating structure disappears.
- **Immediate being through mediation:** The result is an immediacy generated by mediation itself.
- **Threshold crossed:** The restored concept becomes objectivity.
