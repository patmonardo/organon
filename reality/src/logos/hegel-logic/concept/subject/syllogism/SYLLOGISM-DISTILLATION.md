### §1. The Syllogism as the Restoration of the Concept in Judgment

**Lines:** 6–17

**Distillation:**
The syllogism is the concept restored out of the diremption of judgment. In the concept, the moments are held together in immediate unity; in judgment, that unity is broken and the moments stand as self-subsisting extremes. The syllogism is the truth of both: it preserves the self-subsistence of the extremes, but now posits their unity as the mediating ground. For that reason, the syllogism is not an external procedure added to thought after the fact. It is the concept itself returning into explicit mediation.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Concept:** Unity is immediate and inward.
- **Judgment:** Unity is broken into self-subsisting extremes.
- **Syllogism:** The broken unity is restored as explicit mediation.

### §2. The Rational Is Nothing Other Than This Mediated Unity

**Lines:** 19–84

**Distillation:**
The syllogism is the completely posited concept, and therefore the proper form of the rational. Rationality is not a contentless formal procedure, nor a supposed immediate possession of absolute truths apart from inference. It is the movement in which a universal ground mediates determinate differences into a content-rich unity. For that reason, the syllogism is the truth of judgment and the living form of reason itself: not empty universality, but fulfilled universality.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Against formalism:** Rationality is not just correct arrangement.
- **Against immediatism:** Reason is not prior to mediation.
- **Positive result:** The rational is the concept determining itself through mediation.

### §3. The Middle Term Is the Essence of the Syllogism

**Lines:** 86–113

**Distillation:**
The immediate syllogism first appears as a syllogism of the understanding: its terms are abstract and its rationality is not yet visible. What matters, however, is the middle term, because the middle is not a merely external connector placed between two fixed terms. It is the unity of the extremes and the ground that supports them. The defect of the understanding is to spatialize this middle, treating it as one term among others and therefore reducing mediation to an external linkage. The entire development of the syllogism consists in overcoming this abstraction and showing what the middle truly is.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Immediate appearance:** The syllogism first looks merely formal.
- **True center:** The middle is the unity and ground of the extremes.
- **Defect of understanding:** Mediation is reduced to an external connection.

### §4. Existence, Reflection, and Necessity Are the Three Great Forms of the Middle

**Lines:** 115–152

**Distillation:**
The chapter unfolds through three increasingly concrete determinations of the middle term. In the syllogism of **existence**, the terms are immediate and abstract, so the form exposes its own instability: the middle is still external, the premises remain immediate, and the figures rotate until formal mediation collapses into a circle. In the syllogism of **reflection**, the middle becomes gathered or reflected universality: allness, induction, and analogy progressively show that the universal must no longer be externally collected from singulars but must belong to them essentially. In the syllogism of **necessity**, the middle becomes objective universality: the fact's own inner genus or nature, through which the extremes are no longer externally related but internally identical.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Existence:** Immediate mediation proves abstract and unstable.
- **Reflection:** Mediation becomes reflective and gathered, but still not fully objective.
- **Necessity:** Mediation becomes the fact's own objective universal.

---

### §5. The Syllogism of Existence Shows That Abstract Mediation Cannot Stand

**Lines:** 115–127

**Distillation:**
The first form demonstrates that the terms of the syllogism are never as abstract as they first appear. Because a syllogism is already a connection, each term implicitly contains reference to the others. The abstract self-subsistence of the terms therefore undermines itself. The syllogism of existence is important not because it succeeds, but because through its failure it forces the discovery that true mediation cannot be grounded in immediate qualities or fixed term-positions.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Abstract terms fail:** Their supposed self-subsistence breaks down.
- **Implicit mediation:** Each term already contains relation to the others.
- **Result:** Immediate existence cannot be the final form of syllogistic truth.

---

### §6. Reflection and Necessity Carry the Syllogism to Its Truth

**Lines:** 129–152

**Distillation:**
Reflection first corrects the defect of existence by making each term explicitly mediated through the others. But reflective mediation still relies on universality that is gathered, presupposed, or only implicitly objective. Necessity completes this development. Here the mediating factor is the objective nature of the fact itself, so the extremes are determined as totalities whose difference is only the form of one inner identity. Once the middle has become this objective universality, the syllogism reaches the correspondence of concept and existence. At that point it has attained its truth and steps out of subjectivity into objectivity.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Reflection:** Each term is posited as mediated.
- **Necessity:** The middle becomes objective universality.
- **Completion:** Concept and existence correspond.
- **Transition:** The syllogism passes into objectivity.
