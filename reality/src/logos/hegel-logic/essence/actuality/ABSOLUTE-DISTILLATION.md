### AB1. The Absolute Cannot Be Fixed By External Predication

**Distillation:**
The chapter begins by stripping away the temptation to define the absolute by attaching predicates to it from outside. If the absolute is treated as a mere void beyond all determinacy, or as a heap that somehow contains every predicate, thought remains external to it. The task is instead exposition: letting the absolute show what it is by its own movement.

**Structural Moves:**

- External predication fails.
- The absolute is not a mere void.
- The task is exposition rather than attachment of predicates.
- The absolute must display itself.

---

### AB2. Exposition Shows the Absolute As Identity of Form and Content

**Distillation:**
In exposition, the absolute is shown as the unity in which every prior determination of being and essence has foundered. Yet it is not empty. It is absolute form and absolute content at once: each determinateness is transparent, and each moment is itself the whole. The exposition is therefore not something added to the absolute but the absolute's own self-showing.

**Structural Moves:**

- Earlier determinations sink into the absolute.
- The absolute is absolute form and content.
- Each moment is the whole.
- Exposition belongs to the absolute itself.

---

### AB3. Attribute Is the Relative Absolute

**Distillation:**
Attribute is the absolute under a still determinate form. It is the whole content of the absolute, but only in a relative guise, because form has not yet fully returned into content. Attribute therefore carries the absolute, but still as reflective shine or way and manner rather than as fully self-returned totality.

**Structural Moves:**

- Attribute is the relative absolute.
- It contains the whole content.
- Its determinateness is still formal.
- Form has not yet fully returned into the whole.

---

### AB4. Mode Is the Absolute's Own Self-Manifestation

**Distillation:**
Mode first seems to be the absolute's externality, its loss in contingency and alterability. But mode is not just externality. It is externality posited as externality, reflective shine known as shine. In that self-recovering movement, the absolute is finally what it truly is: absolute manifestation of itself for itself. Thus the chapter culminates in actuality.

**Structural Moves:**

- Mode first appears as externality.
- This externality is reflective shine.
- The absolute manifests itself in mode.
- Mode culminates in actuality.
