### AC1. Actuality Is the Unity of Essence and Concrete Existence

**Distillation:**
Actuality begins where the whole labor of Essence finally holds together. Shapeless essence and unstable appearance each lose their one-sidedness. Inner and outer have become one identical substrate, and that identity is now explicitly posited. Actuality is therefore not one more appearance but the achieved unity of essence and existence.

**Structural Moves:**

- Essence and concrete existence are united.
- Appearance loses its merely phenomenal status.
- Inner and outer are one absolute totality.
- The section begins from accomplished identity.

---

### AC2. The `absolute/` Subfolder Fixes the Absolute As Self-Exposition

**Distillation:**
The first chapter of the section does not yet move among differentiated contents. It begins from the absolute as indeterminate identity, rejects external predication, and then shows how the absolute itself must manifest as exposition, attribute, and mode. The point is not to pin predicates onto the absolute but to let the absolute show itself. That self-showing culminates in actuality.

**Structural Moves:**

- The absolute is not grasped by external predicates.
- Exposition shows the absolute as its own movement.
- Attribute is the relative absolute.
- Mode is the absolute's self-manifestation into actuality.

---

### AC3. The `actuality/` Subfolder Develops Modality Into Necessity

**Distillation:**
The middle chapter of the section is actuality proper: actuality, possibility, and necessity in their formal, real, and absolute forms. Formal contingency first shows actual and possible collapsing into one another. Relative necessity then gathers content and conditions. Finally absolute necessity shows necessity including contingency within itself and turning into substance.

**Structural Moves:**

- Formal actuality, possibility, and necessity first appear.
- Real actuality and real possibility deepen the movement.
- Relative necessity becomes absolute necessity.
- Necessity turns into substance.

---

### AC4. The `substance/` Subfolder Completes Essence and Opens the Concept

**Distillation:**
The truth of necessity is absolute relation. Substance first appears as the enduring whole of accidents, then as causality, and finally as reciprocity. In reciprocity, one-sided cause and effect are overcome, necessity becomes transparent as self-mediation, and essence reaches the threshold of freedom. This is the point where the Doctrine of Essence yields to the Concept.

**Structural Moves:**

- Substance is the first shape of absolute relation.
- Causality differentiates substantial power.
- Reciprocity sublates one-sided causality.
- The section opens into the concept.

---

**Structural Moves:**
