### SU1. Absolute Relation Is Necessity Become Transparent

**Distillation:**
Substance begins from the thought that necessity is no longer hidden behind things but has become explicit as relation. Absolute relation is not something added after necessity; it is necessity itself, but now self-expositing. The absolute now stands as self-identical actuality in its own differentiating movement.

**Structural Moves:**

- Necessity is recast as relation.
- Relation is self-exposition of the absolute.
- Absolute actuality is now transparent.
- The chapter begins from absolute relation.

---

### SU2. Substantiality Unites Substance and Accident

**Distillation:**
Substantiality is the first form of absolute relation. Substance is the being in all being, while accidents are its shining and positedness. Their movement is not a clash of independent things but the actuosity of substance itself. Yet the distinction has not yet become fully real; substantiality still passes into causality.

**Structural Moves:**

- Substance is the unity of being and reflection.
- Accidentality is the shining of substance.
- The movement of accidents is substantial actuosity.
- Substantiality passes into causality.

---

### SU3. Causality Makes Substance Active and Differentiated

**Distillation:**
In causality, substance no longer only underlies but posits determinations and distinguishes them from itself. Cause and effect first appear formally, then in determinate finitude, and finally in action and reaction. The key discovery is that cause is truly itself only in effect and effect only in cause. Causality therefore undermines its own one-sidedness.

**Structural Moves:**

- Substance becomes cause.
- Cause and effect first appear formally.
- Finite causality deepens the relation.
- Action and reaction undermine one-sided causality.

---

### SU4. Reciprocity Is the Threshold of the Concept

**Distillation:**
Reciprocity completes the chapter by sublating the mechanism of externally related substances. What had seemed active and passive substances are shown to be transparent moments of one movement. Necessity thereby manifests itself as freedom, and substance differentiates itself as universal, particular, and singular. This is already the concept emerging from essence.

**Structural Moves:**

- Reciprocal causality dissolves external mechanism.
- Active and passive are one movement.
- Necessity manifests itself as freedom.
- The concept comes on the scene.
