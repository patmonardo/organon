# Causality — Distillation

Source: [relation-causality.txt](relation-causality.txt)

---

### §1. Causality Is Substance Becoming Cause by Positing Its Own Effect

**Distillation:**
Once substance is grasped as power reflected into itself, it no longer remains only inward substantiality but becomes cause. The effect is the positedness of substance, while the cause is substance as self-referring originative power. Cause and effect are not two alien terms externally conjoined; the effect is the manifestation of the cause, and the cause is actual only in producing and sustaining its effect.

**Structural Moves:**

- Substance becomes cause by positing determinateness.
- Effect is the cause's own positedness.
- Cause is actual only in the effect.

---

### §2. Formal Causality Reveals the Internal Identity of Cause and Effect

**Distillation:**
Formal causality shows that an effect contains nothing not already in the cause, and the cause contains nothing not present in the effect. Cause as such entails effect, and effect as such entails cause. But precisely because the two are internally identical, the form-difference between originative and posited begins to collapse. Cause extinguishes itself in the effect, and the effect also vanishes as mere determinateness of the cause.

**Structural Moves:**

- Cause and effect share one content.
- Their distinction is purely formal at this level.
- Formal causality therefore undermines its own separation.

---

### §3. Finite Causality Becomes Analytic and Tautological

**Distillation:**
When causality is treated as determinate and finite, content and form fall apart. Then the same fact is repeated once as cause and again as effect under different external descriptions. Causal explanation becomes analytic or tautological, because it merely redescribes one content through the external form of origin and consequence. This is why Hegel treats the ordinary understanding's causal explanations as shallow, even when they appear informative.

**Structural Moves:**

- Finite causality splits form from content.
- One identical content is repeated under two labels.
- Ordinary causal explanation becomes tautological.

---

### §4. The Endless Search for Causes Expresses the Failure of External Reflection

**Distillation:**
Once causality is externalized, explanation runs into bad infinity. A cause becomes an effect requiring a prior cause, and an effect becomes a cause requiring a further effect. The regress is not a sign of depth but of impotence, because reflection cannot hold together the unity it is presupposing. Hegel's point is that this infinite chase after causes belongs to finite reflection, not to truth.

**Structural Moves:**

- External causality generates regress from cause to cause.
- Reflection cannot secure the unity it seeks.
- Bad infinity is the symptom of finite causal thinking.

---

### §5. Causality Is Inadequate for Life, Spirit, and History

**Distillation:**
Hegel sharply limits the range of crude causal explanation. In organic and spiritual realities, what is acted upon does not simply receive an effect but transforms what acts upon it. A living thing or a spiritual totality interrupts and transmutes the alleged cause. That is why nourishment is not simply the cause of blood, climate is not simply the cause of poetry, and anecdotes are not genuine causes of world-historical events. At most they are occasions.

**Structural Moves:**

- Living and spiritual realities transform what acts on them.
- The cause does not remain intact within the effect.
- Many familiar historical and natural explanations are reduced to occasions.

---

### §6. Conditioned Causality Shows That Cause Presupposes Its Own Other

**Distillation:**
The decisive advance comes when causality is seen as conditioned. Cause no longer confronts a merely external substrate; it presupposes a passive substance that is already implicitly identical with it. The cause acts on its own presupposed other, and that other receives the effect only by rejoining itself. Passive substance is thus not sheer inertness but a moment through which causality mediates itself with itself.

**Structural Moves:**

- Cause presupposes a passive other.
- The passive other is implicitly identical with the cause.
- Causality becomes self-mediated through its condition.

---

### §7. Action and Reaction Bend Causality Back Into Reciprocity

**Distillation:**
Because passive substance becomes causal through the effect it receives, reaction emerges. The effect is sublated in the passive side, and the first cause is in turn deprived of its isolated substantiality. Causality thereby ceases to run outward in a straight line and bends back into itself. What was finite causality becomes an action returning to itself, the threshold of reciprocal action.

**Structural Moves:**

- Passive substance becomes a reacting cause.
- The first cause loses its one-sided privilege.
- The causal line curves back into self-return.
- Causality passes into reciprocity.
