# Reciprocity — Distillation

Source: [reciprocity-action.txt](reciprocity-action.txt)

---

### §1. Reciprocity Sublates the Externality of Mechanism

**Distillation:**
Reciprocity begins by overcoming mechanism. In mechanism, substances stand externally over against one another, and the reflection of cause into effect is still accompanied by repulsion into another substrate. In reciprocal action this externality is sublated: the persistence of isolated substantiality disappears, and cause is seen as mediating itself with itself through its negation.

**Structural Moves:**

- Mechanism rests on external causality.
- Reciprocity removes the independence of isolated substrates.
- Self-mediation replaces external transfer.

---

### §2. Each Side Is Both Active and Passive

**Distillation:**
At first reciprocity appears as reciprocal causality among substances that condition one another. But because each side is both active and passive, their difference is already only a transparent shine. The apparent duality of substances survives merely as a formal manner of speaking. In truth, reciprocity is causality returning from its external shape into itself.

**Structural Moves:**

- Each substance is simultaneously active and passive.
- Their difference becomes transparent.
- Reciprocal causality is causality's return to itself.

---

### §3. Necessity Is Elevated Into Freedom Through Self-Returning Causality

**Distillation:**
When causality returns into itself in reciprocity, it reaches its absolute concept. Necessity is no longer merely inner identity hidden beneath relations, but becomes manifest as the identical movement of the sides themselves. This manifestation is freedom: not the disappearance of necessity, but its unveiling as self-mediation rather than external compulsion.

**Structural Moves:**

- Reciprocity returns causality to its absolute concept.
- Necessity becomes explicit rather than merely inward.
- Freedom is the unveiled truth of necessity.

---

### §4. Contingency Too Is Transfigured in Reciprocity

**Distillation:**
Reciprocity does not only transform necessity. It also transforms contingency. The sides that seemed to stand as independent free actualities are now posited as one reflection. Their separation remains, but only as the shine of a deeper identity. Contingency is therefore no longer brute externality but a moment of freedom itself.

**Structural Moves:**

- Independent actualities are re-read as one reflection.
- Difference remains, but as transparent shine.
- Contingency is integrated into freedom.

---

### §5. Absolute Relation Differentiates Itself Into Universal, Singular, and Particular

**Distillation:**
The culmination of reciprocity is the generation of the concept. The formerly passive whole becomes the universal; the formerly causal whole becomes the singular; and their immediate identity is particularity. These are not externally assembled categories but the result of absolute relation differentiating itself into transparent totalities that are each the whole in a determinate mode.

**Structural Moves:**

- The passive totality becomes universal.
- The causal totality becomes singular.
- Their immediate unity is particularity.

---

### §6. Reciprocity Is the Threshold Where Essence Gives Way to Subjectivity

**Distillation:**
Reciprocity is the final form of essence because it removes the last residue of one-sided substantiality and external causality. Once the sides of relation are understood as one self-differentiating reflection, the realm of subjectivity has already begun. The concept appears here not as a foreign addition but as the truth into which absolute relation necessarily develops.

**Structural Moves:**

- Essence reaches completion in reciprocity.
- One-sided relation is overcome.
- The concept emerges as the truth of absolute relation.
