### §1. Absolute Relation Is Necessity No Longer Hidden but Self-Expositing

**Distillation:**
Hegel begins by redefining necessity as relation. Absolute necessity is not a fixed necessity standing behind things, but being as reflection, a self-referring process in which the whole differentiates itself and remains identical with itself in that differentiation. Absolute actuality is therefore not a dead result but manifestation itself.

**Structural Moves:**

- Necessity is recast as self-reflective relation.
- The absolute is not behind manifestation but is its self-exposition.
- Actuality is the self-referring shine of essence.

---

### §2. The Sides of Absolute Relation Are Totalities, Not Attributes

**Distillation:**
The moments of the absolute relation are not attributes externally assigned to a prior absolute. Each side is itself the whole totality appearing in a determinate mode. Because each side is both itself and its opposite in reflective shine, their distinction is only the absolute positing itself in differentiated form.

**Structural Moves:**

- Hegel rejects the attribute-model of the absolute.
- Each side of the relation is itself a totality.
- Distinction is only the self-positing of the whole.

---

### §3. The Chapter Unfolds as Substance, Causality, and Reciprocity

**Distillation:**
The absolute relation first appears as substantiality, where the whole and its accidents are still immediately intertwined. It then becomes causality, where substance distinguishes itself into cause and effect. Finally it becomes reciprocity, where causality returns into itself and the whole is explicitly posited in its determinations. This completed unity is the threshold of the concept.

**Structural Moves:**

- Substantiality: the absolute as substance and accident.
- Causality: the absolute as cause over against effect.
- Reciprocity: the relation refers back into itself.
- Result: the chapter ends in the concept.

---

### §4. Absolute Relation Is the Last Shape of Essence Before Freedom

**Distillation:**
The importance of the chapter is that it carries essence to the point where its inner necessity becomes transparent as self-mediation. As long as relation remains substantial or causal, the whole still appears under one-sided forms. Reciprocity overcomes that one-sidedness and reveals the movement that will become subjectivity, freedom, and concept. Substance is therefore not the terminus but the last great form of essence before the concept emerges.

**Structural Moves:**

- Essence reaches its highest relational articulation here.
- One-sided substantiality and one-sided causality are not final.
- Reciprocity discloses self-mediation.
- The transition is from necessity into freedom.
