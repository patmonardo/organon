# Substantiality — Distillation

Source: [relation-substantiality.txt](relation-substantiality.txt)

---

### §1. Substance Is Absolute Being as Self-Mediating Reflection

**Distillation:**
Substantiality begins from absolute necessity as being that is because it is. Substance is therefore not an inert thing standing behind appearances, but the final unity of essence and being, immediate actuality reflected wholly into itself. It is the being in all being, and its own being is inseparable from its shining and positedness.

**Structural Moves:**

- Substance is being mediated with itself.
- It is not a hidden support behind actuality but actuality itself.
- Being and reflective shining are one in substance.

---

### §2. Accidentality Is Substance's Own Reflective Shine

**Distillation:**
Accidentality is not something merely added to substance from outside. It is substance's own shining totality, the movement in which possibility and actuality, becoming and passing-away, mutually reflect into one another. The whole sphere of contingency is therefore already internal to substance as its own mode of self-exhibition.

**Structural Moves:**

- Accident is internal to substance, not an external appendage.
- Contingency is re-read as a reflective movement.
- Possibility and actuality show themselves as moments of one substantial process.

---

### §3. The Movement of Accidents Is Substance's Own Actuosity

**Distillation:**
What appears as the movement of accidents is in truth the actuosity of substance itself. Substance does not first stand still and then act upon something foreign. Its coming forth is only its acting against itself as simple, unresisting element. The immediate is sublated, but in being sublated it first truly comes to be as what it is.

**Structural Moves:**

- The movement of accidents is not foreign to substance.
- Substance acts only against itself.
- Sublation is the process in which immediacy becomes what it is.

---

### §4. Substance as Absolute Power Is at Once Creative and Destructive

**Distillation:**
Because accidentality is wholly gathered into substance, substance appears as absolute power. It manifests itself by translating the possible into actuality and reducing actuality back into possibility. Creation and destruction are therefore not opposite operations but one and the same substantial activity. The positive and the negative are inseparable within necessity.

**Structural Moves:**

- Substance gathers accidentality into itself as power.
- Power moves actuality into possibility and possibility into actuality.
- Creation and destruction are one movement.

---

### §5. Accidents Have No Independent Power Over One Another

**Distillation:**
Accidents seem to struggle with and displace one another, but this is only appearance. No accident has autonomous power over another. What looks like interaction among finite things is substance itself positing one determination as ceasing and another as emerging. The supposed independence of accidents collapses back into the totality of substance.

**Structural Moves:**

- Accidental powers are only apparent.
- Substance determines the rise and fall of accidental forms.
- Finite conflict is the self-differentiation of the whole.

---

### §6. Substantiality Fails Because Difference Is Still Only Vanishing Shine

**Distillation:**
The inadequacy of substantiality lies in the fact that difference is not yet truly self-positing. Substance is present in accidentality, but accidentality is not yet posited as self-identical substance. Substance still has only accidentality, not itself, as its outward shape. For that reason the relation remains one of immediate vanishing rather than explicit self-relation. This failure forces the transition to causality, where substance will stand over against its own positedness as cause.

**Structural Moves:**

- Difference remains only immediate and vanishing.
- Substance lacks itself as explicitly posited form.
- The relation is still one-sidedly inward.
- Substantiality therefore passes into causality.
