### WD1. Law Is the Essential Appearance

**Distillation:**
Appearance first becomes stable as law. Law is not beyond appearance but the simple, enduring self-identity of what appears in its restless flux. It is appearance reflected into self-identity, the content that persists through the vanishing of immediate determinations.

**Structural Moves:**

- Appearance first stabilizes as law.
- Law is the positive element of appearance's mediation.
- Law is not beyond appearance but within it.
- Law gives the appearing world a substrate.

---

### WD2. The World In Itself Rises Over Against the World of Appearance

**Distillation:**
Because law has become total reflection, the appearing world now stands over against a world existing in and for itself. This is not merely another realm but appearance's own reflective other. The essential world contains the negative unity of appearance and thus appears as its opposite, as the inverted world.

**Structural Moves:**

- Law becomes total reflection.
- A world in itself rises over against the world of appearance.
- The two worlds are essentially opposed.
- Their opposition is one of inversion, not of sheer separation.

---

### WD3. The Two Worlds Are Each the Other's Appearance

**Distillation:**
The opposition of the two worlds does not hold. What is positive in one is negative in the other, and vice versa, but this inversion erases the supposed independence of each side. The world of appearance is already essential within, and the world in itself is itself appearance. Their truth is neither side alone but the unity in which each contains the other.

**Structural Moves:**

- Each world contains the other.
- Inversion collapses fixed opposition.
- The essential world is itself appearance.
- Appearance becomes a total relation.

---

### WD4. The Dissolution of World-Opposition Yields Essential Relation

**Distillation:**
Once the two worlds are seen as totalities that are only in relation to one another, appearance is no longer just law or opposition. It becomes essential relation. The whole theme of worldhood sinks into the relational form in which each side is what it is only through the other.

**Structural Moves:**

- The two worlds are totalities only through each other.
- Their self-subsistence lies in relation.
- World-opposition dissolves.
- The chapter passes into essential relation.
