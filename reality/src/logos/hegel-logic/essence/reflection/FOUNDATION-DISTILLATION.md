### FS1. Foundation Opens As Determined Reflection or Essentiality

**Distillation:**
Foundation begins by restating the result of Reflection Within in a new key. Reflection is now determined reflection, and essence is therefore determined essence or essentiality. Essence is not a blank inward beyond. It is negative simplicity, absolute mediation with itself, shining in distinct moments which are themselves reflected into themselves. The chapter then announces its triad: identity, difference, and contradiction, with contradiction returning into foundation.

**Structural Moves:**

- Reflection becomes determined reflection.
- Essence becomes determined essence or essentiality.
- The moments of essence are reflected into themselves.
- The chapter unfolds through identity, difference, and contradiction.

---

### FS2. Identity Is Essence's Negative Self-Equality

**Distillation:**
Identity is the first form of essentiality. Hegel insists that this identity is not the abstract identity of the understanding, nor the result of a merely relative negation. It is essence as simple self-immediacy, but only because being and every determinateness of being have sublated themselves in themselves. Identity is therefore the negativity of being returned into pure self-equality. It is the first stable form of essence, but it is already charged with the negativity that will force it beyond itself.

**Structural Moves:**

- Identity is simple self-reference.
- It is not abstract or merely comparative identity.
- Being's self-sublation produces identity.
- Identity still carries the negativity of essence within it.

---

### FS3. Difference Is Not External Otherness but Reflection's Own Negativity

**Distillation:**
Difference is the negativity reflection possesses in itself. It is not borrowed from something outside. Absolute difference is self-referring and simple: the difference of essence itself. This is why difference is both itself and identity. It is the whole and its own moment. Once this inner difference unfolds, it becomes diversity, where identity and difference appear as indifferent to one another, and then external reflection takes over through likeness and unlikeness. But the entire development shows that difference is not a mere comparison by a third party. It is reflection's own restless self-differentiation.

**Structural Moves:**

- Difference is an essential moment of identity itself.
- Absolute difference is self-referring, not externally imposed.
- Difference contains identity within itself.
- Diversity and comparison are degraded forms of this deeper difference.

---

### FS4. Diversity and Comparison Destroy Themselves

**Distillation:**
When difference becomes diversity, the moments stand indifferent to one another. External reflection then compares them as like or unlike. But likeness and unlikeness cannot hold themselves apart. Each means itself only through the other. The very strategy that tries to keep them separated by saying that things are alike in one respect and unlike in another becomes their undoing. External comparison thus reveals itself as unstable. Difference must pass into opposition.

**Structural Moves:**

- Diversity treats the moments as indifferent.
- Likeness and unlikeness are products of external reflection.
- Each depends on the other for its meaning.
- Their separation collapses and pushes difference into opposition.

---

### FS5. Contradiction Is The Truth of Difference

**Distillation:**
In opposition, the sides of difference are no longer merely indifferent. Each contains the other within itself and excludes it for that very reason. This is contradiction. Positive and negative are not accidental opposites laid alongside each other; each is the whole opposition and each internally excludes itself. So contradiction is not a defect added from outside. It is the truth of self-subsisting opposition itself.

**Structural Moves:**

- Opposition internalizes the other within each side.
- Positive and negative are self-mediated wholes.
- Each excludes itself in excluding the other.
- Contradiction is the truth of opposition.

---

### FS6. Contradiction Resolves Itself By Foundering To Ground

**Distillation:**
Contradiction does not merely destroy. It resolves itself. The first result is nullity, the ceaseless vanishing of the opposites. But Hegel immediately adds the stronger point: contradiction also contains the positive. Its result is not only the null but the grounding of self-subsistence. When opposition turns its own self-subsistence into positedness and sublates that positedness, it founders to the ground. The resolved contradiction is therefore ground.

**Structural Moves:**

- Contradiction first yields nullity.
- But contradiction also contains a positive result.
- Self-subsistence collapses into positedness.
- Resolved contradiction becomes ground.

---

### FS7. Ground Preserves and Removes Contradiction At Once

**Distillation:**
Ground is not something added after contradiction from elsewhere. It is contradiction restored as self-identical essence. In ground, opposition and contradiction are both removed and preserved. Ground is positive self-identity, but only as self-identity in negativity. It determines itself, posits itself, excludes itself, and yet remains with itself in that negation. Ground is thus the first explicit form in which essence stands as self-identical through its own contradiction.

**Structural Moves:**

- Ground is essence restored through contradiction.
- Opposition is both removed and preserved in it.
- Ground is self-identity in negativity.
- Ground is the chapter's achieved unity.

---

### FS8. Foundation Is the Threshold to Ground Proper

**Distillation:**
The Foundation chapter therefore does not end with contradiction as a dead end. It shows why identity, difference, diversity, comparison, opposition, and contradiction all belong to one movement whose truth is ground. That is why contradiction is not the chapter's terminus but its engine. The chapter closes by handing essence over to ground as the next explicit field.

**Structural Moves:**

- Identity, difference, and contradiction are one movement.
- Contradiction is the engine, not the end.
- Ground is the achieved truth of the chapter.
- Foundation hands the doctrine onward to the Ground chapter.
