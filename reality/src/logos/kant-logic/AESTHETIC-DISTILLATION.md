# Aesthetic - Distillation

---

### TA1. Sensibility Gives Intuition, and the Aesthetic Is the Science of A Priori Receptivity

**Distillation:**
Kant opens by fixing the basic division of cognition. Objects are given only through sensibility, while they are thought through understanding. Sensation provides the matter of appearance; the form in which appearances are ordered must therefore already lie in the mind a priori. The Transcendental Aesthetic is the science of these a priori conditions of receptivity. Its method is to strip away what understanding thinks and what sensation contributes until only pure intuition remains. On that route it discovers two pure forms of sensible intuition: space and time. In our later architectonic presentation, this already shows why Transcendental Logic is no stranger to General Logic: thought must ultimately terminate in intuition if objects are to be given at all.

**Structural Moves:**

- Sensibility gives objects; understanding thinks them.
- Appearance divides into matter and form.
- Pure intuition is the a priori form of sensibility.
- The Aesthetic isolates space and time as the two pure forms.

---

### TA2. Space Is a Pure Intuition of Outer Sense and the Ground of Geometry

**Distillation:**
The exposition of space argues that it is not abstracted from experience but is already presupposed in outer experience. Space is necessary, singular, and infinite, and for that reason it is not a discursive concept but a pure intuition. This immediately explains geometry. Geometrical cognition is synthetic, a priori, and apodictic only because space is given beforehand as a pure intuition in the subject. Space is therefore not a property of things in themselves but the form of outer sense through which appearances can be ordered.

**Structural Moves:**

- Space is not derived from experience.
- Space is necessary and singular.
- Space is pure intuition rather than concept.
- Geometry is possible only because space is a priori intuition.

---

### TA3. Time Is a Pure Intuition of Inner Sense and the Condition of Alteration

**Distillation:**
Time is developed in parallel but with a wider reach. It is not drawn from experience, since simultaneity and succession are only possible under its presupposition. Like space, it is necessary, singular, and given as intuition rather than concept. But time also explains alteration and motion, because only in time can contradictorily opposed predicates belong to one and the same thing successively. Time is thus the form of inner sense and, because all representations belong to inner sense, the mediate condition of all appearances in general. It is empirically real for whatever can appear, yet transcendentally ideal with respect to things in themselves.

**Structural Moves:**

- Time grounds simultaneity and succession a priori.
- Time is a pure intuition, not a general concept.
- Alteration and motion are intelligible only in time.
- Time is the form of inner sense and the mediate condition of all appearances.

---

### TA4. Space and Time Are Empirically Real Yet Transcendentally Ideal

**Distillation:**
The decisive Aesthetic thesis is not that space and time are unreal, but that they are real only as forms of appearance. Space is valid for all outer objects insofar as they appear to us. Time is valid for all appearances insofar as they are given under inner sense. In this way Kant can affirm their objective validity for experience while denying that they belong to things in themselves. This is the pivot that preserves both mathematics and critique. Space and time are not nothing, but neither are they self-subsisting entities or intrinsic properties of mind-independent things.

**Structural Moves:**

- Space and time are valid for appearances.
- They do not belong to things in themselves.
- Empirical reality is compatible with transcendental ideality.
- The Aesthetic secures mathematics without dogmatic metaphysics.

---

### TA5. Appearance Is Neither Confused Thinghood Nor Mere Illusion

**Distillation:**
Kant then sharpens the doctrine by rejecting two errors. First, sensibility does not give a confused cognition of things in themselves; it gives appearances and only appearances. This is the anti-Leibnizian point. The sensible/intellectual difference is not merely one of clarity but of origin and content. Second, appearance is not illusion. To say that objects appear under the forms of space and time is not to say that they are fictional. Illusion begins only when predicates that belong to objects as they appear are transferred to things in themselves. Outer and inner sense alike give only relations under sensible form, and even the subject intuits itself only as it appears through inner sense, not as it would know itself by intellectual intuition.

**Structural Moves:**

- Sensibility does not know things in themselves indistinctly.
- The difference between sensibility and intellect is transcendental, not merely logical.
- Appearance is not illusion.
- Even self-intuition is sensible and appearance-bound for us.

---

### TA6. The Aesthetic Solves Synthetic A Priori Cognition Only Within Experience

**Distillation:**
The closing remarks gather the full result. Space and time make synthetic a priori cognition possible because pure intuition contains what cannot be extracted from concepts alone. This explains geometry and, more broadly, the possibility of synthetic a priori judgments concerning appearances. But the same source that grants this power also fixes its boundary. Because space and time are only forms of sensibility, their valid use extends only to objects of possible experience. They cannot be carried over to things in themselves, nor even to God, whose intuition cannot be assumed to stand under our sensible conditions. The Aesthetic therefore gives transcendental philosophy one of its required pieces: the lawful power of synthetic a priori cognition, together with the limit that keeps it from dogmatic misuse.

**Structural Moves:**

- Pure intuition explains synthetic a priori cognition.
- Geometry is the clearest example of this power.
- Space and time cannot be extended beyond appearances.
- The Aesthetic grants both transcendental power and transcendental limit.
