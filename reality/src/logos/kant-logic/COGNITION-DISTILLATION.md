# Cognition - Distillation

Source: [cognition.md](cognition.md)

---

### SS1. Cognition Is the Content-Bearing Expansion of Knowing

**Distillation:**
Cognition is the second major development on the Knowing side of Idea. If Life gave the working discipline of method, Cognition gives the fuller doctrine of perfection under the four moments of quantity, relation, quality, and modality. It therefore expands the logical field from methodological preparation into horizon, truth, marks, distinctness, certainty, and science. The chapter is larger because it is not a narrow operator inventory but a broad map of perfected cognition.

**Structural Moves:**

- Cognition develops the Knowing side of Idea.
- Cognition is organized by quantity, relation, quality, and modality.
- Cognition extends beyond method into content-bearing perfection.
- The chapter maps a broad logical field rather than a short appendix.

---

### SS2. Quantity Begins with Extension, Content, and Horizon

**Distillation:**
Quantity is understood in two senses. Extensive quantity concerns the extension and manifoldness of cognition. Intensive quantity concerns content, richness, logical importance, and fruitfulness as ground of many consequences. But Kant's real operator here is horizon: the congruence of the quantity of cognition with the subject's capacities and ends. Quantity therefore becomes not a mere count of cognitions but a regulated measure of how far cognition can and should go.

**Structural Moves:**

- Quantity divides into extensive and intensive forms.
- Intensive quantity concerns richness and consequence.
- Horizon aligns cognition with capacities and ends.
- Quantity becomes purposive self-measurement.

---

### SS3. Horizon Is Differentiated by Interest, Standpoint, and Boundary

**Distillation:**
Horizon can be determined logically, aesthetically, and practically. It can also be considered objectively as historical or rational, and subjectively as universal or private. Kant then differentiates several boundary-relations: what lies beyond the horizon, what lies outside it, and what lies beneath it. Quantity therefore becomes a topology of cognitive limit, relevance, and prohibition rather than a blunt line between known and unknown.

**Structural Moves:**

- Horizon divides logically, aesthetically, and practically.
- Horizon also divides by object and by subject.
- Beyond, outside, and beneath the horizon are distinct relations.
- Quantity is organized by differentiated boundaries.

---

### SS4. Quantity Closes in Learned Ignorance, Architectonic Plan, and Importance

**Distillation:**
Opposed to quantitative perfection stands ignorance, a negative imperfection inseparable from our finitude. Yet ignorance itself can be learned when one has insight into the limits of cognition. Kant then argues that cognition has inner worth beyond immediate utility and must be ordered by a plan, not pursued as rhapsodic accumulation. Architectonic supplies this map of the sciences. Intensive quantity finally measures importance not by difficulty but by the fertility of consequences. Quantity therefore closes with modesty, system, and consequence rather than mere expansion.

**Structural Moves:**

- Ignorance is a negative imperfection opposed to quantitative perfection.
- Learned ignorance includes insight into cognitive limits.
- Architectonic orders cognitions by plan and kinship.
- Importance is measured by consequences, not difficulty.

---

### SS5. Relation Begins from Truth and the Split Between Material and Formal Criteria

**Distillation:**
Relation opens from truth as the essential condition of all perfection in cognition. But the simple formula that truth is agreement of cognition with its object generates a circle if treated naively. Kant therefore asks whether there is a universal criterion of truth. The answer is decisive: no universal material criterion is possible, but a universal formal criterion is possible because it concerns the agreement of cognition with itself under the laws of the understanding and reason. Relation therefore secures truth first formally before it can ever claim objective agreement.

**Structural Moves:**

- Truth is the essential condition of perfection.
- The naive agreement-formula is circular if left uninterpreted.
- No universal material criterion of truth is possible.
- A universal formal criterion of truth is possible.

---

### SS6. Formal Truth Is Tested Through Principles, Grounds, and Consequences

**Distillation:**
The formal criteria of truth are contradiction, sufficient reason, and excluded middle. These govern logical possibility, actuality, and necessity. Kant then treats grounds and consequences as testing operators. A false consequence proves a false ground negatively; the totality of true consequences supports the true ground positively. Relation therefore becomes not just a definition of truth but an operating doctrine of validity-testing.

**Structural Moves:**

- Contradiction governs logical possibility.
- Sufficient reason governs grounded actuality.
- Excluded middle governs necessity.
- Grounds and consequences test truth negatively and positively.

---

### SS7. Error Arises Through Illusion and Is Checked by Reflective Maxims

**Distillation:**
Falsehood taken for truth is error, and error belongs only to judgment. Its enabling source is illusion: the confusion of merely subjective grounds with objective ones. Ignorance comes from finitude, but error comes from our own misuse of judgment under the unnoticed influence of sensibility. Kant's practical lesson is that one must expose the illusion generating error, not merely refute isolated mistakes. Others' judgments, common understanding, and the three maxims of thinking provide the external and reflexive touchstones for this work.

**Structural Moves:**

- Error is falsehood taken for truth.
- Illusion confuses the subjective with the objective.
- Ignorance comes from limitation, error from illicit judgment.
- Error is checked by others, common understanding, and the three maxims.

---

### SS8. Quality Begins from Discursive Cognition Through Marks

**Distillation:**
Quality opens from the side of the understanding: human cognition is discursive and proceeds through marks. A mark is a partial representation considered as ground of cognition of a whole representation. Marks have internal use in derivation and external use in comparison. Kant then unfolds a broad grammar of marks: analytic and synthetic, coordinate and subordinate, affirmative and negative, important and unimportant, sufficient and insufficient, necessary and accidental. Quality therefore begins by specifying the determinative units of thought itself.

**Structural Moves:**

- Human cognition is discursive.
- Discursive cognition proceeds through marks.
- Marks have internal and external uses.
- Quality unfolds a grammar of mark-types.

---

### SS9. Logical Essence and Distinctness Organize the Perfection of Quality

**Distillation:**
From the complex of essential marks Kant derives logical essence, while refusing any claim on the real essence of things. Quality then ascends from clarity to distinctness. Logical distinctness is objective clarity through concepts; aesthetic distinctness is subjective clarity through intuition and examples. Complete distinctness divides into exhaustiveness, profundity, and precision, and adequacy is their ordered combination. Quality therefore organizes perfection as the clarification of marks up to adequate cognition.

**Structural Moves:**

- Logic treats logical essence, not real essence.
- Distinctness is a higher degree of clarity.
- Logical and aesthetic distinctness must be distinguished.
- Adequacy combines exhaustiveness, profundity, and precision.

---

### SS10. Analysis Makes Concepts Distinct, Synthesis Makes Distinct Concepts

**Distillation:**
Kant then draws a decisive distinction. Analysis makes a given concept distinct by clarifying what was already thought in it; it changes form but does not extend content. Synthesis makes a distinct concept by adding marks and extending content through intuition. Logic is concerned principally with the analytic side. Quality closes by setting out a ladder of cognition from representation to comprehension, and by noting that the field of understanding is broader than the field of reasoned comprehension.

**Structural Moves:**

- Analysis makes a concept distinct.
- Synthesis makes a distinct concept.
- Analysis alters form without extending content.
- Cognition rises by degrees from representation to comprehension.

---

### SS11. Modality Begins from Holding-to-be-True and Its Three Modi

**Distillation:**
Modality opens by distinguishing truth from holding-to-be-true. Truth is objective; holding-to-be-true is the subjective relation of judgment to truth. This relation may be certain or uncertain, and it divides into the three basic modi of opinion, belief, and knowledge. Modality therefore shifts attention from what is true to how judgment stands toward truth.

**Structural Moves:**

- Truth is objective.
- Holding-to-be-true is subjective.
- Certainty and uncertainty differ by necessity and contingency.
- Holding-to-be-true divides into opinion, belief, and knowledge.

---

### SS12. Opinion, Belief, and Knowledge Distribute Warrant by Domain and Sufficiency

**Distillation:**
Opinion is provisional judging under both subjective and objective insufficiency, and it belongs only to empirical domains. Belief is objectively insufficient but subjectively sufficient holding-to-be-true, grounded practically and morally rather than theoretically. Knowledge is sufficient on both sides and divides into empirical and rational certainty, the latter into mathematical and philosophical forms. Modality therefore becomes a doctrine of different warrants rather than a single scale of assent.

**Structural Moves:**

- Opinion is provisional and empirically restricted.
- Belief is practical and morally grounded.
- Knowledge is objectively and subjectively sufficient.
- Knowledge divides by source and type of certainty.

---

### SS13. Modality Closes in Proof, Science, and Conviction

**Distillation:**
Certainty may be immediate or mediated, and mediated certainty requires proof. Proof divides into direct and indirect forms, while mathematical and philosophical certainty differ as demonstration and acroamatic proof. Kant then moves from knowing to science, defined as cognition organized as a system rather than an aggregate. The chapter closes by distinguishing logical from practical conviction. Cognition therefore ends with a doctrine of proof, system, and the subjective posture of assured judgment.

**Structural Moves:**

- Certainty is immediate or mediated.
- Proof divides into direct and indirect forms.
- Science is cognition as system rather than aggregate.
- Conviction divides into logical and practical forms.
