# Cognition - Distillation

Source: [cognition.md](cognition.md)

---

### SS1. Cognition Reworks Perfection Through the Four Moments

**Distillation:**
Cognition takes the four moments already named in Idea and expands them into a far richer operator-field. Quantity becomes horizon, extent, and importance. Relation becomes truth, criterion, error, and the disclosure of illusion. Quality becomes the whole doctrine of marks, essence, clarity, distinctness, and the degrees of cognition. Modality becomes the doctrine of holding-to-be-true, with its divisions into opinion, belief, knowledge, certainty, proof, and conviction. Cognition therefore extends Life by supplying the contentful perfection of logic rather than its merely methodological scaffolding.

**Structural Moves:**

- Cognition is organized by quantity, relation, quality, and modality.
- Each moment expands into a large operator-family.
- Cognition goes beyond methodological form into contentful perfection.
- Cognition is the strongest internal extension of Idea.

---

### SS2. Quantity Divides into Extensive and Intensive Magnitudes of Cognition

**Distillation:**
The quantity of cognition has two senses. Extensive quantity concerns the extension of cognition, its multitude and manifoldness. Intensive quantity concerns its content, richness, logical importance, and fruitfulness as a ground of many and great consequences. Cognition therefore measures quantity not only by how much is included, but also by how consequentially fertile the cognition is.

**Structural Moves:**

- Extensive quantity concerns extension and manifoldness.
- Intensive quantity concerns content and richness.
- Intensive quantity concerns importance and fruitfulness.
- Quantity measures both spread and consequence.

---

### SS3. Horizon Is the Quantity-Operator That Aligns Cognition with Ends and Capacities

**Distillation:**
Once cognition is expanded, it must be measured against the subject's powers and ends. This congruence is horizon. Horizon is therefore the quantity-operator that judges how far cognition can and should be pursued. It is not merely a limit but a regulated relation between cognitive extension and the subject's capabilities, interests, and purposes.

**Structural Moves:**

- Horizon measures the congruence of cognition with ends.
- Horizon measures cognition against capacities.
- Horizon is a regulated limit, not mere privation.
- Quantity becomes purposive self-measurement.

---

### SS4. Horizon Divides Logically, Aesthetically, and Practically

**Distillation:**
Horizon itself has three modes of determination. Logically, it is determined in relation to the understanding and the question of how far cognition can or must go as means to principal ends. Aesthetically, it is determined by taste and the aim of popularity or communicability. Practically, it is determined by use in relation to the will and morality. Quantity already exceeds bare extension here: cognition is measured by intellectual, communicative, and moral interests.

**Structural Moves:**

- Logical horizon concerns the interest of the understanding.
- Aesthetic horizon concerns communicability and popularity.
- Practical horizon concerns use and morality.
- Quantity is differentiated by faculty and end.

---

### SS5. Horizon Also Divides by Object and by Subject

**Distillation:**
The logically determined horizon can be considered objectively or subjectively. In relation to objects, the horizon is historical or rational: historical cognition has no fixed limit, rational cognition can be delimited by its proper object. In relation to the subject, the horizon is absolute or private: either the horizon of humanity as such, or the conditioned horizon of a particular person, class, station, or talent. Cognition thus acquires a map of limits both by domain and by standpoint.

**Structural Moves:**

- Objective horizon divides into historical and rational.
- Historical horizon is immeasurably broad.
- Rational horizon is delimitable by kind of object.
- Subjective horizon divides into universal-human and private-conditioned forms.

---

### SS6. Beyond, Outside, and Beneath the Horizon Are Distinct Boundary Relations

**Distillation:**
Kant then differentiates the horizon by several boundary relations. What cannot be known lies beyond it. What need not be known for a given purpose lies outside it. What ought not to be known because it is harmful lies beneath it. These are relative determinations, not absolute dismissals. Cognition is therefore ordered by a nuanced topology of limit, relevance, and prohibition rather than a blunt distinction between known and unknown.

**Structural Moves:**

- Beyond the horizon marks incapacity.
- Outside the horizon marks relative dispensability.
- Beneath the horizon marks relative harmfulness.
- Horizon is a topology of differentiated limits.

---

### SS7. Quantity Includes Rules for Determining and Expanding One's Horizon

**Distillation:**
Horizon is not only described but normed. One should determine it early, not change it lightly, not impose one's own horizon on others, and neither overextend nor overrestrict it. One should also determine the universal human horizon, locate one's science within the whole of cognition, consider one's own capabilities and duties, and seek expansion rather than contraction. Quantity therefore culminates in rules of cognitive self-government.

**Structural Moves:**

- Horizon must be determined deliberately.
- It must not be confused with another's horizon.
- It must be neither swollen nor stunted.
- It should be expanded under principled orientation.

---

### SS8. Ignorance Is the Negative Imperfection Opposed to Quantitative Perfection

**Distillation:**
Opposed to perfection in extension stands ignorance, a negative imperfection inseparable from our finite understanding. Ignorance can be objective or subjective. Objectively it is material or formal, depending on whether historical or rational cognition is lacking. Subjectively it is learned or common, depending on whether one has insight into the limits of cognition. Cognition of ignorance is therefore not mere absence but a structured relation to finitude, and learned ignorance is a positive epistemic achievement.

**Structural Moves:**

- Ignorance is a negative imperfection.
- Objective ignorance divides into material and formal.
- Subjective ignorance divides into learned and common.
- Learned ignorance includes insight into cognitive limits.

---

### SS9. Quantity Ends in Architectonic Planning and the Internal Worth of Cognition

**Distillation:**
Kant rejects the utilitarian question that asks only for material gain from science. Logically perfect cognition always has possible use, even where it is not yet known. The understanding also takes satisfaction in insight itself. Because cognitions are many and manifold, they must be ordered by a plan in which one principal science can orient the rest as means. Architectonic supplies this map by arranging sciences according to kinship and systematic connection within a whole that interests humanity. The quantitative perfection of cognition therefore ends in planned, systematic expansion rather than rhapsodic accumulation.

**Structural Moves:**

- Cognition has inner worth beyond immediate utility.
- Manifold cognitions require purposive planning.
- One principal science can organize subordinate means.
- Architectonic maps the sciences as a whole.

---

### SS10. Intensive Quantity Is Importance Measured by Consequences Rather Than Difficulty

**Distillation:**
The intensive side of quantity concerns not mere spread but importance. A cognition dealing with what is great in the use of the understanding differs from micrological subtlety. Logical importance is determined by consequences, not by difficulty. A difficult cognition may be trivial, while an important cognition may be simple. Cognition without important consequences is cavilling. Intensive quantity therefore judges cognition by fertility of consequence rather than by labor or obscurity.

**Structural Moves:**

- Intensive quantity concerns greatness and consequence.
- Logical importance differs from practical utility.
- Difficulty is not a criterion of importance.
- Consequence-fertility determines importance.

---

### SS11. Relation Begins from Truth as the Essential Condition of All Perfection

**Distillation:**
The relation-moment opens with truth, which is not merely one perfection among others but the essential and inseparable condition of all perfection in cognition. Yet the nominal definition of truth as agreement of cognition with its object generates a circle if taken naively, since the object can be compared with cognition only through cognition. Cognition therefore enters the truth-problem by exposing the insufficiency of the simplest formula and demanding criteria.

**Structural Moves:**

- Truth is the essential condition of perfection.
- Mere agreement-with-object is only a nominal definition.
- Naive appeal to agreement generates circularity.
- Relation begins by reformulating the question of truth.

---

### SS12. Material Truth Has No Universal Criterion, but Formal Truth Does

**Distillation:**
The truth-question splits into material and formal criteria. A universal material criterion is impossible because such a criterion would have to abstract from all differences among objects and yet determine agreement with determinate objects. A universal formal criterion is possible because it concerns only the agreement of cognition with itself under the universal laws of the understanding and reason. Formal criteria are not sufficient for objective truth, but they are its indispensible condition. Cognition therefore secures truth first formally before it can ever claim material agreement.

**Structural Moves:**

- No universal material criterion of truth is possible.
- A universal formal criterion is possible.
- Formal truth is agreement of cognition with itself.
- Formal truth is the conditio sine qua non of objective truth.

---

### SS13. Formal Truth Is Governed by Contradiction, Sufficient Reason, and Excluded Middle

**Distillation:**
The logical criteria of truth are then fixed more precisely. The principle of contradiction determines logical possibility. The principle of sufficient reason determines logical actuality through groundedness. The principle of the excluded middle determines logical necessity, especially for apodeictic judgment. Relation thereby distributes cognition across problematic, assertoric, and apodeictic validity by means of formal principles.

**Structural Moves:**

- Contradiction governs logical possibility.
- Sufficient reason governs logical actuality.
- Excluded middle governs logical necessity.
- Truth-criteria align with modal force.

---

### SS14. Grounds and Consequences Supply Positive and Negative Tests of Truth

**Distillation:**
Formal truth is tested through the relation between grounds and consequences. If a false consequence flows from a cognition, the cognition is false: this is the apagogic or negative route. If the totality of consequences is true, one may infer the true ground: this is the positive route, though difficult because the totality of consequences is rarely available. Cognition thus acquires both a negative falsification operator and a positive grounding operator.

**Structural Moves:**

- False consequence proves false ground negatively.
- Totality of true consequences supports a true ground positively.
- Modus tollens is the apagogic route.
- Modus ponens is the positive but harder route.

---

### SS15. Error Is Falsehood Taken for Truth Through Illusion

**Distillation:**
The opposite of truth is falsehood, and falsehood taken for truth is error. Error belongs only to judgment. Its enabling condition is illusion: the confusion of merely subjective grounds with objective ones. Because sensibility supplies material under laws different from those of the understanding, illusion arises from the unnoticed influence of sensibility upon judgment. Error therefore is not a sheer production of the understanding nor simply a consequence of ignorance; it is misjudgment under the sway of subjective seeming.

**Structural Moves:**

- Error is falsehood taken for truth.
- Error belongs only to judgment.
- Illusion confuses the subjective with the objective.
- Sensibility's unnoticed influence enables illusion.

---

### SS16. Ignorance Comes from Limitation, Error from Ourselves

**Distillation:**
Kant sharply distinguishes ignorance from error. The limits of the understanding are responsible for ignorance, but not for error. Nature leaves us finite; it does not mislead us. Error is ours because we judge where we are not entitled to judge, allowing illusion to pass as objective validity. Every error remains partial, moreover, since a total opposition to the laws of thought would not even count as judgment. Relation therefore binds epistemic responsibility to the subject's own misuse of its powers.

**Structural Moves:**

- Limitation causes ignorance, not error.
- Error arises from illicit judgment.
- Nature leaves us finite but not false.
- Every error is partial rather than total.

---

### SS17. Exactness, Roughness, Subtlety, and the Disclosure of Illusion Regulate Error-Avoidance

**Distillation:**
Truth and error also bring differentiations in epistemic style. Cognition may be exact or rough depending on its purpose; broad determination is sometimes allowable, strict determination necessary in cognitions of reason and morality. Subtlety, as a subjective perfection, must be distinguished from exactness, as an objective perfection. The decisive practical rule is that one must not merely refute errors but disclose the illusion that generates them, since otherwise the same illusion will produce new errors elsewhere. Error-avoidance is therefore diagnostic before it is polemical.

**Structural Moves:**

- Exactness and roughness are purpose-relative.
- Subtlety differs from exactness.
- Broad determination can be legitimate in limited contexts.
- Disclosure of illusion is the chief means of avoiding error.

---

### SS18. Common Understanding and the Three Maxims Supply External Touchstones of Truth

**Distillation:**
An external touchstone of truth is comparison of one's judgments with those of others, since the same subjective illusion will not be identically present in everyone. Common understanding also serves as a check on the artificial use of the understanding. The final rules are the three maxims: think for oneself, think from the standpoint of others, and think consistently with oneself. Relation culminates not only in truth-criteria but in social and reflexive disciplines of judgment.

**Structural Moves:**

- Others' judgments can expose subjective illusion.
- Common understanding is a touchstone.
- Enlightened thought means thinking for oneself.
- Extended and coherent thought complete the triad of maxims.

---

### SS19. Quality Begins from Discursive Cognition as Cognition Through Marks

**Distillation:**
The quality-moment opens by defining human cognition from the side of the understanding as discursive. We cognize through marks, partial representations that function as grounds of cognition for a whole representation. All concepts are marks, and thought itself is representing through marks. Quality therefore begins from a structural account of how cognition carries determinacy at all.

**Structural Moves:**

- Human cognition is discursive.
- Discursive cognition proceeds through marks.
- Marks are partial representations that ground wholes.
- All thought is representation through marks.

---

### SS20. Marks Have Internal and External Uses and a Complex Classification

**Distillation:**
Marks are not static items but functional grounds of cognition. Internally they serve derivation within a thing; externally they serve comparison among things according to identity and diversity. Kant then classifies them as analytic or synthetic, coordinate or subordinate, affirmative or negative, important or empty, sufficient or insufficient, necessary or accidental. Quality thus acquires a full grammar of determinative components.

**Structural Moves:**

- Internal use of marks serves derivation.
- External use of marks serves comparison.
- Marks divide into multiple operator pairs.
- Quality is built from a grammar of mark-types.

---

### SS21. Coordinate and Subordinate Marks Generate Aggregates, Series, and Distinctness Growth

**Distillation:**
The coordinate/subordinate distinction carries especially rich consequences. Coordinate marks aggregate into the totality of a concept; subordinate marks form a series. With the synthesis of coordinate marks, extensive distinctness grows. With the analysis of subordinate marks, intensive or deep distinctness grows. The series of subordinate marks has no final lowest species on the consequence side, while empirical coordinate aggregation can never be complete. Quality here directly links mark-order to kinds of distinctness and to the architecture of concept-development.

**Structural Moves:**

- Coordinate marks form aggregates.
- Subordinate marks form series.
- Aggregation grows extensive distinctness.
- Analysis of subordination grows intensive distinctness.

---

### SS22. Negative, Important, Sufficient, and Necessary Marks Regulate Correct Cognition

**Distillation:**
Other mark-types regulate cognition differently. Negative marks do not teach what a thing is, but prevent misunderstanding and error. Important marks yield many consequences. Sufficient marks distinguish a thing from all others relative to intended ends. Necessary marks belong always to the thing and divide into primitive constitutive marks and derived attributes. Quality therefore includes defensive, fruitful, discriminative, and essential roles of marks.

**Structural Moves:**

- Negative marks prevent error.
- Important marks are consequence-rich.
- Sufficient marks secure discrimination.
- Necessary marks divide into primitive and derivative forms.

---

### SS23. Logical Essence Is the First Basic Concept of Necessary Marks, Not Real Essence

**Distillation:**
From the complex of essential marks Kant derives the notion of essence. Logic, however, can treat only logical essence, not real or natural essence. Logical essence is the first basic concept of all the necessary marks of a thing, determined through its concept. Real essence would require insight into the grounds of the thing's existence, which logic cannot provide. Quality thereby fixes the legitimate scope of formal determination.

**Structural Moves:**

- Essence is the complex of essential marks.
- Logic treats only logical essence.
- Real essence lies beyond formal logic.
- Logical essence is the first basic concept of necessary marks.

---

### SS24. Distinctness Is a Higher Degree of Clarity and Divides Logically and Aesthetically

**Distillation:**
The first stage of quality-perfection is clarity; the second is distinctness. Distinctness is clarity of marks. Logical distinctness is objective clarity through concepts, while aesthetic distinctness is subjective clarity through intuition, examples, and understandability. These can support but also damage one another. Lucidity is their successful combination. Quality therefore refuses to collapse rigor and communicability into one simple standard.

**Structural Moves:**

- Clarity is the first stage of quality.
- Distinctness is the second stage.
- Logical and aesthetic distinctness differ by medium.
- Lucidity combines both forms.

---

### SS25. Complete Distinctness Divides into Exhaustiveness, Profundity, and Adequacy

**Distillation:**
Distinctness can be complete in two ways. Extensively complete distinctness is total clarity of coordinate marks and is called exhaustiveness. Intensively complete distinctness is total clarity of subordinate marks and is called profundity. Precision is non-superfluous extensive quantity of distinctness, and together exhaustiveness and precision constitute adequacy. The consummate perfection of cognition as to quality is therefore intensively and extensively adequate cognition.

**Structural Moves:**

- Exhaustiveness is total clarity of coordinate marks.
- Profundity is total clarity of subordinate marks.
- Precision is non-superfluous extensive distinctness.
- Adequacy combines exhaustiveness and precision.

---

### SS26. Analysis Makes Concepts Distinct, Synthesis Makes Distinct Concepts

**Distillation:**
Kant then distinguishes two very different operations often confused. Analysis makes a given concept distinct by clarifying what was already thought in it; this changes form but not content. Synthesis makes a distinct concept by adding marks and extending content through intuition, whether pure or empirical. Logic is concerned principally with the analytic procedure. Cognition therefore draws a decisive line between formal clarification and content-expansion.

**Structural Moves:**

- Analysis clarifies a given concept.
- Synthesis constructs a distinct concept.
- Analysis alters form without extending content.
- Logic is concerned primarily with analytic distinctness.

---

### SS27. The Degrees of Cognition Ascend from Representation to Comprehension

**Distillation:**
Quality ends with a ladder of degrees of cognition. One first represents, then perceives with consciousness, then is acquainted through comparison, then cognizes with consciousness, then understands through concepts, then has rational insight, and finally comprehends sufficiently for a purpose. The higher the degree, the fewer cognitions attain it. Understanding ranges more widely than reasoned comprehension. Cognition therefore organizes perfection as a graded ascent rather than a simple possession.

**Structural Moves:**

- Cognition advances by seven degrees.
- Perception adds consciousness.
- Understanding and insight surpass acquaintance.
- Comprehension is relative to purpose and rarer than mere understanding.

---

### SS28. Modality Begins from Holding-to-be-True as the Subjective Side of Truth

**Distillation:**
Modality opens by distinguishing truth from holding-to-be-true. Truth is the objective property of cognition; holding-to-be-true is the subjective relation of judgment to an understanding. Holding-to-be-true may be certain or uncertain depending on consciousness of necessity or contingency. Cognition thus re-describes modality as the subjective stance taken toward truth.

**Structural Moves:**

- Truth is objective.
- Holding-to-be-true is subjective.
- Certainty includes consciousness of necessity.
- Uncertainty includes consciousness of contingency.

---

### SS29. Opinion, Belief, and Knowledge Are the Three Modi of Holding-to-be-True

**Distillation:**
The three basic modalities are then fixed. Opinion is problematic judging. Belief is assertoric judging with subjective sufficiency but objective insufficiency. Knowledge is apodeictic judging with both objective and subjective sufficiency. These modes concern the power of judgment in subsuming under objective rules. Modality therefore distributes cognition by degrees of warrant rather than by content.

**Structural Moves:**

- Opinion is problematic.
- Belief is assertoric but only subjectively sufficient.
- Knowledge is apodeictic and sufficient on both sides.
- Modality organizes warrant.

---

### SS30. Opinion Is a Provisional and Empirically Restricted Mode of Cognition

**Distillation:**
Opinion is provisional judging under suspension. It is indispensable as a starting point, but must never be mistaken for more than opinion. Opinion belongs only to empirical cognitions, never to sciences grounded a priori such as mathematics, metaphysics, or morals. It arises where a cognition is possible in itself yet inaccessible to us under our present empirical limitations. Modality therefore begins from a guarded permission to judge under insufficiency.

**Structural Moves:**

- Opinion is provisional judging.
- Opinion must not be overvalued.
- Opinion belongs only to empirical domains.
- Opinion arises under limited access to otherwise possible cognition.

---

### SS31. Belief Is Free Practical Holding-to-be-True Under Moral Interest

**Distillation:**
Belief is subjectively sufficient but objectively insufficient holding-to-be-true. It concerns objects that cannot be known, opined, or even rendered probable theoretically, but can be accepted on moral grounds where their contradiction is unprovable. Belief is not empirical knowledge, not theoretical rational knowledge, and not legal or moral permissiveness in matters demanding certainty. Its ground is practical necessity and moral interest. Modality therefore includes a specifically practical form of certainty that does not become knowledge.

**Structural Moves:**

- Belief is free but practically necessary holding-to-be-true.
- Belief is neither empirical knowledge nor theoretical science.
- Belief is grounded in moral interest.
- Belief yields personal but not universally communicable conviction.

---

### SS32. Knowledge Is Objective and Subjective Sufficiency, Dividing into Empirical and Rational Certainty

**Distillation:**
Knowledge is holding-to-be-true grounded sufficiently both objectively and subjectively. It divides first into empirical and rational certainty according to whether it rests on experience or reason. Rational certainty further divides into mathematical and philosophical certainty, the former intuitive and evident, the latter discursive. Empirical certainty divides into original and historical forms. Modality therefore develops a full topology of certainty by source and mode.

**Structural Moves:**

- Knowledge is sufficient on both sides.
- Knowledge divides into empirical and rational certainty.
- Rational certainty divides into mathematical and philosophical forms.
- Empirical certainty divides into original and historical forms.

---

### SS33. Certainty Requires Immediate Starting Points, Proof, and the Distinction Between Demonstration and Acroamatic Proof

**Distillation:**
Certainty may be immediate or mediated. Even mediated certainty through proof must ultimately rest on something immediately certain. Proofs are direct or indirect, and indirect proofs require contradictory opposition. Mathematical certainty rests on demonstration, philosophical certainty on acroamatic proof. Matter and form of proof are ground of proof and consequentia. Modality thus culminates in a formal doctrine of proof.

**Structural Moves:**

- Certainty is immediate or mediated.
- Mediated certainty requires proof.
- Proof divides into direct and indirect forms.
- Demonstration and acroamatic proof distinguish mathematical and philosophical certainty.

---

### SS34. Science Is Systematic Knowing, and Conviction Divides Logically and Practically

**Distillation:**
From knowing comes science: the complex of cognition as a system rather than an aggregate. A system rests on an idea of the whole that precedes the parts. Historical sciences and sciences of reason are both possible. The chapter ends by dividing conviction into logical and practical forms. Logical conviction rests on objective grounds free of subjective interference; practical conviction rests on subjective grounds that hold with practical force. Practical conviction or moral belief can even be firmer than knowledge. Cognition therefore closes by converting modality into the systematic and existential posture of a thinking subject.

**Structural Moves:**

- Science is cognition as system.
- System is ordered by an idea of the whole.
- Conviction divides into logical and practical forms.
- Practical conviction may exceed knowledge in firmness.
