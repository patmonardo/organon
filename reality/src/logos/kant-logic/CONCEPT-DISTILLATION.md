### SS1. Every Concept Has Content and Extension

**Distillation:**
Kant's first move is to distinguish two inseparable aspects of a concept. As partial concept, a concept is contained in the representation of things; as ground of cognition or mark, things are contained under it. In the first respect a concept has content, in the second it has extension. Concept is therefore already a two-sided machine: it has an inside made of marks and an outside made of what falls under it.

**Structural Moves:**

- Content concerns what the concept contains in itself as marks.
- Extension concerns what is contained under the concept.
- Concept has an inside of marks and an outside of subsumed instances.

---

### SS2. Content and Extension Stand in an Inverse Law

**Distillation:**
Content and extension of a concept stand in inverse relation to one another. The more a concept contains under itself, the less it contains in itself, and conversely. Conceptual order is therefore not accidental classification but a lawful economy: breadth of sphere is bought at the price of inner determinateness, and inner determinateness is bought at the price of breadth.

**Structural Moves:**

- More extension means less content.
- More content means less extension.
- Conceptual order is governed by an inverse law.

---

### SS3. Extension Is Measured as Sphere

**Distillation:**
The extension of a concept is its sphere. The more things stand under a concept and can be thought through it, the greater its extension. Kant is therefore already using a spatial protocol for concepts: conceptual breadth is measured as the size of a sphere of subordination.

**Structural Moves:**

- Extension is measured by the multiplicity of what stands under a concept.
- Sphere names the extent of conceptual reach.
- Conceptual quantity is spherical, not merely numerical.

---

### SS4. Higher and Lower Concepts Are Remote-Mark Relations

**Distillation:**
Higher and lower concepts express logical subordination. A concept is higher insofar as it has other concepts under it, and lower in relation to a concept over it. Kant defines the higher concept as a mark of a mark, a remote mark. The lower concept is what stands in relation to that remote mark. Conceptual hierarchy is therefore not just broader and narrower scope but a chain of mediated marks.

**Structural Moves:**

- Higher concepts contain lower concepts under themselves.
- A higher concept is a remote mark, a mark of a mark.
- Lower concepts are mediated through that remote mark.

---

### SS5. Genus and Species Are Positional, Not Essential, Differences

**Distillation:**
Genus and species rest on the same structure as higher and lower concepts. The higher concept is genus with respect to its lower; the lower concept is species with respect to its higher. They differ not by kind of being but only by their relation to one another in logical subordination. Genus and species are therefore operator-positions inside conceptual order, not fixed ontological ranks.

**Structural Moves:**

- Genus names the higher position.
- Species names the lower position.
- The distinction is relational, not essential.

---

### SS6. Conceptual Order Includes Limits, Continuity, and Convertibility

**Distillation:**
Kant then refines conceptual ordering. The highest genus is what is not itself a species; the lowest species is what is not itself a genus. Yet by the law of continuity there can be neither a lowest nor a next species. Higher and lower can also be redescribed as broader and narrower. Concepts with one and the same sphere are convertible concepts. Conceptual order therefore includes not just hierarchy but continuity and reciprocity.

**Structural Moves:**

- Highest genus is not species.
- Lowest species is not genus, yet continuity blocks a final lowest species.
- Broader and narrower rename higher and lower by sphere.
- Convertible concepts share one and the same sphere.

---

### SS7. Lower Concepts Are Under Higher Ones, Not Inside Them

**Distillation:**
The lower concept is not contained in the higher one, because it contains more in itself than the higher. It is contained under the higher, however, because the higher contains the ground of cognition of the lower. This is one of the key protocols in the file. Conceptual subordination is not enclosure of richer content inside poorer content. It is grounding: the higher supplies the cognitive basis under which the lower is ordered.

**Structural Moves:**

- The lower is not contained in the higher as richer content.
- The lower is contained under the higher as grounded by it.
- The higher contains the ground of cognition of the lower.
- Subordination is grounding, not stuffing.

---

### SS8. Subordination Carries Universal Transmission Rules

**Distillation:**
Conceptual subordination is governed by universal rules. First, what belongs to or contradicts higher concepts also belongs to or contradicts all lower concepts under them. Second, what belongs to or contradicts all lower concepts also belongs to or contradicts their higher concept. Conceptual order therefore carries bidirectional rule transfer: downward through inclusion and upward through commonality.

**Structural Moves:**

- Predicates and contradictions transmit downward from higher to lower.
- What is common to all lowers transmits upward to the higher.
- Logical hierarchy is a rule-transfer machine.

---

### SS9. Abstraction and Determination Generate the Ladder

**Distillation:**
Higher and lower concepts arise through two opposed logical operations. Continued abstraction produces higher and higher concepts by thinking away determinations. Continued determination produces lower and lower concepts by adding determinations. The greatest possible abstraction yields the highest or most abstract concept; completed determination would yield a thoroughly determinate concept. The conceptual ladder is therefore generated by reversible ascent and descent.

**Structural Moves:**

- Abstraction strips away determinations and ascends.
- Determination adds determinations and descends.
- Highest abstraction yields the most abstract concept.
- Completed determination yields the thoroughly determinate concept.

---

### SS10. Abstract and Concrete Are Relational Uses of Concepts

**Distillation:**
Abstract and concrete are not fixed properties of isolated concepts. Every concept can be used universally or particularly, in abstracto or in concreto. The lower concept is used in abstracto in relation to its higher one; the higher concept is used in concreto in relation to its lower one. Abstract and concrete are therefore functional roles determined by position in the ladder, not intrinsic properties.

**Structural Moves:**

- Every concept can be used in abstracto or in concreto.
- Lower is abstract relative to higher.
- Higher is concrete relative to lower.
- Abstract and concrete are relational use-modes.
