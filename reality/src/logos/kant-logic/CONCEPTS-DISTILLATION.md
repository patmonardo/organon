# Concepts - Distillation

Source: [concepts.md](concepts.md)

---

### TC1. The Analytic of Concepts Seeks a System, Not a Rhapsody

**Distillation:**
The Transcendental Analytic begins by insisting that pure understanding must be treated systematically rather than by opportunistic concept-collecting. A transcendental inventory of concepts cannot proceed by chance or by empirical accumulation; it must arise from a principle that shows both the place and the completeness of the pure concepts of understanding.

**Structural Moves:**

- Pure understanding must be grasped as a self-sufficient system.
- A priori concepts require principled discovery, not rough aggregation.
- The Analytic of Concepts researches the birthplace of pure concepts in understanding itself.

---

### TC2. Understanding Is Discursive and Operates Through Judgment

**Distillation:**
Kant next defines understanding as a discursive faculty: it does not intuit but thinks through concepts. Concepts are functions for ordering representations under common unity, and judgments are the mediate cognition in which those functions become explicit. This is why understanding can be represented as a faculty for judging.

**Structural Moves:**

- Human understanding cognizes through concepts rather than intuition.
- Concepts rest on functions of unifying representations.
- Judgment reveals the operative form of understanding.

---

### TC3. The Table of Judgments Gives the Clue to the Categories

**Distillation:**
Because the same function of unity that orders judgments also orders the synthesis of intuitions, the logical table of judgments becomes the clue to the pure concepts of understanding. Quantity, quality, relation, and modality provide not just a logical classification but the architectonic route by which categories can be generated a priori rather than assembled by induction.

**Structural Moves:**

- The understanding's logical functions can be exhaustively displayed in judgments.
- The same functions ground unity in intuition.
- The table of judgments becomes the principled route to the categories.

---

### TC4. The Categories Form a Complete and Structured Table

**Distillation:**
The categories are presented as the original pure concepts of synthesis through which the understanding can think an object of intuition in general. Their table is complete because it is generated from the faculty of judging itself. Kant then adds two important structural remarks: the classes divide into mathematical and dynamical families, and within each class the third category arises through a special act that combines the first two without being reducible to them.

**Structural Moves:**

- Categories are the ancestral concepts of pure understanding.
- Their completeness is systematic, not inductive.
- Mathematical and dynamical classes mark two different orientations of the table.
- The third category in each class is synthetic, not merely derivative.

---

### TC5. Unity, Truth, and Perfection Are Not Additional Categories

**Distillation:**
Kant closes by rejecting the scholastic thought that unity, truth, and perfection add further transcendental predicates to the table. These are logical criteria of cognition's agreement with itself, not additional pure concepts of objects. The chapter therefore protects the category table from inflation while clarifying that formal criteria of cognition must not be mistaken for new ontological titles.

**Structural Moves:**

- Unity, truth, and perfection are logical criteria, not new categories.
- The category table is complete without scholastic supplementation.
- Formal requirements of cognition must not be mistaken for properties of things.
