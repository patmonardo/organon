# Deduction Meta - Distillation

Source: [deduction-meta.md](deduction-meta.md)

---

### DM1. Deduction Asks for Lawful Entitlement, Not Mere Origin

**Distillation:**
The chapter opens by distinguishing the factual origin of a concept from its lawful claim to objective validity. Empirical concepts can rely on experience for their use, but pure concepts used a priori require a transcendental deduction. The issue is not how they happen to arise in us, but how they can rightfully relate to objects a priori.

**Structural Moves:**

- Deduction concerns quid juris rather than quid facti.
- Empirical derivation cannot justify pure a priori concepts.
- The categories require a transcendental birth certificate.

---

### DM2. Categories Must Be Conditions of Possible Experience

**Distillation:**
Kant argues that the only viable path for a deduction is to show that the categories are a priori conditions of possible experience. If all empirical cognition already presupposes intuition plus concept, then the pure concepts of understanding must be what make objects of experience thinkable in the first place. Their objective validity therefore depends on their role in the possibility of experience.

**Structural Moves:**

- Experience contains both given intuition and object-concepts.
- Categories must be tied to possible experience if they are to have content.
- Objective validity is secured through transcendental conditions of experience.

---

### DM3. Sense, Imagination, and Apperception Ground Experience

**Distillation:**
The deduction then turns to the three original sources of cognition: sense, imagination, and apperception. These ground synopsis, synthesis, and unity. Experience is possible only if the manifold can be apprehended, reproduced, and recognized as one cognition rather than dispersing into isolated impressions.

**Structural Moves:**

- Sense supplies the manifold.
- Imagination synthesizes and reproduces it.
- Apperception unifies the synthesis into one consciousness.

---

### DM4. The Object Is the Necessary Unity of Synthesis Under Apperception

**Distillation:**
An object is not something separately intuited beyond representation, but that X in relation to which the manifold must stand under necessary unity. The objectivity of cognition arises when the manifold is connected according to rules that make recognition possible in one consciousness. Transcendental apperception is therefore the law of unity that every object of experience presupposes.

**Structural Moves:**

- Objectivity is tied to necessary unity rather than arbitrary association.
- Recognition in the concept presupposes the identity of consciousness.
- The transcendental object = X names the objectivity-correlate of lawful synthesis.

---

### DM5. Affinity and Nature Rest on Categories, Not Induction

**Distillation:**
Kant closes by arguing that appearances stand under a transcendental affinity because they must be combinable in one apperception. Categories are therefore not empirical habits abstracted by induction; they are the conditions that first make law-governed experience possible. Nature as lawful appearance depends on this prior unity of synthesis.

**Structural Moves:**

- Empirical association presupposes deeper affinity of appearances.
- Affinity arises from transcendental unity, not repeated habit.
- Categories ground the lawfulness that experience later displays.
