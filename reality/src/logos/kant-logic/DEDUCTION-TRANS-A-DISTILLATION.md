# Deduction Trans A - Distillation

Source: [deduction-trans-a.md](deduction-trans-a.md)

---

### DM1. Deduction Asks for Lawful Entitlement, Not Mere Origin

**Distillation:**
The chapter opens with a juridical distinction that governs everything that follows. A deduction is not a story about where a concept came from, but a justification of its right to objective use. Empirical concepts can point to experience as their witness. Pure concepts cannot. If they are to be used a priori, they need a transcendental deduction that explains how they can relate to objects without borrowing anything from experience. Locke-style genealogy may have utility for the history of concept-acquisition, but it cannot supply the required title of legitimacy.

**Structural Moves:**

- Deduction concerns quid juris rather than quid facti.
- Pure concepts need a transcendental, not empirical, deduction.
- Physiological or genealogical derivation cannot justify objective validity.

---

### DM2. The Categories Present a Special Difficulty Beyond Space and Time

**Distillation:**
Kant then explains why the categories require a harder deduction than space and time. Space and time are forms of intuition through which appearances can be immediately given, so their objective validity is more readily seen. Categories, by contrast, do not present conditions under which objects are given, but conditions under which objects are thought. That is why their objective validity is initially doubtful. Nothing in mere appearance, taken by itself, guarantees that the manifold will stand under rules such as causality. If cause were merely abstracted from repetition, necessity would evaporate into habit. The whole challenge of the deduction is therefore to show how subjective conditions of thinking can have objective validity for experience.

**Structural Moves:**

- Categories do not give objects in intuition.
- Their objective validity is therefore not immediately evident.
- Causality cannot be explained by empirical association alone.
- The key problem is how subjective conditions of thought bind objects.

---

### DM3. The Only Viable Deduction Shows the Categories as Conditions of Possible Experience

**Distillation:**
The argument then narrows to its decisive path. There are only two ways representation and object can meet: either the object makes the representation possible, or the representation is a condition for cognizing an object. Since pure concepts are a priori, their legitimacy must lie in the second route. Every object of experience requires both intuition, through which it is given, and concept, through which it is thought. Hence if the categories are the a priori concepts under which alone anything can be thought as an object of experience, their objective validity rests on the fact that experience itself is possible only through them. This is the principle of the transcendental deduction.

**Structural Moves:**

- Representation and object can meet only through two basic routes.
- Objects of experience require both intuition and concept.
- Categories have objective validity only as conditions of possible experience.
- Illustration from experience is not the same as deduction.

---

### DM4. Experience Depends on Three Original Sources and Three Syntheses

**Distillation:**
Once experience is taken as the decisive field, Kant lays out its transcendental sources. Sense, imagination, and apperception ground the synopsis of the manifold, its synthesis, and the unity of that synthesis. In the second section this becomes the threefold synthesis of apprehension, reproduction, and recognition. The manifold must be run through, retained, and recognized as belonging to one act of cognition. Without this coordinated structure there would be only dispersing impressions, never an experience.

**Structural Moves:**

- Sense provides the manifold.
- Imagination synthesizes and reproduces it.
- Apperception gives the unity in one consciousness.
- Experience requires apprehension, reproduction, and recognition together.

---

### DM5. The Object Is the Necessary Unity of Synthesis Under Apperception

**Distillation:**
Kant then makes the decisive move from synthesis to objectivity. An object is not something separately encountered outside cognition and then compared with it. For us, the object is that = X in relation to which the manifold must stand under necessary unity. Recognition in the concept requires consciousness that what is now thought is the same as what was thought before. This identity cannot come from empirical self-observation, which is variable, but only from transcendental apperception, the original and unchanging unity of consciousness. Objectivity is therefore the lawful unity of the manifold under rules that make one consciousness possible.

**Structural Moves:**

- Recognition requires identity of consciousness across the synthesis.
- The object = X names the correlate of necessary unity, not a separately intuited thing.
- Transcendental apperception grounds the unity required for objectivity.
- Rules of synthesis are the condition for objects of experience.

---

### DM6. Affinity, Nature, and Experience Rest on the Categories

**Distillation:**
The chapter closes by turning lawful synthesis into lawful nature. Association in imagination is not enough; it presupposes that appearances themselves stand under a transcendental affinity. Because all appearances must belong to one self-consciousness, they must be combinable according to universal rules. This affinity grounds the possibility of empirical association, reproduction, and finally experience. In the third section Kant restates the point from above downward: there is one experience, one synthetic unity, and the categories are the pure concepts through which the understanding serves as the formal and synthetic principle of all experience. Nature, as the law-governed totality of appearances, depends on this prior transcendental unity, not on induction.

**Structural Moves:**

- Empirical association presupposes transcendental affinity.
- Affinity arises from the unity of apperception.
- The productive imagination mediates this lawful unity.
- Categories are the formal and synthetic principles of all experience.
