# Deduction Trans - Distillation

Source: [deduction-trans.md](deduction-trans.md)

---

### DT1. Combination Comes from Spontaneity, Not from Sense

**Distillation:**
The B-deduction opens by insisting that combination is never given by the senses. Sensibility provides a manifold, but combination is an act of spontaneity. The unity of combination presupposes a prior unity of consciousness that makes any combined object thinkable at all.

**Structural Moves:**

- Sense gives manifoldness; understanding combines.
- Combination presupposes unity of consciousness.
- The deduction begins from synthesis rather than from isolated givenness.

---

### DT2. Original Synthetic Unity of Apperception Is the Supreme Principle

**Distillation:**
The famous “I think” thesis states that all representations must be able to accompany one self-consciousness. This original synthetic unity of apperception is not derived from experience but is the a priori condition under which the manifold can belong to one cognition. It is therefore the supreme principle of all use of the understanding.

**Structural Moves:**

- The I think must be able to accompany all representations.
- Apperception is original, pure, and synthetic.
- The unity of consciousness grounds the possibility of cognition.

---

### DT3. Judgment and Category Express Objective Unity

**Distillation:**
Kant then shows that judgment is the way cognitions are brought to objective unity of apperception. The categories are those same functions of judging insofar as they determine the manifold of intuition. Hence all sensible intuitions stand under categories as conditions of possible objectivity.

**Structural Moves:**

- Judgment is objective unity, not mere association.
- Categories are judgment-functions applied to intuition.
- Sensible intuitions can become objects only under categorical unity.

---

### DT4. Categories Need Sensible Intuition and Imagination

**Distillation:**
The deduction also limits the categories: they yield cognition only when applied to objects of possible experience. Their application requires the transcendental synthesis of imagination, which mediates between understanding and sensibility. This is why self-consciousness alone is not self-knowledge, and why inner sense still yields only appearance.

**Structural Moves:**

- Categories are empty without intuition.
- Figurative synthesis mediates understanding and sensibility.
- Inner sense gives appearance, not intellectual self-intuition.

---

### DT5. Categories Legislate the Form of Nature Because They Make Experience Possible

**Distillation:**
In the culminating move, Kant argues that the categories prescribe laws to nature as the form of connected appearances. This is not because understanding invents things in themselves, but because appearances as possible experience must conform to the unity that the understanding requires. The deduction therefore ends by grounding the lawfulness of experience in the categories and rejecting both empiricist derivation and preformationist shortcuts.

**Structural Moves:**

- Categories are conditions of the connectedness of experience.
- Nature as appearance must conform to understanding's lawful unity.
- The categories arise as conditions of experience, not implanted habits.
