# Deduction Trans - Distillation

Source: [deduction-trans.md](deduction-trans.md)

---

### DT1. Combination Comes from Spontaneity, Not from Sense

**Distillation:**
The B-deduction begins by relocating the source of combination. Sensibility can give a manifold, but it cannot give the manifold as combined. Combination is an act of spontaneity, hence an act of understanding. This already means that objectivity cannot be passively read off from the sensible given. Even analysis presupposes a prior synthesis. Kant therefore starts from self-activity: we can represent nothing as combined in the object unless we have first combined it ourselves. But combination also implies unity, and that unity cannot itself be extracted from the manifold. It must be sought in a still higher source.

**Structural Moves:**

- Sense gives manifoldness, but not conjunction.
- Combination is an act of spontaneity.
- Analysis presupposes prior synthesis.
- The unity of combination must be grounded above the category of unity.

### DT2. The I Think Names the Original Synthetic Unity That Grounds Cognition

**Distillation:**
Kant then introduces the decisive principle: the I think must be able to accompany all my representations. This is not an empirical observation about consciousness but the condition under which anything can count as mine at all. Pure apperception is original because it is not itself accompanied by any further representation; it is synthetic because the identity of consciousness is possible only through the combination of the manifold in one consciousness. Hence the analytical unity of apperception presupposes a synthetic one. This original synthetic unity of apperception is the highest principle of human cognition, because without it no manifold could belong to one object for one subject.

**Structural Moves:**

- The I think must be able to accompany all representations.
- Pure apperception is original, not empirical.
- Analytical unity presupposes synthetic unity.
- Original synthetic unity of apperception is the supreme principle of cognition.

### DT3. Objective Unity, Judgment, and Category Are One Line of Argument

**Distillation:**
The next movement clarifies what sort of unity is at stake. Objective unity of self-consciousness is not the contingent unity of associated inner states, but the necessary unity through which the manifold is united in the concept of an object. From here Kant redefines judgment: a judgment is not merely a relation between concepts, but the way given cognitions are brought to the objective unity of apperception. The copula marks precisely this objective tie. Categories then appear as those same functions for judging insofar as they determine the manifold of intuition. Thus all sensible intuitions stand under categories because only under them can their manifold come together in one consciousness as an object.

**Structural Moves:**

- Objective unity differs from merely subjective association.
- Judgment brings cognitions to objective unity of apperception.
- The copula marks objective, not merely associative, connection.
- Categories are judgment-functions determining sensible manifold.

---

### DT4. The Categories Cognize Only What Can Be Given in Experience

**Distillation:**
Kant then fixes the boundary. To think an object and to cognize one are not the same. Cognition requires both concept and intuition. Since all intuition available to us is sensible, the categories can yield cognition only when related to objects of the senses, hence to possible experience. Beyond that they remain empty forms of thought. They may be extended in abstraction to any sensible intuition whatever, but without a corresponding intuition they provide no genuine cognition. This is the strict critical limit of the deduction: the categories have no legitimate cognitive use beyond objects of possible experience.

**Structural Moves:**

- Thinking an object is not yet cognizing it.
- Cognition requires both concept and intuition.
- For us, all intuition is sensible.
- Categories have cognitive use only within possible experience.

---

### DT5. Figurative Synthesis Shows How Understanding Reaches Sensibility

**Distillation:**
The deduction then turns to application. Categories are mere forms of thought unless they acquire objective reality in relation to our form of sensible intuition. This happens through figurative synthesis, the transcendental synthesis of imagination. Imagination belongs to sensibility insofar as it supplies intuition, but to spontaneity insofar as it determines sensibility a priori in accordance with apperception. It is therefore the first application of understanding to the sensible field. This is also the point at which Kant makes the paradox of inner sense intelligible: the understanding affects inner sense through the imagination, and so even self-intuition yields only appearance, not intellectual self-presence.

**Structural Moves:**

- Categories acquire objective reality only for appearances.
- Figurative synthesis differs from merely intellectual combination.
- Productive imagination mediates understanding and sensibility.
- Inner sense is determined by understanding and still yields only appearance.

### DT6. Self-Consciousness Is Not Yet Self-Knowledge

**Distillation:**
Section 25 draws the limit inward. In original apperception I am conscious not of what I am, but only that I am. Self-consciousness is a thought, not an intuition. To cognize myself I would need an intuition through which the manifold in me is given, and for us that intuition remains bound to inner sense and its temporal form. Hence even the subject knows itself only as appearance, not as it is in itself. The deduction therefore refuses any leap from transcendental self-consciousness to intellectual self-cognition.

**Structural Moves:**

- I am conscious that I am, not intuitively of what I am.
- Self-consciousness is thinking, not self-intuition.
- Self-knowledge requires intuition in addition to thought.
- Inner self-cognition remains appearance-bound.

---

### DT7. The Categories Prescribe the Form of Nature Because They Make Experience Possible

**Distillation:**
The culminating movement of the B-deduction returns from the pure condition to experienced nature. Every synthesis of apprehension in perception already stands under the categories. The house exemplifies quantity; the freezing of water exemplifies causality. Because all connected perception stands under categories, they prescribe laws a priori to appearances, that is, to nature as the sum of appearances. This is not because understanding invents things in themselves, but because appearances, as representations, have lawfulness only relative to the subject that combines them. The result is decisive: no a priori cognition is possible for us except of objects of possible experience, yet those objects are possible as experience only because the categories contain the grounds of that possibility. Kant therefore rejects both empiricist derivation and any preformationist shortcut, and presents the categories as principles of the epigenesis of experience.

**Structural Moves:**

- Perception itself stands under categorical synthesis.
- Categories prescribe laws a priori to appearances as nature.
- Nature's lawfulness concerns appearances, not things in themselves.
- The result of the deduction is experience-bound a priori cognition.
- Empiricism and preformationism are both rejected.
