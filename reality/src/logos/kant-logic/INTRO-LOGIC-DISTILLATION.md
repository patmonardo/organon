# Intro Logic - Distillation

Source: [intro-logic.md](intro-logic.md)

---

### TL1. Logic Begins from the Split Between Sensibility and Understanding

**Distillation:**
Kant opens by separating the receptive giving of objects from the spontaneous thinking of objects. Aesthetic treats the rules of sensibility; logic treats the rules of understanding. General logic therefore concerns the form of thinking, not the sensible matter through which objects are given.

**Structural Moves:**

- Sensibility gives; understanding thinks.
- Aesthetic and logic are distinguished by cognitive role.
- General logic abstracts from the object-difference of cognitions.

---

### TL2. Transcendental Logic Is Not Mere A Priori Formalism

**Distillation:**
Not every a priori representation is transcendental. Transcendental cognition asks how a priori representations or concepts are possible and how they can relate to objects a priori. Transcendental logic therefore studies pure understanding and reason only insofar as they concern objects, not merely the form of thinking in the abstract.

**Structural Moves:**

- The transcendental concerns the possibility and use of a priori cognition.
- Pure intuition by itself is not yet transcendental.
- Transcendental logic is object-related a priori logic.

---

### TL3. General Logic Supplies Only a Negative Criterion of Truth

**Distillation:**
Because general logic abstracts from the content of cognition, it can test only whether cognition agrees with the formal laws of thinking. It provides a negative criterion of truth, never a sufficient criterion of the truth of objects. When treated as an organon for producing objective knowledge, it degenerates into dialectic in the pejorative sense: a logic of illusion.

**Structural Moves:**

- Formal consistency is necessary but not sufficient for truth.
- Logic cannot decide the material truth of objects by itself.
- Misused as an organon, general logic becomes dialectical illusion.

---

### TL4. Transcendental Logic Divides into Analytic and Dialectic

**Distillation:**
Once understanding is isolated from sensibility, transcendental logic divides according to lawful and lawless use. The transcendental analytic treats the elements and principles without which no object can be thought at all, and so functions as a logic of truth. The transcendental dialectic critiques the illusion that arises when pure understanding or reason is used beyond possible experience as though it could determine objects in general.

**Structural Moves:**

- Analytic concerns the valid a priori use of understanding for possible experience.
- Dialectic concerns the critique of transcendent illusion.
- The introduction establishes the architectonic of the whole transcendental logic.
