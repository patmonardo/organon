# Intro Logic - Distillation

Source: [intro-logic.md](intro-logic.md)

---

### TL1. Logic Begins from the Split Between Sensibility and Understanding

**Distillation:**
Kant opens by separating the receptive giving of objects from the spontaneous thinking of objects. Sensibility gives through intuition; understanding thinks through concepts. Neither can replace the other. Without sensibility nothing is given, without understanding nothing is thought, and only their unification yields cognition. This is why aesthetic and logic must be sharply distinguished from the start. The one treats the rules of sensibility, the other the rules of understanding.

**Structural Moves:**

- Sensibility gives; understanding thinks.
- Cognition requires the union of intuition and concept.
- Aesthetic and logic are distinguished by cognitive role.

---

### TL2. General Logic Treats Only the Form of Thinking and Divides into Pure and Applied Use

**Distillation:**
Once logic is separated from sensibility, Kant specifies its field more closely. General logic abstracts from the difference of objects and sets out the absolutely necessary rules of thinking as such. It also divides into pure and applied logic. Pure logic is a canon of understanding and reason insofar as their use is merely formal and a priori. Applied logic, by contrast, deals with the contingent subjective conditions under which understanding is exercised in human beings. It therefore belongs to empirical reflection on our use of thought rather than to a strict science of its form.

**Structural Moves:**

- General logic abstracts from differences among objects.
- Pure logic excludes empirical conditions and remains a priori.
- Applied logic treats contingent psychological conditions of use.
- Pure logic is canon, not a science-producing organon of objects.

---

### TL3. Transcendental Logic Is Not Mere A Priori Formalism

**Distillation:**
Transcendental logic begins where general logic stops. Because there are pure intuitions as well as empirical ones, Kant can ask whether there is also a pure thinking of objects that does not abstract from all content. Not every a priori representation is transcendental. The transcendental concerns the cognition of how certain representations are possible and how they can be applied a priori to objects of experience. Transcendental logic therefore studies pure understanding and reason only insofar as they concern objects, not merely the form of thinking in the abstract.

**Structural Moves:**

- The transcendental concerns the possibility and use of a priori cognition.
- Pure intuition by itself is not yet transcendental.
- Transcendental logic is object-related a priori logic.

---

### TL4. General Logic Supplies Only a Negative Criterion of Truth

**Distillation:**
The famous question concerning a general criterion of truth forces Kant to restate the limit of logic. Because truth concerns relation to an object, there can be no general mark of the material truth of cognition once one abstracts from objects altogether. Logic can therefore test only the form of thinking. It supplies a necessary condition of truth, never a sufficient criterion of objective truth. For that reason, logic may examine whether cognition contradicts itself, but it cannot by itself determine whether cognition agrees with its object.

**Structural Moves:**

- Formal consistency is necessary but not sufficient for truth.
- No general material criterion of truth is possible.
- Logic cannot decide the truth of objects by itself.

---

### TL5. Dialectic Begins When Logic Is Misused as an Organon

**Distillation:**
General logic becomes dialectical only when it is misused. In itself it is merely analytic: a negative touchstone for testing the form of cognition. But because form is seductive, logic is often treated as though it were an organon capable of producing objective cognition by itself. Then it becomes a logic of illusion, a sophistical art that gives empty claims the appearance of rigor. Dialectic in the critical sense must therefore be understood not as a productive art but as a critique of such illusion.

**Structural Moves:**

- Analytic is the formal decomposition of lawful thinking.
- Dialectic is the misuse of logic as though form alone yielded objectivity.
- Critique must expose this illusion rather than reproduce it.

---

### TL6. Transcendental Logic Divides into Analytic and Dialectic Under the Condition of Intuition

**Distillation:**
When transcendental logic isolates pure understanding, it still remains bound to intuition as the condition under which objects can be given. Its analytic expounds the pure elements and principles without which no object can be thought at all, and is therefore a logic of truth. Its dialectic critiques the seduction to use pure understanding beyond experience as if it could legislate for objects in general. The introduction therefore installs the entire architectonic of lawful and lawless object-related thought in advance.

**Structural Moves:**

- Pure understanding still requires given intuition if it is to have objects.
- Transcendental analytic is a logic of truth.
- Transcendental dialectic critiques hyperphysical misuse.
- The introduction sets the full architectonic of transcendental logic.

---

### TL7. The Distinction from General Logic Generates a Demand for Reunion

**Distillation:**
In our presentation, the separation of general from transcendental logic does not leave the two as strangers. On the contrary, once general logic is seen as the articulated formal life of thinking, transcendental logic appears as the necessary completion that secures how those same acts can relate to objects a priori. The distinction therefore generates a pressure toward reunion. One begins to feel that the two belong together as one thinking machine viewed under different restrictions. Pushed to its strongest derivative consequence, this pressure can be read from the Hegelian side as the demand for one uninterrupted pipeline: transcendental logic begins the cycle by securing object-related identity a priori, and general logic immediately continues that same act as the articulated self-movement of thought. In that machine idiom, a complete cycle of the concept-processing unit is logic itself. One may even call this the demand for intellectual intuition, provided the phrase is heard here not as a literal Kantian claim that we possess such an intuition, but as the name for pure reason's demand that the two logics exhibit certainty of identity as one act.

**Structural Moves:**

- General and transcendental logic are distinct but not foreign to one another.
- The distinction generates a demand for higher unity.
- The two logics can be read as one thinking machine under different conditions.
- In a stronger derivative register, that unity can be read as one uninterrupted pipeline or CPU cycle.
- The felt demand for that unity belongs to pure reason's inner pressure.
