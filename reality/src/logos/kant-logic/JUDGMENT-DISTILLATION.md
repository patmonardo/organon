### SS1. Judgment Is Ordered by Four Logical Moments

**Distillation:**
Judgment is organized by four principal moments: quantity, quality, relation, and modality. These are not accidental classifications but the basic axes through which logic determines how consciousness can bind representations into a judgment at all.

**Structural Moves:**

- Quantity orders extent.
- Quality orders placement.
- Relation orders subordination.
- Modality orders the judgment's stance toward cognition.

---

### SS2. Quantity Is a Protocol of Spheres

**Distillation:**
Quantity determines whether judgment is universal, particular, or singular by fixing how the sphere of one concept is enclosed under another. In the universal judgment, the sphere of one concept is wholly enclosed within the sphere of another. In the particular judgment, only a part of the former is enclosed under the sphere of the other. In the singular judgment, a concept with no sphere at all is enclosed, merely as a part, under the sphere of another. Quantity therefore does not merely count cases; it specifies the extent of conceptual subsumption through the protocol of spheres.

**Structural Moves:**

- Universal wholly encloses one conceptual sphere within another.
- Particular encloses only part of one sphere under another.
- Singular subordinates a concept without a sphere as a part under another sphere.
- Quantity is the protocol of extent through spherical inclusion.

---

### SS3. Quality Is a Protocol of Placement

**Distillation:**
Quality determines whether judgment is affirmative, negative, or infinite by fixing where the subject is placed with respect to the sphere of the predicate. In the affirmative judgment, the subject is thought under the sphere of a predicate. In the negative judgment, it is posited outside the sphere of that predicate. In the infinite judgment, it is posited in the sphere of a concept that lies outside the sphere of another. The infinite judgment therefore matters because it does more than negate: it assigns the subject to a determinate outside. Quality is thus a protocol of positional determination, not just a list of verbal forms.

**Structural Moves:**

- Affirmative places the subject within the predicate sphere.
- Negative places the subject outside the predicate sphere.
- Infinite places the subject within an external sphere outside another sphere.
- Quality is the protocol of positional determination.

---

### SS4. Relation Gives the Three Protocols of Subordination

**Distillation:**
Relation states how given representations are subordinated to one another for the unity of consciousness. Categorical judgment subordinates predicate to subject. Hypothetical judgment subordinates consequence to ground. Disjunctive judgment subordinates member to the divided concept as a whole of division. These are not merely sentence-types but three protocols by which consciousness orders representations into logical unity.

**Structural Moves:**

- Categorical relation binds predicate to subject.
- Hypothetical relation binds consequence to ground.
- Disjunctive relation binds members to the divided whole they complete.
- Relation is the protocol of subordination for conscious unity.

---

### SS5. Categorical Judgment Has Matter and Form

**Distillation:**
In categorical judgment, the matter is subject and predicate. The form is the copula, through which the relation of agreement or opposition between subject and predicate is determined and expressed. Categorical judgment therefore does not merely juxtapose two terms. It operates by holding a subject and predicate together through a formal act of relating them. This matters later for substance: judgment already fixes the logical subject-position under which predicates can inhere, but it does not yet positively unfold a monistic One Substance. It gives the form of subjecthood that transcendental logic will later require for objectivity in appearance.

**Structural Moves:**

- Subject and predicate constitute the matter.
- The copula constitutes the form.
- The copula determines and expresses agreement or opposition.
- Categorical judgment is the operator of predicative binding.
- Categorical judgment fixes the logical subject-position later read transcendently as substance in appearance.

---

### SS6. Hypothetical Judgment Has Matter and Form

**Distillation:**
In hypothetical judgment, the matter consists of two judgments connected as ground and consequence. The judgment containing the ground is the antecedent; the judgment related to it as consequence is the consequent. The form is the representation of this connection itself, called the consequentia. Hypothetical judgment is therefore not just a pair of propositions but an operator that binds two judgments into a ground-consequence order for the unity of consciousness.

**Structural Moves:**

- Antecedent and consequent constitute the matter.
- Ground and consequence define their order.
- Consequentia constitutes the form.
- Hypothetical judgment is the operator of inferential linkage.

---

### SS7. Hypothetical Form Operates by Positing and Denying

**Distillation:**
The form of hypothetical connection has two modes. In modus ponens, if the ground is true, the consequence determined by it is also true. In modus tollens, if the consequence is false, the ground is false. The hypothetical operator therefore already contains rule-governed transitions of positing and denying. Its form is not static connection but lawful consequence.

**Structural Moves:**

- Modus ponens posits consequence from a posited ground.
- Modus tollens denies ground from a denied consequence.
- Hypothetical form carries lawful transition.

---

### SS8. Disjunctive Judgment Has Complementary Matter and Form

**Distillation:**
Disjunctive judgment begins from the parts of the sphere of a given concept and determines them toward a whole as complements. Its matter is the several judgments that function as the members of the disjunction or opposition. Its form is the disjunction itself: the relation of the various judgments as members of the whole sphere of the divided cognition, where they mutually exclude one another and yet complement one another. Disjunctive judgment is therefore the operator of distributed totality: it does not merely list alternatives but divides a conceptual whole into members whose relation is fixed by the whole they complete.

**Structural Moves:**

- Members of disjunction constitute the matter.
- Disjunction itself constitutes the form.
- Members mutually exclude one another.
- Members also complement one another toward the whole.
- Disjunctive judgment is the operator of distributed totality.

---

### SS9. Disjunctive Judgment Carries an Exhaustive Truth Protocol

**Distillation:**
The peculiar character of disjunctive judgment is that its members are all problematic judgments, yet together they exhaust the sphere of the divided cognition. Each is the complement of the others toward the whole. Because the sphere includes nothing outside them under the given conditions, one of them must contain the truth or hold assertorically, while neither something outside them nor more than one among them can be true. Disjunctive form therefore carries an exhaustion protocol: modality is internally distributed across the members as possibility, but the completed division forces assertoric determinacy within the whole.

**Structural Moves:**

- Disjunctive members are problematic individually.
- Together they exhaust the divided sphere.
- One member must hold assertorically.
- Nothing outside them and no plurality among them can be true under the given conditions.

---

### SS10. Modality Is the Consciousness Operator of Judgment

**Distillation:**
Modality does not alter the content of the judgment but states how the whole judgment stands for consciousness. A judgment may be problematic, assertoric, or apodeictic. This means judgment is not only about content but also about the mode in which consciousness holds that content to be possible, actual, or necessary. In this sense modality is the consciousness operator of judgment: it marks not what is thought, but how it is held.

**Structural Moves:**

- Problematic marks possibility.
- Assertoric marks actuality.
- Apodeictic marks necessity.
- Modality determines how the judgment is held by consciousness.

---

### SS11. Relation and Modality Cross One Another in Disjunctive Judgment

**Distillation:**
Disjunctive judgment shows most clearly that relation and modality are not externally juxtaposed moments. Its members are problematic as isolated alternatives, but as members of a complete division they are ordered so that one must become assertoric. Relation here structures the whole, and modality marks the status of its members within that whole. The disjunctive operator therefore prefigures a machinery in which division, exclusion, and truth-status are internally coordinated.

**Structural Moves:**

- Relation distributes the whole into members.
- Modality marks those members first as problematic.
- Exhaustive division forces assertoric resolution.
- Disjunction is the clearest crossing of operator and status.

---

### SS12. The File Ends by Connecting Logical Form to Scientific Practice

**Distillation:**
The later sections broaden judgment into a proto-methodology of knowledge. Theoretical and practical propositions, demonstrable and indemonstrable propositions, principles, axioms, postulates, theorems, and judgments of experience all show that logical form is not an isolated scholastic exercise. It is already the formal infrastructure of proof, science, and experience.

**Structural Moves:**

- Judgment opens into proof and demonstration.
- Principles are classified by how they are grounded.
- Logical form becomes the infrastructure of science.
