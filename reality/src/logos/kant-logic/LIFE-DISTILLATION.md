# Life - Distillation

Source: [life.md](life.md)

---

### SS1. Life Advances Logical Perfection Through Operators of Clarification and Order

**Distillation:**
The Life chapter no longer speaks at the height of Idea's architectonic summary, but at the level of working operators. Its task is to further the logical perfection of cognition by making concepts distinct, dividing them lawfully, and arranging scientific treatment methodically. Life is therefore the practical side of method: it specifies the means by which cognition becomes clearer, more ordered, and more exact.

**Structural Moves:**

- Life furthers logical perfection.
- It operates on conceptual distinctness.
- It operates on logical division.
- It operates on scientific method.

---

### SS2. Definition Is Complete Distinctness and Precision of a Concept

**Distillation:**
Definition is introduced as a sufficiently distinct and precise concept, a concept adequate to the thing in minimal terms and completely determined. Life therefore begins from a high standard of conceptual completion. A definition is not a loose explanation but a concept brought to adequacy, precision, and determinacy.

**Structural Moves:**

- Definition is a concept.
- It must be sufficiently distinct.
- It must be precise.
- It must be adequate and completely determined.

---

### SS3. Definitions Divide by Origin Into Analytic and Synthetic Forms

**Distillation:**
Kant's first major operator split in definition is between analytic and synthetic definition. Analytic definition concerns a concept that is given; synthetic definition concerns a concept that is made. This distinction is then crossed by the a priori and a posteriori status of the concepts involved. Life thus treats definition not just as a finished product but as a procedure whose character depends on whether the concept is received or produced.

**Structural Moves:**

- Analytic definition works on a given concept.
- Synthetic definition works on a made concept.
- Given concepts may be a priori or a posteriori.
- Made concepts may be a priori or a posteriori.

---

### SS4. Synthetic Definition Splits into Exposition and Construction, but Empirical Synthesis Cannot Reach Definition

**Distillation:**
When concepts are made, their synthesis is either exposition or construction. Exposition synthesizes empirically from given appearances as matter; construction synthesizes arbitrarily and belongs paradigmatically to mathematics. Yet because empirical synthesis is never complete, empirical concepts can never be defined through synthetic procedure. Life therefore limits the synthetic route: arbitrary construction can define; empirical synthesis can at best prepare material.

**Structural Moves:**

- Synthetic definition proceeds by exposition or construction.
- Exposition synthesizes from appearances.
- Construction synthesizes arbitrarily and grounds mathematical concepts.
- Empirical synthesis cannot yield complete definition.

---

### SS5. Analytic Definition Makes Given Concepts Distinct by Successive Mark-Analysis

**Distillation:**
All given concepts, whether a priori or a posteriori, can be defined only analytically. The procedure is successive clarification of the marks already contained in the concept. When all marks are made clear, the concept becomes completely distinct; if the number of marks is not excessive, it is also precise. Analytic definition therefore works by extracting and ordering the content already present rather than by adding new matter.

**Structural Moves:**

- Given concepts are defined only analytically.
- Analysis makes marks successively clear.
- Complete clarity yields distinctness.
- Measured economy of marks yields precision.

---

### SS6. Exposition and Description Are Approximation Operators Below Full Definition

**Distillation:**
Not all concepts can be defined, and not all need to be. Kant therefore introduces two subordinate clarification forms: exposition and description. Exposition is the connected representation of marks found through analysis. Description is exposition without precision and belongs only to empirically given concepts. Life therefore includes approximation operators for cases where complete definition is unavailable: exposition organizes marks, description gathers provisional material.

**Structural Moves:**

- Exposition presents marks in connected succession.
- Description is imprecise exposition.
- Description applies only to empirically given concepts.
- Approximation belongs inside the life of method.

---

### SS7. Nominal and Real Definitions Separate Logical Essence from Inner Possibility

**Distillation:**
Definitions also divide according to what they provide. Nominal definitions fix the meaning arbitrarily assigned to a name and thus state only the logical essence needed for distinction from other objects. Real definitions suffice for cognition of the object through its inner determinations because they present the possibility of the object from inner marks. Life therefore distinguishes naming from genuine cognition: one can define a word nominally without yet exhibiting the object's inner possibility.

**Structural Moves:**

- Nominal definition fixes the meaning of a name.
- Nominal definition states logical essence for distinction.
- Real definition presents the object's inner determinations.
- Real definition exhibits the possibility of the object from inner marks.

---

### SS8. Definition Is Governed by the Four Moments of Quantity, Quality, Relation, and Modality

**Distillation:**
The completeness of definition is judged through the same four principal moments that organize judgment. As to quantity, definition and definitum must be convertible and neither broader nor narrower than one another. As to quality, the definition must be detailed and precise. As to relation, it must not be tautological: the marks that ground cognition of the definitum must differ from the definitum itself. As to modality, the marks must be necessary rather than borrowed from experience. Definition is therefore a four-moment operator of conceptual adequacy.

**Structural Moves:**

- Quantity requires convertibility of definition and definitum.
- Quality requires detail and precision.
- Relation forbids tautology and demands distinct grounding marks.
- Modality requires necessary rather than empirical marks.

---

### SS9. Testing and Preparing Definitions Follow the Same Four-Act Discipline

**Distillation:**
Kant then turns the requirements of definition into a procedural discipline. Testing asks whether the definition is true as a proposition, distinct as a concept, detailed as a distinct concept, and determinate or adequate as a detailed concept. Preparation repeats the same structure constructively: gather true propositions, avoid predicates that presuppose the thing, compare several propositions to the thing for adequacy, and ensure that one mark does not merely lie within or under another. Life therefore makes definition into a repeatable protocol of verification and construction.

**Structural Moves:**

- Testing definition follows four acts.
- Preparation of definition follows the same four acts.
- Truth, distinctness, detail, and determinacy are sequential checkpoints.
- Marks must not collapse into hidden subordination.

---

### SS10. Logical Division Determines a Concept by Lawful Partition of Its Sphere

**Distillation:**
The second half of the chapter moves from definition to division. Every concept contains a manifold under itself that both agrees and differs. Logical division determines the concept with respect to everything possible contained under it insofar as the members are opposed and distinct. The higher concept is the divided concept; the lower concepts are the members of division. Division is therefore the operator that articulates the sphere of a concept into opposed lower determinations.

**Structural Moves:**

- Logical division works on the manifold under a concept.
- Division determines what is possible under the higher concept.
- The higher concept is the divided concept.
- The lower concepts are the members of division.

---

### SS11. Division Is Governed by Opposition, Common Genus, and Exhaustive Equality to the Sphere

**Distillation:**
Kant gives three universal rules for logical division. The members must exclude or oppose one another. They must fall under one higher common concept. Taken together, they must constitute the full sphere of the divided concept or be equal to it. Life therefore makes division lawful by three coordinated requirements: exclusivity, common ground, and completeness.

**Structural Moves:**

- Members must exclude one another.
- Members must belong under one common higher concept.
- Members must together equal the sphere of the divided concept.
- Division is lawful only where opposition, unity, and completeness coincide.

---

### SS12. Codivision, Subdivision, Dichotomy, and Polytomy Are Formal Variants of Partition

**Distillation:**
Once the rules of division are fixed, Kant identifies several formal variants. Codivisions are several divisions made in different respects. Subdivision is division applied to the members of an earlier division. Dichotomy divides into two members; polytomy divides into more than two. Life therefore keeps track not only of the law of partition but of its recursive and numerical forms.

**Structural Moves:**

- Codivision varies the same concept by different respects.
- Subdivision recurses on members.
- Dichotomy partitions into two.
- Polytomy partitions into more than two.

---

### SS13. Method Divides into Multiple Scientific Presentation Operators

**Distillation:**
The chapter then returns from conceptual division to divisions of method itself. Scientific method differs from popular method by beginning from elementary propositions and aiming at thoroughness rather than entertainment. Systematic method differs from fragmentary method by making transitions explicit in exposition. Analytic method proceeds regressively from conditioned to principles, while synthetic method proceeds progressively from principles to consequences. Syllogistic method expounds science as a chain of inferences, while tabular method exhibits the completed system in total connection. Acroamatic method teaches by exposition alone, whereas erotematic method teaches through questioning, whether dialogic or catechistic. Life therefore culminates in a family of presentation operators for science.

**Structural Moves:**

- Scientific and popular methods differ by aim and starting point.
- Systematic and fragmentary methods differ by ordered exposition.
- Analytic and synthetic methods differ by regress and progress.
- Syllogistic and tabular methods differ by inferential chain and completed display.
- Acroamatic and erotematic methods differ by telling and questioning.

---

### SS14. Meditation Is the Reflective Operator That Must Accompany Reading and Learning

**Distillation:**
The chapter ends with meditation. Meditation is reflection or methodical thought. It must accompany all reading and learning by first undertaking provisional investigations and then ordering or connecting thoughts according to method. Life therefore closes not with a static form but with an active reflective operator that keeps the whole chapter in motion.

**Structural Moves:**

- Meditation is reflection.
- Meditation is methodical thought.
- It must accompany reading and learning.
- It orders and connects thought according to method.
