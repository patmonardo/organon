### SS1. The Object Is First Judged Under Four Kantian Titles

**Distillation:**
Before the object is tracked through concept, judgment, and syllogism, it can be front-loaded as a fourfold object of judgment. Kant's table gives four titles under which the object can be judged into concept: quantity, quality, relation, and modality. Because the same understanding that gives unity to judgment also gives categories to object-cognition, these four titles are not external labels. They are the first conceptual grip by which the object becomes judgeable for thought.

**Structural Moves:**

- The object is first judged under quantity, quality, relation, and modality.
- These four titles belong to the same understanding that gives judgment its form.
- This is an initial Kantian genetic pass on the object.
- The object is being judged into concept rather than merged into a Hegelian graph.

---

### SS2. Quantity Takes the Object as Unity, Plurality, and Totality

**Distillation:**
Under quantity the object is first taken as one, then as many, and then as a gathered whole. Kant adds that totality is plurality considered as a unity, and schematically quantity becomes number, the successive synthesis of homogeneous units. Genetically this matters because concept does not receive a formless object. It receives an object already liable to be counted, distributed, and recollected into totality.

**Structural Moves:**

- Unity first gives the object its one-ness.
- Plurality opens the object into many instances or moments.
- Totality recollects plurality into one whole.
- Quantity prepares the object for conceptive universality.

---

### SS3. Quality Takes the Object as Reality, Negation, and Limitation

**Distillation:**
Under quality the object is posited, denied, and bounded. Kant adds that limitation is reality combined with negation, and in sensible application quality becomes the filling of time by sensation and its degrees. This gives the object a more genetic determinacy: not mere being-there, but a bounded real with intensities, privations, and intermediate degrees.

**Structural Moves:**

- Reality posits the object as something.
- Negation withdraws or cancels determination.
- Limitation combines the two into bounded determinacy.
- Quality gives the judged object degree and determinacy.

---

### SS4. Relation Generates the Object as Substance, Causality, and Community

**Distillation:**
Relation is the richest genetic front of the four. The object is judged first as substance, then as causal sequence, and finally as community or reciprocal whole. In schematized form this becomes persistence, succession according to rule, and simultaneity according to rule. This is why relation most strongly prepares the later subjective path: it gives the object as bearer, lawful transition, and coordinated whole before that object is taken further into concept, judgment, and syllogistic mediation.

**Structural Moves:**

- Substance gives the object persistence.
- Causality gives the object rule-governed succession.
- Community gives the object reciprocal coexistence within one whole.
- Relation most clearly prepares the passage from judged object to conceptual articulation.

---

### SS5. Modality Places the Judged Object in Possibility, Actuality, and Necessity

**Distillation:**
Modality does not add a new piece of content to the object. It fixes how the judged object stands in relation to cognition. Objective possibility requires agreement with the formal conditions of possible experience, actuality requires perception or lawful connection to perception, and necessity is only hypothetical within experience. Modality therefore closes the fourfold front-end by determining not what more the object is, but how it counts for us as thinkable, given, and lawfully required.

**Structural Moves:**

- Possibility asks whether the object agrees with conditions of experience.
- Actuality asks whether the object is given in perception or empirical connection.
- Necessity asks whether the object is lawfully required within experience.
- Modality closes the judged object without transcending experience.

---

### SS6. Object() Gives the Grounds of Division of Object in General into Phenomenon and Noumenon

**Distillation:**
Before the path passes into Subjectivity, the higher Object() section has to be fixed. Here object in general is not yet a determinate object positively known by us. If the manner of intuition is omitted, what remains is only the transcendental object-thought, the indeterminate thought of an object in general. The division then becomes lawful only by respect: as phenomenon, the object is taken under the conditions of our sensible intuition; as noumenon, it is thought apart from those conditions. But noumenon is for us only problematic and negative. It serves as a boundary-concept that prevents appearance from being mistaken for thing in itself without opening a positive science of supersensible objects.

**Structural Moves:**

- Object in general stands as the higher heading of division.
- Phenomenon names the object as given under sensible intuition.
- Noumenon names the object only negatively, as not object of our senses.
- Positive noumenon would require intellectual intuition, which we do not have.

---

### SS7. Kantian Subjectivity Names the Path From Object to Syllogistic Mediation

**Distillation:**
Here the path should be read as a Kantian subjective movement. It names the movement by which the fourfold judged object, once sorted through the Object() boundary of phenomenon and noumenon, is taken up into concept, then made explicit in judgment, and finally carried forward by syllogistic derivation. This file keeps that path within the Kantian graph rather than merging it with a Hegelian one.

In that sense Logogenesis here is literally one framework joining Kant's Transcendental Logic and General Logic. The Transcendental side supplies the fourfold object and the Object() boundary of phenomenon and noumenon; the General Logic side supplies concept, judgment, and syllogism as the explicit movement by which the object is judged into concept.

That also means General Logic is not merely a later supplement to the Kantian path. If Kant derives the categories from the functions of logical judgment, then the general-logical articulation of concept, judgment, and inference is already a presupposition of the transcendental derivation, even where that derivation later exceeds General Logic by fixing the objective conditions of possible experience.

**Structural Moves:**

- The Kantian path is first fixed as a determinate path.
- The fourfold object is the front edge of that path.
- Object() supplies the higher boundary division that precedes Subjectivity proper.
- Transcendental Logic and General Logic are combined here in one judged-into-concept sequence.
- General Logic is a presupposition of the transcendental derivation, not an optional appendix.
- The sequence runs through concept, judgment, and syllogism.
- The central operator to track is the development of matter and form.
- Hegelian recombination is intentionally deferred.

---

### SS8. Concept Takes Object as Matter and Universality as Form

**Distillation:**
The first stage is concept. Kant's basic distinction is exact: the matter of concept is the object, and the form of concept is universality. This means that object is already inside the path of thought, not as a finished external thing but as what concept takes up as its matter. Universality is not an empty shell; it is the formal side through which the object becomes thinkable at all.

**Structural Moves:**

- Concept has object as matter.
- Concept has universality as form.
- The Kantian path begins by taking object up into thought.
- Universality is the first formal side of the path.

---

### SS9. The Form of Concept Becomes Active Through Content, Extension, and Subordination

**Distillation:**
Once object has been taken up into concept, the form-side becomes operational. Content and extension give the inside and outside of concept. Higher and lower concepts, genus and species, and abstraction and determination show how universality is used, ordered, and intensified. This is why universality does not remain a static predicate of concept. It becomes a living formal ladder.

For the LG spine, the point is not to restate the whole doctrine of concept-formation, but to preserve the hinge: once object has been taken up as conceptual matter, universality becomes an operative order rather than a bare formal label.

**Structural Moves:**

- Content and extension articulate the inner and outer of concept.
- Higher and lower concepts order universality through subordination.
- Genus and species give relational positions within that order.
- Abstraction and determination generate ascent and descent.

---

### SS10. Judgment Takes the Fourfold Object Into Conceptual Unity

**Distillation:**
Judgment is the second stage because the concept no longer remains merely possessed. The object now becomes explicitly judgeable in conceptual form. At the formal level this still means the unity of consciousness of various representations, but genetically the stress falls on the object being taken into subject, predicate, ground, consequence, and divided whole. Matter consists in the given representations combined in the judgment. Form consists in the determination of how those representations belong to one consciousness. Judgment is therefore the first explicit act by which the fourfold judged object is conceptually articulated.

**Structural Moves:**

- Judgment takes the object into conceptual articulation.
- Judgmental matter is combined representations.
- Judgmental form is their belonging-to-one-consciousness.
- Judgment makes conceptive unity explicit.

---

### SS11. The Relation-Forms of Judgment Differentiate Matter and Form Three Ways

**Distillation:**
The judgment chapter then specifies this general unity through three relation-families. In categorical judgment, matter becomes subject and predicate and form becomes copula. In hypothetical judgment, matter becomes antecedent and consequent judgments and form becomes consequentia. In disjunctive judgment, matter becomes the member-judgments of a divided whole and form becomes the disjunction itself. So judgment does not have one material articulation only. It differentiates its own matter and form in three distinct ways.

**Structural Moves:**

- Categorical judgment: subject-predicate and copula.
- Hypothetical judgment: antecedent-consequent and consequentia.
- Disjunctive judgment: member-judgments and disjunction.
- Judgmental matter and form are internally differentiated.

---

### SS12. Immediate Inference Preserves Matter and Alters Form

**Distillation:**
Syllogism begins with the understanding-side of inference. Its governing law is simple and important: one judgment passes into another by alteration of form alone, while the matter, subject and predicate, remains the same. Syllogism therefore first appears as formal mobility of already given judgmental matter. On the present reconstruction, this is the last stage at which judgmental matter still stands in a comparatively fixed, object-side or empirical way while form only varies its outer logical posture. This is the first derivational expansion of the Kantian path, but not yet its full rational form.

For the LG spine, only the hinge matters here: derivation begins first as formal mobility before it becomes explicit rational mediation.

**Structural Moves:**

- Immediate inference changes form only.
- Subject and predicate remain materially fixed.
- Judgment now becomes mobile.
- Derivation begins as formal transformation.
- Rational form has not yet fully emerged here.

---

### SS13. Reason Introduces Mediation Through Premises and Conclusion

**Distillation:**
Inference of reason introduces necessity. Its triad is major premise, minor premise, and conclusion. The matter of inferences of reason consists in the premises. The form lies in the conclusion insofar as it contains the consequentia. This is the crucial deepening of the path: judgment no longer merely changes shape, but is mediated through rule, subsumption, and concluded necessity. If immediate inference shows form moving over fixed judgmental matter, inference of reason is the first clear emergence of rational form proper, because the conclusion does not merely restate a judgment but carries the consequentia as an achieved mediation. In Jasche's General Logic presentation, Kant even names the principle of ground explicitly at the hypothetical stage. For our reconstruction, this is important because it lets General Logic say openly what Transcendental Logic had already needed in regressive use whenever it moved from conditioned cognition back toward its conditions.

This also gives the formal spine of the present Subjectivity pass. Copula is the formal bond of judgment, consequentia is the explicit form of mediate conclusion, and disjunctio is the whole-form in which inferential truth is distributed across an exhaustive division. Before descending into figures and their reduction, this copula-to-consequentia-to-disjunctio ladder is the clearest matter/form backbone of the Kantian path.

**Structural Moves:**

- Reason introduces major, minor, and conclusion.
- Premises are the matter of mediate inference.
- Conclusion is the form insofar as it contains consequentia.
- The Kantian path becomes genuinely mediated.
- Rational form first becomes explicit in the consequentia.
- The principle of ground is explicitly named on the General Logic side.
- Copula, consequentia, and disjunctio form the formal spine of Subjectivity.

---

### SS14. The Three Syllogistic Families Make Mark, Ground, and Division Explicit

**Distillation:**
Categorical syllogism mediates through mark, hypothetical syllogism through ground and conditioned sequence, and disjunctive syllogism through whole-member division. For the LG spine, the crucial point is that reason here becomes explicit in three distinct truth-protocols: binding, grounding, and division. Matter advances from subject-predicate material, to ground-consequence, and finally to membra of a whole; form advances from copula, to consequentia, to disjunctio.

Because Kant grounds the division of inferences of reason in three possible conditions of the objective unity of consciousness, this division can be read, on the present reconstruction, as a principle of rationality: reason must mediate through mark, ground, or whole. That is the architectonic result LG needs. The fuller doctrine of figures, reductions, and local inferential machinery belongs in the component syllogism workbook.

This also clarifies the twist in the three principles. Mark, ground, and excluded middle should not be flattened into one operator, even if a mark can function as a ground of cognition. They are distinct protocols internal to the Concept's own mediation. On a derivative Hegelian resonance, this is why syllogism can appear as the restored unity of the Concept; within the present Kantian pass, it is enough to say that Concept(), raised from Object(), becomes explicitly self-mediating through its own rational forms.

**Structural Moves:**

- Categorical syllogism mediates through middle mark.
- Hypothetical syllogism mediates through ground and conditioned.
- Disjunctive syllogism mediates through exhaustive division.
- Syllogism widens the Kantian path into explicit mediation.
- Matter advances from subject-predicate, to ground-consequence, to membra of a whole.
- Form advances from copula, to consequentia, to disjunctio.
- Hypothetical inference states the principle of ground explicitly.
- The division of inferences of reason can be read as a principle of rationality.
- Mark, ground, and excluded middle function as distinct truth-protocols internal to Concept().
- Concept() requires internal protocols for binding, grounding, and division.

---

### SS15. Syllogism Completes the Present Kantian Pass

**Distillation:**
By the time syllogism is in place, the Kantian path has become a real grammar of grounded transition. Immediate inference preserves matter while varying form. Mediate inference turns premises into concluded necessity. Reflective inference searches for a common ground from particulars. In this way the present pass becomes capable of lawful derivation, grounded sequence, and systematic division. Summarily stated: syllogism first receives judgmental matter in comparatively fixed form, then lets rational form emerge as consequentia, and finally distributes that rational form across mark, ground, and division. That is enough to complete this Kantian distillation without forcing a Hegelian continuation into the same graph.

**Structural Moves:**

- The Kantian path culminates in grounded derivation.
- Immediate, mediate, and reflective inference are all now in play.
- Ground, consequence, and division have become explicit operators.
- The matter/form lifecycle of judgment is gathered up into rational mediation.
- Any later dialectical comparison with Hegel must be carried by a separate workbook.
