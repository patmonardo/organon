# Principles - Distillation

Source: [principles.md](principles.md)

---

### TP1. The Analytic of Principles Shifts the Analytic from Concept to Judgment-Use

**Distillation:**
The Analytic of Principles opens by fixing a new terrain within the Analytic as a whole. General logic could treat understanding, judgment, and reason together because it was concerned only with form. Transcendental logic cannot do that, because reason in its transcendental use no longer belongs to the logic of truth but to dialectical illusion. The analytic side is therefore restricted to understanding and the power of judgment. This second book is accordingly a canon for judgment: it teaches how pure concepts of understanding can be validly applied to appearances.

**Structural Moves:**

- General logic can include a canon for reason because it abstracts from content.
- Transcendental logic cannot, because reason's transcendental use is dialectical.
- The Analytic of Principles is a canon for the transcendental power of judgment.

---

### TP2. Judgment Is the Faculty of Subsuming Under Rules and Needs Critique

**Distillation:**
Kant then explains why judgment must be treated separately. Understanding is the faculty of rules; judgment is the faculty of subsuming under them. No general logic can provide an infinite regress of further rules for how to apply rules. Judgment is therefore a special talent that can be practiced and sharpened, but not mechanically taught. Yet transcendental philosophy has a peculiar advantage here: because it deals with pure concepts whose objects must be given under a priori conditions, it can indicate in advance the case to which the rule applies. Its utility is critical rather than expansive. It does not enlarge pure understanding so much as protect it from misuse.

**Structural Moves:**

- Judgment subsumes under rules rather than generating them.
- Judgment cannot be fully taught by further rules.
- Examples sharpen judgment but do not replace it.
- Transcendental critique can still secure judgment by indicating the a priori case of application.

### TP3. Schematism Solves the Heterogeneity Between Category and Appearance

**Distillation:**
The first chapter of the Analytic of Principles begins from a precise difficulty. In every subsumption there must be homogeneity between concept and object. But pure concepts of understanding and sensible appearances are entirely heterogeneous. How, then, can a category apply to an appearance at all? Kant's answer is that there must be a third thing that is both intellectual and sensible: the transcendental schema. The schema is the mediating representation that makes subsumption possible by standing in rule-governed homogeneity with both category and appearance.

**Structural Moves:**

- Subsumption requires homogeneity.
- Category and appearance are otherwise heterogeneous.
- A mediating third thing is therefore necessary.
- The transcendental schema makes application possible.

---

### TP4. The Schema Is a Rule of Imagination, Not an Image

**Distillation:**
The schema is not a picture. It is a rule for generating a corresponding image or sensible determination. This is why Kant distinguishes the schema of number from any particular numeral-image, the schema of triangle from any specific triangle, and the schema of an empirical concept such as dog from any single visible shape. The schema belongs to imagination, but not as empirical fancy. It is the rule-governed work of transcendental imagination that determines inner sense in time. This is why Kant calls schematism a hidden art in the depths of the soul.

**Structural Moves:**

- A schema is a rule, not a particular image.
- Images are always too particular for concepts.
- The schema belongs to productive imagination.
- Schematism is the time-bound method by which concepts gain sensible application.

---

### TP5. All Schemas Are A Priori Time-Determinations

**Distillation:**
Because time is contained in every empirical representation and is the form of inner sense, it provides the universal medium of schematism. Quantity becomes number, the successive synthesis of homogeneous units. Quality becomes the filling of time by sensation and its degrees. Relation becomes persistence, succession according to rule, and simultaneity according to rule. Modality becomes the relation of representation to time as possibility, actuality, and necessity. The schema of each category is therefore a specific a priori time-determination governed by a rule.

**Structural Moves:**

- Time is the universal medium of schema.
- Quantity, quality, relation, and modality each have rule-governed temporal schemas.
- Schemas translate categories into sensible determinability.
- The unity of inner sense and apperception is mediated through time-determination.

---

### TP6. Schematism Both Realizes and Restricts the Categories

**Distillation:**
The final consequence is double. Schemas first give categories empirical significance by relating them to objects in possible experience. But they also limit them, because this relation is only possible under conditions of sensibility. Once sensible restriction is removed, categories retain only the logical form of possible unity, not any determinate objective use. Thus schematism is not an optional bridge. It is the very condition under which categories become meaningful for us, and the critical boundary that prevents them from claiming an object beyond possible experience.

**Structural Moves:**

- Schemas first realize the categories for possible empirical use.
- The same schemas also restrict the categories to sensibility.
- Without schemata categories retain only the logical form of unity.
- Schematism secures empirical meaning and critical limit together.
