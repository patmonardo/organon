# Principles Ground - Distillation

Source: [principles-ground.md](principles-ground.md)

---

### TPG1. The Chapter First Re-draws the Boundary of Truth as the Boundary of Possible Experience

**Distillation:**
The chapter opens by returning to the island image: the Analytic has charted the land of truth, but truth is bounded. The point of the review is not to add a new body of objects, but to secure the title under which the understanding legitimately occupies its domain. Everything the understanding brings forth from itself has use only for experience. The real gain of critique is therefore not extra information about supersensible things, but knowledge of the limits within which the understanding's claims are valid at all. The island is the domain in which cognition can justify its claim to objectivity; it is the bounded science of knowing as genuine knowledge for finite knowers.

**Structural Moves:**

- The Analytic has mapped the land of truth.
- This land is bounded by nature itself.
- The island names the domain of objectively valid cognition for us.
- Pure understanding has use only for experience.
- Critique's practical gain is boundary-knowledge.

---

### TPG2. Categories Without Intuition Have Significance Only by Borrowing from Sensibility

**Distillation:**
Kant then tightens the restriction. A concept has sense only if an object can be given for it in intuition. Even mathematics, though generated a priori, must exhibit its concepts in appearance. The same holds still more strongly for the categories. Once the sensible condition is removed, they lose objective validity. This is why they cannot be fully defined from pure understanding alone: whatever real sense they have comes from their application to possible experience through schema.

**Structural Moves:**

- Concepts require an object given in intuition.
- Mathematics already depends on exhibitability in appearance.
- Categories likewise lose objective validity when sensibility is removed.
- Schema is what gives them determinate significance for us.

---

### TPG3. “Object in General” Is Only the Indeterminate Thought of an Object, Not a Cognized Noumenon

**Distillation:**
This is the first major clarification. When intuition is not specified, the understanding can still think an object in general. What remains in that case is only the transcendental object-thought: the indeterminate thought of an object as such under the unity-functions of thought. But this is not yet a known thing beyond appearance. Since no schema and no intuition are given, nothing is determined. So the category may retain transcendental significance, but it has no transcendental use.

**Structural Moves:**

- Thinking relates intuitions to an object.
- If the manner of intuition is omitted, only a transcendental object-thought remains.
- Pure categories then express merely the thought of an object in general.
- Without schema or given intuition, no object is determined.

---

### TPG4. The Misleading Step Is to Treat That Empty Object-Thought as a Cognizable Being of Understanding

**Distillation:**
The confusion begins because once appearances are distinguished from things as they might be in themselves, the understanding is tempted to oppose to phenomena a second class of objects supposedly thinkable through understanding alone. Since categories are not themselves drawn from sensibility, they seem fit for such a task. But this is the deception. The understanding illicitly turns an entirely undetermined “something outside our way of sensing” into a determinate object of cognition. That move is exactly what Kant is blocking.

**Structural Moves:**

- Phenomenon seems to imply a beyond of appearance.
- Categories seem available for that beyond because they are not sensible in origin.
- The understanding therefore mistakes an indeterminate beyond for a determinately thinkable object.
- The chapter identifies this as the central deception.

---

### TPG5. Noumenon Has a Legitimate Negative Sense but No Available Positive Sense for Us

**Distillation:**
Kant resolves the ambiguity by splitting the term. In the negative sense, a noumenon is just a thing insofar as it is not considered under our sensible mode of intuition. This is legitimate as an abstraction. In the positive sense, however, a noumenon would be an object of a non-sensible, intellectual intuition. That would require a kind of intuition we neither possess nor can comprehend. So the positive noumenon is not an available object of cognition for us.

**Structural Moves:**

- Negative noumenon means: not object of our sensible intuition.
- Positive noumenon means: object of intellectual intuition.
- Intellectual intuition is not ours.
- Therefore noumenon is licit negatively but unavailable positively.

---

### TPG6. The Concept of Noumenon Is a Boundary-Concept, Not a Second Territory of Knowledge

**Distillation:**
The final payoff is critical. Noumenon is not a positively knowable world standing alongside the world of appearance. It is a problematic and necessary boundary-concept of negative use. It prevents us from identifying appearance with thing in itself and thereby limits the pretension of sensibility. But it does not supply a new object-domain. The field beyond appearance is empty for us, not because it is nothing absolutely, but because our understanding lacks the intuition by which any such objects could be given and known.

**Structural Moves:**

- Noumenon is not contradictory.
- It is needed to limit the claim of sensible cognition.
- It does not yield positive cognition of a supersensible domain.
- It marks an empty boundary for us rather than a second science.

---

### TPG7. The “World of Understanding” and “Objects as They Are” Must Be Read Empirically, Not Transcendentally

**Distillation:**
Kant closes by rejecting an easy verbal escape. One may call the lawful connection of appearances an intelligible world only in a loose, empirical sense. The understanding shows how appearances must be represented as objects of experience, not how things are apart from all relation to our sensibility. So when we say that the senses present objects as they appear and the understanding as they are, the only acceptable meaning is: as they are within the thoroughgoing empirical connection of appearances. Any stronger claim would smuggle noumenal cognition back in.

**Structural Moves:**

- “World of understanding” talk can be harmless only in a weak empirical sense.
- Understanding determines lawful connection of appearances.
- It does not cognize things apart from possible experience.
- The chapter ends by re-closing the door on synthetic transcendental claims about noumena.
