# Principles I - Distillation

Source: [principles-I.md](principles-I.md)

---

### TPI1. The First Principle Makes All Intuition Magnitudinal

**Distillation:**
The Axioms of Intuition state the first mathematical principle of appearance: every intuition is an extensive magnitude. Kant's path is not that appearances happen to be measurable after the fact, but that apprehension itself already requires a synthesis of the homogeneous manifold in space and time. Because an object of appearance can be represented only through that synthetic composition, magnitude belongs to intuition as a condition of objectivity, not as a later empirical add-on.

**Structural Moves:**

- Appearances are given only in space and time.
- Apprehension therefore requires synthesis of a homogeneous manifold.
- The concept of magnitude arises with the consciousness of that synthetic unity.
- Every intuition of appearance is therefore already an extensive magnitude.

---

### TPI2. Extensive Magnitude Means That the Parts Must Be Generated Before the Whole

**Distillation:**
Kant then specifies what kind of magnitude is at issue. An extensive magnitude is one whose parts make the whole possible and therefore must be represented first. A line is not first present and then analyzed into parts; it is drawn through the successive generation of parts from a point. Time is the same: a determinate duration is produced only through the successive addition of moments. Appearance as intuition is therefore cognized through part-to-whole synthesis in apprehension.

**Structural Moves:**

- Extensive magnitude is defined by the priority of parts to whole.
- Spatial intuition is generated through successive construction.
- Temporal intuition is generated through successive progression.
- Appearance as intuition is apprehended as an aggregate of antecedently generated parts.

---

### TPI3. Geometry Supplies Proper Axioms Because It Constructs the Form of Appearance

**Distillation:**
Once intuition is understood as successively generated magnitude, the connection to geometry becomes straightforward. Geometry expresses the a priori conditions under which outer intuition can be constructed at all, and those conditions are the same ones under which appearances are apprehended. This is why geometrical axioms properly concern quanta as such: they articulate the constructive rules of sensible intuition itself.

**Structural Moves:**

- Productive imagination generates figures through successive synthesis.
- Geometry formulates the a priori conditions of that construction.
- These axioms concern magnitude as quantum.
- The same synthesis underlies empirical apprehension of appearances.

---

### TPI4. Numerical Truths Are Synthetic but Are Not Proper Axioms

**Distillation:**
Kant then narrows the sense of axiom. Numerical propositions such as 7 + 5 = 12 are synthetic, but they are singular constructions rather than universal axioms in the geometrical sense. Their synthesis can proceed only in one way, so they function more like numerical formulas than axioms. The point is not to downgrade arithmetic, but to keep clear the special universality and constructive scope of geometrical axioms within the doctrine of appearances.

**Structural Moves:**

- Analytic equalities are not axioms.
- Numerical propositions are synthetic but singular.
- Their synthesis is fixed rather than universally schematic like geometry.
- They should therefore be called numerical formulas rather than proper axioms.

---

### TPI5. The Principle Grounds the Applicability of Mathematics to Experience

**Distillation:**
The payoff is critical and architectural. Because appearances are not things in themselves but objects given only under pure intuition, what geometry proves of space and time must hold for appearances as appearances. The principle therefore secures the application of pure mathematics to experience while blocking the false move of treating sensible objects as if they could be exempted from the formal conditions of sensibility. If that exemption were allowed, geometry would lose its object along with its necessity.

**Structural Moves:**

- Pure mathematics applies to appearances because appearances are given only through pure intuition.
- The principle expands a priori cognition by legitimizing that application.
- It blocks evasions that treat appearances as things in themselves.
- Geometry and experience stand together because the same form of intuition grounds both.
