### TPR1. Transcendental Reflection Asks Which Cognitive Power a Comparison Belongs To

**Distillation:**
The appendix begins by redefining reflection with unusual precision. Reflection is not first about objects directly, but about the state of mind in which we determine the source of our representations. Before we compare or judge objectively, we must ask whether the compared representations belong together in sensibility or in understanding. This is what Kant calls transcendental reflection. It is the precondition for correctly determining how representations may relate to one another at all.

**Structural Moves:**

- Reflection concerns the source-conditions of concepts.
- Comparison without source-discrimination is insufficient.
- The key question is whether the representations belong to sensibility or understanding.
- Transcendental reflection is therefore a duty for any a priori judgment.

---

### TPR2. The Four Concepts of Reflection Change Their Meaning Between Appearance and Pure Understanding

**Distillation:**
Kant then runs through four reflection-pairs: identity and difference, agreement and opposition, inner and outer, matter and form. In each case the relation looks one way if objects are treated as objects of pure understanding and another way if they are treated as appearances. Numerical difference can come from place in space, real opposition can arise among phenomenal forces, the inner of matter can be relational rather than monadic, and in sensible intuition form can precede matter. The same comparison-titles therefore yield very different objective judgments depending on their transcendental place.

**Structural Moves:**

- Reflection-pairs are not self-interpreting.
- Pure-understanding use and sensible use diverge.
- Appearance introduces distinctions not visible from mere concepts.
- Wrong placement of the comparison yields false metaphysics.

### TPR3. Leibniz’s “Intellectual System” Comes From Treating Appearances As If They Were Pure Objects of Understanding

**Distillation:**
The core diagnosis is that Leibniz compared sensible objects merely through concepts and so treated appearances as if they were things available to pure understanding. That mistake generates the whole Leibnizian package: identity of indiscernibles as a law of nature, merely logical agreement of realities, monadology, pre-established harmony, and the intellectualization of space and time. Kant’s point is not local correction but architectural exposure: amphiboly of reflection-concepts produces a whole false ontology.

**Structural Moves:**

- Leibniz failed to determine the transcendental place of compared concepts.
- He treated sensibility as confused understanding rather than a distinct source.
- The resulting system intellectualizes appearances.
- The amphiboly therefore explains a whole metaphysical style of error.

### TPR4. The Inner of Matter Is Only Comparatively Inner, Not Noumenally Simple

**Distillation:**
One of the most important consequences concerns matter. Once matter is taken as appearance, its so-called inner determinations are still only relational and dynamically phenomenal. Matter is known through attraction, repulsion, impenetrability, motion, and the like, not through some hidden simple soul-like core. The absolutely internal in matter, when posited by pure understanding alone, is a mere fancy. This is where Kant most forcefully blocks any move from phenomenal substance to noumenal monad.

**Structural Moves:**

- Matter as appearance is known only through relations and forces.
- Its “inner” is only comparatively inner.
- Pure-understanding demands for an absolutely internal substratum overreach.
- Monadology is the result of that overreach.

---

### TPR5. Form Precedes Matter in Appearance Because Sensibility Is an Original Source

**Distillation:**
The matter/form contrast completes the anti-Leibnizian turn. If pure understanding directly determined things in themselves, matter would precede form. But for us objects are given only in sensible intuition, and there form is original. Space and time, as forms of intuition, precede all appearances and make them possible. The intellectualist philosopher cannot tolerate this because he assumes things are intuited as they are in themselves, only confusedly. Kant rejects that assumption at the root.

**Structural Moves:**

- Pure-understanding metaphysics puts matter before form.
- Sensible intuition reverses that order for appearances.
- Space and time are prior formal conditions.
- This marks sensibility as an original cognitive source.

---

### TPR6. The Appendix Returns to “Object in General” in Two Critical Registers

**Distillation:**
The chapter returns, in a sharpened way, to the problem of object in general. First, it revisits the same transcendental object-thought fixed in the ground chapter: if reflection-concepts are applied to object in general in the transcendental sense, without deciding whether it is object of sensible or intellectual intuition, contradiction and illusion follow. Second, near the close Kant introduces the concept of an object in general taken problematically as the highest concept prior to the division between something and nothing. In both cases object in general is only a formal placeholder unless the mode of givenness is supplied. It is not yet a cognized noumenal object.

**Structural Moves:**

- The first register repeats the transcendental object-thought already fixed in the ground chapter.
- It cannot simply be converted into a knowable intelligible object.
- Object in general also appears as the highest problematic genus before something/nothing.
- Both uses are formal and limiting, not positive noumenal cognition.

---

### TPR7. Noumenon Remains Problematic and the Four Senses of Nothing Close the Appendix Negatively

**Distillation:**
The late sections repeat and radicalize the lesson of the ground chapter. Intelligible objects thought through mere categories are impossible for us; noumenon in the negative sense remains only a problematic boundary-concept of negative use tied to the limits of sensibility. The final table of the four senses of nothing then closes the Analytic by showing how far empty concepts, empty intuitions, and contradictory concepts can be distinguished once “object in general” is taken as the highest problematic heading. The ending is not an opening into noumenal science, but a cleanup of the remaining logical debris at the boundary of experience.

**Structural Moves:**

- Mere intelligible objects are impossible for our cognition.
- Noumenon remains only negatively and problematically admissible.
- The concept of object in general is divided under something/nothing.
- The four senses of nothing complete the boundary-logic of the Analytic.
