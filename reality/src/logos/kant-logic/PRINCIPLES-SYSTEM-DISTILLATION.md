# Principles System - Distillation

Source: [principles-system.md](principles-system.md)

---

### TPS1. The System of Principles Must Follow the Categories and Possible Experience

**Distillation:**
The System of Principles opens by fixing its scope. It is not a miscellaneous list of maxims but the ordered unfolding of the judgments that pure understanding actually brings about a priori. Because these judgments concern the relation of categories to possible experience, the table of categories provides the natural guide. Kant also distinguishes sharply what belongs here and what does not. Aesthetic principles and mathematical constructions are not themselves members of the system, though the possibility of mathematics still depends on what this chapter explains. The field is therefore strictly the principled empirical use of the categories in relation to sensibility and possible experience.

**Structural Moves:**

- The system of principles is guided by the table of categories.
- Principles are grounded in the possibility of experience.
- Aesthetic principles and mathematics are distinguished from this system.

---

### TPS2. The Principle of Contradiction Governs Analytic Truth Only Negatively and Formally

**Distillation:**
Kant first treats the principle of contradiction in order to clear the ground. It is the universal and sufficient principle of analytic cognition because analytic judgments do nothing but make explicit what is already contained in the concept. Its role is indispensable but limited. It is a negative criterion of truth and a sufficient principle only within the analytic domain. It cannot determine the truth of synthetic cognition. Kant also rejects the common formula that adds the condition “at the same time,” because that imports time into a merely logical principle and obscures the difference between analytic contradiction and synthetic opposition.

**Structural Moves:**

- The principle of contradiction is sufficient for analytic cognition.
- Its authority is negative and formal.
- It cannot determine synthetic truth.
- Temporalized formulas distort its logical function.

---

### TPS3. Synthetic Judgments Need a Third Thing: Time, Imagination, and Apperception

**Distillation:**
Synthetic judgment cannot be explained through identity or contradiction. If two concepts are to be synthetically connected, a third thing is needed as their medium. Kant locates this medium in inner sense, time, imagination, and the unity of apperception. Objective validity arises only when an object can be given, and for us that means relation to actual or possible experience. The possibility of experience therefore gives all a priori cognition its objective reality. Without that relation, synthetic a priori judgment would be empty, and even pure representations such as space and time would have no significance.

**Structural Moves:**

- Synthetic judgment cannot be grounded in identity alone.
- A third mediating ground is required.
- Time, imagination, and apperception provide this ground.
- Objective validity rests on relation to possible experience.

---

### TPS4. The Supreme Principle of Synthetic Judgments Is the Possibility of Experience

**Distillation:**
The decisive formula follows: every object stands under the necessary conditions of the synthetic unity of the manifold of intuition in a possible experience. This means that the conditions of the possibility of experience are at the same time the conditions of the possibility of objects of experience. Here the Analytic of Principles gathers the work of Aesthetic, Deduction, and Schematism into one principle. Synthetic a priori judgments are possible because their form, synthesis, and unity are already tied to the structure of possible experience.

**Structural Moves:**

- Objects of experience stand under conditions of possible experience.
- The possibility of experience is the supreme principle of synthetic judgment.
- Synthetic a priori cognition gains objective validity only through this relation.

---

### TPS5. The Table of Principles Divides Into Mathematical and Dynamical Use

**Distillation:**
Kant then presents the architectonic of the principles themselves. The pure understanding is the source of principles because it is the source of the rules under which objects must stand. The table of principles follows the table of categories: axioms of intuition, anticipations of perception, analogies of experience, and postulates of empirical thinking in general. But the four are not all of the same type. Mathematical principles concern the intuition of appearances and are immediately evident; dynamical principles concern existence and relation in experience and therefore have a mediated but complete certainty. The whole system is thus already internally articulated before its individual members are unfolded.

**Structural Moves:**

- Principles follow the categories.
- The four principle-families map the empirical use of the categories.
- Mathematical and dynamical principles differ in evidence and mode of use.
- The system is architectonically ordered before its detailed execution.
