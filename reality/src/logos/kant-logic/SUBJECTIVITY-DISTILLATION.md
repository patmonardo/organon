### SS1. Subjectivity Names the Formal Architectonic of Discursive Cognition

**Distillation:**
In this Kantian register, subjectivity is not empirical inwardness or private feeling. It is the formal architectonic of discursive cognition: the ordered way consciousness can relate representations to an object through concept, judgment, and inference. The file therefore presents subjectivity not as a psychology of states but as a container-logic specifying the internal regions of thought.

**Structural Moves:**

- Subjectivity is treated formally rather than psychologically.
- Consciousness is defined through object-related representation.
- Subjectivity contains concept, judgment, and inference as internal formal regions.

---

### SS2. Concept Is the First Internal Region of Subjectivity

**Distillation:**
The first moment of subjectivity is concept. A concept is a universal or reflected representation, distinct from intuition as singular representation. Kant further distinguishes matter and form here: the matter of concepts is the object, their form is universality. The logical genesis of concepts as to form then proceeds through comparison, reflection, and abstraction. Subjectivity therefore begins with the formal region in which universality, marks, and subordination first become thinkable.

**Structural Moves:**

- Intuition and concept are distinguished as singular and universal representation.
- Thought is defined as cognition through concepts.
- Concept has matter and form as object and universality.
- Concept-formation as to form proceeds through comparison, reflection, and abstraction.

---

### SS3. Judgment Makes the Unity of Consciousness Explicit

**Distillation:**
Judgment is the second moment because concept does not remain merely held. A judgment is the representation of the unity of consciousness of various representations, or of their relation insofar as they constitute a concept. Kant distinguishes matter and form here too: the matter of judgment is the given representations combined in consciousness, the form is the way they belong to one consciousness. Logical reflection then performs its restriction: it abstracts from the matter of judgments and from the content of concepts, attending only to formal differences among judgments. Subjectivity therefore contains a second internal region where unity is explicit, but formal logic isolates only its admissible forms.

**Structural Moves:**

- Judgment expresses unity, not mere juxtaposition.
- Matter and form are distinguished within the judgment.
- Logical reflection brackets matter of judgments and content of concepts.
- Judgment introduces the formal differences later unfolded as quantity, quality, relation, and modality.

---

### SS4. Inference Completes the Formal Movement of Subjectivity

**Distillation:**
The third moment is inference. Here thought derives one judgment from another, either immediately or mediately. Kant does not leave this region generic. Immediate inference belongs to the understanding. Mediate inference is divided into inference of reason and inference of the power of judgment. Subjectivity therefore culminates not in a static inventory of forms, but in a faculty-differentiated transition from what is already judged to what follows from it.

**Structural Moves:**

- Inference is the derivation of one judgment from another.
- Immediate inference belongs to the understanding.
- Mediate inference bifurcates into reason and the power of judgment.
- The inferential moment is distributed by faculty, not left generic.

---

### SS5. The Whole File Defines a Formal Container Logic

**Distillation:**
Taken as a whole, the file outlines a compact grammar for a thinking machine: universal representation, explicit unity of representations, and rule-governed derivation. But its stronger significance is architectonic. Subjectivity is the formal container logic within which concept, judgment, and inference are explicitly articulated as component forms of discursive cognition. Concept supplies universality, judgment supplies explicit unity, and inference supplies derivational transition. In that sense the file gives a Kantian schema for how a logical agent can be structured before richer transcendental or speculative development begins.

**Structural Moves:**

- Concept supplies universality as the first formal region.
- Judgment supplies explicit unity as the second formal region.
- Inference supplies derivation as the third formal region.
- Subjectivity appears as architectonic container logic rather than a narrative self.
