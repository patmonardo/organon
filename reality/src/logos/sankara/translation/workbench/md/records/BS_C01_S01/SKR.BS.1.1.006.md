### Source Text
अतःशब्दः हेत्वर्थः । यस्मा द्वे द एव अग्निहोत्रादीनां श्रेयःसाधनानामनित्यफलतां दर्शयति — ‘ तद्यथेह कर्मचितो लोकः क्षीयत एवमेवामुत्र पुण्यचितो लोकः क्षीयते ’ (छा. उ. ८ । १ । ६) इत्यादिः ; तथा ब्रह्मविज्ञानादपि परं पुरुषार्थं दर्शयति — ‘ ब्रह्मविदाप्नोति परम् ’ (तै. उ. २ । १ । १) इत्यादिः । तस्मात् यथोक्तसाधनसम्पत्त्यनन्तरं ब्रह्मजिज्ञासा कर्तव्या ॥

### Transliteration (IAST)
ataḥśabdaḥ hetvarthaḥ | yasmā dve da eva agnihotrādīnāṃ śreyaḥsādhanānāmanityaphalatāṃ darśayati — ‘ tadyatheha karmacito lokaḥ kṣīyata evamevāmutra puṇyacito lokaḥ kṣīyate ’ (chā. u. 8 | 1 | 6) ityādiḥ ; tathā brahmavijñānādapi paraṃ puruṣārthaṃ darśayati — ‘ brahmavidāpnoti param ’ (tai. u. 2 | 1 | 1) ityādiḥ | tasmāt yathoktasādhanasampattyanantaraṃ brahmajijñāsā kartavyā ||

## Translation

### Literal
The word 'atah' has causal force: because scripture itself shows the impermanence of results from works such as Agnihotra ('as here a world won by action perishes, so too in the next world the merit-won world perishes'), and also shows the supreme human end beyond that through Brahman-knowledge ('the knower of Brahman attains the Highest'). Therefore, after acquiring the stated preparatory qualifications, inquiry into Brahman should be undertaken.

### Technical
Bhashya reads 'atah' as hetu: finitude of karma-phala and supremacy of brahma-vidya motivate transition to Brahma-jijnasa.

### Interpretive Note
Moves the argument from lexical parsing to existential motive.
