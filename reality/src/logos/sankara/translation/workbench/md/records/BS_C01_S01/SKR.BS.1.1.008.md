### Source Text
ज्ञातु मि च्छा जिज्ञासा । अवगति पर्यन्तं ज्ञानं सन्वाच्याया इच्छायाः कर्म , फल विषयत्वादिच्छायाः । ज्ञानेन हि प्रमाणेनावगन्तुमिष्टं ब्रह्म । ब्रह्मावगतिर्हि पुरुषार्थः , निःशेष संसारबीजाविद्याद्यनर्थनिबर्हणात् । तस्मा द्ब्र ह्म जिज्ञासितव्यम् ॥

### Transliteration (IAST)
jñātu mi cchā jijñāsā | avagati paryantaṃ jñānaṃ sanvācyāyā icchāyāḥ karma , phala viṣayatvādicchāyāḥ | jñānena hi pramāṇenāvagantumiṣṭaṃ brahma | brahmāvagatirhi puruṣārthaḥ , niḥśeṣa saṃsārabījāvidyādyanarthanibarhaṇāt | tasmā dbra hma jijñāsitavyam ||

## Translation

### Literal
The desire to know is jijnasa. Knowledge culminating in ascertainment is the object of that verbalized desire, since desire has result as its object. For Brahman is what is desired to be known through the pramana of knowledge. Realization of Brahman is indeed the highest human end, because it removes the entire complex of evils beginning with ignorance, the seed of samsara. Therefore Brahman must be inquired into.

### Technical
Bhashya defines jijnasa as iccha directed toward avagati and ties brahma-avagati to purushartha via complete avidya-nivritti.

### Interpretive Note
Defines inquiry as purposive cognition aimed at liberation, not curiosity.

  - Grammar: abstract noun

- **avagati**
  - Gloss: determinate understanding
  - Context: Serves as proper fulfillment of inquiry rather than mere curiosity.
  - Grammar: noun
  - Alternate glosses: ascertainment, comprehending cognition
- **jnana-iccha-karma-sambandha**
  - Gloss: relation of desire to knowledge as its object
  - Context: Clarifies semantic structure of jijnasa expression.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: desire-object relation, inquiry-object semantics
- **brahmavagati-purushartha**
  - Gloss: Brahman-realization as human end
  - Context: Teleological justification for Brahma-jijnasa in the sutra opening.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: liberating realization goal, ultimate human aim
- **avidya-bija-nivritti**
  - Gloss: cessation of ignorance-seed
  - Context: Explains why Brahman-knowledge alone removes samsara root.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: eradication of nescience-root, samsara-seed dissolution
