### Source Text
अथवा यथोक्त मृ ग्वेदादिशास्त्रं योनिः कारणं प्रमाण मस्य ब्रह्मणो यथावत्स्वरूपाधिगमे । शास्त्रा देव प्रमाणात् जगतो जन्मादिकारणं ब्रह्माधिगम्यत इत्यभिप्रायः । शास्त्र मु दाहृतं पूर्वसूत्रे — ‘ यतो वा इमानि भूतानि जायन्ते ’ (तै. उ. ३ । १ । १) इत्यादि । किमर्थं तर्हीदं सूत्रम् , यावता पूर्वसूत्र एव एवंजातीयकं शास्त्रमुदाहरता शास्त्रयोनित्वं ब्रह्मणो दर्शितम् । उच्यते — तत्र सूत्राक्षरेण स्पष्टं शास्त्रस्यानुपादानाज्जन्मादिसूत्रेण केवलमनुमानमुपन्यस्तमित्याशङ्क्येत ; तामाशङ्कां निवर्तयितुमिदं सूत्रं प्रववृते — ‘ शास्त्रयोनित्वात् ’ इति ॥ ३ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
Or, the previously described scripture beginning with the Rgveda is the source - the means of valid knowledge - for proper realization of this Brahman. The intent is: Brahman as the cause of origination and so on of the world is known from scripture. That scripture was already cited in the previous sutra ('from which indeed these beings are born,' etc.). Then why this sutra, if the prior birth-etc. sutra had already displayed Brahman's scriptural sourcehood? Reply: because in the birth-etc. sutra scripture was not explicitly named in the sutra wording, one might suspect that only inference was being presented. To remove that doubt this sutra is introduced: 'because of scriptural sourcehood.' (3)

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.1.3 clarifies shastra-yonitvat as explicit anti-misreading: janmadi-sutra is not bare anumana but Vedanta-vakya grounded.

### Interpretive Note
This is a methodological safeguard against reducing Vedanta to independent inference.
