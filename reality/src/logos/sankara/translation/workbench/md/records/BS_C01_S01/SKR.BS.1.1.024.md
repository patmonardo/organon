‘ सचक्षु रचक्षुरिव सकर्णोऽकर्ण इव सवागवागिव समना अमना इव सप्राणोऽप्राण इव ’ इति च । स्मृतिरपि — ‘ स्थितप्रज्ञस्य का भाषा ’ (भ. गी. २ । ५४) इत्याद्या स्थितप्रज्ञस्य लक्षणान्याचक्षाणा विदुषः सर्वप्रवृत्त्यसम्बन्धं दर्शयति । तस्मान्ना वगतब्रह्मात्मभावस्य यथा पूर्वं संसारित्वम् । यस्य तु यथापूर्वं संसारित्वं नासाववगतब्रह्मात्मभाव इत्यनवद्यम् ॥

## Translation

### Literal
As for what was said - that without entry into injunction to act, mere statement of an existent thing would be meaningless like 'the earth has seven continents' - this has been answered. Even mere object-statement is meaningful, as in 'this is a rope, not a snake,' where purpose is directly seen. If one says that even after hearing Brahman one still sees samsara as before, so unlike rope-cognition no practical effect follows, reply: once Brahman-self identity is understood, prior samsaric self-identification cannot stand, because it is opposed by knowledge generated from Vedic pramana. One cannot say that because the body-identified person had fear and sorrow, the same continues after that identification is removed by Brahman-knowledge. Just as grief from loss of wealth belongs to one who identifies with wealth, not to a renunciate free of that identification; and pleasure from wearing ornaments does not belong to one who has no ornament-identification. Thus sruti says: 'the bodiless is not touched by pleasant and unpleasant.' If one says bodilessness comes only after physical death, not while living, no - because embodiedness is rooted in false cognition. Apart from mistaken self-identification with body, embodiedness cannot be coherently posited for self. We have already said bodilessness is eternal, not produced by karma. If one says embodiedness is produced by dharma/adharma, this fails: relation to body is itself unproven, so actions ascribed to self are unproven; and making body-relation caused by dharma/adharma while dharma/adharma depend on that relation leads to mutual dependence and beginningless blind regress. Also self's agency is untenable due to lack of action-inherence. If one says kings are agents by mere presence, that works only because of servitor relations established through transactions like payment; no such ownership relation can be shown for self and body. False identification alone is the evident cause of relation. By this, yajamana-status attribution to self is explained. If one says identification of self with body etc. is figurative, not false, no: figurative usage presupposes established distinction between two known entities. Here that supposed relation is error-driven, not a stable primary-secondary semantic transfer.

### Technical
Bhashya deepens anti-kriyarthatva defense via rope-snake model, arguing sasariratva as avidya-nimitta and exposing karmic body-relation theories to anyonyashraya/anavastha problems.

### Interpretive Note
This section ties liberation-effect directly to de-identification rather than ritual production.

## Analysis

### Lexical Notes
- **rajju-sarpa-dṛṣṭānta**
  - Gloss: rope-snake analogy
  - Context: Shows object-level disclosure can be meaningful without ritual injunction.
  - Grammar: illustrative compound
  - Alternate glosses: error-correction analogy, illusion-sublation model
- **sāśarīratva-avidyā-nimitta**
  - Gloss: embodied status due to ignorance
  - Context: Reframes body-self relation as adhyasa-conditioned rather than ontologically final.
  - Grammar: compound doctrinal phrase
  - Alternate glosses: ignorance-conditioned embodiment, avidya-based corporeality
- **anyonyāśraya**
  - Gloss: mutual dependence fallacy
  - Context: Used against theories that require circular grounding of body-self relations.
  - Grammar: logical term
  - Alternate glosses: circular dependence, reciprocal presupposition defect
- **anavasthā-doṣa**
  - Gloss: infinite regress defect
  - Context: Exposes instability in karmic-body explanatory models when pushed recursively.
  - Grammar: logical term
  - Alternate glosses: regress fault, non-terminating dependency problem
- **vastu-kathana-prayojana**
  - Gloss: purpose of reality-statement
  - Context: Defends non-injunctive factual teaching as pragmatically and liberatively relevant.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: function of descriptive teaching, utility of object-disclosure
