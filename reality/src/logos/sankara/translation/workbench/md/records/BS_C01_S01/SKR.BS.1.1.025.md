यत्पुन रुक्तं श्रवणात्पराचीनयो र्मनननिदिध्यासनयोर्दर्शनाद्विधिशेषत्वं ब्रह्मणः , न स्वरूपपर्यवसायित्वमिति , त न्न । श्रवणव दवगत्यर्थत्वा न्मनननिदिध्यासनयोः । यदि ह्य वगतं ब्रह्मान्यत्र विनियुज्येत , भवेत्तदा विधिशेषत्वम् । न तु तदस्ति , मनननिदिध्यासनयोरपि श्रवणवदवगत्यर्थत्वात् । तस्मा न्न प्रतिपत्तिविधिविषयतया शास्त्रप्रमाणकत्वं ब्रह्मणः सम्भवती त्यतः स्वतन्त्र मे व ब्रह्म शास्त्रप्रमाणकं वेदान्तवाक्यसमन्वयादिति सिद्धम् । एवं च सति ‘ अथातो ब्रह्मजिज्ञासा ’ इति तद्विषयः पृथक्शास्त्रारम्भ उपपद्यते । प्रतिपत्ति विधिपरत्वे हि ‘ अथातो धर्मजिज्ञासा ’ इत्येवारब्धत्वान्न पृथक्शास्त्रमारभ्येत । आरभ्यमाणं चै वमारभ्येत — अथा तः परिशिष्टधर्मजिज्ञासेति , ‘ अथा तः क्रत्वर्थपुरुषार्थयोर्जिज्ञासा ’ (जै. सू. ४ । १। १) इतिवत् । ब्रह्मा त्मैक्यावगतिस्त्वप्रतिज्ञातेति तदर्थो युक्तः शास्त्रारम्भः — ‘ अथातो ब्रह्मजिज्ञासा ’ इति । तस्मात् अहं ब्रह्मास्मी त्येतदवसाना एव सर्वे विधयः सर्वाणि चेतराणि प्रमाणानि । न ह्य हेयानुपादेयाद्वैतात्मावगतौ , निर्विषया ण्यप्रमातृकाणि च प्रमाणानि भवितुमर्हन्तीति । अपि चा हुः — ‘ गौणमिथ्यात्मनो ऽसत्त्वे पुत्रदेहादिबाधनात् । सद्ब्रह्मात्माहमित्येवं बोधे कार्यं कथं भवेत् ॥ अन्वेष्टव्या त्म विज्ञानात्प्राक्प्रमातृत्वमात्मनः । अन्विष्टः स्यात्प्रमातैव पाप्मदोषादिवर्जितः ॥ देहा त्मप्रत्ययो यद्वत्प्रमाणत्वेन कल्पितः । लौकिकं तद्वदेवेदं प्रमाणं त्वा ऽऽत्मनिश्चयात् ’ इति ॥ ४ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
As for the claim that because hearing is followed by reflection and contemplation, Brahman must be only subsidiary to injunction and not culminate in direct nature-knowledge - no. Reflection and contemplation, like hearing, are for understanding. Only if already-understood Brahman were to be assigned elsewhere would subsidiary-to-injunction status follow; that is not the case. Since reflection and contemplation are themselves understanding-oriented like hearing, Brahman cannot be scripturally known as merely object of a cognition-injunction. Therefore Brahman is independently established by scripture through harmonized Vedanta sentences. On this basis, a distinct scripture-opening 'now therefore inquiry into Brahman' is coherent. If everything were cognition-injunction within dharma-framework, there would be no separate shastra-opening beyond 'now therefore inquiry into dharma'; at most it would begin as an extension, like inquiries into subdivisions of ritual means and human ends. But brahman-atman identity realization had not yet been undertaken, so a new opening is proper. Thus all injunctions and other pramanas culminate in 'I am Brahman.' Once nondual self-knowledge free of reject/accept structures is realized, pramanas cannot continue as objectified and knower-dependent in the previous way. Therefore the verse says: when false figurative-self identifications in son/body etc. are sublated by true knowledge 'I am Brahman-self,' what further action remains? Before self-knowledge, knowerhood functions for inquiry; after inquiry, that very knower is found to be free from sin and defects. Just as body-self cognition is provisionally accepted for ordinary pramana function, so too this scriptural pramana operates until self-certainty.

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.1.4 rejects vidhi-shesha reading by treating shravana-manana-nididhyasana as avagati-sadhana, and frames all pramana activity as sublated in aham brahmasmi aparoksha-niscaya.

### Interpretive Note
Clarifies that post-knowledge cessation is structural, not anti-scriptural.
