## Translation

### Literal
What was said - that non-conscious Pradhana denoted by sat can have only figurative seeing, like waters and fire - is false. Why? Because of the word self (atman). After beginning with 'Being alone was this in the beginning' and then stating 'it saw' and creation of fire-water-food, the text re-mentions that very sat as seer and those elements as deities: 'that deity saw: let me enter these three deities with this jiva-self and differentiate names and forms.' If non-conscious Pradhana were the primary topic and seer only figuratively by guna activity, then that same topic should be referenced there; in that case deity would not designate jiva by the word atman. But jiva is conscious, body-governor, life-sustainer by usage and derivation; how can conscious jiva be the self (essential nature) of non-conscious Pradhana? Atman denotes intrinsic identity. If instead one takes conscious Brahman as principal seer, then use of atman for jiva is coherent. Likewise in 'that subtle essence, this all has that as self, that is the real, that is the self, that thou art, Svetaketu,' the text first presents the subtle sat as self and then teaches it as the self of conscious Svetaketu. Fire and water are non-conscious as objects, and in name-form differentiation they are shown as instruments, with no basis for primary self-word use; so their seeing is rightly figurative like 'the bank wants to fall.' Even there, their seeing depends on sat as substrate. But for sat, due to atman-word, seeing is not figurative.

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.1.6 uses atman-sabda and tat tvam asi sequence to block figurative ikshana reading for sat; conscious Brahman alone satisfies self-reference continuity.

### Interpretive Note
Textual self-reference continuity defeats Pradhana substitution.

## Analysis

### Lexical Notes
- **gauṇa-īkṣaṇa-nirākaraṇa**
  - Gloss: rejection of figurative intending
  - Context: Bhashya denies metaphorical intent-reading for sat in this context.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: anti-metaphorical willing, secondary-sense denial
- **ātma-śabda-pramāṇa**
  - Gloss: authority of the word atman
  - Context: Self-designation binds the referent to conscious selfhood, not insentient substrate.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: self-term evidentiary force, atman-word constraint
- **prakaraṇa-anugati**
  - Gloss: continuity with discourse-context
  - Context: Beginning, middle, and culmination are read as one semantic current.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: contextual carry-through, sectional continuity
- **tat tvam asi-anvaya**
  - Gloss: identity linkage in tat tvam asi
  - Context: Identity instruction would fracture if sat were shifted to non-conscious Pradhana.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: great-sentence coherence, identity-statement alignment
- **upakrama-upasaṃhāra-sāmya**
  - Gloss: opening-closing concordance
  - Context: Hermeneutic seal that preserves one referent across creation and self-teaching segments.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: initial-final symmetry, framing concordance
