### Source Text
अथो च्येत — अचेतनेऽपि प्रधाने भवत्यात्मशब्दः , आत्मनः सर्वार्थकारित्वात् ; यथा राज्ञः सर्वार्थकारिणि भृत्ये भवत्यात्मशब्दः ‘ ममात्मा भद्रसेनः ’ इति । प्रधानं हि पुरुषस्यात्मनो भोगापवर्गौ कुर्वदुपकरोति , राज्ञ इव भृत्यः सन्धिविग्रहादिषु वर्तमानः । अथ वै क एवात्मशब्दश्चेतनाचेतनविषयो भविष्यति , ‘ भूतात्मा ’ ‘ इन्द्रियात्मा ’ इति च प्रयोगदर्शनात् ; य थै क एव ज्योतिःशब्दः क्रतुज्वलनविषयः । तत्र कुत एतदात्मशब्दादीक्षतेरगौणत्वमित्यत उत्तरं पठति —

## Translation

### Literal
Suppose one says: even in non-conscious Pradhana the word atman applies because it serves all purposes of the self, like a servant called 'my self' by a king because he accomplishes everything for him. Pradhana helps the self (purusha) attain enjoyment and liberation, as servant helps king in treaty-conflict matters. Or let atman be one common word for conscious and non-conscious, since usage like 'element-self' and 'sense-self' is seen, just as one jyotis-word applies to ritual and luminous fire. Then why insist atman and seeing here are non-figurative? To that, the answer sutra is read -

### Technical
Final purvapaksha before 1.1.7 proposes instrumental-functional and polysemous defenses for Pradhana under atman usage.

### Interpretive Note
Last attempt to salvage Pradhana through semantic flexibility.
