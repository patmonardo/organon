### Source Text
स्वशब्दे नै व च सर्वज्ञ ईश्वरो जगतः कारणमिति श्रूयते , श्वेताश्वतराणां मन्त्रोपनिषदि सर्वज्ञमीश्वरं प्रकृत्य — ‘ स कारणं करणाधिपाधिपो न चास्य कश्चिज्जनिता न चाधिपः ’ (श्वे. उ. ६ । ९) इति । तस्मा त्सर्वज्ञं ब्रह्म जगतः कारणम् , नाचेतनं प्रधान मन्यद्वेति सिद्धम् ॥ ११ ॥

### Transliteration (IAST)
svaśabde nai va ca sarvajña īśvaro jagataḥ kāraṇamiti śrūyate , śvetāśvatarāṇāṃ mantropaniṣadi sarvajñamīśvaraṃ prakṛtya — ‘ sa kāraṇaṃ karaṇādhipādhipo na cāsya kaścijjanitā na cādhipaḥ ’ (śve. u. 6 | 9) iti | tasmā tsarvajñaṃ brahma jagataḥ kāraṇam , nācetanaṃ pradhāna manyadveti siddham || 11 ||

## Translation

### Literal
And in a direct word too it is heard that the omniscient Lord alone is cause of the world: in the Svetasvatara mantra-upanishad, after introducing the omniscient Lord, it says, 'He is the cause, the lord of lords of instruments; He has no producer and no overlord.' Therefore it is established that omniscient Brahman is world-cause, not non-conscious Pradhana or anything else. (11)

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.1.11 invokes explicit shruti testimony (svasabda/shrutatva) naming omniscient Ishvara as cause, further excluding Pradhana.

### Interpretive Note
Direct scriptural naming supplements prior inferential-hermeneutic exclusions.
