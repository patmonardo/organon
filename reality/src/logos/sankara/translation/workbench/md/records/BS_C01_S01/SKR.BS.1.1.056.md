## Translation

### Literal
Here one objects: anandamaya cannot be the Supreme Self. Why? Because it is a transformation-word. This term 'anandamaya,' occurring in the present context, is understood as denoting modification, since the suffix mayat can indicate vikara. Therefore, like annamaya etc., anandamaya too should denote a derivative entity. Reply: no, because mayat is also remembered in the sense of abundance (pracurya). Grammar teaches mayat in the sense of fullness too; as in 'annamayo yajna' meaning food-abundant, so Brahman rich in bliss is called anandamaya. And Brahman's abundance of bliss is established since, after describing each higher level as a hundredfold increase from human bliss upward, scripture finally determines Brahman-bliss as unsurpassed. Therefore here mayat is in the abundance sense. (13)

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.1.13 rebuts vikara reading by invoking mayat-pracurya usage; anandamaya denotes plenitude of bliss in Brahman, not a produced sheath-entity.

### Interpretive Note
Suffix semantics is resolved by contextual excess-bliss gradation, not by defaulting to modification.
