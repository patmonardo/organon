## Translation

### Literal
And for this reason too anandamaya is the Supreme Self: beginning with 'the knower of Brahman attains the Highest' and 'Brahman is truth, knowledge, infinite,' that Brahman defined by those qualifiers - from which space onward and all moving/non-moving beings are born, which having created beings enters them and abides in the cave as innermost, and for whose realization the sequence 'another inner self, another inner self' is undertaken - that very Brahman described in the mantra section is what is sung here as 'another inner self, anandamaya.' It is proper that mantra and brahmana portions share one meaning when no contradiction exists. Otherwise one would abandon the introduced topic and begin an unintroduced one. Nor is any self further interior than anandamaya stated as with annamaya etc. And the Bhrgu-Varuni knowledge also culminates in this very one: 'he knew bliss as Brahman.' Therefore anandamaya is the Supreme Self alone. (15)

### Technical
