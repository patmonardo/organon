## Translation

### Literal
And for this reason too anandamaya is the Supreme Self, not the other; 'the other' means the transmigrating individual self distinct from Ishvara. The jiva is not denoted by anandamaya. Why? Because it is untenable. For after introducing anandamaya, scripture says: 'He desired: may I become many, may I be born; He performed tapas; having done tapas He created all this, whatever there is.' Here intentional resolve prior to body-production, non-difference from the entities to be produced, and creation of all modifications are intelligible only in the Supreme Self, not in another.

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.1.16 excludes jiva-reading: pre-cosmic willing, total creation, and causal non-difference predicates fit only Ishvara/Brahman.

### Interpretive Note
Creative intentionality is used to distinguish Supreme Self from bound individual self.
