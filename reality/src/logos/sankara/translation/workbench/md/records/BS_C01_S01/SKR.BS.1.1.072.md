## Translation

### Literal
It is taught: 'this golden person seen within the sun, golden beard, golden hair, wholly golden to the nails... his name is Udita; he rises above all sins for one who knows thus... this as adhidaivata... and as adhyatma ... this person seen within the eye,' etc. There doubt arises: is the one taught as worship-worthy in sun and eye some transmigrating being elevated by excellence of knowledge/ritual, or the eternally accomplished Supreme Lord? Prima facie: transmigrating being. Why? Because form is heard. For the solar person is described as golden-bearded etc.; and by transference the eye-person gets the same form ('his form is that very form of the other'). But formfulness does not fit Supreme Lord, according to texts such as 'soundless, touchless, formless, undecaying.' Also because support-location is heard ('within the sun,' 'within the eye'); the all-pervasive, self-grounded Lord should not be assigned a support. Your own cited texts say He is established in His own greatness and all-pervasive like space. Further, limited scope of lordship is heard: the solar person rules worlds above and divine desires; the ocular person rules worlds below and human desires. Such bounded lordship does not fit Supreme Lord, for scripture states universal sovereignty without limitation. Therefore one may conclude the inner sun/eye person is not Supreme Lord. In this obtained position, we say -

### Technical
Purvapaksha before 1.1.20 reads antaraditya/antarakshi purusha as an exalted samsarin, based on form, locative conditioning, and apparently bounded sovereignty predicates.

### Interpretive Note
This sets up a classic dispute between symbolic local manifestation and absolute divinity.

## Analysis

### Lexical Notes
- **antar-āditya-puruṣa-saṃśaya**
  - Gloss: doubt about the inner-solar person
  - Context: Debate opens over whether luminous inner form denotes Brahman or a highly endowed empirical being.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: inner-sun referential doubt, solar-indweller ambiguity
- **hiraṇmaya-rūpa-vacana**
  - Gloss: golden-form description
  - Context: Form-language is cited by the objection to argue limitation and qualified embodiment.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: radiant form predication, golden-body description
- **deśa-paricchinna-ākṣepa**
  - Gloss: objection from spatial confinement
  - Context: Location in sun/eye is used to contest absolute, all-pervasive interpretation.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: locative limitation challenge, site-boundedness objection
- **aiśvarya-parimāṇa-vāda**
  - Gloss: graded-lordship thesis
  - Context: Opponent treats sovereignty predicates as relative excellence within samsara, not supreme status.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: limited divinity argument, comparative lordship reading
- **upāsya-devatā-kalpanā**
  - Gloss: constructing a meditational deity referent
  - Context: Purvapaksha frames the passage as symbolic worship-object teaching rather than ontological absoluteness.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: upasana-deity interpretation, symbolic worship referent
