### Source Text
अस्ति चादित्यादिशरीराभिमानिभ्यो जीवेभ्योऽन्य ईश्वरोऽन्तर्यामी — ‘ य आदित्ये तिष्ठन्नादित्यादन्तरो य मा दित्यो न वेद य स्या दित्यः शरीरं य आदित्यमन्तरो यमय त्येष त आत्मान्तर्याम्यमृतः ’ (बृ. उ. ३ । ७ । ९) इति श्रुत्यन्तरे भेदव्यपदेशात् । तत्र हि ‘ आदित्यादन्तरो यमादित्यो न वेद ’ इति वेदितुरादित्याद्विज्ञानात्मनोऽन्योऽन्तर्यामी स्पष्टं निर्दिश्यते । स एवेहाप्यन्तरादित्ये पुरुषो भवितुमर्हति , श्रुतिसामान्यात् । तस्मात्परमेश्वर एवेहोपदिश्यत इति सिद्धम् ॥ २१ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
And there is another Lord, distinct from the jivas who identify with solar and other bodies: 'He who dwells in the sun, within the sun, whom the sun does not know, whose body is the sun, who controls the sun from within - He is your self, the inner controller, immortal.' In that other scripture, difference is explicitly declared. There, by saying 'within the sun whom the sun does not know,' an inner controller distinct from the conscious sun-associated self is clearly indicated. That very one should also be the person within the sun here, because the scriptural pattern is the same. Therefore it is established that the Supreme Lord alone is taught here. (21)

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.1.21 adduces antaryamin-brāhmana bheda-vyapadesha to confirm that inner solar person is Ishvara distinct from conditioned jiva.

### Interpretive Note
Cross-text identity via shared 'inner controller' structure.
