## Translation

### Literal
Some cite examples such as 'prana of prana' and 'mind indeed bound to prana.' That is not suitable as an example here, because by word-difference and by context there is no real doubt there. As in 'father of father,' the nominative father is understood as different from the genitive-indicated father; so in 'pranasya pranam,' a prana different from the familiar prana is determined, since one thing is not fit to be specified as its own different support in that way. And what is indicated in a context, even when named differently, is understood as that contextual topic - as in jyotistoma contexts where jyotis refers to the ritual. Thus in a context about Supreme Brahman, how could 'mind bound to prana' denote mere air-modification? Therefore those are not proper doubt-generating examples. In the prastava-deity case, however, doubt-purvapaksha-decision is properly established. (23)
