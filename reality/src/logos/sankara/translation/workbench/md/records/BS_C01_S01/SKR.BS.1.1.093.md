## Translation

### Literal
There is the Kausitaki narrative of Indra and Pratardana, beginning 'Pratardana, son of Divodasa, came to Indra's beloved abode by battle and valor.' There it is heard: 'I am prana, the conscious self; worship me as life and immortality.' Later too: 'this very prana, conscious self, taking hold of this body raises it'; also 'do not seek speech, know the speaker'; and at the end: 'this very prana, conscious self, blissful, ageless, immortal.' Here doubt arises: does prana denote mere air, deity-self, jiva, or Supreme Brahman? One may ask: was not prana's Brahman-reference already established in 'for that very reason, prana'? And here too Brahman-indicators appear ('blissful, ageless, immortal'). How then doubt again? Because multiple indicators appear. Not only Brahman-indicators are present: 'know me alone' spoken by Indra indicates deity-self; 'raises this body' indicates vital air; 'know the speaker, not speech' indicates jiva. Therefore doubt is reasonable. In that situation, given lexical familiarity, one may first take prana as air -

### Technical
Opening purvapaksha for 1.1.28: Kausitaki prana is ambiguous due to overlapping linga sets (deity, vital force, jiva, Brahman), requiring higher-order textual integration.

### Interpretive Note
This marks a multi-indicator adjudication case, not a simple lexical one.
