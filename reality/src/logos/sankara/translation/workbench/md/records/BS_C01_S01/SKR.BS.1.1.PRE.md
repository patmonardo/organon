### Source Text
युष्मदस्मत्प्रत्ययगोचरयो र्विषयविषयिणो स्तमःप्रकाशवद्विरुद्धस्वभावयो रितरेतर भावानुपपत्तौ सिद्धायाम् , तद्धर्माणामपि सुतरा मि तरेतरभावानुपपत्तिः — इत्यतः अस्मत्प्रत्ययगोचरे विषयिणि चिदात्मके युष्म त्प्रत्ययगोचरस्य विषयस्य तद्धर्माणां चा ध्यासः तद्विपर्ययेण विषयिणस्तद्धर्माणां च विषयेऽध्यासो मि थ्येति भवितुं युक्तम् । तथा प्य न्योन्यस्मि न्नन्योन्यात्मकता मन्योन्यधर्मांश्चा ध्य स्येतरेतराविवेकेन अत्यन्त विविक्तयो र्धर्मधर्मिणोः मिथ्या ज्ञाननिमित्तः सत्या नृते मिथुनीकृत्य ‘ अह मिदम् ’ ‘ ममेदम् ’ इति नैसर्गिको ऽयं लोक व्यवहारः ॥

## Translation

### Literal
Since it is established that there can be no mutual superimposition between subject and object - opposed in nature like darkness and light, one being the domain of 'you' cognition and the other of 'I' cognition - all the more there can be no real transfer of each other's properties. Therefore it is reasonable that, in the conscious subject given in 'I'-cognition, the object given in 'you'-cognition and its properties are falsely superimposed; and conversely, the subject and its properties are superimposed on the object. Yet through non-discrimination, people mutually project identity and attributes between these utterly distinct substratum and properties, conjoining the real and unreal through false cognition, and thus arises this natural worldly practice: 'I am this,' 'this is mine.'

### Technical
Preamble establishes adhyasa as mithya-jnana: reciprocal superimposition of atman/anatman and their dharmas due to aviveka, generating the basic aham-idam/mamedam structure of samsaric cognition.

### Interpretive Note
The opening move grounds Vedanta in diagnosing the primordial confusion of subject and object before any further doctrinal argument.

## Analysis

### Lexical Notes
- **adhyāsa**
  - Gloss: superimposition
  - Context: Erroneous reciprocal attribution of self and non-self, and of their properties
  - Grammar: noun
  - Alternate glosses: misattribution, cognitive overlay
- **mithyā**
  - Gloss: false, dependent, not absolutely real
  - Context: Marks the false status of the superimposed world-process
  - Grammar: adjective
  - Alternate glosses: unreal, falsely presented
