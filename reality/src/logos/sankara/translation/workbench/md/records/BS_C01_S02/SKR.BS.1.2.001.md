### Source Text
प्रथमे पादे ‘ जन्माद्यस्य यतः ’ इत्याकाशादेः समस्तस्य जगतो जन्मादिकारणं ब्रह्मेत्युक्तम् । तस्य समस्तजगत्कारणस्य ब्रह्मणो व्यापित्वं नित्यत्वं सर्वज्ञत्वं सर्वशक्तित्वं सर्वात्मत्वमित्येवंजातीयका धर्मा उक्ता एव भवन्ति । अर्थान्तर प्रसिद्धानां च केषाञ्चिच्छब्दानां ब्रह्मविषयत्वहेतुप्रतिपादनेन कानिचिद्वाक्यानि स्पष्टब्रह्मलिङ्गानि सन्दिह्यमानानि ब्रह्मपरतया निर्णीतानि । पुनरप्यन्यानि वाक्यान्यस्पष्टब्रह्मलिङ्गानि सन्दिह्यन्ते — किं परं ब्रह्म प्रतिपादयन्ति , आहोस्विदर्थान्तरं किञ्चिदिति । तन्निर्णयाय द्वितीयतृतीयौ पादावारभ्येते —

## Translation

### Literal
In the first pada, by 'janmady asya yatah,' Brahman was stated to be the cause of the origin and so on of the entire world beginning with space. For that Brahman, cause of the whole world, such properties as pervasiveness, eternality, omniscience, omnipotence, and being the self of all are already established. And by establishing the reason why certain words known elsewhere in other senses apply to Brahman, some passages with explicit marks of Brahman that were doubtful were settled as referring to Brahman. Yet other passages, whose marks of Brahman are not explicit, remain doubtful: do they teach the supreme Brahman, or something else? To settle that, the second and third padas are begun.

### Technical
Pada 1 established from 'janmady asya yatah' that Brahman is the universal causal ground (origin, sustenance, dissolution) of the cosmos. Accordingly, attributes such as all-pervasiveness, eternality, omniscience, omnipotence, and sarvatmatva follow for that Brahman. It also resolved several contextually ambiguous sentences as Brahman-statements by showing why terms otherwise famous in different senses are legitimately Brahman-signifiers. However, additional passages remain under interpretive doubt because their brahma-linga is less explicit. Hence padas 2 and 3 are opened as a nirnaya procedure to determine whether such passages denote para-brahman or an alternative referent.

### Interpretive Note
Programmatic preface for the hermeneutic work of padas 2-3.
