### Source Text
इद मा म्नायते — ‘ सर्वं खल्विदं ब्रह्म तज्जलानिति शान्त उपासीत । अथ खलु क्रतुमयः पुरुषो यथा क्रतुरस्मिँल्लोके पुरुषो भवति तथेतः प्रेत्य भवति स क्रतुं कुर्वीत ’ (छा. उ. ३ । १४ । १) , ‘ मनो मयः प्राणशरीरः ’ (छा. उ. ३ । १४ । २) इत्यादि । तत्र संशयः — किमिह मनोमयत्वादिभिर्धर्मैः शारीर आत्मोपास्यत्वेनोपदिश्यते , आहोस्वित्परं ब्रह्मेति । किं तावत्प्राप्तम् ? शारीर इति । कुतः ? तस्य हि कार्यकरणाधिपतेः प्रसिद्धो मनआदिभिः सम्बन्धः , न परस्य ब्रह्मणः ; ‘ अप्राणो ह्यमनाः शुभ्रः ’ (मु. उ. २ । १ । २) इत्यादिश्रुतिभ्यः । ननु ‘ सर्वं खल्विदं ब्रह्म ’ इति स्वशब्देनैव ब्रह्मोपात्तम् ; कथमिह शारीर आत्मोपास्यत्वेनाशङ्क्यते ? नैष दोषः । नेदं वाक्यं ब्रह्मोपासनाविधिपरम् । किं तर्हि ? शमविधिपरम् ; यत्कारणम् ‘ सर्वं खल्विदं ब्रह्म तज्जलानिति शान्त उपासीत ’ इत्याह । एतदुक्तं भवति — यस्मात्सर्वमिदं विकारजातं ब्रह्मैव , तज्जत्वात् तल्लत्वात् तदनत्वाच्च — न च सर्वस्यैकात्मत्वे रागादयः सम्भवन्ति — तस्मात् शान्त उपासीतेति । न च शम विधिपरत्वे सत्यनेन वाक्येन ब्रह्मोपासनं नियन्तुं शक्यते । उपासनं तु ‘ स क्रतुं कुर्वीत ’ इत्यनेन विधीयते । क्रतुः सङ्कल्पो ध्यानमित्यर्थः । तस्य च विषयत्वेन श्रूयते — ‘ मनोमयः प्राणशरीरः ’ इति जीवलिङ्गम् । अतो ब्रूमः — जीवविषयमेतदुपासनमिति । ‘ सर्वकर्मा सर्वकामः ’ इत्याद्यपि श्रूयमाणं पर्यायेण जीवविषयमुपपद्यते । ‘ एष म आत्मान्तर्हृदयेऽणीयान्व्रीहेर्वा यवाद्वा ’ इति च हृदयायतनत्वमणीयस्त्वं चा राग्रमात्रस्य जीवस्यावकल्पते , नापरिच्छिन्नस्य ब्रह्मणः । ननु ‘ ज्यायान्पृथिव्याः ’ इत्याद्यपि न परिच्छिन्नेऽवकल्पत इति । अत्र ब्रूमः — न तावदणीयस्त्वं ज्यायस्त्वं चोभयमेकस्मिन्समाश्रयितुं शक्यम् , विरोधात् । अन्यतराश्रयणे च , प्रथमश्रुतत्वादणीयस्त्वं युक्तमाश्रयितुम् । ज्यायस्त्वं तु ब्रह्मभावापेक्षया भविष्यतीति । निश्चिते च जीवविषयत्वे यदन्ते ब्रह्मसङ्कीर्तनम् — ‘ एतद्ब्रह्म ’ (छा. उ. ३ । १४ । ४) इति , तदपि प्रकृतपरामर्शार्थत्वाज्जीवविषयमेव । तस्मान्मनोमयत्वादिभिर्धर्मैर्जीव उपास्य इत्येवं प्राप्ते ब्रूमः —

## Translation

### Literal
Here the text is recited: 'All this indeed is Brahman; from That it is born, into That it dissolves, in That it breathes; one should worship in calmness. Now, the person is made of resolve; as a person resolves in this world, so he becomes after death; therefore he should form resolve,' and 'mind-formed, with prana as body,' etc. Doubt arises: is the embodied self taught here as the object of worship through attributes such as mind-formedness, or is it the supreme Brahman? Prima facie it is the embodied self, because relation with mind and the like is well known for the lord of body and instruments, not for the supreme Brahman, from passages such as 'without prana, without mind, pure.' If one says Brahman is already named by its own word in 'all this indeed is Brahman,' how can embodied-self worship be suspected here? No fault: that sentence is not a Brahman-upasana injunction, but an injunction to tranquility. For it says, 'therefore, calm, one should worship.' Meaning: since this whole manifold is only Brahman, being born from That, dissolved in That, and living in That, and since attachment etc. cannot stand where all is one self, therefore one should meditate in tranquility. And with this calmness-injunction, Brahman-upasana cannot be fixed by that sentence. Upasana is enjoined instead by 'he should form resolve.' 'Kratu' means intention, that is, meditation. Its object is heard in 'mind-formed, with prana as body,' which is a sign of jiva. Therefore we say: this upasana concerns the jiva. Also what is heard as 'doer of all actions, possessor of all desires' etc. can secondarily fit the jiva. And 'this self within the heart, smaller than rice or barley' indicates heart-location and minuteness, fitting the individual self, not unlimited Brahman. If one objects that 'greater than earth' etc. does not fit the limited self, we reply: both smallness and greatness cannot be in one locus due to contradiction. If one must choose, smallness should be taken because it is first taught; greatness can then be with reference to Brahman-nature. And when Brahman is mentioned at the end, 'this is Brahman,' that too refers to the prior topic and remains jiva-related. Thus, with mind-formedness etc. the jiva is the object of worship - this is the prima facie position.

### Technical
This bhashya opens the ChU 3.14 debate by posing a vishaya-samsaya: do attributes like mano-maya and prana-sarira qualify the embodied individual (sarira-atman/jiva) or para-brahman? The purvapaksha argues for jiva-upasana: (1) mind/prana association is empirically and scripturally linked to embodied agency, while pure Brahman is described as aprana, amanas; (2) 'sarvam khalv idam brahma' is read not as a direct upasana-vidhi but as a shama-vidhi that grounds dispassion through non-dual vision; (3) the operative upasana injunction is 'sa kratum kurvita,' where kratu = meditative resolve; (4) descriptors like hrdaya-location and anu-size better match finite individuality than niravacchinna Brahman. Even terminal brahma-reference ('etad brahma') is interpreted as anuvada of the ongoing jiva-topic. So the provisional conclusion (prapti) is jiva as the immediate upasya under mano-mayatva etc., pending siddhanta reversal in the next block.
