### Source Text
परमेव ब्रह्मेह मनोमयत्वादिभिर्धर्मैरुपास्यम् । कुतः ? सर्वत्र प्रसिद्धोपदेशात् । यत्सर्वेषु वेदान्तेषु प्रसिद्धं ब्रह्मशब्दस्यालम्बनं जगत्कारणम् , इह च ‘ सर्वं खल्विदं ब्रह्म ’ इति वाक्योपक्रमे श्रुतम् , तदेव मनोमयत्वादिधर्मैर्विशिष्टमुपदिश्यत इति युक्तम् । एवं च सति प्रकृतहानाप्रकृतप्रक्रिये न भविष्यतः । ननु वाक्योपक्रमे शमविधिविवक्षया ब्रह्म निर्दिष्टं न स्वविवक्षयेत्युक्तम् ; अत्रोच्यते — यद्यपि शमविधिविवक्षया ब्रह्म निर्दिष्टम् , तथापि मनोमयत्वादिषूपदिश्यमानेषु तदेव ब्रह्म सन्निहितं भवति , जीवस्तु न सन्निहितः , न च स्वशब्देनोपात्त इति वैषम्यम् ॥ १ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
It is the supreme Brahman alone that is to be worshiped here through attributes such as mind-formedness. Why? Because of instruction of what is universally known. That which in all Vedanta is known as the referent of the word Brahman, the cause of the world, is heard here at the beginning of the passage as 'all this indeed is Brahman'; that very thing is taught as qualified by mind-formedness and the rest - this is proper. Thus there is no abandonment of the topic and no shift to an untopical procedure. If one says Brahman was indicated only with intent to enjoin tranquility at the opening and not for its own sake, we reply: even if indicated for shama-vidhi, still when mind-formedness etc. are taught, that very nearby Brahman is available; jiva is neither nearby nor directly introduced by its own term.

### Technical
Siddhanta: the upasya is para-brahman, not the embodied individual. The hetu is sarvatra-prasiddhopadesa: the referent already established across Vedanta as brahma-sabda-vacya and jagat-karana is explicitly introduced at passage opening ('sarvam khalv idam brahma'). Therefore subsequent qualifiers (mano-maya etc.) are to be read as vishesana of that same prakrta Brahman, avoiding prakrta-hana and aprakrta-prakriya (topic break and forced shift). Even granting that the opening sentence functionally supports shama-vidhi, textual proximity and explicit naming still privilege Brahman as the continuing referent; jiva lacks equivalent immediate textual establishment.

### Interpretive Note
Siddhanta turn: continuity of prakarana fixes Brahman as referent.
