### Source Text
वक्तुमिष्टा विवक्षिताः । यद्यप्यपौरुषेये वेदे वक्तुरभावात् नेच्छार्थः सम्भवति , तथाप्युपादानेन फलेनोपचर्यते । लोके हि यच्छब्दाभिहितमुपादेयं भवति तद्विवक्षितमित्युच्यते , यदनुपादेयं तदविवक्षितमिति । तद्वद्वेदेऽप्युपादेयत्वेनाभिहितं विवक्षितं भवति , इतरदविवक्षितम् । उपादानानुपादाने तु वेदवाक्यतात्पर्यातात्पर्याभ्यामवगम्येते । तदिह ये विवक्षिता गुणा उपासनायामुपादेयत्वेनोपदिष्टाः सत्यसङ्कल्पप्रभृतयः , ते परस्मिन्ब्रह्मण्युपपद्यन्ते । सत्यसङ्कल्पत्वं हि सृष्टिस्थितिसंहारेष्वप्रतिबद्धशक्तित्वात्परमात्मन एवावकल्पते । परमात्मगुणत्वेन च ‘ य आत्मापहतपाप्मा ’ (छा. उ. ८ । ७ । १) इत्यत्र ‘ सत्यकामः सत्यसङ्कल्पः ’ इति श्रुतम् , ‘ आकाशात्मा ’ इति च आकाशवदात्मा अस्येत्यर्थः । सर्वगतत्वादिभिर्धर्मैः सम्भवत्याकाशेन साम्यं ब्रह्मणः । ‘ ज्यायान्पृथिव्याः ’ इत्यादिना चैतदेव दर्शयति । यदापि आकाश आत्मा यस्येति व्याख्यायते , तदापि सम्भवति सर्वजगत्कारणस्य सर्वात्मनो ब्रह्मण आकाशात्मत्वम् । अत एव ‘ सर्वकर्मा ’ इत्यादि । एवमिहोपास्यतया विवक्षिता गुणा ब्रह्मण्युपपद्यन्ते । यत्तूक्तम् — ‘ मनोमयः प्राणशरीरः ’ इति जीवलिङ्गम् , न तद्ब्रह्मण्युपपद्यत इति ; तदपि ब्रह्मण्युपपद्यत इति ब्रूमः । सर्वात्मत्वाद्धि ब्रह्मणो जीवसम्बन्धीनि मनोमयत्वादीनि ब्रह्मसम्बन्धीनि भवन्ति । तथा च ब्रह्मविषये श्रुतिस्मृती भवतः — ‘ त्वं स्त्री त्वं पुमानसि त्वं कुमार उत वा कुमारी । त्वं जीर्णो दण्डेन वञ्चसि त्वं जातो भवसि विश्वतोमुखः ’ (श्वे. उ. ४ । ३) इति ; ‘ सर्वतःपाणिपादं तत्सर्वतोऽक्षिशिरोमुखम् । सर्वतःश्रुतिमल्लोके सर्वमावृत्य तिष्ठति ’ (भ. गी. १३ । १३) इति च । ‘ अप्राणो ह्यमनाः शुभ्रः ’ इति श्रुतिः शुद्धब्रह्मविषया , इयं तु श्रुतिः ‘ मनोमयः प्राणशरीरः ’ इति सगुणब्रह्मविषयेति विशेषः । अतो विवक्षितगुणोपपत्तेः परमेव ब्रह्म इहोपास्यत्वेनोपदिष्टमिति गम्यते ॥ २ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
'Intended' means intended to be conveyed. Though in the authorless Veda no speaker-intent is possible in the ordinary sense, the term is used figuratively based on what is to be accepted as result. In common usage, what a word-expression presents as to-be-adopted is called intended; what is not to be adopted is unintended. So also in Veda: what is stated as adoptable is intended, the rest unintended. And adoption/non-adoption are known by the purport/non-purport of the Vedic sentence. Here, the attributes intended as upasana-relevant, such as true resolve, fit in the supreme Brahman. True-resolve belongs only to the supreme self, whose power in creation, maintenance, and dissolution is unobstructed. And as qualities of the supreme self these are heard elsewhere: 'that self free from evil ... whose desires are true, whose resolves are true,' and 'whose self is space.' 'Whose self is space' means whose nature is like space; likeness to space by all-pervasiveness etc. is possible for Brahman. Even if interpreted as 'for whom space is self,' that too fits Brahman as cause and self of all. Hence also 'doer of all actions' etc. Thus the qualities intended here for meditation fit Brahman. As for the objection that 'mind-formed, with prana as body' is a jiva-sign and does not fit Brahman, we say it too fits Brahman: because Brahman is the self of all, jiva-related predicates can belong to Brahman. So scripture and smriti say of Brahman: 'You are woman, you are man ...' and 'with hands and feet everywhere...' The text 'without prana, without mind, pure' concerns pure Brahman, while this text concerns qualified Brahman; that is the distinction. Therefore, because intended qualities are fitting, it is the supreme Brahman that is taught here as the object of worship. (2)

### Technical
This block defines vivaksa functionally through upadeyata (what the passage normatively asks the meditator to take up), not through psychological author-intention. Hermeneutic criterion: vedavakya-tatparya identifies which predicates are operative in upasana. Predicates such as satya-sankalpatva, akasa-atmatva, and sarva-karmatva are shown to be coherent for saguna-brahman, especially given jagat-karanatva and sarvatmatva. Apparent jiva-lingas (mano-maya, prana-sarira) are re-read under total-self logic: predicates of individuated loci can be secondarily predicated of Brahman as universal self. Scriptural differentiation resolves conflict: aprana/amanas statements target nirguna-shuddha-brahman, while this context is saguna-upasana. Hence the sutra-ground is established: vivaksita-guna-upapatti confirms Brahman-reference, not jiva-reference.
