## Translation

### Literal
The previous sutra established that the intended attributes fit Brahman. This sutra states their non-fit in the embodied individual. The particle tu is restrictive. By the prior reasoning, mind-made and related attributes belong to Brahman alone, not to the embodied jiva. Why? Because attributes such as 'whose will is true,' 'whose self is space,' 'speechless,' 'without dependence,' 'greater than earth,' and similar do not straightforwardly fit the embodied self. 'Embodied' means situated in body. Objection: Isvara too is in body. True, He is in body, but He is not body-bound as body itself, since scripture teaches His pervasiveness ('greater than earth, greater than mid-space,' 'all-pervading and eternal like space'). The jiva, however, is body-bound for experience and does not function apart from body in that mode. (3)

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.2.3 denies sharira-jiva as referent of manomaya predicates; the listed mahat-gunas fit only the all-pervasive Brahman, not body-delimited experiencer-consciousness.

### Interpretive Note
Core move: predicate-fit test excludes embodied jiva while preserving antaryami possibility.
