### Source Text
इतश्च न शारीरो मनोमयत्वादिगुणः ; यस्मात्कर्मकर्तृव्यपदेशो भवति — ‘ एतमितः प्रेत्याभिसम्भवितास्मि ’ (छा. उ. ३ । १४ । ४) इति । एतमिति प्रकृतं मनोमयत्वादिगुणमुपास्यमात्मानं कर्मत्वेन प्राप्यत्वेन व्यपदिशति ; अभिसम्भवितास्मीति शारीरमुपासकं कर्तृत्वेन प्रापकत्वेन । अभिसम्भवितास्मीति प्राप्तास्मीत्यर्थः । न च सत्यां गतावेकस्य कर्मकर्तृव्यपदेशो युक्तः । तथोपा स्यो पासकभावोऽपि भेदाधिष्ठान एव । तस्मादपि न शारीरो मनोमयत्वादिविशिष्टः ॥ ४ ॥

### Transliteration (IAST)
itaśca na śārīro manomayatvādiguṇaḥ ; yasmātkarmakartṛvyapadeśo bhavati — ‘ etamitaḥ pretyābhisambhavitāsmi ’ (chā. u. 3 | 14 | 4) iti | etamiti prakṛtaṃ manomayatvādiguṇamupāsyamātmānaṃ karmatvena prāpyatvena vyapadiśati ; abhisambhavitāsmīti śārīramupāsakaṃ kartṛtvena prāpakatvena | abhisambhavitāsmīti prāptāsmītyarthaḥ | na ca satyāṃ gatāvekasya karmakartṛvyapadeśo yuktaḥ | tathopā syo pāsakabhāvo'pi bhedādhiṣṭhāna eva | tasmādapi na śārīro manomayatvādiviśiṣṭaḥ || 4 ||

## Translation

### Literal
For this reason too, the mind-made qualifier and related attributes do not belong to the embodied self: there is designation of object and agent in the statement 'having departed from here, I shall attain Him.' Here 'Him' designates the previously discussed object of meditation with mind-made and related qualities as object/goal; 'I shall attain' designates the embodied meditator as agent/attainer. Thus 'I shall attain' means 'I shall reach.' In real movement, one and the same entity cannot coherently be both object and agent in this way. Likewise, the meditated and meditator roles presuppose difference. Therefore, the one qualified by mind-made attributes is not the embodied self. (4)

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.2.4: karmakartṛ-vyapadesha (goal/attainer distinction) and upasya/upasaka structure require referential difference, excluding jiva self-reference.

### Interpretive Note
Syntax-level role distinction becomes ontological indicator.
