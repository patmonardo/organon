## Translation

### Literal
And for this reason too, the one with mind-made and related attributes is other than the embodied self: there is a specific wording in a parallel passage of the same topic - 'as a grain of rice or barley... this golden person in the inner self.' The word denoting the embodied self appears in locative form ('in the inner self'), while a distinct nominative word 'person' denotes another self qualified by mind-made and related attributes. Thus, difference between the two is understood. (5)

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.2.5 uses grammatical distinction (saptami vs prathama reference) in parallel shruti to infer bheda between sharira-jiva locus and worshipped purusha.

### Interpretive Note
Grammatical case contrast is treated as interpretive evidence.
