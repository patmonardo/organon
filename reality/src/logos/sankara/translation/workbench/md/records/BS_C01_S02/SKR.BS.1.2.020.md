## Translation

### Literal
In the Katha section it is read: 'For whom brahmana and kshatra both are food, and death is condiment - who thus knows where He is?' Here an eater is indicated by the food-condiment imagery. The doubt is: is the eater fire, or the jiva, or the Supreme Self? There is no immediate determination by special mark, and in this textual region all three - fire, jiva, and Supreme - are encountered in question and exposition. Prima facie one may take fire as eater, from scriptural/common usage ('fire is eater of food'). Or one may take jiva, as seen in texts where one of two birds eats the sweet fruit. Not the Supreme, since another text says one (the higher) does not eat but merely witnesses. With this prima facie view, we reply -
