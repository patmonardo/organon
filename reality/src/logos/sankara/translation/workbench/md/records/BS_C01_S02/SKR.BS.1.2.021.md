### Source Text
अत्तात्र परमात्मा भवितुमर्हति । कुतः ? चराचरग्रहणात् । चराचरं हि स्थावरजङ्गमं मृत्यूपसेचनमिहाद्यत्वेन प्रतीयते । तादृशस्य चाद्यस्य न परमात्मनोऽन्यः कार्त्स्न्येनात्ता सम्भवति । परमात्मा तु विकारजातं संहरन्सर्वमत्तीत्युपपद्यते । नन्विह चराचरग्रहणं नोपलभ्यते , कथं सिद्धवच्चराचरग्रहणं हेतुत्वेनोपादीयते ? नैष दोषः , मृत्यूपसेचनत्वेनेहाद्यत्वेन सर्वस्य प्राणिनिकायस्य प्रतीयमानत्वात् , ब्रह्मक्षत्रयोश्च प्राधान्यात्प्रदर्शनार्थत्वोपपत्तेः । यत्तु परमात्मनोऽपि नात्तृत्वं सम्भवति ‘ अनश्नन्नन्योऽभिचाकशीति ’ इति दर्शनादिति , अत्रोच्यते — कर्मफलभोगस्य प्रतिषेधकमेतद्दर्शनम् , तस्य सन्निहितत्वात् । न विकारसंहारस्य प्रतिषेधकम् , सर्ववेदान्तेषु सृष्टिस्थितिसंहारकारणत्वेन ब्रह्मणः प्रसिद्धत्वात् । तस्मात्परमात्मैवेहात्ता भवितुमर्हति ॥ ९ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
The eater here must be the Supreme Self. Why? Because what is taken as eaten is the moving and unmoving whole. The entire stationary and moving cosmos, with death as condiment, is implied as food here. For such an object, no one other than the Supreme can be total eater in full extent. The Supreme, as dissolver of all modifications, is coherently said to eat all. Objection: explicit moving-unmoving language is not directly present; how then is this reason treated as established? No fault: since death as condiment and food-status implies the total aggregate of beings, and brahmana-kshatra are shown as principal exemplars. Objection again: even for Supreme, eaterhood is impossible due to texts saying 'the other does not eat but only looks on.' Reply: that statement negates karmic-fruit enjoyment in that context, not cosmic dissolution. It cannot negate dissolution, since all Vedanta establishes Brahman as cause of creation, maintenance, and dissolution. Therefore here the eater is the Supreme Self alone. (9)

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.2.9 determines attā as Brahman via caracara-totality and saṃhāra-function, while restricting 'non-eating witness' texts to karma-bhoga contexts.

### Interpretive Note
Interprets 'eating' as total withdrawal/dissolution, not sensory consumption.
