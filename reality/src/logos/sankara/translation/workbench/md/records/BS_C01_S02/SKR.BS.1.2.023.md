itaśca paramātmaivehāttā bhavitumarhati ; yatkāraṇaṃ prakaraṇamidaṃ paramātmanaḥ — ‘ na jāyate mriyate vā vipaścit ’ (ka. u. 1 | 2 | 18) ityādi | prakṛtagrahaṇaṃ ca nyāyyam | ‘ ka itthā veda yatra saḥ ’ iti ca durvijñānatvaṃ paramātmaliṅgam || 10 ||

## Translation

### Literal
And for this reason too, the eater here must be the Supreme Self: this whole section concerns the Supreme ('the knower is not born, nor dies,' etc.), and taking the already-in-topic referent is methodologically proper. Also, the statement 'who indeed knows where He is?' indicates difficult knowability, which is a mark of the Supreme. (10)

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.2.10 reinforces the atta-as-Brahman reading by prakarana continuity and durvijnyatva-linga.

### Interpretive Note
