## Translation

### Literal
The two taught here should be the knowing self and the Supreme Self. Why? Both are selves, conscious, and same in kind; in ordinary usage, when two are mentioned, the second is expected from same class. If told 'find a second cow for this cow,' one seeks another cow, not a horse or person. Here, once one member is fixed as conscious knower by the sign of drinking, the second is naturally grasped as another conscious same-kind reality, namely the Supreme. Objection: cave-entry shows it is not Supreme. Reply: cave-reference itself supports Supreme, since shruti and smriti repeatedly describe the Supreme as cave-seated ('hidden in the cave,' 'deposited in the cave in highest ether,' 'seek the self entered into the cave'). Also, teaching a particular locus for realizing even all-pervasive Brahman is non-contradictory, as already explained. Residence in merit-world can be understood for both as in cases like umbrella-designation. 'Shadow and light' too is consistent: samsaric and non-samsaric are mutually distinct like shadow and sunlight; samsaric status is ignorance-made, while non-samsaric status is ultimate. Therefore the pair entered in the cave should be taken as knowing self and Supreme Self. (11)

- **vijnanatma-paramatma**
  - Gloss: individual cognizer and Supreme Self pair
  - Context: Siddhanta pairing accepted for the dual reference in the mantra.
  - Grammar: compound dyad
  - Alternate glosses: jiva-Ishvara pair, knower-Supreme dyad
- **samanasvabhava-dvaya**
  - Gloss: dual reference among same-class entities
  - Context: Principle that dual mention normally picks two of one ontological class (both conscious selves).
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: same-nature duality, class-consistent pairing rule
- **guhahita-pramana**
  - Gloss: scriptural evidence for indwelling in the cave
  - Context: Cited passages support both jiva and Paramatma as heart-cavity occupants.
  - Grammar: compound noun
  - Alternate glosses: cave-indwelling testimony, inner-heart witness text

  - Gloss: interpretation of shade-sun imagery
  - Context: Read as asymmetrical relation within jiva-Paramatma dyad rather than buddhi-jiva pairing.
  - Grammar: compound noun
