### Source Text
कथं पुनराकाशवत्सर्वगतस्य ब्रह्मणोऽक्ष्यल्पं स्थानमुपपद्यत इति , अत्रोच्यते — भवेदेषानवकॢप्तिः , यद्येतदेवैकं स्थानमस्य निर्दिष्टं भवेत् । सन्ति ह्यन्यान्यपि पृथिव्यादीनि स्थानान्यस्य निर्दिष्टानि — ‘ यः पृथिव्यां तिष्ठन् ’ (बृ. उ. ३ । ७ । ३) इत्यादिना । तेषु हि चक्षुरपि निर्दिष्टम् ‘ यश्चक्षुषि तिष्ठन् ’ इति । स्थानादिव्यपदेशादित्यादिग्रहणेनैतद्दर्शयति — न केवलं स्थानमेवैकमनुचितं ब्रह्मणो निर्दिश्यमानं दृश्यते । किं तर्हि ? नाम रूपमित्येवंजातीयकमप्यनामरूपस्य ब्रह्मणोऽनुचितं निर्दिश्यमानं दृश्यते — ‘ तस्योदिति नाम ’ (छा. उ. १ । ६ । ७) ‘ हिरण्यश्मश्रुः ’ इत्यादि । निर्गुणमपि सद्ब्रह्म नामरूपगतैर्गुणैः सगुणमुपासनार्थं तत्र तत्रोपदिश्यत इत्येतदप्युक्तमेव । सर्वगतस्यापि ब्रह्मण उपलब्ध्यर्थं स्थानविशेषो न विरुध्यते , सालग्राम इव विष्णोरित्येतदप्युक्तमेव ॥ १४ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
How can a very small eye-location be fitting for Brahman who is all-pervasive like space? Reply: this objection would stand if that alone were the only location taught. But many other locations are taught too, such as earth and so forth ('who stands in earth...'), and among them eye is also taught ('who stands in the eye'). By saying 'because of designation of place and the like,' the intent is this: it is not only one seemingly unsuitable location that is taught for Brahman. Rather, other seemingly unsuitable designations are also given for the nameless-formless Brahman - names/forms such as 'its name is Uditi,' 'golden-bearded,' etc. The attributeless Brahman is taught in many places with name-form predicates for the sake of upasana; this has already been explained. And even for all-pervasive Brahman, teaching a specific location for realization is not contradictory, like Vishnu in a Salagrama; this too has already been explained. (14)

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.2.14 generalizes the hermeneutic: multiple location/name-form designations are upasana-upaya conventions, not ontological limitations on nirguna Brahman.

### Interpretive Note
Transforms locality-objection into a general rule of pedagogical specification.
