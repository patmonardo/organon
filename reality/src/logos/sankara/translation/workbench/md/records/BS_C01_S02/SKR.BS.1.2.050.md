### Source Text
यदि प्रधान मा त्मत्वद्रष्टृत्वाद्यसम्भवान्नान्तर्याम्यभ्युपगम्यते , शारीरस्तर्ह्यन्तर्यामी भवतु । शारीरो हि चेतनत्वाद्द्रष्टा श्रोता मन्ता विज्ञाता च भवति , आत्मा च प्रत्यक्त्वात् । अमृतश्च , धर्माधर्मफलोपभोगोपपत्तेः । अदृष्टत्वादयश्च धर्माः शारीरे प्रसिद्धाः । दर्शनादिक्रियायाः कर्तरि प्रवृत्तिविरोधात् । ‘ न दृष्टेर्द्रष्टारं पश्येः ’ (बृ. उ. ३ । ४ । २) इत्यादिश्रुतिभ्यश्च । तस्य च कार्यकरणसङ्घातमन्तर्यमयितुं शीलम् , भोक्तृत्वात् । तस्माच्छारीरोऽन्तर्यामीत्यत उत्तरं पठति —

## Translation

### Literal
If Pradhana is not accepted as inner controller due to impossibility of selfhood and seerhood etc., then let the embodied self be inner controller. For the embodied self, being conscious, is seer, hearer, thinker, and knower, and is self by inwardness. It is also immortal, given the intelligibility of experiencing fruits of dharma and adharma. Invisibility and related properties are known for the embodied self too, since perceptual actions cannot operate upon their own agent, and from srutis such as 'you cannot see the seer of seeing.' It is also disposed to regulate the body-sense aggregate from within, because it is the enjoyer. Therefore the embodied self is inner controller. To this, the answer-sutra is read -

### Technical
Purvapaksha before 1.2.20 shifts from Pradhana to jiva: conscious-agent and inward-self predicates are marshaled to claim antaryamin status.

### Interpretive Note
Sets up the jiva-candidate case before its scriptural and structural rejection.
