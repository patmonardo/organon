### Source Text
इतश्च परमेश्वर एव भूतयोनिः , नेतरौ — शारीरः प्रधानं वा । कस्मात् ? विशेषणभेदव्यपदेशाभ्याम् । विशिनष्टि हि प्रकृतं भूतयोनिं शारीराद्विलक्षणत्वेन — ‘ दिव्यो ह्यमूर्तः पुरुषः सबाह्याभ्यन्तरो ह्यजः । अप्राणो ह्यमनाः शुभ्रः ’ (मु. उ. २ । १ । २) इति । न ह्येतद्दिव्यत्वादिविशेषणम् अविद्याप्रत्युपस्थापितनामरूपपरिच्छेदाभिमानिनः तद्धर्मान्स्वात्मनि कल्पयतः शारीरस्योपपद्यते । तस्मात्साक्षादौपनिषदः पुरुष इहोच्यते । तथा प्रधानादपि प्रकृतं भूतयोनिं भेदेन व्यपदिशति — ‘ अक्षरात्परतः परः ’ इति । अक्षरमव्याकृतं नामरूपबीजशक्तिरूपं भूतसूक्ष्ममीश्वराश्रयं तस्यैवोपाधिभूतम् , सर्वस्माद्विकारात्परो यो ऽविकारः , तस्मात्परतः परः इति भेदेन व्यपदिशन् परमात्मानमिह विवक्षितं दर्शयति । नात्र प्रधानं नाम किञ्चित्स्वतन्त्रं तत्त्वमभ्युपगम्य , तस्माद्भेदव्यपदेश उच्यते । किं तर्हि ? यदि प्रधानमपि कल्प्यमानं श्रुत्यविरोधेनाव्याकृतादिशब्दवाच्यं भूतसूक्ष्मं परिकल्प्येत , परिकल्प्यताम् । तस्माद्भेदव्यपदेशात् परमेश्वरो भूतयोनिरित्येतदिह प्रतिपाद्यते ॥ २२ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
For this reason too, only the Supreme Lord is the source of beings, not the other two - embodied self or Pradhana. Why? From qualifying attributes and declarations of distinction. The text qualifies the discussed source of beings as distinct from embodied self: 'for that divine, formless Person, both within and without, unborn, without breath, without mind, pure.' Such divine qualifiers do not fit the embodied self that, through ignorance, superimposes body-name-form limits and their properties upon itself. Therefore the directly Upanishadic Person is taught here. Likewise it declares the discussed source distinct even from Pradhana: 'higher than the imperishable is the highest.' 'Imperishable' here means the unmanifest seed-power of name and form, subtle being-element, dependent on Ishvara and serving as His adjunct. By distinguishing the Supreme Self as beyond that changeless principle, beyond all modifications, the text shows its intent is Paramatman. No independent Sankhya Pradhana is admitted here as a separate ultimate. If one still imagines some unmanifest subtle principle denoted by terms like avyakrta without opposing sruti, let it be imagined; even then, by declaration of distinction from it, the teaching here is that the source of beings is the Supreme Lord. (22)

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.2.22 confirms bhuta-yoni as Brahman via visesana and bheda-vyapadesha: textually marked transcendence over jiva and over akshara/avyakrta adjunct-principle.

### Interpretive Note
Differentiating predicates and 'beyond-the-imperishable' language block both jiva and Pradhana readings.
