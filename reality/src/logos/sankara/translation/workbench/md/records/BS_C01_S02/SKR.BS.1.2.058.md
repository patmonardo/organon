## Translation

### Literal
Others, however, think: this is not a form-presentation of the source of beings, because the sequence presents things as born. Earlier it specified prana through earth as generated; later too it will continue generation language ('from Him fire...') through herbs and essence. Why suddenly insert in the middle a form-description of the source? Even all-selfhood will be taught later after finishing creation ('the Person alone is this all, action...'). Sruti and smrti also describe birth etc. of Prajapati with the three-world body ('Hiranyagarbha arose at first...'; 'that embodied first one is called Purusha...'). Even the effected cosmic person may be inner self of all beings by abiding as life-principle in all. On this view, passages such as 'the Person alone is this all' should be interpreted instead as establishing the Supreme Lord.

### Technical
Alternative reading before 1.2.23 resists immediate rupa-upanyasa by treating the section as uninterrupted cosmogenesis and reallocating universal-form predicates to Hiranyagarbha/vikara-purusha.

### Interpretive Note
Records the rival interpretive strategy that delays Brahman-identification.
