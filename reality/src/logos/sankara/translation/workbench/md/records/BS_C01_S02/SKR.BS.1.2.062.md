## Translation

### Literal
It is said: Vaishvanara ought to be the Supreme Self. Why? Because of specification of common words. The two terms 'self' and 'Vaishvanara' are indeed common words - Vaishvanara common to three, self common to two - yet a specific determination appears by which both are accepted as referring to the Supreme Lord: 'of this Vaishvanara self, heaven is head...' etc. Here the Supreme Lord alone, qualified by heaven-as-head and so on, is presented as inward self in another mode for meditation, because He is cause. For the cause can stand in all states found in effects; hence having heaven-world and others as limbs is fitting. Also the fruit heard - 'he eats food in all worlds, all beings, all selves' - fits only when the supreme cause is taken. And hearing that all sins of the knower are burned, and the opening with self/Brahman terms ('Who is our self? what is Brahman?'), all these indicators point only to the Supreme Lord. Therefore Vaishvanara is indeed the Supreme Lord. (24)

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.2.24 resolves Vaishvanara polysemy via sadharana-sabda-visesa: cosmic-limb specification, Brahmanic fruits, and opening brahman-atman markers establish Ishvara-reference.
