### Source Text
अत्राह — न परमेश्वरो वैश्वानरो भवितुमर्हति । कुतः ? शब्दादिभ्योऽन्तःप्रतिष्ठानाच्च । शब्दस्तावत् — वैश्वानरशब्दो न परमेश्वरे सम्भवति , अर्थान्तरे रूढत्वात् । तथाग्निशब्दः ‘ स एषोऽग्निर्वैश्वानरः ’ इति । आदिशब्दात् ‘ हृदयं गार्हपत्यः ’ (छा. उ. ५ । १८ । २) इत्याद्यग्नित्रेताप्रकल्पनम् ; ‘ तद्यद्भक्तं प्रथममागच्छेत्तद्धोमीयम् ’ (छा. उ. ५ । १९ । १) इत्यादिना च प्राणाहुत्यधिकरणतासङ्कीर्तनम् । एतेभ्यो हेतुभ्यो जाठरो वैश्वानरः प्रत्येतव्यः । त था न्तःप्रतिष्ठानमपि श्रूयते — ‘ पुरुषेऽन्तः प्रतिष्ठितं वेद ’ इति । तच्च जाठरे सम्भवति । यदप्युक्तम् ‘ मूर्धैव सुतेजाः ’ इत्यादेर्विशेषात्कारणात्परमात्मा वैश्वानर इति , अत्र ब्रूमः — कुतो ह्येष निर्णयः , यदुभयथापि विशेषप्रतिभाने सति परमेश्वरविषय एव विशेष आश्रयणीयो न जाठरविषय इति । अथवा भूताग्नेरन्तर्बहिश्चावतिष्ठमानस्यैष निर्देशो भविष्यति । तस्यापि हि द्युलोकादिसम्बन्धो मन्त्रवर्णा दवगम्यते — ‘ यो भानुना पृथिवीं द्यामुतेमामाततान रोदसी अन्तरिक्षम् ’ (ऋ. सं. १० । ८८ । ४) इत्यादौ । अथवा तच्छरीराया देवताया ऐश्वर्ययोगात् द्युलोकाद्यवयवत्वं भविष्यति । तस्मान्न परमेश्वरो वैश्वानर इति ॥

## Translation

### Literal
Here one may object: Vaishvanara cannot be the Supreme Lord. Why? Because of word-usage and related indicators, and because of inner-location. First, the word 'Vaishvanara' is established in other senses, not in the Supreme; likewise 'agni' in 'this indeed is the Vaishvanara fire.' Also by 'and so on': the triple-fire construction such as 'the heart is Garhapatya,' and statements about offering life-breath oblations beginning 'whatever food first arrives is fit for oblation.' From these reasons one should understand digestive fire as Vaishvanara. Further, inner-location is heard: 'know it as established within the person,' which fits digestive fire. As for your claim that specifications like 'heaven is head' require causal Supreme Self, we reply: how is it decided that, though specification appears possible both ways, the specification must be taken as belonging to the Supreme and not to digestive fire? Or the designation might refer to elemental fire present both within and without. Its relation to heaven and so on is also known from mantras. Or perhaps the deity whose body is that fire, by possessing lordly powers, could have heaven and other cosmic limbs. Therefore Vaishvanara is not the Supreme Lord.

### Technical
Purvapaksha before 1.2.26 argues jatharagni primacy from lexical convention, ritual-fire correspondences, and antah-pratisthana, while offering bhutagni/devata alternatives for cosmic-limb predicates.

### Interpretive Note
Objection stacks lexical, ritual, and locative evidence against Ishvara reading.
