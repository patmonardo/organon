### Source Text
यद्यपि प्राणभृतो विज्ञानात्मन आत्मत्वं चेतनत्वं च सम्भवति , तथाप्युपाधिपरिच्छिन्नज्ञानस्य सर्वज्ञत्वाद्यसम्भवे सति अस्मादेवातच्छब्दात् प्राणभृदपि न द्युभ्वाद्यायतनत्वेनाश्रयितव्यः । न चोपाधिपरिच्छिन्नस्याविभोः प्राणभृतो द्युभ्वाद्यायतनत्वमपि सम्यक्संभवति । पृथ ग्योगकरणमुत्तरार्थम् ॥ ४ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
Although in the living knower one may admit selfhood and consciousness, still, because knowledge limited by adjuncts cannot sustain attributes such as omniscience, this same 'not-that-word' reason excludes the embodied self too from being the support of heaven and earth. Also, for the non-all-pervasive embodied self limited by adjuncts, supporthood of heaven and earth is not coherently possible. The separate mention is for a later purpose.

### Technical
Sutra 1.3.4 bhashya extends atachchabda and scope arguments to reject jiva as ayatana: upadhi-limited cognition and non-vibhu status cannot ground cosmic support; separate treatment prepares subsequent discussion.

### Interpretive Note
Refines exclusion: even conscious but limited jiva cannot be ayatana.
