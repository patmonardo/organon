भेदव्यपदेशश्चेह भवति — ‘ तमेवैकं जानथ आत्मानम् ’ इति ज्ञेयज्ञातृभावेन । तत्र प्राणभृत् तावन्मुमुक्षुत्वाज्ज्ञाता । परिशेषादात्मशब्दवाच्यं ब्रह्म ज्ञेयं द्युभ्वाद्यायतनमिति गम्यते , न प्राणभृत् ॥ ५ ॥

### Transliteration (IAST)
bhedavyapadeśaśceha bhavati — ‘ tamevaikaṃ jānatha ātmānam ’ iti jñeyajñātṛbhāvena | tatra prāṇabhṛt tāvanmumukṣutvājjñātā | pariśeṣādātmaśabdavācyaṃ brahma jñeyaṃ dyubhvādyāyatanamiti gamyate , na prāṇabhṛt || 5 ||

## Translation

### Literal
And here there is also explicit statement of difference: 'Know that one alone as the Self' is said in the relation of known and knower. There, the life-bearer, as one desiring liberation, is the knower. By residual elimination it is understood that Brahman, denoted by the word Self, is the knowable support of heaven and earth, not the life-bearer.

### Technical
Sutra 1.3.5 bhashya: jneya-jnatṛ distinction in the Muṇḍaka sentence blocks identity of seeker-jiva with the designated ayatana; parisesa yields Brahman as the object-term.

### Interpretive Note
Uses epistemic role-separation (knower vs known) as referential test.
