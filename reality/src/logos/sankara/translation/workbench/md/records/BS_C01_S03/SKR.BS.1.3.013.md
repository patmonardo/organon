### Source Text
प्रकरणं चेदं परमात्मनः — ‘ कस्मिन्नु भगवो विज्ञाते सर्वमिदं विज्ञातं भवति ’ (मु. उ. १ । १ । ३) इत्येकविज्ञानेन सर्वविज्ञानापेक्षणात् । परमात्मनि हि सर्वात्मके विज्ञाते सर्वमिदं विज्ञातं स्यात् , न केवले प्राणभृति ॥ ६ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
And this whole context concerns the supreme Self: 'Revered sir, by knowing what does all this become known?' There is expectation of all-knowledge through one knowledge. Only when the supreme all-self is known can all this be known, not by knowing merely the life-bearer.

### Technical
Sutra 1.3.6 argument from prakarana: Muṇḍaka's ekavijnanena sarvavijnana frame is explicitly Brahman-oriented and cannot terminate in a finite individual knower.

### Interpretive Note
Context rule: one-knowledge/all-knowledge demand selects Brahman.
