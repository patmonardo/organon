### Source Text
‘ एत द्वै सत्यकाम परं चापरं च ब्रह्म यदोंकारस्तस्माद्विद्वानेतेनैवायतनेनैकतरमन्वेति ’ (प्र. उ. ५ । २) इति प्रकृत्य श्रूयते — ‘ यः पुन रेतं त्रिमात्रेणोमित्येतेनैवाक्षरेण परं पुरुषमभिध्यायीत ’ (प्र. उ. ५ । ५) इति । किम स्मिन्वाक्ये परं ब्रह्माभिध्यातव्यमुपदिश्यते , आहोस्विदपरमिति । एतेनैवायतनेन परमपरं वैकतरमन्वेतीति प्रकृतत्वात्संशयः । तत्रापर मिदं ब्रह्मेति प्राप्तम् । कस्मात् ? ‘ स तेजसि सूर्ये सम्पन्नः ’ ‘ स सामभिरुन्नीयते ब्रह्मलोकम् ’ इति च तद्विदो देशपरिच्छिन्नस्य फलस्योच्यमानत्वात् । न हि परब्रह्मविद्देशपरिच्छिन्नं फलमश्नुवीतेति युक्तम् ; सर्वगतत्वात्परस्य ब्रह्मणः । न न्वपरब्रह्मपरिग्रहे ‘ परं पुरुषम् ’ इति विशेषणं नोपपद्यते । नैष दोषः — पिण्डापेक्षया प्राणस्य परत्वोपपत्तेः ; इत्येवं प्राप्ते , अभिधीयते —

## Translation

### Literal
After introducing 'This Om indeed, O Satyakama, is both higher and lower Brahman; by this support the knower reaches either of the two,' scripture continues, 'He who meditates by this very imperishable Om in three measures on the supreme Person.' In this sentence, is meditation taught on supreme Brahman or on lower? Since the context mentioned both higher and lower attainable through this support, doubt arises. Prima facie, this is lower Brahman. Why? Because the fruits spoken of for this knower are spatially delimited: becoming merged in the light of the sun and being led by Sama hymns to Brahma-loka. It is not fitting that a knower of supreme Brahman should attain a spatially bounded fruit, since supreme Brahman is all-pervasive. One might object that the qualifier 'supreme Person' would then be unsuitable; reply: not necessarily, as prana too can be called superior relative to the body. Thus the prima facie reading stands until answered.

### Technical
Purvapaksha of pranava-upasana section: despite para-purusa language, finite phala descriptions (solar path/Brahma-loka ascent) motivate reading the object as apara-brahman.

### Interpretive Note
Sets up para-vs-apara dispute in Om-based meditation teaching.
