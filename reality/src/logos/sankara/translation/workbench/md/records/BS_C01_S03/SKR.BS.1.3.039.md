### Source Text
धृते श्च हेतोः परमेश्वर एवायं दहरः । कथम् ? ‘ दहरोऽस्मि न्नन्तराकाशः ’ इति हि प्रकृत्य आकाशौपम्यपूर्वकं तस्मिन्सर्वसमाधानमुक्त्वा तस्मिन्नेव चात्मशब्दं प्रयुज्यापहतपाप्मत्वादिगुणयोगं चोपदिश्य तमेवानतिवृत्तप्रकरणं निर्दिशति — ‘ अथ य आत्मा स सेतुर्विधृतिरेषां लोकानामसम्भेदाय ’ (छा. उ. ८ । ४ । १) इति । तत्र विधृतिरित्यात्मशब्दसामानाधिकरण्याद्विधारयितोच्यते ; क्तिचः कर्तरि स्मरणात् । यथोदक सन्तानस्य विधारयिता लोके सेतुः क्षेत्रसम्पदामसम्भेदाय , एवमयमात्मा एषामध्यात्मादिभेदभिन्नानां लोकानां वर्णाश्रमादीनां च विधारयिता सेतुः , असम्भेदाय असङ्करायेति । एवमिह प्रकृते दहरे विधारणलक्षणं महिमानं दर्शयति । अयं च महिमा परमेश्वर एव श्रुत्यन्तरादुपलभ्यते — ‘ एतस्य वा अक्षरस्य प्रशासने गार्गि सूर्याचन्द्रमसौ विधृतौ तिष्ठतः ’ इत्यादेः । त थान्यत्रापि निश्चिते परमेश्वरवाक्ये श्रूयते — ‘ एष सर्वेश्वर एष भूताधिपतिरेष भूतपाल एष सेतुर्विधरण एषां लोकानामसम्भेदाय ’ इति । एवं धृतेश्च हेतोः परमेश्वर एवायं दहरः ॥ १६ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
And from the reason of support, this small one is the supreme Lord. How? Beginning with 'small space within the heart,' after comparing with space and stating all is gathered there, the text applies the word Self and qualities like freedom from sin, then without leaving the topic again states: 'this Self is the bridge, the support of these worlds, for non-confusion.' There 'support' stands in apposition with Self and means supporter. As in ordinary life a bridge supports streams so fields do not mix, so this Self supports worlds differentiated as inner and outer domains, and social orders too, preventing confusion. Thus, in the present dahara topic, the greatness marked by supporting power is shown. This greatness is known elsewhere of the supreme Lord alone, as in 'under the command of this Imperishable sun and moon stand held.' Elsewhere too in a settled Lord-passage: 'this is the Lord of all, ruler of beings, protector of beings, the bridge and support of these worlds for non-confusion.' Therefore from support as reason, this small one is the supreme Lord.

### Technical
Sutra 1.3.16: dhriti-mahima in the dahara section is read as explicit Ishvara-function (world-order maintenance), with cross-upanishadic corroboration; hence dahara cannot be finite locus or inert substrate.

### Interpretive Note
Support-function argument seals the dahara interpretation.
