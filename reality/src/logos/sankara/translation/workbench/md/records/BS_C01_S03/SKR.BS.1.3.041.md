## Translation

### Literal
Further, it is the supreme Lord alone that is called 'the small inner space,' because the word akasha is well-established for the supreme Lord, as seen in usages such as 'Akasha is the bearer of name and form' and 'all beings arise from Akasha.' But nowhere do we see akasha used for the individual soul. As for elemental space: even if that word is commonly used there, it cannot be taken here because features like comparison structure and the rest are impossible for that reading, as already explained.

### Technical
Sutra 1.3.17 bhashya: lexical prasiddhi of akasha in Brahman-sense, plus prior impossibility arguments against bhutakasha, secures dahara as Ishvara.

### Interpretive Note
Word-usage precedence is made explicit as a supporting pramana.
