### Source Text
यदप्युक्तम् — ‘ दहरोऽस्मिन्नन्तराकाशः ’ इत्याकाशस्याल्पत्वं श्रूयमाणं परमेश्वरे नोपपद्यते , जीवस्य तु आराग्रोपमितस्याल्पत्वमवकल्पत इति ; तस्य परिहारो वक्तव्यः । उक्तो ह्यस्य परिहारः — परमेश्वरस्याप्यापेक्षिकमल्पत्वमवकल्पत इति , ‘ अर्भकौकस्त्वात्तद्व्यपदेशाच्च नेति चेन्न निचाय्यत्वादेवं व्योमवच्च ’ (ब्र. सू. १ । २ । ७) इत्यत्र ; स एवेह परिहारोऽनुसन्धातव्य इति सूचयति । श्रुत्यैव च इद मल्पत्वं प्रत्युक्तं प्रसिद्धेनाकाशेनोपमिमानया ‘ यावान्वा अयमाकाशस्तावानेषोऽन्तर्हृदय आकाशः ’ इति ॥ २१ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
As was objected: hearing smallness in 'the small inner space' does not fit the supreme Lord, while it can fit the individual soul likened to a tiny point. This must be answered. That answer has already been given - even for the supreme Lord a relative smallness can be admitted - in the earlier discussion ('not so... because to be determined thus, as with space'). The same resolution should be carried forward here. And scripture itself rebuts that smallness objection by comparing with the well-known vast space: 'as great as this space is that inner-heart space.'

### Technical
Sutra 1.3.21 reuses prior hermeneutic: dahara-alpatva is pedagogical/relative, not an ontic size-constraint; explicit upamana with vast space neutralizes jiva-only inference.

### Interpretive Note
Cross-adhikarana continuity: earlier alpatva solution is imported here.
