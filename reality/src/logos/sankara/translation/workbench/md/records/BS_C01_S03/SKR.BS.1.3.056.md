## Translation

### Literal
It is heard: 'The person of thumb-size stands in the middle within the Self,' and also, 'The thumb-sized person, like a smokeless light, lord of past and future - this indeed is That.' Concerning this thumb-sized person, doubt arises: is it the cognitive individual self, or the supreme Self? Prima facie it is the individual self, because a size-instruction is given. Such thumb-size cannot fit the infinite supreme Self. But for the cognitive self, due to adjunct-conditioning, some constructed thumb-size can be admitted. Smriti too says Yama forcefully drew out a thumb-sized person bound by noose from Satyavan's body. The supreme Lord cannot be extracted by Yama's force; hence that case certainly concerns the transmigrating individual. So one may initially carry the same reading here.

### Technical
Opening purvapaksha for angushtha-matra adhikarana: finite-measure language and smriti narrative are marshaled to support jiva-reading against para-brahman interpretation.
