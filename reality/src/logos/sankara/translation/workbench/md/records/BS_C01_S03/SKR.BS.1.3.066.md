मा नाम विग्रहवत्त्वे देवादीनामभ्युपगम्यमाने कर्मणि कश्चिद्विरोधः प्रसञ्जि । शब्दे तु विरोधः प्रसज्येत । कथम् ? औत्पत्तिकं हि शब्दस्यार्थेन सम्बन्धमाश्रित्य ‘ अनपेक्षत्वात् ’ इति वेदस्य प्रामाण्यं स्थापितम् । इदानीं तु विग्रहवती देवताभ्युपगम्यमाना यद्यप्यैश्वर्ययोगाद्युगपदनेककर्मसम्बन्धीनि हवींषि भुञ्जीत , तथापि विग्रहयोगादस्मदादिवज्जननमरणवती सेति , नित्यस्य शब्दस्य नित्येनार्थेन नित्ये सम्बन्धे प्रतीयमाने यद्वैदिके शब्दे प्रामाण्यं स्थितम् , तस्य विरोधः स्यादिति चेत् , नायम प्यस्ति विरोधः । कस्मात् ? अतः प्रभवात् । अत एव हि वैदिकाच्छब्दाद्देवादिकं जगत्प्रभवति ॥

## Translation

### Literal
Grant that no contradiction arises in ritual if embodied gods are admitted. Yet contradiction may arise in language. How? Vedic authority was established by relying on an original relation between word and meaning and on non-dependence. If embodied deities are admitted, they may indeed, through divine power, receive many offerings at once; still, being embodied, they would, like us, be subject to birth and death. Then the authority of eternal Vedic words, grounded in an eternal relation to eternal meanings, would be undermined. Reply: this contradiction too does not arise. Why? Because beings arise from that very word. From Vedic word itself the world of gods and others comes forth.

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.3.28 opens the shabda-virodha objection: if deities are contingent individuals, eternal word-meaning relation seems threatened; reply introduces veda-shabda-prabhava as reconciliation.

### Interpretive Note
Shifts dispute from ritual compatibility to semantic-ontological grounding.
