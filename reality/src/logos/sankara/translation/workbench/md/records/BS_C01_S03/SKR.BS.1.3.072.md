### Source Text
स्वतन्त्रस्य कर्तु रस्मरणादिभिः स्थिते वेदस्य नित्यत्वे देवादिव्यक्तिप्रभवाभ्युपगमेन तस्य विरोधमाशङ्क्य ‘ अतः प्रभवात् ’ इति परिहृत्य इदानीं तदेव वेदनित्यत्वं स्थितं द्रढयति — अत एव च नित्यत्वमिति । अत एव नियताकृतेर्देवादेर्जगतो वेदशब्दप्रभवत्वात् वेदशब्दनित्यत्वमपि प्रत्येतव्यम् । तथा च मन्त्रवर्णः — ‘ यज्ञेन वाचः पदवीयमायन् तामन्वविन्दन्नृषिषु प्रविष्टाम् ’ (ऋ. सं. १० । ७ । ३) इति स्थितामेव वाचमनुविन्नां दर्शयति । वेदव्यासश्चैवमेव स्मरति — ‘ युगान्तेऽन्तर्हितान्वेदान्सेतिहासान्महर्षयः । लेभिरे तपसा पूर्वमनुज्ञाताः स्वयंभुवा ’ इति ॥ २९ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
Having accepted Veda's eternality on grounds such as non-remembrance of any independent author, and after removing the possible contradiction from admitting production of divine individuals through 'from that arises,' the text now strengthens that same established Veda-eternality: 'and therefore also eternality.' Precisely because the world of gods and others of determinate forms arises from Vedic word, eternality of Vedic word should also be understood. The mantra line 'by sacrifice they followed the track of speech and found her entered into the seers' shows speech as already existing and then discovered. Vyasa too recalls this: at the end of the age, when Vedas and Itihasas were hidden, great seers regained them through tapas, permitted by the Self-born. (29)

### Technical
Transition to 1.3.29: argument tightens from shabda-prabhava to explicit veda-nityatva, supported by recovered-not-produced speech motif in shruti and smriti.

### Interpretive Note
Moves from defensive compatibility argument to affirmative eternality thesis.
