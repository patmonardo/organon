## Translation

### Literal
When words denoting deities such as Sun are used, they are used with reference to that luminous sphere in heaven that revolves through day and night and illumines the world, as known from common usage and the continuation of scriptural sentence-context. And that luminous sphere cannot be understood as possessing an embodied interiority (heart, etc.), consciousness, desire, and the like; it is known to be insentient like clay and similar objects. The same has already been explained for fire and others.

### Technical
Jaimini-side reinforcement: many deva-terms in upasana passages denote insentient astronomical/elemental loci by primary usage, undermining claims of embodied sentient deity-subjects for those contexts.

### Interpretive Note
Pushes semantic literalism toward non-agentive referents.
