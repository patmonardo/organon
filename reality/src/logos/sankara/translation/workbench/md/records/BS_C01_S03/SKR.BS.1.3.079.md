## Translation

### Literal
One might reply: there is no fault, because embodiment and similar attributes of gods are known from mantra, arthavada, itihasa, purana, and common tradition. Reply: no. 'Common tradition' is not an independent pramana. What is called popularly known is only what is known through already operative pramanas like perception, without unsettled contrary specification. Here no such pramana is available. Itihasa and purana, being authored texts, require grounding in another pramana. Arthavadas, being sentence-united with injunction and serving praise-function, do not independently establish embodiment of gods as separate factual ground. Mantras too, assigned by sruti and used within ritual application for denotation, are said not to function as independent pramana for arbitrary extra assertions. Therefore, gods and others lack eligibility.

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.3.32 tightens the epistemic filter: loka/itihasa/purana/arthavada/mantra are denied independent probative force for deva-vigraha claims in this context, yielding anadhikara conclusion.

### Interpretive Note
Epistemology is used to undercut ontological premises of deva-eligibility.
