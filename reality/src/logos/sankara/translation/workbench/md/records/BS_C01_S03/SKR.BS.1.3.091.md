## Translation

### Literal
And for this reason too there is no eligibility for the Shudra: in sections teaching vidya, rites such as initiation are referred to - 'he initiated him,' 'approach, sir, teach me,' and 'those intent on Brahman, with fuel in hand, approached Pippalada.' Even the line 'he taught only after initiating them' shows initiation as prerequisite. And absence of such sacraments for the Shudra is stated, including remembrance that the fourth varna is single-born, and statements such as 'there is no sacrament for the Shudra.' (36)

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.3.36 argues anadhikara from saṃskāra-preconditions (especially upanayana) explicitly tied to vidya entry in shruti and denied in smriti for Shudra status.

### Interpretive Note
Procedural gatekeeping argument: vidya access is linked to initiation structure.
