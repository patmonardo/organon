## Translation

### Literal
And for this reason too there is no eligibility for the Shudra: smriti lays down prohibition regarding hearing, study, and application of Veda for him. Prohibition of hearing is stated, as are statements against Vedic recitation in proximity and punitive rules regarding utterance and retention. From that follows prohibition of study: one forbidden even proximity to recitation cannot study what he has not heard. From that further follows prohibition of Vedic meaning-knowledge and its ritual implementation, supported by rules such as not imparting Vedic counsel to Shudra and by restricting study/sacrifice/gift to twice-born groups. Yet where knowledge arises through prior-life saṃskāra - as in cases like Vidura and Dharmavyadha - the resulting fruit cannot be denied, because knowledge has determinate fruit. Also, because Itihasa-Purana study is remembered as available to all four varnas, access there is acknowledged. The settled conclusion is: Shudras have no eligibility in what is Veda-based.

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.3.38 distinguishes Veda-mediated adhikara (denied) from possible non-Vedic knowledge channels and Itihasa-Purana access (admitted), while preserving the core anadhikara claim.

### Interpretive Note
Strong smriti-based prohibition argument with a narrow exception-space outside Veda-study framework.
