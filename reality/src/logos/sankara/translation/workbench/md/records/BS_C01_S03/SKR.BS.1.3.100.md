## Translation

### Literal
The text says: 'This serene one, rising from this body, attaining the supreme light, manifests in its own form.' Here doubt arises: does 'light' denote ordinary visible light that dispels darkness, or the Supreme Brahman? Prima facie one may take ordinary light, because that is the established lexical sense of jyotis. In another case ('because jyotis and quarters are mentioned') jyotis is read as Brahman only by force of context after giving up its primary sense. Here no such reason for abandoning primary sense appears. Also in the nadi section it is said that the seeker ascends upward through rays and reaches the sun. Therefore one may conclude that jyotis here means ordinary light. To that we reply -

Opening purvapaksha for 1.3.40 frames para-jyotis either as physical luminosity or Brahman, pressing lexical default and nadi/rays ascent evidence for the former.
