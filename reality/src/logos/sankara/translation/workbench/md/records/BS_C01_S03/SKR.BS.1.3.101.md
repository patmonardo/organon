## Translation

### Literal
The word jyotis here denotes the Supreme Brahman alone. Why? Because the section shows continuous reference to that topic. At the beginning, the Self qualified by freedom from sin and similar attributes is pledged as what is to be sought and known. The text then reconnects, 'I shall explain that very one again.' It also says attainment of this jyotis leads to bodilessness ('being bodiless, one is not touched by pleasant and unpleasant'), and such absolute bodilessness is not coherent apart from Brahman-realization. The qualifiers too are decisive: 'supreme light' and 'that is the highest person.' As for the claim that sun-attainment is taught for the seeker, that is not final liberation, since it belongs to a context of path and departure; we shall explain that in final liberation there is no going or departing.

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.3.40 establishes para-jyotis as Brahman from prakarana-continuity, anuvyakhyana linkage, aśarīratva consequence, and uttama-puruṣa qualifiers; solar attainment belongs to lower/mediate soteriological pathing.

### Interpretive Note
Final criterion is soteriological: only Brahman reading sustains absolute bodiless freedom.
