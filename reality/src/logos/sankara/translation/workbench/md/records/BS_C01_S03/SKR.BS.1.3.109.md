## Translation

### Literal
And for this reason too this passage should be understood as teaching the non-samsaric nature: in this passage words such as 'lord' and related terms positively teach non-samsaric nature and negate samsaric traits. Expressions like 'controller of all, ruler of all, overlord of all' present the non-samsaric nature. Expressions like 'he is not greater by good action nor smaller by bad action' negate the defining marks of transmigrating existence. Therefore it is understood that the non-samsaric Supreme Lord is what is taught here. (43)

### Technical
Bhashya on 1.3.43 adds patyadi-shabda evidence: sovereignty predicates and karma-transcendence clauses signal Ishvara, not conditioned jiva.
