### Source Text
ब्रह्म जिज्ञासां प्रतिज्ञाय ब्रह्मणो लक्षणमुक्तम् — ‘ जन्माद्यस्य यतः ’ (ब्र. सू. १ । १ । २) इति । तल्लक्षणं प्रधानस्यापि समानमित्याशङ्क्य तदशब्दत्वेन निराकृतम् — ‘ ईक्षतेर्नाशब्दम् ’ (ब्र. सू. १ । १ । ५) इति । गतिसामान्यं च वेदान्तवाक्यानां ब्रह्मकारणवादं प्रति विद्यते , न प्रधानकारणवादं प्रतीति प्रपञ्चितं गतेन ग्रन्थेन । इदं त्विदानीमवशिष्टमाशङ्क्यते — यदुक्तं प्रधानस्याशब्दत्वम् , तदसिद्धम् , कासुचिच्छाखासु प्रधानसमर्पणाभासानां शब्दानां श्रूयमाणत्वात् । अतः प्रधानस्य कारणत्वं वेदसिद्धमेव महद्भिः परमर्षिभिः कपिलप्रभृतिभिः परिगृहीतमिति प्रसज्यते । तद्यावत्तेषां शब्दानामन्यपरत्वं न प्रतिपाद्यते , तावत्सर्वज्ञं ब्रह्म जगतः कारणमिति प्रतिपादितमप्याकुलीभवेत् । अतस्तेषामन्यपरत्वं दर्शयितुं परः सन्दर्भः प्रवर्तते ॥

## Translation

### Literal
Having announced inquiry into Brahman, the characteristic of Brahman was given as "that from which origin and so on proceed". Doubting that this mark might equally fit Pradhana, it was rejected as non-scriptural by the aphorism "not non-scriptural, because of seeing". It was also shown that the general movement of Vedanta sentences supports Brahman as cause, not Pradhana. Yet one residual doubt is now raised: in some recensions words seem to indicate Pradhana; if so, Pradhana's causality could appear Veda-established and sanctioned by sages such as Kapila. Until those words are shown to have another purport, even the established conclusion that omniscient Brahman is world-cause would be unsettled. Therefore the next context proceeds to show that those terms intend something else.

### Technical
Introductory framing for the avyakta-adhikarana: the bhashya recalls earlier anti-pradhana conclusions and identifies the remaining hermeneutic pressure point, namely seeming scriptural support in branch-specific wording. The task is to prove anyaparatva of those terms so Brahman-causality remains intact.
