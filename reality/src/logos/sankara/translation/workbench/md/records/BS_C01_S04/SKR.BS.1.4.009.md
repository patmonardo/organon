### Source Text
ज्ञेयत्वेन च साङ्ख्यैः प्रधानं स्मर्यते , गुणपुरुषान्तरज्ञानात्कैवल्यमिति वदद्भिः — न हि गुणस्वरूपमज्ञात्वा गुणेभ्यः पुरुषस्यान्तरं शक्यं ज्ञातुमिति । क्वचिच्च विभूतिविशेषप्राप्तये प्रधानं ज्ञेयमिति स्मरन्ति । न चेदमिहाव्यक्तं ज्ञेयत्वेनोच्यते । पदमात्रं ह्यव्यक्तशब्दः , नेहाव्यक्तं ज्ञातव्यमुपासितव्यं चेति वाक्यमस्ति । न चानुपदिष्टं पदार्थज्ञानं पुरुषार्थमिति शक्यं प्रतिपत्तुम् । तस्मा दपि नाव्यक्तशब्देन प्रधानमभिधीयते । अस्माकं तु रथरूपककॢप्तशरीराद्यनुसरणेन विष्णोरेव परमं पदं दर्शयितुमयमुपन्यास इत्यनवद्यम् ॥ ४ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
Sankhyas also remember Pradhana as an object to be known, saying liberation comes through knowledge of distinction between gunas and purusa; and elsewhere they present Pradhana as knowable for attainment of special powers. But here this "unmanifest" is not taught as an object to be known. The word occurs only as a term in sequence; there is no statement here that the unmanifest should be known or meditated upon. Nor can one claim an un-taught object-knowledge as a valid human end. Therefore too, "unmanifest" here does not denote Pradhana. For us, this whole presentation follows the chariot-body sequence in order to show precisely Vishnu's supreme state; thus it is faultless.

### Technical
Final pramana filter: absence of explicit vidhi/upadesa for knowing or worshipping avyakta blocks Sankhya epistemic import. The passage is pedagogically teleological (toward parama-pada), not ontologically pradhana-instructional.

### Interpretive Note
Concludes by denying jneyatva-based Sankhya appropriation and reaffirming Vedantic soteriological intent.
