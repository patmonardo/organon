नानेन मन्त्रेण श्रुतिमत्त्वं साङ्ख्यवादस्य शक्यमाश्रयितुम् । न ह्ययं मन्त्रः स्वातन्त्र्येण कञ्चिदपि वादं समर्थयितुमुत्सहते । सर्वत्रापि यया कयाचित्कल्पनया अजात्वादिसम्पादनोपपत्तेः , साङ्ख्यवाद एवेहाभिप्रेत इति विशेषावधारणकारणाभावात् । चमसवत् — यथा हि ‘ अर्वाग्बिलश्चमस ऊर्ध्वबुध्नः ’ (बृ. उ. २ । २ । ३) इत्यस्मिन्मन्त्रे स्वातन्त्र्येणायं नामासौ चमसोऽभिप्रेत इति न शक्यते नियन्तुम् , सर्वत्रापि यथाकथञ्चिदर्वाग्बिलत्वादिकल्पनोपपत्तेः , एवमिहाप्यविशेषः ‘ अजामेकाम् ’ इत्यस्य मन्त्रस्य । नास्मिन्मन्त्रे प्रधानमेवाजाभिप्रेतेति शक्यते नियन्तुम् ॥ ८ ॥

## Translation

### Literal
This mantra cannot be used to establish scriptural authority for Sankhya doctrine. The mantra is not capable, by independent force, of securing any one doctrine. Since by various constructions one can make notions like 'unborn' fit in different ways, there is no decisive ground to fix that Sankhya alone is intended here. It is like the 'camasa' passage: just as in 'a cup with opening below and base above' one cannot independently determine this or that specific cup as intended, because such features can be variously imagined, so too here there is no specificity in the mantra 'one unborn.' Therefore one cannot determine that this mantra intends pradhana alone as aja. (8-bhashya)

### Technical
Siddhanta denies standalone doctrinal determinacy of the Svetasvatara mantra. A mantra lacking decisive contextual constraints cannot function as exclusive support for a system-ontology when multiple semantic constructions are possible. The 'camasa' analogy formalizes this underdetermination: descriptive predicates without restricting markers do not yield fixed referent identity. Hence the aja-mantra cannot be monopolized as pradhana-proof; Sankhya's claim to unique shruti-basis remains unestablished.

### Interpretive Note
Refutation via semantic underdetermination: polyvalent imagery cannot bear exclusive metaphysical assignment.
