kathaṃ punastejobannānāṃ trairūpyeṇa trirūpā ajā pratipattuṃ śakyate , yāvatā na tāvattejobanneṣvajākṛtirasti , na ca tejobannānāṃ jātiśravaṇādajātinimitto'pyajāśabdaḥ sambhavatīti ; ata uttaraṃ paṭhati —

## Translation

### Literal
How can the threefold aja be understood through the threefoldness of fire, water, and food, when those elements do not literally have goat-form, and when the word aja cannot arise from hearing a species-name for those elements? To this, the answer is now read.

### Technical
Leading objection before sutra 1.4.10: challenges literal eligibility of aja-predication for the elemental triad, forcing an account of non-literal semantic transfer.

### Interpretive Note
Sets up the kalpana-upadesa solution by first exhausting literal options.
