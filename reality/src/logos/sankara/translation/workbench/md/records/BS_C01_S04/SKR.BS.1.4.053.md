अस्त्यत्र प्रतिज्ञा — ‘ आत्मनि विज्ञाते सर्वमिदं विज्ञातं भवति ’ ‘ इदꣳ सर्वं यदयमात्मा ’ इति च । तस्याः प्रतिज्ञायाः सिद्धिं सूचयत्येतल्लिङ्गम् , यत्प्रियसंसूचितस्यात्मनो द्रष्टव्यत्वादिसङ्कीर्तनम् । यदि हि विज्ञानात्मा परमात्मनोऽन्यः स्यात् , ततः परमात्मविज्ञानेऽपि विज्ञानात्मा न विज्ञात इत्येकविज्ञानेन सर्वविज्ञानं यत्प्रतिज्ञातम् , तद्धीयेत । तस्मात्प्रतिज्ञासिद्ध्यर्थं विज्ञानात्मपरमात्मनोरभेदांशेनोपक्रमणमित्याश्मरथ्य आचार्यो मन्यते ॥ २० ॥

## Translation

### Literal
There is a standing proposition here: 'when the self is known, all this becomes known,' and also 'all this is that self.' This proposition's fulfillment is indicated by the mark that the self introduced through dearness is taught as to-be-seen, heard, reflected upon, and meditated upon. If this were only the empirical conscious self, the universal-knowledge claim would fail. Therefore the linga here supports the proposition's completion at the level of the supreme self.

### Technical
Ashmarathya's line emphasizes pratijna-siddhi: interpretive markers (linga) must be read so that the text's explicit epistemic vow (atma-vijnana -> sarva-vijnana) is satisfiable. Hence priya-upakrama references cannot be frozen at a narrow empirical subject; they are elevated to Brahman-level scope to preserve proposition coherence.

### Interpretive Note
Doctrinal control principle: no linga reading is valid if it breaks the text's declared proposition.
