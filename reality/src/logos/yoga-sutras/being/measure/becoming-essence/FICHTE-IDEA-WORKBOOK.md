## The Bhasya: The Principle of Methodological Self-Erasure

Sūtras I.50 and I.51 describe the dramatic, final collapse of the entire first chapter of Yoga: the truth-bearing insight acts as a massive inhibitor (_pratibandhī_) burning away discursive thought, but then the inhibitor itself must playfully cease (_tasyāpi nirodhe_), plunging the mind into seedless absorption (_nirbīja_). Johann Gottlieb Fichte’s _Science of Knowing_ provides the brilliant philosophical _Bhāṣya_ for this terrifying double-move: it maps precisely to the weaponization, and ultimate self-erasure, of the Methodological Maxim.

Fichte recognized that normal human consciousness is highly resistant to truth; it constantly fights to return to dead objects (the Spurious Infinity / Ratio). To combat this, the philosopher deploys the "Methodological Maxim"—a strict, unforgiving philosophical rule demanding the genetic deduction of the Living Act. This rule acts exactly as the _pratibandhī_: it violently inhibits and shuts down all lower, lazy forms of factual thinking. It forces consciousness into the white-hot intensity of Real Measure.

But Fichte insists on the ultimate paradox of philosophy: the science of Knowing is only a ladder. It is a formal, structural method. If the philosopher stands at the top of the ladder and worships the ladder, they are still "with seed." The method must not become a new idol.

Therefore, Sūtra I.51 represents Fichte's demand that the Methodological Maxim must consume itself. Once the Living _Esse_ is reached, the rigorous rules, deductions, and philosophical posturing must be dropped. The "Science" must cease (_tasyāpi nirodhe_). When the formal structure of Knowing collapses entirely, what remains is not an empty, dead void, but the immediate, unutterable life of True Religion. The pure Light rests entirely in itself without needing a philosophical method to sustain it. The Doctrine of Being collapses, and the unfathomable depths of Essence open.
