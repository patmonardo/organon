### Entry heg-i-050-051 — The Derivative Comment: The Critique of Nibbana and the Transition to Essence

Span:

- sourceFile: `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex` (Lines 567-573, 644)
- reference: Hegel's Doctrine of Being (Derivative Commentary on Nirbīja Samādhi)

Summary:
Acting as the derivative comment on Sūtras I.50-51, Hegel provides a severe structural critique of the Indian concept of Cessation (_nirodhe_) and Seedless Absorption (_nirbīja_). Patañjali declares the ultimate goal is the cessation of the inhibiting truth itself, collapsing all mental structures into an objectless state. Hegel explicitly tackles this Oriental drive toward absolute cessation (Nibbana) in the source text.

Hegel writes: "the highest end for man is to sink into unconsciousness, into unity with Brahma, annihilation; the Buddhist Nirvana, Nibbana, etc... a sinking of all content into an only formal unity void of content." Hegel critiques the _manner_ in which the Oriental mind interprets this collapse. To an un-dialectical mind, _sarva-nirodhān_ (cessation of all) looks like pure, empty annihilation. However, Hegel argues the true logical reality of this collapse is not dead emptiness, but rather the sublation of the immediate surface of Being into the reflective depth of Essence. He concludes: "Measure, as we have just seen, has sublated itself; its determinateness... has passed over into essence" (Line 644).

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Critique of Annihilationism (Nibbana):** Hegel directly addresses the Oriental goal of absolute cessation, identifying the risk of interpreting "seedless absorption" as merely sinking into a contentless void.
- k2. **The Formal Unity Void of Content:** If the mind merely drops Measure without dialectical sublation, it falls back into the empty Nothingness of Sūtra I.41, mistaking annihilation for liberation.
- k3. **The Sublation of Measure:** The true meaning of "tasyāpi nirodhe" (the cessation of even the truth-bearing insight). The immediate structure of Being logically destroys itself because its surface cannot hold the absolute contradiction of the Infinite.
- k4. **Transition to Essence:** The formal exit from the Doctrine of Being. The surface of Real Measure is shattered, and the logic plunges into the subterranean, mediated, reflective world of Essence.
