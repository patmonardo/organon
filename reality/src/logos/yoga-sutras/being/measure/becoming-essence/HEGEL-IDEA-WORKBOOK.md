## The Varttika: The Critique of Nibbana and the Descent into Essence

While Fichte provides the governing philosophical principle (the philosopher's brave decision to drop the methodological ladder), Hegel supplies the _Varttika_—the massive, transcendental critique of exactly _what happens_ when the ladder falls.

Patañjali concludes the Doctrine of Being (and the first chapter of the Sūtras) with a staggering command. The yogi must not only drop the false, quantitative world; they must drop the absolute, truth-bearing insight itself. "_Tasyāpi nirodhe sarva-nirodhān nirbījaḥ samādhiḥ_" (Upon the cessation of even that... there is seedless absorption).

Hegel’s _Science of Logic_ (from the extracted text in `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex`) zeroes in precisely on this Oriental drive to totally annihilate the structures of consciousness. He notes that the highest religious goal of the East "is to sink into unconsciousness, into unity with Brahma, annihilation; the Buddhist Nirvana, Nibbana, etc."

Hegel issues a profound dialectical warning here. If _nirbīja samādhi_ is simply the deletion of all content until you hit a blank wall, it is a regression. It is just a "sinking of all content into an only formal unity void of content." If the yogi simply turns off the lights, they have not achieved the Absolute; they have practically returned to the empty, naive Void of Sūtra I.41.

But Hegel also provides the true dialectical reading of Patañjali's triumph. The collapse of Measure is not an arbitrary choice to go to sleep; it is a logical inevitability. The immediate surface of reality (Being/Measure) simply cannot hold the massive contradiction of the Absolute. Real Measure must shatter itself.

Therefore, when the "inhibitor inhibits itself," it is not annihilation; it is _sublation_. Hegel triumphantly concludes: "Measure, as we have just seen, has sublated itself; its determinateness... has passed over into essence."

The seedless state is not a dead end. It is the violent rupture of the surface of Being, plunging the yogi into the dark, reflective, subterranean depths of **Essence** (the second major division of the Logic). The Doctrine of Being is complete, and the long, terrifying journey through Essence begins.
