## Narrative Synthesis: The Self-Consuming Fire

Sūtras I.48 and I.49 established _ṛtaṃbharā prajñā_ (truth-bearing insight) as the absolute peak of the Doctrine of Being. The yogi grasped the living, specific reality of the universe (Real Measure). But in Sūtras I.50 and I.51, Patañjali reveals that even this magnificent achievement must be destroyed to achieve true liberation.

In I.50, Patañjali explains the immediate effect of truth-bearing insight: _taj-jaḥ saṃskāro 'nya-saṃskāra-pratibandhī_. The living experience of absolute truth creates a massive, overpowering momentum (_saṃskāra_) in the mind. This momentum functions as a universal "inhibitor" (_pratibandhī_). It is a fire that burns away all other lingering traces of ordinary, discursive thought. The yogi can no longer fall back into arguing about words (I.42) or getting trapped in quantitative math (I.46). Real Measure blocks the return of finite Existence.

However, Patañjali issues his final edict in I.51: _tasyāpi nirodhe sarva-nirodhān nirbījaḥ samādhiḥ_.

Even though the "inhibitor" is a divine, truth-bearing fire, it is still a structural _thing_. It is still a state of immediate Being. To be truly free, the yogi must not cling even to the experience of truth. The inhibitor must inhibit itself. The fire must run out of fuel and extinguish itself.

When "even that" ceases, absolute cessation (_sarva-nirodhān_) occurs. There are no more objects, no more limits, no more measures. The mind enters _Nirbīja Samādhi_ (Seedless Absorption). This is the absolute end of the Sūtras' first chapter (Samādhi-pāda), and logically, the absolute end of the Doctrine of Being. The immediate surface of reality has entirely collapsed, forcing consciousness into the deep, subterranean, reflective domain of **Essence**.
