### Entry fic-i-048-049 — The Genetic Deduction and the Living Act

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_48_49.ts`
- reference: Fichte's Doctrine of Being (Higher Commentary on Ṛtaṃbharā Prajñā)

Summary:
Acting as the higher comment on Sūtras I.48-49, Fichte's _Science of Knowing_ (1804) maps truth-bearing insight to the philosopher's mastery of the Genetic Deduction over factical inference. For Fichte, ordinary consciousness relies on inference and dead historical facts (precisely Patañjali's _anumāna_ and _śruta_). Inference assumes that reality consists of pre-existing, dead objects (the Not-I), and thought merely tries to link them together rhetorically.

_Ṛtaṃbharā prajñā_ translates to Fichte's requirement that the philosopher must not rely on history or logic. The philosopher must perform a _Genetic Deduction_, witnessing the absolute Living Act of Knowing as it constructs reality in real-time. This is why the object is specific (_viśeṣa-arthatvāt_): Knowing does not apprehend abstract shadows or classes; it brilliantly grasps its own specific, vital, present action. The Living _Esse_ replaces dead conceptuality.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Genetic Deduction (Ṛtaṃbharā Prajñā):** The requirement to witness the generative Act of the Absolute, yielding immediate truth rather than abstract rhetorical conclusions.
- k2. **Rejection of Dead Facticity (Śruta/Anumāna):** Fichte ruthlessly dismisses formal logic and historical fact (hearsay) as dead husks left behind by the Living Light.
- k3. **The Living Act:** The absolute "Specific" (_viśeṣa_) is not a tiny particular object, but the specific, concrete living reality of the _Esse_ producing itself.
- k4. **Translation into Real Measure:** Real Measure is not a math equation; it is the concrete, specific realization that the "I" livingly measures and generates all experience without mediation.
