### Entry heg-i-048-049 — The Derivative Comment: Real Measure, Spinoza, and the Indian Trinity

Span:

- sourceFile: `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex` (Lines 509-541)
- reference: Hegel's Doctrine of Being (Derivative Commentary on Ṛtaṃbharā Prajñā)

Summary:
Acting as the derivative comment on Sūtras I.48-49, Hegel structures the peak of the Doctrine of Being as **Real Measure**. Patañjali declares this state to be truth-bearing insight (_ṛtaṃbharā prajñā_) capable of grasping the specific, absolute reality of the universe. The source text perfectly anchors this. Real Measure is when a system is completely saturated by its own measure, possessing "its own specific being-for-itself."

Hegel explicitly links this absolute saturation of Measure to Spinoza's Absolute Substance and its Modes. But crucially, the text explicitly grounds this dialectic in the Indian Trinity (Trimurti). Hegel writes: "The Indian pantheism, in its monstrous fantasies... did nevertheless receive a conceptual formulation." He identifies the univalent Substance as Brahma, but notes it is an abstract void. It requires manifestation (Vishnu) and finally, the sublation of those forms back into the Absolute: "to the third, Shiva... The determination of this third is that of mode, alteration, coming-to-be and passing-away." Shiva acts as the Great Measure, the living pulse (mode) where forms rise and dissolve, translating the truth-bearing insight into a terrifying, dynamic cosmic absolute.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Real Measure as Absolute Substance:** Just as Patañjali posits _ṛtaṃbharā prajñā_ as the ultimate, unmediated truth, Hegel posits Real Measure as the precursor to Spinoza's Absolute—a system perfectly saturating itself.
- k2. **The Rejection of Abstract Inference:** Spinoza's mode and Hegel's real measure cannot be grasped by formal logic (_anumāna_); they demand the immediate intuition of the Whole specifically manifesting itself in the part.
- k3. **The Indian Trinity (Trimurti):** The text explicitly identifies the dialectic with Brahma (the abstract universal), Vishnu (the preserver/manifestation), and Shiva (the destroyer/alteration).
- k4. **Shiva as Mode / Measure:** Hegel structurally maps Shiva to the concept of "Mode" and "Measure"—the violent, truth-bearing point where all diverse quantitative existences (Vishnu) are measured and returned to the qualitative singularity (Brahma) through alteration/death.
