## The Varttika: The Deep Demonstration of the Indian Trinity and Spinoza's Mode

While Fichte supplies the governing philosophical principle (the Genetic Deduction over Factical Inference), Hegel supplies the _Varttika_—the microscopic transcendental proof of how this "Truth-Bearing Insight" logically manifests as the terrifying cosmic engine of Real Measure.

Patañjali describes _ṛtaṃbharā prajñā_ as the ultimate, unmediated grasp of the specific object, discarding mere universal concepts (inference). In the extracted text from `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex`, Hegel models this exact achievement as **Real Measure** taking the form of Spinoza's Absolute Substance, and he explicitly anchors it in the Indian system.

When Measure is fully realized, it ceases to be a mere rule applied to an object; it becomes the Absolute Reality of the universe itself. The universe is a single, massive, self-saturating Absolute, expressing itself in specific Modes. To grasp the "Specific Object" (_viśeṣa-arthatvāt_) is not to see a tiny isolated thing, but to see the Absolute fully vibrating _within_ that specific mode.

Hegel remarkably maps this logic directly onto the Indian Trimurti. He identifies Brahma as the initial, abstract universal (Pure Being / Quality). But Brahma is empty. It requires manifestation and development—Vishnu (Quantity / Sprawling Existence).

But the truth-bearing insight does not rest in the abstract universal (Brahma) or the sprawling externality (Vishnu). Hegel writes: "The determination of this third [Shiva] is that of mode, alteration, coming-to-be and passing-away." Shiva is Real Measure.

Shiva is the absolute truth that all specific, finite things (Vishnu) are constantly being measured, destroyed, and returned to their source. To achieve _ṛtaṃbharā prajñā_ is to look at a singular, specific object and instantly see the "Shiva-Mode" operating within it—to witness its immediate, continuous alteration and dissolution back into the Absolute. It is the highest insight of the Doctrine of Being, because it grasps the terror and beauty of the universe as a single, living, self-measuring organism.
