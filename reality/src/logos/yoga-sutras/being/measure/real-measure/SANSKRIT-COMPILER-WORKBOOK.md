### Entry san-i-048-049 — Truth-Bearing Insight and the Specific Object

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_48_49.ts`
- reference: Yoga Sūtra I.48-49

Summary:
Sūtras I.48 and I.49 constitute the peak cognitive achievement of the Doctrine of Being prior to its collapse. Patañjali says: _ṛtaṃbharā tatra prajñā_ (I.48) and _śruta-anumāna-prajñābhyām anya-viṣayā viśeṣa-arthatvāt_ (I.49). ("There, the insight/wisdom is truth-bearing (ṛtaṃbharā). Its sphere of action is different from the insight gained by hearing/scripture (śruta) or inference (anumāna), because it has for its object the specific/particular (viśeṣa-arthatvāt).") The inner clarity (_prasādaḥ_) of Measure established in I.47 now yields actual knowledge: _prajñā_. This insight is "truth-bearing" because it effortlessly bridges the universal law and the specific object without mediation. Sūtra I.49 sharply critiques normal epistemology (inference and hearsay), arguing they only ever deal in generalities or concepts. _Ṛtaṃbharā-prajñā_ grasps the absolute, intensely _specific_ reality of the object directly, representing the culmination of "Real Measure."

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Ṛtaṃbharā Prajñā (Truth-Bearing Insight):** The cognitive state arising from the unification of Quality and Quantity. It holds the absolute truth immediately, without ratio or deliberation.
- k2. **Śruta and Anumāna (Scripture and Inference):** The rejected epistemologies. Inference only deals with classes, ratios, and generalities. It can never grasp the living truth of the singular object (the "Real").
- k3. **Anya-Viṣayā (Different Sphere):** The Domain of Measure operates on completely different principles than finite, discursive existence (Existence/Savitarkā).
- k4. **Viśeṣa-Arthatvāt (For the Specific Object):** The absolute hallmark of Real Measure. It does not think abstractly; it cognizes the absolute, living, particular _specificity_ of the object in its perfect measure.
