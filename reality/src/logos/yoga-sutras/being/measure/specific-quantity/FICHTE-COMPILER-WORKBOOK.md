### Entry fic-i-047 — The Return to Pure Immanence

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_47.ts`
- reference: Fichte's Doctrine of Being (Higher Commentary on Vaiśāradya and Adhyātma-Prasādaḥ)

Summary:
Acting as the higher comment on Sūtra I.47, Fichte's _Science of Knowing_ (1804) maps _vaiśāradya_ (proficiency) and _adhyātma-prasādaḥ_ (inner clarity) to the philosopher's catastrophic rejection of the Spurious Infinity and the triumphant return to Pure Immanence. The _hiatus irrationalis_ (the quantitative trap of I.46) proved that outward, mathematical progress always leaves the "I" alienated from the limit it posits.

Through absolute proficiency (the mastery of the philosophical method), Knowing realizes that this outward reaching is an illusion. The limitless mathematical spread is actually the "I" itself. The Light stops extending; it recoils back to its own source. This is the _adhyātma_—the turning inward. When Knowing rests entirely within its own activity, rather than projecting numerical limits outward, it achieves absolute clarity (_prasāda_). The Light is revealed not as an endless number line, but as the living, immanent _Esse_ that contains all limits within itself without being fractured by them.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Rejection of the Spurious Infinity:** The proficiency of the philosopher enables them to see through the illusion of numerical progress. Knowing stops chasing the "seed" of the limit.
- k2. **The Recoil inward (Adhyātma):** Knowing abandons the external projection of the Not-I entirely. The infinite expansive activity is turned sharply back onto the Subject itself.
- k3. **Pure Immanence:** The "I" rests within the "I". This is not an empty void, but a massive, self-contained luminosity where knowing and being are completely fused.
- k4. **Translation into Inner Clarity:** Fichte's immanent _Esse_ perfectly translates the Sanskrit _prasāda_. It is a state of absolute, unbreakable peace because the dualism driving the Anstoß has been structurally bypassed.
