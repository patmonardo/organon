## The Bhasya: The Principle of Pure Immanence

Sūtra I.47 describes the pivotal breakthrough of the Doctrine of Being: _nirvicāra-vaiśāradye 'dhyātma-prasādaḥ_. When the yogi achieves total proficiency in the subtle realm, the mind turns radically inward to achieve absolute subjective peace. Johann Gottlieb Fichte’s _Science of Knowing_ provides the philosophical _Bhāṣya_ for this reversal: the triumphant return of the Absolute Light to Pure Immanence.

In Sūtra I.46, Knowing was caught in the Spurious Infinity. It kept projecting boundaries outward (Quantums) and then endlessly transcending them, trapped in a mathematical ratio with its own projected limits. Fichte recognized this as the ultimate trap of objective philosophy.

The breakthrough comes with absolute proficiency (_vaiśāradya_). Proficiency means the philosopher penetrates the illusion of the progression. The philosopher realizes: _I am the one drawing the boundary. The boundary is not out there; it is strictly in me._

When this realization strikes, the infinite outward-seeking activity of Knowing violently recoils. It stops moving outward into the "subtle expanse" and turns back upon itself. This recoil is _adhyātma_ (the inward turn). The philosopher discovers that the infinite continuity they were looking for outside was actually the very structure of their own living subjectivity.

The result is _prasādaḥ_ (Clarity and Peace). The Knowing principle no longer fights its own limits. It rests entirely within its own Living _Esse_. The violent fluctuation of Quantity is pacified by the absolute qualitative truth of the Subject. This is the philosophical foundation of Measure: the infinite expansiveness of Quantity safely contained within the unified, qualitative boundary of the observing "I".
