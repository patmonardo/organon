### Entry heg-i-047 — The Derivative Comment: Measure as the Unity of Quality and Quantity

Span:

- sourceFile: `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex` (Lines 462-473)
- reference: Hegel's Doctrine of Being (Derivative Commentary on Vaiśāradya and Adhyātma-Prasādaḥ)

Summary:
Acting as the derivative comment on Sūtra I.47, Hegel explicitly defines the breakthrough of Measure as the sublation of the Quantitative Ratio. Patañjali notes that via proficiency (_vaiśāradya_), the mind turns inward to attain clarity (_adhyātma-prasādaḥ_). The source text perfectly defines this inward turn out of quantitative externality: "Quantity is being that has returned to itself... [Measure is] the externality that refers itself to itself... as taken back into itself, the qualitative."

Hegel explains that pure Quantity was totally indifferent and external (the ratio). But when Quantity "refers itself to itself," it ceases to be purely external magnitude; it acquires a specific qualitative nature again. "Measure is the qualitative quantum," Hegel writes. It is a specific amount that constitutes a specific quality. By returning "into itself," the logic perfectly maps Patañjali's _adhyātma_ (the inward, subjective turn). The mind is no longer hopelessly expanding; it has found the specific, qualitative measure of its own objective magnitude.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Measure (The Qualitative Quantum):** The absolute unity of Being. It is not just "how much" (Quantity) nor just "what" (Quality), but the specific amount required to _be_ what it is.
- k2. **Externality Referring to Itself:** The mechanism of sublation. The outward, quantitative continuity stops radiating outward and bends back to reference its own origin.
- k3. **The Return to the Qualitative:** When mathematical externality turns back upon itself, it ceases to be indifferent and regains the intense, qualitative uniqueness of Sūtra I.41, but now fully expanded.
- k4. **Translation of Adhyātma-Prasādaḥ:** This structural "return to itself" translates the subjective clarity/peace of the yogi. The infinite progress of the quantum ratio is pacified by the self-contained Measure.
