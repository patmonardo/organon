## The Varttika: The Deep Demonstration of the Turn Inward

While Fichte supplies the governing philosophical principle (the philosopher's recoil from the Spurious Infinity back into Immanence), Hegel supplies the _Varttika_—the microscopic transcendental proof of how Quantity logically consumes itself to become Measure.

Patañjali states that reaching ultimate proficiency in the subtle realm causes a massive inward turn, resulting in subjective clarity (_adhyātma-prasādaḥ_). The mind is no longer running on the treadmill of the _aliṅga_ ratio.

Hegel's _Science of Logic_ (from the extracted text in `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex`) models this exact reversal. In Sūtra I.46, the Quantum ratio proved that "being-outside-itself" (externality) was a trap. But what happens when the logic pushes through this externality completely? Hegel reveals the dialectical pivot: "[Measure is] the externality that refers itself to itself."

This is the logical equivalent of _adhyātma_ (the inward turn). The quantitative ratio, realizing it cannot find truth by constantly pointing to another number, suddenly points _back at itself_. When magnitude refers strictly to itself, it stops being a math equation and becomes a specific **Quality**.

Hegel writes: "Quantity is being that has returned to itself... as taken back into itself, the qualitative." Measure is the Qualitative Quantum. It is the exact, specific amount of something required for it to maintain its unique nature. (For example, water is liquid, but at a specific quantitative measure—0 degrees Celsius—its quality radically changes to solid).

By uniting Quality and Quantity, Measure establishes profound peace (_prasādaḥ_). The mind no longer has to choose between the intense singularity of the crystal (I.41) or the sprawling magnitude of the subtle continuum (I.44). They are unified. The yogi discovers that absolute subjective clarity is precisely the state of being the Perfect Measure of the universe.
