### Entry san-i-047 — The Subjective Clarity of Proficiency

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_47.ts`
- reference: Yoga Sūtra I.47

Summary:
Sūtra I.47 establishes the grand unification of the gross and subtle domains, marking the transition into Measure. Patañjali declares: _nirvicāra-vaiśāradye 'dhyātma-prasādaḥ_. ("Upon attaining the utmost proficiency [vaiśāradye] in the non-reflective state [nirvicāra], there is an inner subjective clarity or peace [adhyātma-prasādaḥ].") In Sūtra I.46, the mind was warned that relying on mathematical or subtle boundaries left it "with seed" (trapped in ratio). To overcome this, the mind must achieve _vaiśāradya_ (absolute proficiency or lucid flow) in the very state of _nirvicāra_ (the meditation on the subtle continuum without spatial/temporal deliberation). When this proficiency peaks, the mind stops expanding outwardly across the continuum. It returns to itself. The result is _adhyātma-prasādaḥ_: a brilliant, internalized clarity that sublates the quantitative ratio, uniting the intensity of pure Quality with the expanse of pure Quantity within the subjectivity of the yogi.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Nirvicāra (Non-reflective):** The perfected meditation upon the subtle continuum (Quantity) devoid of spatial or temporal conceptualization.
- k2. **Vaiśāradye (Utmost Proficiency):** The absolute stabilization of the nirvicāra state. The mind no longer struggles to hold the subtle object; the focus becomes a continuous, effortless flow.
- k3. **Adhyātma (Inner / Subjective):** The outward trajectory of Quantity is reversed. The mind ceases exploring mathematical extension and turns back inward upon its own spiritual ground.
- k4. **Prasādaḥ (Clarity / Peace):** The resulting unification. It is not an empty void, but a massive, lucid serenity. It is the qualitative state of the subject perfectly measuring and absorbing the quantitative expanse.
