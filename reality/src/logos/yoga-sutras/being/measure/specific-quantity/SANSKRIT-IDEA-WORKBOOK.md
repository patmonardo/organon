## Narrative Synthesis: The Turn Inward to Clarity

The devastating conclusion of Sūtra I.46 left the yogi stranded. The mind had expanded to the very limit of the unmanifest universe (_aliṅga_), yet it remained "with seed" (_sabīja_)—trapped in an endless, mathematical ratio, relying on external boundaries to maintain its focus. How does the mind break this loop?

Sūtra I.47 reveals the mechanism of sublation: _nirvicāra-vaiśāradye 'dhyātma-prasādaḥ_.

The mind does not escape the subtle realm by retreating backward. Instead, it pushes _through_ it by attaining absolute, unbroken proficiency (_vaiśāradya_). The yogi holds the non-reflective (_nirvicāra_) focus so perfectly that the effort of holding the object vanishes. The mind stops acting like a mathematically expanding vector constantly reaching out to grasp the next limit.

When this outward-striving exhaustion ceases, a massive structural reversal occurs. The mind turns back upon itself. This is _adhyātma_ (the inner, or pertaining to the Self). The sprawling, cold, objective continuum of the universe is suddenly recognized as resting entirely within the lucid ground of the subject.

The immediate result is _prasādaḥ_ (profound peace and clarity). This clarity is precisely the unity of Quality and Quantity. It has the intense, singular focus of the transparent crystal (Quality from I.41), but it now organically contains the entire infinite expanse of the subtle universe (Quantity from I.44). The mind has become a Perfect Measure: it is a qualitative state big enough to hold absolute quantity without losing itself.
