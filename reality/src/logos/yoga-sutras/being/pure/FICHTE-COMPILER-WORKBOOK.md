### Entry fic-i-050-051 — Model Inhibition, the Living Esse, and the Absolute Unity

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_50.ts`, `ys_i_51.ts`
- reference: Lecture 15 of the Science of Knowing (1804/2).

Summary:
The closing mechanism of the Yoga baseline (_Sūtras I.50-I.51_) maps perfectly onto the climactic conclusions of Fichte's Lecture 15. Patañjali specifies that the _Prajñā_ (Principle) generates a purely configured _Saṃskāra_ (Model) whose sole function is to inhibit (_pratibandhī_) all other phenomenological noise. For Fichte, this is exactly the function of the "Maxim of absolute genesis" acting as Negative Freedom: it abstracts from and suppresses the illusion of immediate, factical consciousness (the "proiectio per hiatum").

Sūtra I.51 culminates the trajectory: when this final Model has exhausted its inhibitory function, it too ceases, yielding seedless (_nirbīja_) samādhi. Fichte duplicates this exact terminus in Lecture 15's Main Conclusion: having abandoned every relational mark to the "Object" (the "not-in-itself"), the Mind stands in the living _Esse_. It is immediate, undivided, self-enclosed living oneness (the absolute I), possessing no duality, no "seed" of Otherness, and no projection-gap into objectivity. The Doctrine of Truth is consummated.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **I.50 (Taj-ja Saṃskāra & Inhibition):** Translates to Fichte's Methodological Maxim (Negative Freedom). The Principle (Pure Reason) generates a Model (the Maxim: "abstract from immediate consciousness"). This Model systematically acts as an inhibitor against the "proiectio per hiatum irrationale" (the unwarranted projection of objects via an irrational gap).
- k2. **The End of Objective Disjunction:** Fichte concludes in Lecture 15 that both idealism and realism err when they project across a gap. The highest method is simply to abstain. The "truth-born saṃskāra" is simply this abstinence holding the field against all other projections.
- k3. **I.51 (Nirbīja Samādhi / The Living Esse):** Translates to Fichte's Main Conclusion of the Prolegomena. Once the abstinence is absolute, even the mechanism of abstinence drops away. All that remains is the simple, pure _Being_ as immediate, self-enclosed life.
- k4. **The Absolute I (We):** Fichte declares that this seedless _Being_ arises only in itself, as an absolute I (or We). It contains no duality and no external relationship (it is _nirbīja_, without a seed / without an object). To reach this is to attain the pure living act itself.
