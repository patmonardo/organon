### Entry heg-i-050-051 — The Self-Consuming Concept and the Absolute Idea

Span:

- reference: The final transition within the Subjective Logic (The Concept) to the Absolute Idea.

Summary:
Sūtras I.50 and I.51 describe the final epistemological victory of the Yoga system within the locus of _Samādhi_. Sūtra I.50 establishes that the Truth-bearing Principle (_Prajñā_) establishes a massive, inhibiting Model (_Taj-ja saṃskāra_) that sweeps the field clean of all residual errors and prior logical models. In Hegelian terms, this acts as the Dialectical Method enforcing the absolute sovereignty of the Concept over all lingering determinations of the Understanding (Essence and Being). Sūtra I.51 brings the final sublation: the Method itself subdues its own process (_tasyāpi nirodhe_). Once the Truth has totally saturated the terrain and eradicated the "Other," the dialectic no longer needs an oppositional or mediating structure. The Subject/Object gap vanishes entirely. This maps exactly to Hegel's climactic "Absolute Idea," the final category of the _Science of Logic_, where the Concept comprehends itself as the totality of all reality without external differentiation: seedless (_nirbīja_) and absolute.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. _Taj-ja saṃskāra_ (I.50) maps to the Concept determining itself objectively. The _Prajñā_ (Universal Concept) generates its own _Saṃskāra_ (Particular Model).
- k2. _Anya-saṃskāra-pratibandhī_ (I.50) mirrors the sublation (_Aufhebung_) enacted by Speculative Reason. The living Concept violently inhibits and dissolves the rigid, dead categories posited by earlier modes of consciousness (the _vṛttis_ / Understanding).
- k3. _Tasyāpi nirodhe_ (I.51) translates to the final self-sublation of the Method. Hegel notes that the dialectical method itself must ultimately be recognized as identically one with its content. The action of "separating" or "inhibiting" ceases.
- k4. _Nirbīja samādhi_ (I.51) is perfectly collinear with Hegel's "Absolute Idea." In the Absolute Idea, the logical process reaches its seedless completion. It has no external object (_seed_) pushing it forward; it is "the sole subject matter and content of philosophy... possessing the highest, strictest, objectively immanent necessity." The Concept is finally at rest in itself.
