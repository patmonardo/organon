## Sūtras I.50-I.51: The Method's Sublation and the Absolute Idea

### I. Conceptual Alignment

- **Taj-ja Saṃskāra (Model born of truth) $\leftrightarrow$ The Concept Determining objectivity**: The Universal Concept (Prajñā) generates its own Particular, concrete model to rule the field.
- **Anya-saṃskāra-pratibandhī (Inhibiting other traces) $\leftrightarrow$ Sublation (_Aufhebung_)**: The Dialectical Method actively dissolving and negating the rigid, dead categories posited by earlier modes of consciousness (Understanding/Essence).
- **Tasyāpi Nirodhe (Cessation of the truth-model) $\leftrightarrow$ The Self-Sublation of the Method**: The realization that the Method (the process of dialectical separation and synthesis) is not distinct from its content but identically one with it.
- **Nirbīja Samādhi (Seedless Samādhi) $\leftrightarrow$ The Absolute Idea**: The climax of the _Science of Logic_. The Concept comprehends itself as the totality of all reality without external differentiation; it is seedless, requiring no external object to mediate its truth.

### II. Philosophical Essay: From Method to Absolute Totality

The final two Sūtras of Patañjali's _Samādhi-pada_ represent the complete epistemological victory within the locus of Being. This victory and its subsequent self-cancellation correspond exactly to the final chapters of Hegel’s _Science of Logic_, where the Subjective Concept realizes itself as the **Absolute Idea**.

**I.50: The Dialectical Sublation of Legacies**
Patañjali stipulates that the _Ṛtambharā Prajñā_ (the Universal Principle of Truth) generates its own structural model—a _taj-ja saṃskāra_. This model systematically inhibits (_pratibandhī_) all legacy models (_anya-saṃskāras_).

In Hegelian architecture, this is the action of the **Dialectical Method**. When the Concept fully deploys Speculative Reason (_Vernunft_), it does not merely ignore the lower, finite categories of the Understanding (the legacy _saṃskāras_ of ordinary consciousness). It violently sublates them. It swallows them. Speculative Reason demonstrates the logical inconsistency of every lower category—every abstract definition of "Being" or "Essence"—and dissolves them into the movement of the Concept itself. The Truth-bearing Concept "inhibits" all other concepts by proving they are merely incomplete fragments of its own totality.

**I.51: The Self-Cancellation of the Method**
But the Dialectical Method is still a _process_. It still implies an action of "overcoming" an "Other." A Subject is still conquering an Object. This is why Patañjali declares _tasyāpi nirodhe_: even this magnificent, truth-born inhibitory model must ultimately cease.

Hegel arrives at this identical ontological limit at the extreme end of the _Logic_. He reveals that the Dialectical Method itself cannot remain a separate "tool" applied _to_ reality. The Method must sublate itself. The Concept recognizes that it has already saturated all reality; there is no "Other" left to conquer. The act of knowing and the object known are absolutely identical. The Method cancels its own procedural momentum.

**Nirbīja Samādhi and the Absolute Idea**
When all impressions and actions cease (_sarva-nirodhāt_), the Yogi enters **Nirbīja Samādhi** (Seedless absorption).

It is seedless because there is no objective reference point left to think about. Hegel calls this exact state the **Absolute Idea**. The Absolute Idea is the end of the _Logic_ precisely because it has swallowed its own tail. It is "the sole subject matter and content of philosophy... possessing the highest, strictest, objectively immanent necessity." The Concept is finally at rest in itself. It needs no external object (_seed_); it generates its own determinations out of absolute, concrete, self-referential Freedom.

At this precise point, both Yoga and Hegelian Logic declare the Ontology of Being complete. The logical scaffolding falls away, and the Subject is ready to externalize this absolute freedom into Nature and Praxis (the Spirit).
