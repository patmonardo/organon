### Entry ys-i-041 — The Definition of Samāpatti

Span:

- reference: YS I.41 (kṣīṇa-vṛtter abhijātasyeva maṇer grahītṛ-grahaṇa-grāhyeṣu tat-stha-tad-añjanatā samāpattiḥ)

Summary:
When the fluctuations of the mind are weakened (_kṣīṇa-vṛtteḥ_), the mind becomes like a transparent crystal (_abhijātasya iva maṇeḥ_). It attains the power of coalescence or absorption (_samāpattiḥ_), taking on the form and color (_tat-stha-tad-añjanatā_) of whatever it rests upon—whether it be the knower, the cognitive act, or the known object.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. _Samāpatti_ is the cognitive apotheosis of the Doctrine of Being. It is not mere concentration, but a structural isomorphism where the mind becomes identical to its object.

### Entry ys-i-042-044 — The Four Modes of Objective Absorption

Span:

- sourceFile: `src/logos/ys/ys_i_43.ts`
- reference: YS I.42 (savitarkā), I.43 (nirvitarkā), I.44 (savicārā, nirvicārā)

Summary:
Patañjali divides objective _samāpatti_ into four stages arranged in a 2x2 matrix: based on the macro/subtle nature of the object, and the composite/purified state of the cognition. _Savitarkā_ (I.42) is mixed with words, meaning, and conceptual knowledge. _Nirvitarkā_ (I.43) is the purified seed wherein memory is cleansed (_smṛti-pariśuddha_), the ego-form drops away (_svarūpa-śūnya-iva_), and only the object shines forth (_artha-mātra-nirbhāsā_). _Savicārā_ and _Nirvicārā_ (I.44) apply this exact same methodology to subtle (_sūkṣma_) objects.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. _Savitarkā_ is entangled: the raw object is mixed with the word used to signify it (_śabda_) and the conceptual knowledge of it (_jñāna_). It is an impure "Being."
- k2. _Nirvitarkā_ strictly purifies the cognitive pipeline. By scrubbing residual memory traces (_smṛti-pariśuddha_), the linguistic and conceptual noise is attenuated. The object alone (_artha-mātra_) illuminates the mind.
- k3. The qualifier "as if" (_iva_) in "as if the self-form were empty" (_svarūpa-śūnya-iva_) marks that the ego/subject isn't ontologically annihilated natively, but its phenomenological gravity has dropped to zero so it doesn't distort the object.
- k4. I.44 escalates the terrain: _Vicāra_ (subtle reflection) targets the deeper structural causes of matter and mind (the _tanmātras_ and _guṇas_), applying the same mixed (_sa_, Savicāra) and purified (_nir_, Nirvicāra) logic.

### Entry ys-i-045-046 — The Hierarchy of the Subtle and Sabīja Samādhi

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_45_1.ts`, `ys_i_45_2.ts`, `ys_i_45_3.ts`, `ys_i_46.ts`
- reference: YS I.45 (sūkṣma-viṣayatvaṃ cāliṅga-paryavasānam), I.46 (tā eva sabījaḥ samādhiḥ)

Summary:
Sūtra I.45 establishes the absolute limit of the subtle domain (_sūkṣma-viṣaya_). Subtle objects are not homogenous. They form a descending ontological hierarchy extending through un-specialized forms (_aviśeṣa_), downward to pure marks/indicators (_liṅga-mātra_ or _tanmātras_), and finally terminating in the unmarked, unmanifest ground of nature itself (_āliṅga-paryavasānam_, referring to _Prakṛti_ or _Pradhāna_). Sūtra I.46 concludes this block by defining all previously mentioned stages (from _savitarkā_ to _āliṅga-vicārā_) as _sabīja samādhi_—samādhi "with a seed," meaning cognition remains anchored to an objective referent, regardless of how refined or unmanifest that object might be.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. _Aviśeṣa_ (non-specific) refers to subtle domains where objects lack gross, individuated contours.
- k2. _Liṅga-mātra_ (mark-only) refers to the _tanmātras_ or the bare archetypal signatures of reality before manifestation.
- k3. _A-liṅga_ (unmarked) is the absolute terminus of objective contemplation: _Prakṛti_ in perfect equilibrium, devoid of any phenomenal signifiers. It is the ontological ground.
- k4. _Sabīja Samādhi_ (I.46) categorizes the entire _Vitarka_ and _Vicāra_ spectrum as "seed-bearing." The "seed" (_bīja_) is the object of concentration. Even when contemplating the unmanifest _A-liṅga_, the mind retains an objective tether.

### Entry ys-i-047-049 — Ṛtambharā Prajñā: Truth-Bearing Insight

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_47.ts`, `ys_i_48.ts`, `ys_i_49.ts`
- reference: YS I.47 (nirvicāra-vaiśāradye 'dhyātma-prasādaḥ), I.48 (ṛtambharā tatra prajñā), I.49 (śrutānumāna-prajñābhyām anya-viṣayā viśeṣārthatvāt)

Summary:
Having traversed the absolute boundary of objective _sabīja samādhi_, Sūtras I.47-I.49 establish the epistemological crown of the method. When the subtle reflectionless state (_nirvicāra_) achieves ultimate maturity and crystalline lucidity (_vaiśāradya_), an unprecedented serenity and transparency dawns in the inner field (_adhyātma-prasādaḥ_, I.47). In this precise state, the cognitive faculty (_prajñā_) becomes _ṛtambharā_—literally "truth-bearing" (I.48). It is an insight that holds the cosmic order (_ṛta_) directly without admixture. Sūtra I.49 seals its absolute authority: this truth-bearing insight operates on a completely different epistemic domain (_anya-viṣayā_) than ordinary testimony (_śruta_) or logical inference (_anumāna_). While ordinary cognition delivers mediated, general concepts, _ṛtambharā prajñā_ targets the unmediated, determinate particular exactly as it is (_viśeṣārthatvāt_).

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. _Nirvicāra-vaiśāradya_ (I.47) is the necessary precondition. _Vaiśāradya_ means expert fluency, ripeness, or crystalline lucidity. It triggers _adhyātma-prasāda_, the total transparency of the inner instrument (_buddhi_/_citta_).
- k2. _Ṛtambharā Prajñā_ (I.48) is the apex of cognitive validity. It is not ordinary wisdom; it is an impersonal principle (_prajñā_) that bears truth (_ṛta_) in itself. It conforms strictly to reality without any subjective distortion.
- k3. The Epistemic Rupture (I.49): Patañjali categorically separates this insight from verbal/scriptural testimony (_śruti_) and logical inference (_anumāna_). Those are mediated tools of ordinary consciousness.
- k4. _Viśeṣārthatvāt_ points out that ordinary knowledge is trapped in universals and abstractions (generality). _Prajñā_ alone grasps the pure, determinate particular (_viśeṣa_) in its immediate suchness.

### Entry ys-i-050-051 — Saṃskāra Inhibition and Nirbīja Samādhi

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_50.ts`, `ys_i_51.ts`
- reference: YS I.50 (taj-jaḥ saṃskāro 'nya-saṃskāra-pratibandhī), I.51 (tasyāpi nirodhe sarva-nirodhān nirbījaḥ samādhiḥ)

Summary:
Sūtras I.50 and I.51 provide the closing mechanism for the entire _Samādhi_ chapter. Sūtra I.50 describes the active output of the truth-bearing insight (_rtambharā prajñā_): it generates its own structural impression (_taj-jaḥ saṃskāraḥ_). This truth-born saṃskāra is immensely powerful; it explicitly suppresses and inhibits (_pratibandhī_) all other, previous saṃskāras (the legacy impressions born from _vṛttis_ and _kleśas_). It establishes an absolute cognitive monopoly. Sūtra I.51 delivers the final stroke: once the truth-born saṃskāra has completely inhibited all others, its functional purpose is exhausted, and it too ceases (_tasyāpi nirodhe_). With the cessation of all saṃskāras and vṛttis (_sarva-nirodhāt_), the absolute seedless samādhi (_nirbījaḥ samādhiḥ_) is attained.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. _Taj-ja saṃskāra_ (I.50) is the "Model" generated by the "Principle" (_prajñā_). It is the structural imprint of Truth on the mind.
- k2. _Anya-saṃskāra-pratibandhī_ (I.50) establishes competitive inhibition. The truth-born saṃskāra actively blocks legacy saṃskāras from reactivating, enforcing a strict alignment with _ṛta_ (cosmic order).
- k3. _Tasyāpi nirodhe_ (I.51) confirms that even the highest, purest state of the mind (_prajñā_ and its _saṃskāra_) is still phenomological and must ultimately be abandoned.
- k4. _Nirbīja samādhi_ (I.51) is the absolute terminus. It is "seedless" because no objective referent remains, not even the _a-liṅga_ ground, and no subjective trace (saṃskāra) remains to process it. The Spirit (_Puruṣa_) rests entirely in its own unmediated nature.
