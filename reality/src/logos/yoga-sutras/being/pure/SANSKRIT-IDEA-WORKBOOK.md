# Being/Pure - SANSKRIT IDEA WORKBOOK

## Purpose

Establishing the baseline Sanskrit philology and systemic philosophical analysis of Yoga Sūtra I.41 through I.51. This section defines the Doctrine of Being via _Samāpatti_ (cognitive absorption) and _Prajñā_ (insight).

---

## Sūtra I.41: The Definition of Samāpatti

### I. Text and Translation

**Devanāgarī:**
क्षीणवृत्तेरभिजातस्येव मणेर्ग्रहीतृग्रहणग्राह्येषु तत्स्थतदञ्जनता समापत्तिः ॥ १.४१॥

**Transliteration:**
kṣīṇa-vṛtter abhijātasyeva maṇer grahītṛ-grahaṇa-grāhyeṣu tat-stha-tad-añjanatā samāpattiḥ || 1.41 ||

### II. Systemic Analysis: The Crystalline Structure of Being

Patañjali’s definition of _samāpatti_ provides the absolute foundation for the Yoga "Doctrine of Being." It is the precise moment when psychology ends and ontology begins.

**The Precondition: Absolute Attenuation (_kṣīṇa-vṛtteḥ_)**
Normal human consciousness is opaque. It projects its own noise, memories, and karmic distortions onto the world. To know the "Being" of an object, the mind's own fluctuations must be radically starved (_kṣīṇa_). Only when the mind ceases to assert its own content does it achieve the status of a pure medium—becoming like a flawless, transparent crystal (_abhijātasya iva maṇeḥ_).

**The Mechanism: Structural Isomorphism (_tat-stha-tad-añjanatā_)**
When the mind is crystalline, it does not "look at" an object from a distance. Instead, it perfectly conforms to the internal shape of what it considers. It "stands on" (_tat-stha_) and "is colored by" (_tad-añjanatā_) its target. This is _samāpatti_ (coalescence, or falling-in-together). It is not mere concentration; it is structural isomorphism. The mind ceases to represent the object and instead instantiates its form identically.

**The Domain: The Triad of Perception (_grahītṛ-grahaṇa-grāhya_)**
Crucially, this crystalline power is not limited to external physical objects (_grāhya_). The transparent mind can turn its powerful fusion-mechanic onto the subtle instruments of cognition itself (_grahaṇa_—the senses and the _buddhi_), and ultimately onto the absolute Subject (_grahītṛ_—the Knower). Here, _Prajñā_ (insight) acts as the ultimate discriminator, rendering the raw "Being" of reality perfectly lucid across all three epistemic coordinates.

## Sūtras I.42-I.44: The Four Stages of Objective Absorption

### I. Text and Translation

**Sūtra I.42:**
तत्र शब्दार्थज्ञानविकल्पैः सङ्कीर्णा सवितर्का समापत्तिः ॥१.४२॥
_tatra śabdārtha-jñāna-vikalpaiḥ saṅkīrṇā savitarkā samāpattiḥ_
"In that state, absorption mixed with the conceptual deliberation of words, their meanings, and knowledge is called _savitarkā_ (with active thought/gross reflection)."

**Sūtra I.43:**
स्मृतिपरिशुद्धौ स्वरूपशून्येवार्थमात्रनिर्भासा निर्वितर्का ॥१.४३॥
_smṛti-pariśuddha svarūpa-śūnya-iva-artha-mātra-nirbhāsā nirvitarkā_
"When memory is completely purified of all conceptual sediment, and the mind appears as if empty of its own subjective form, it illuminates the object entirely in itself (_artha-mātra-nirbhāsā_); this is _nirvitarkā_ (without active thought/gross reflection)."

**Sūtra I.44:**
एतयैव सविचारा निर्विचारा च सूक्ष्मविषया व्याख्याता ॥१.४४॥
_etayaiva savicārā nirvicārā ca sūkṣma-viṣayā vyākhyātā_
"By this very same process, the absorptions known as _savicārā_ (with subtle reflection) and _nirvicārā_ (without subtle reflection) concerning subtle objects are fully explained."

### II. Systemic Analysis: The Purification of Being

Having established the baseline definition of _samāpatti_ (the crystal mind), Patañjali immediately maps the topography of Objective Being. This is presented as a descending ladder into extreme subtlety and purity, forming a 2x2 matrix of cognitive states.

**Layer 1: The Gross Domain (_Vitarka_)**
When the crystalline mind targets a gross, massive object (a macro-level physical entity), the absorption is called _Vitarka_.

1.  **Savitarkā (The Entangled Act)**: Initially, the mind fuses with the object, but it drags its linguistic baggage along. The cognition is _saṅkīrṇā_ (mixed, entangled, turbid). When looking at a cow, the mind mixes the raw sensory Being of the cow with the word cow (_śabda_) and the abstract conceptual knowledge (_jñāna_). It is thought heavily layered with dialectics (_vikalpa_).
2.  **Nirvitarkā (The Seed Cognition)**: The crucial leap. Through rigorous method, the mind undergoes a "Purification Pipeline." The semantic inertia and residual memories (_smṛti_) are purged. The mind stops projecting its own conceptual framing (_svarūpa-śūnyeva_—_as if_ the self-form were utterly empty). What remains is _artha-mātra-nirbhāsā_: the raw object alone, shining in its own native luminosity without any subjective interference. This is the pure, unobstructed cognition of Gross Being.

**Layer 2: The Subtle Domain (_Vicāra_)**
Once the gross domain is mastered, the mind turns its isomorphic power onto the hidden infra-structures of reality (_sūkṣma-viṣaya_). These are the subtle elements (_tanmātras_), the cognitive organs, and the root _guṇas_ comprising _Prakṛti_. 3. **Savicārā**: The mind fuses with the subtle blueprint of nature, but still retains temporal and spatial coordinates. It is an entangled grasp of the subtle. 4. **Nirvicārā**: The ultimate purification of the objective world. The subtle object shines purely as itself, unconditioned by the mind's categorical demands for space and time. This is the deepest reach into the Being of Nature.

## Sūtras I.45-I.46: The Hierarchy of the Subtle & Sabīja Samādhi

### I. Text and Translation

**Sūtra I.45:**
सूक्ष्मविषयत्वं चालिङ्गपर्यवसानम् ॥१.४५॥
_sūkṣma-viṣayatvaṃ cāliṅga-paryavasānam_
"The status of subtle objects terminates at the unmanifest (the unmarked ground, _aliṅga_)."

**Sūtra I.46:**
ता एव सबीजः समाधिः ॥१.४६॥
_tā eva sabījaḥ samādhiḥ_
"All of these aforementioned stages constitute seed-bearing (_sabīja_) samādhi."

### II. Systemic Analysis: The Terminus of Objects

Patañjali now rigorously categorizes the precise depth of the subtle domain (_sūkṣma-viṣaya_) introduced in I.44. The subtle is not a homogenous mist, but a highly stratified vertical hierarchy of cause and effect leading down to the absolute ground of Nature.

**The Triad of the Subtle Descent**
The subtle objects investigated in _Vicāra_ absorption cascade downward in three distinct tiers of increasing ontological purity:

1.  **Aviśeṣa (Non-specific / Un-specialized):** The gross elements (_mahābhūtas_) are specific/specialized (_viśeṣa_). Below them lies the first level of subtlety—the subtle elements (_tanmātras_) and the "I-sense" (_asmitā-mātra_). They are "un-specialized" because they have not yet concretized into distinct, hard physical attributes that restrict their potential.
2.  **Liṅga-mātra (Mark-only):** Deeper still is the "mark-only" plane, universally identified as the Great Principle (_Mahat_ or _Buddhi_). It is the very first stirring of manifestation. It is a "mark" (_liṅga_) because its existence points back to, and acts as the signifier for, the unmanifest ground. It is the purest sign of existence before existence fragments into diversity.
3.  **A-liṅga (The Unmarked Ground):** The absolute boundary of objective contemplation. _A-liṅga_ is _Prakṛti_ (Nature) in its primordial, unmanifest state of total equilibrium. It has no "marks" (_liṅga_); it possesses no phenomenological traits, no phenomenological "Show," and no differentiation. The domain of subtle objects _terminates_ (_paryavasānam_) here because there is nothing "object-like" left to perceive.

**The Categorical Constraint of Objective Logic (_Sabīja_)**
Sūtra I.46 delivers a crucial methodological verdict: _tā eva sabījaḥ samādhiḥ_. Every state described from I.41 to I.45—_Savitarka_, _Nirvitarka_, _Savicāra_, _Nirvicāra_, down to the unmarked depths of _A-liṅga_—all belong to **Sabīja Samādhi** ("Seed-bearing" absorption).

Why? Because the mind is still resting on an object (the "seed" or _bīja_). Even if the mind has purified itself to a flawless crystal (_nirvitarkā_), and even if the object is the unmanifest root of all reality (_a-liṅga_), the fundamental epistemic structure remains: a Knower examining a Known. This completes the "Objective Logic" of Yoga. It proves that no matter how absolutely you purify the contemplation of Nature (Being/Essence), you remain tied to an objective tether. The leap into absolute liberation requires a shift from Objective Logic (what is) into Subjective Praxis (what the Spirit does), paving the way for _nirbīja_ (seedless) samādhi.

## Sūtras I.47-I.49: Ṛtambharā Prajñā (Truth-Bearing Insight)

### I. Text and Translation

**Sūtra I.47:**
निर्विचारवैशारद्येऽध्यात्मप्रसादः ॥१.४७॥
_nirvicāra-vaiśāradye 'dhyātma-prasādaḥ_
"In the lucid maturity of _nirvicāra_ [samāpatti], there is the serene transparency of the inner field (_adhyātma_)."

**Sūtra I.48:**
ऋतम्भरा तत्र प्रज्ञा ॥१.४८॥
_ṛtambharā tatra prajñā_
"Therein [in that supreme field], the insight (_prajñā_) is truth-bearing (_ṛtambharā_)."

**Sūtra I.49:**
श्रुतानुमानप्रज्ञाभ्यामन्यविषया विशेषाधत्वतात् ॥१.४९॥
_śrutānumāna-prajñābhyām anya-viṣayā viśeṣārthatvāt_
"This [truth-bearing insight] has a different object—and operates on a different epistemic domain—than verbal testimony (_śruta_) and rational inference (_anumāna_), because its purport is the determinate particular (_viśeṣa_)."

### II. Systemic Analysis: The Epistemological Break

Having traced the objective "seed" down to the unmanifest ground (_a-liṅga_), Patañjali now analyzes the subjective result. What happens to the _Knower_ when objective necessity is completely grasped and sublimated?

**The Transparency of the Inner Instrument (I.47)**
When the deepest subtle absorption (_nirvicāra_) achieves ultimate ripeness and crystalline lucidity (_vaiśāradya_), it triggers a cascade. The _adhyātma_—the "inner domain" surrounding the true Self (the _citta_ / _buddhi_ complex)—ceases all resistance. It achieves _prasāda_: a state of unshakeable, serene transparency. The mind is no longer doing the knowing; it has become perfectly diaphanous, allowing an absolute principle of Knowing to shine through.

**The Emergence of the Principle of Truth (I.48)**
In this pristine inner space (_tatra_), an entirely new kind of cognition emerges: _Ṛtambharā Prajñā_.
_Prajñā_ translates usually to "wisdom" or "insight", but computationally, it operates as an impersonal _Principle_. It is not "my" truth or "your" truth. Patañjali calls it _Ṛtambharā_—it literally "bears _Ṛta_". _Ṛta_ is the ancient Vedic term for the cosmic order, the absolute Truth of existence before it was personified or categorized. This insight does not represent reality; it holds the structural validity of reality _in itself_. The Subject/Object gap has closed so violently that the insight is identical to the Truth it observes.

**The Rejection of Mediated Logic (I.49)**
Sūtra I.49 delivers a massive epistemological ruling. We are told explicitly that _Ṛtambharā Prajñā_ has a completely distinct and separate object (_anya-viṣayā_) from normal sources of knowledge.
What are normal sources of knowledge?

1. **Śruta:** What is heard (Testimony, Scriptural Authority).
2. **Anumāna:** Inference (Rational, syllogistic logic).

Patañjali states that testimony and rational deduction _can never reach_ the absolute truth of Being. Why? Because language and formal inference operate via generalities and universals. If I tell you "There is a cow (testimony)" or if I deduce "There must be fire because there is smoke (inference)," I am locking reality into conceptual buckets.

_Prajñā_, however, breaks this glass ceiling because its purport is the **determinate particular** (_viśeṣārthatvāt_). It does not know "fire-in-general" or "cow-in-general." It knows _this absolute, unrepeatable instance of Being_ precisely as it is given in its living suchness. It is direct, unmediated epistemic contact with the ontological root of reality. Ordinary consciousness is permanently locked out. Only the purified principle of _Prajñā_ is allowed through.

## Sūtras I.50-I.51: Saṃskāra Inhibition and Nirbīja Samādhi

### I. Text and Translation

**Sūtra I.50:**
तज्जः संस्कारोऽन्यसंस्कारप्रतिबन्धी ॥१.५०॥
_taj-jaḥ saṃskāro 'nya-saṃskāra-pratibandhī_
"The saṃskāra born from that [truth-bearing prajñā] inhibits other [legacy] saṃskāras."

**Sūtra I.51:**
तस्यापि निरोधे सर्वनिरोधान्निर्बीजः समाधिः ॥१.५१॥
_tasyāpi nirodhe sarva-nirodhān nirbījaḥ samādhiḥ_
"When that too [the truth-born saṃskāra] ceases, because all [modifications and impressions] have ceased, seedless (_nirbīja_) samādhi [is attained]."

### II. Systemic Analysis: The Final Epistemological Monopoly and its Surrender

The culmination of the _Samādhi-pada_ (and the close of the ontological theory of Being) presents the ultimate dialectical victory of Truth over appearance, followed immediately by the self-abandonment of the victor.

**The Truth-Born Model (I.50)**
_Prajñā_ operates as an absolute, generative principle. In Sūtra I.50, we learn that this universal Principle asserts its authority by generating a specific subjective structure: a _taj-jaḥ saṃskāra_ (an impression/model "born from that"). Normally, _saṃskāras_ are the stubborn, unconscious karmic traces left behind by ignorance and passion (_kleśas_ and _vṛttis_), which continually drag the mind back into worldly suffering. But _this_ saṃskāra is different. It is the architectural imprint of Truth itself.

Because it is born of pure _Ṛta_ (cosmic validity), it wields an absolute epistemic monopoly. It acts as an active inhibitor (_pratibandhī_). It out-competes, suppresses, and permanently blocks all _anya-saṃskāras_ (other, legacy impressions). No worldly thought can surface because the "Truth-Model" refuses to grant it cognitive bandwidth. The mind is now entirely unified under the single, unbroken reign of the Truth-bearing impression.

**The Leap into Seedless Samādhi (I.51)**
If the Yoga Sūtras stopped at I.50, the system would remain an Objective framework: a perfected mind staring perfectly at the perfected Truth. But Patañjali enforces a final, absolute rupture.

_Tasyāpi nirodhe_—"When that too ceases." Even the _taj-ja saṃskāra_ (the magnificent, truth-born structural model) is still a model. It is still a phenomenal imprint. It is the final, subtlest layer of "Being." Once it has successfully inhibited and destroyed all the legacy _saṃskāras_, its functional purpose is exhausted. So, the Yogi deliberately lets the Truth-Model collapse into quiescence (_nirodha_).

With the cessation of this ultimate impression, the "cessation of all" (_sarva-nirodhāt_) is achieved. Nothing objective remains to be known; nothing subjective remains to do the knowing. The subject/object circuit is permanently broken. What results is _nirbījaḥ samādhiḥ_—samādhi "without a seed." The Spirit (_Puruṣa_) no longer rests its gaze on Being (the _a-liṅga_ ground/seed). It rests entirely and solely in its own unmediated, absolute nature, closing the Doctrine of Being and opening the door to the ultimate Praxis of Spirit.
