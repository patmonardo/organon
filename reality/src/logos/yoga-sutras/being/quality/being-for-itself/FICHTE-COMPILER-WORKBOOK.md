### Entry fic-i-043 — True Infinity and Absolute Self-Reference

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_43.ts`
- reference: Fichte's Doctrine of Being (Higher Commentary on Nirvitarkā)

Summary:
Acting as the higher comment on Sūtra I.43, Fichte's _Science of Knowing_ translates the non-deliberative singularity into the achievement of True Infinity and Absolute Self-Reference. The finite, deliberative state (I.42) was driven by the Anstoß (the check) which fractured the Light into an observing subject opposing an external empirical object (the Not-I). The purification of memory (_smṛti-pariśuddhau_) corresponds to Fichte's philosophical Act where Knowing penetrates the illusion of the Check. By realizing that empirical facts are just projections of its own activity, the "I" drops the dead memory of the Not-I.

By emptying itself of its rigid subjective isolation (_svarūpa-śūnyeva_), the "I" no longer stands against the object. Rather, it realizes that it _is_ the object. Knowing and Being converge into the "One" shining alone (_artha-mātra-nirbhāsā_). For Fichte, this is True Infinity. The Light does not encounter a boundary outside itself; it relates only to itself. It is absolute self-reference.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Sublation of the Check (Smṛti-Pariśuddhau):** Knowing sees through the empirical boundary (the Anstoß). It purifies itself of the habit of positing dead external facts.
- k2. **Emptying of the Finite Subject (Svarūpa-Śūnyeva):** The philosopher ceases to define himself as a finite observer arguing against a world. The finite subjective form is dropped.
- k3. **The One Shining (Artha-Mātra-Nirbhāsā):** Absolute Truth. The Object is no longer an alien thing-in-itself; it is the living activity of Knowing apprehending its own singularity.
- k4. **True Infinity:** The state of _nirvitarkā_ is not mere unbroken expansion; it is the infinite turning back of Knowing upon Knowing, perfectly unified without internal division.
