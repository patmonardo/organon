### Entry heg-i-043 — The Derivative Comment: Being-for-itself and the One

Span:

- sourceFile: `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex` (Lines 322-361)
- reference: Hegel's Doctrine of Being (Derivative Commentary on Nirvitarkā and Smṛti-Pariśuddhau)

Summary:
Acting as the derivative comment on Sūtra I.43, Hegel structures the transition out of finite Existence (I.42) into Being-for-itself. Patañjali notes that through the purification of memory, the mind empties its subjective form and shines purely as the object (_artha-mātra-nirbhāsā_). The source text perfectly defines this structural recovery: "In being-for-itself, qualitative being is brought to completion; it is infinite being."

Hegel explains that in Existence (I.42), being was only "relatively... determined" because it leaned on an external "other." However, by sublating this finite limit, "negation is simple self-reference and in it, therefore, the equalization with being: absolutely determinate being." This self-referential singularity is exactly _nirvitarkā_ (non-deliberative). Because it does not mediate itself through an other, "First, being-for-itself is immediately an existent-for-itself, the one." The mind acting as a transparent crystal reflecting only the object has become the absolute logical "One." But Hegel warns of the immediate instability of this state: "Second, the one passes over into a multiplicity of ones, repulsion... [which] passes over into quantity."

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Infinite Being (True Infinity):** Being-for-itself represents qualitative being brought to completion. It is no longer finite existence bounded by an other.
- k2. **Simple Self-Reference:** Negation of the negation forces the logic to turn entirely back upon itself. This perfectly translates the "shining as the object alone," completely bypassing external linguistic memory.
- k3. **The One:** The immediate form of Being-for-itself. Because the subject empties itself to become the object entirely, there is no longer a duality; there is only the singular "One."
- k4. **Repulsion and Transition into Quantity:** Immediately upon arriving at the massive singularity of the One (the non-deliberative climax of Quality), it splinters. The One repels itself into a multiplicity of ones, launching the dialectic headlong into Quantity (I.44).
