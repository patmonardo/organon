### Entry san-i-043 — Nirvitarkā Samāpatti, The Non-Deliberative State

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_43.ts`
- reference: Yoga Sūtra I.43

Summary:
Sūtra I.43 documents the recovery from the contaminated, deliberative state of I.42. Patañjali says: _smṛti-pariśuddhau svarūpa-śūnyeva-artha-mātra-nirbhāsā nirvitarkā_. ("Upon the purification of memory, [the mind appears] as if devoid of its own nature, shining forth only as the object. This is the non-deliberative state [nirvitarkā].") The mental pollution—the _vikalpa_ equating the word with the object—is cleansed entirely through the purification of memory (_smṛti-pariśuddhau_). The mind stops referring back to past linguistic categorizations. Because conceptual memory is wiped, the mind loses its subjective scaffolding (appearing "devoid of its own nature," _svarūpa-śūnyeva_). What remains is _artha-mātra-nirbhāsā_: pure illumination, shining strictly as the object itself, without linguistic mediation. This profound singularity overcomes the finite dualism of the previous state.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Smṛti-Pariśuddhau (Purification of Memory):** The active cleansing of the stored linguistic and conceptual associations (_śabda-jñāna_) that previously mediated cognition.
- k2. **Svarūpa-Śūnyeva (Devoid of Own Nature):** The subject (the "I" or cognitive apparatus) ceases to assert its own independence. It empties itself out to become completely receptive.
- k3. **Artha-Mātra-Nirbhāsā (Shining as Object Alone):** The object is no longer known _through_ a concept; it shines directly through the transparent crystal of the mind. It is absolute self-reference without external opposition.
- k4. **Nirvitarkā (Non-deliberative):** The cognitive state defined by this singularity. It overcomes the fragile, argumentative opposition of existence, attaining an infinite, non-mediated focus on the "One."
