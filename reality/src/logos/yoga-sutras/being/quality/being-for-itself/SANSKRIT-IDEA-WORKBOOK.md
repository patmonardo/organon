## Narrative Synthesis: The Purification of the Sign

If Sūtra I.42 documented the mind's fall into the dualistic, deliberative trap of linguistic definitions (_śabda_ and _vikalpa_), Sūtra I.43 provides the profound yogic escape route: _smṛti-pariśuddhau svarūpa-śūnyeva-artha-mātra-nirbhāsā_.

Patañjali locates the anchor of the discursive trap in memory (_smṛti_). When the mind encounters an object, it instantly dives into its memory banks, retrieving a word or a past concept to "know" the object. This constant backward-looking reliance on past conceptualizations is what forces the mind to "deliberate."

The remedy is the absolute purification of memory (_smṛti-pariśuddhau_). The mind stops indexing reality against stored language. It cuts the cord to the signifier entirely. When this conceptual baggage is stripped away, the subjective intellect no longer has any definitions to exert against the object. Therefore, the mind appears "devoid of its own form or nature" (_svarūpa-śūnyeva_). The "I" that was arguing with the object vanishes.

What remains is the sheer, blinding singularity of the object itself. It shines forth alone (_artha-mātra-nirbhāsā_). The object does not stand "over there" against a subject "over here"; it becomes the entire universe of cognition. It is absolute self-reference. The transparent crystal is restored, but it is no longer the empty, indeterminate crystal of I.41. It is a crystal perfectly, infinitely saturated by the singularity of the object. This is the non-deliberative state (_nirvitarkā_)—a state that has shattered finite existence and achieved an infinite, unshakeable focus.
