### Entry fic-i-041 — The Methodological Suspension and the Absolute Light

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_41.ts`
- reference: Fichte's Doctrine of Being (Higher Commentary on Samāpatti)

Summary:
Acting as the higher comment on Sūtra I.41, Fichte's _Science of Knowing_ translates the state of the transparent crystal into the pure apprehension of the Absolute Light. To reach the pure transparency of the crystal (where _vṛtti_ or fluctuations dwindle), Fichte explicitly requires the strict methodological suspension of factical, empirical consciousness. Everyday consciousness is characterized by its restless projection of dead objects. By suspending this, the philosopher arrives at the pure principle of Knowing itself: the absolute I as the Living Light.

For Fichte, this Light is structurally identical to Patañjali's crystal. The absolute Light is pure self-transparency. In its immediate, pristine state, it perfectly unites the act of knowing (Subject) with the truth known (Object). There is no gap, no diremption. The principle of the "I" is the absolute immediate identity of the cognizer and the cognized.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Methodological Suspension:** The reduction of fluctuations (_kṣīṇa-vṛtti_) corresponds precisely to Fichte's demand to bracket off and suspend ordinary, factical, multi-form consciousness to reach the single Absolute.
- k2. **The Absolute Light:** Fichte’s equivalent to the immaculate crystal. The Light is not empty nothingness; it is the vital, active principle of pure self-transparent Knowing.
- k3. **Immediate Identity:** The _samāpatti_ of subject, act, and object is Fichte's foundational absolute I—the state before the hiatus irrationalis rips the subject and object apart.
- k4. **Pure Immanence:** The Light illuminates only itself; it is the resting point of absolute reality.
