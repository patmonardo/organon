### Entry heg-i-041 — The Derivative Comment: Pure Indeterminate Being

Span:

- sourceFile: `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex` (Lines 125-140)
- reference: Hegel's Doctrine of Being (Derivative Commentary on Kṣīṇa-vṛtti and the Crystal)

Summary:
Acting as the derivative comment on Sūtra I.41, Hegel begins his _Science of Logic_. Where Patañjali posits a transparent crystal (_maṇi_) resulting from the dwindling of fluctuations, Hegel posits the absolute beginning of logic: Pure Being. The source text explicitly defines Being as "pure indeterminateness and emptiness... In its indeterminate immediacy it is equal only to itself and also not unequal with respect to another." Hegel mirrors the exact epistemological absorption of _samāpatti_ when he writes: "There is nothing to be intuited in it... or, it is only this pure empty intuiting itself. Just as little is anything to be thought in it, or, it is equally only this empty thinking." The mind, stripped of all conceptual and objective fluctuations, becomes identical with its completely empty object.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Pure Indeterminateness:** Being is defined perfectly negatively. It is void of determination. This is the exact dialectical equivalent to the state achieved when _vṛtti_ (fluctuations) have dwindled to zero.
- k2. **Empty Intuiting / Empty Thinking:** The structural map to _samāpatti_ (absorption). The distinction between the act of thinking (grahaṇa) and the object thought dissolves because the object is entirely empty. It is merely the form of "pure empty intuiting itself"—the transparent crystal without an external object to color it.
- k3. **Absolute Immediacy:** Being has "no difference within it, nor any outwardly." It relies on no external relation to define itself, resting in absolute immediacy.
- k4. **The Boundary of Nothingness:** Hegel explicitly concludes this initial state by revealing its absolute fragility: "Being, the indeterminate immediate is in fact nothing." Pure transparency is indistinguishable from empty nothingness.
