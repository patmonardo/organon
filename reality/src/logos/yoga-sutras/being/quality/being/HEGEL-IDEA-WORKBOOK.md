## The Varttika: The Deep Demonstration of Pure Being

While Fichte supplies the governing philosophical principle (the _Bhāṣya_)—that the mind is a self-illuminating crystal of Absolute Light—Hegel supplies the _Varttika_. The Hegelian Varttika is the massive, meticulous, transcendental demonstration of how that principle is methodically enacted, and crucially, how it inevitably fractures.

Hegel begins precisely where Sūtra I.41 begins: the attempt to conceive of the absolute beginning, stripped of all fluctuations and determinations. He calls this **Pure Being**. Patañjali describes this state as a transparent crystal. Hegel takes this metaphor of absolute transparency to its rigorous dialectical extreme. If the mind is perfectly transparent, if it has dwindled all internal and external differences away, what exactly is it thinking?

Hegel draws directly from the provided text in `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex`: "There is nothing to be intuited in it... or, it is only this pure empty intuiting itself." This is the precise logical mechanism of _samāpatti_. Because the object (Being) has absolutely no qualities, the subject attempting to intuit it has nothing to hold onto. The boundary between the cognizer and the cognized collapses, because holding "nothing" makes the act of cognition entirely empty. It is merely the form of intuiting resting on itself.

But here, the Varttika provides its deep critical turn. Patañjali presents the crystal as a stable achievement. Fichte presents the Light as the eternal Living _Esse_. But Hegel demonstrates that this pure, un-fluctuating state is structurally impossible to maintain. If Being truly has "no difference within it, nor any outwardly," it is completely indistinguishable from pure emptiness. Thus, at the end of this node, Hegel derives a catastrophic conclusion for the Yogi attempting to rest in immediate Being: "Being, the indeterminate immediate is in fact nothing, and neither more nor less than nothing."

The crystal is so transparent that it is invisible. This absolute emptiness immediately drives the logic forward into becoming and existence, setting the entire dialectical engine of the _Science of Logic_ (and the subsequent sutras) into restless, unstoppable motion.
