### Entry san-i-041 — The State of Samāpatti (Cognitive Absorption)

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_41.ts`
- reference: Yoga Sūtra I.41

Summary:
Sūtra I.41 provides the foundational definition of the mind in its most immediate, resting state. Patañjali says: _kṣīṇa-vṛtter abhijātasyeva maṇer grahītṛ-grahaṇa-grāhyeṣu tat-stha-tad-añjanatā samāpattiḥ_. ("For one whose fluctuations have dwindled, there is total absorption [samāpatti], which is the mind's resting on and taking the color of the cognizer, the cognitive process, and the cognized, just like a pure, transparent crystal.") Once the mental fluctuations (_vṛtti_) have subsided (_kṣīṇa_), the mind operates like a pristine crystal (_abhijātasyeva maṇer_). It is perfectly transparent, assuming the exact color/form of whatever it rests upon (_tat-stha-tad-añjanatā_). In this state, the stark boundaries separating the subject (_grahītṛ_), the act (_grahaṇa_), and the object (_grāhyeṣu_) are dissolved into an immediate, seamless unity of pure cognitive absorption.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Kṣīṇa-vṛtti (Dwindling of Fluctuations):** The precondition for this state is the exhaustion or quieting of active, disturbed mental states.
- k2. **Abhijātasyeva Maṇer (The Transparent Crystal):** The mind is fundamentally defined by its absolute clarity. It has no intrinsic "color" or form of its own; its nature is pure transparency.
- k3. **Tat-stha-tad-añjanatā (Taking the Color):** The transparent crystal perfectly reflects whatever rests within it. There is an absolute, unquestioned identity between the knower and the known.
- k4. **Grahītṛ-grahaṇa-grāhyeṣu:** The triad of Subject, Act, and Object are locked into an immediate unity (samāpatti), where distinction exists but is entirely smoothed over by the transparent nature of the absorption.
