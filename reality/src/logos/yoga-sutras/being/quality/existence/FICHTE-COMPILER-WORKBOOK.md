### Entry fic-i-042 — The Anstoß and Factical Discursion

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_42.ts`
- reference: Fichte's Doctrine of Being (Higher Commentary on Savitarkā)

Summary:
Acting as the higher comment on Sūtra I.42, Fichte maps the contaminated/mixed state of _savitarkā_ directly to his profound concept of the _Anstoß_ (the Check) and the subsequent generation of factical, discursive consciousness. The pristine identity of the Absolute Light (the crystal / I.41) suffers a "check." The infinite outwardly-expanding activity of Knowing is bounded. This check causes the mind to reflect back upon itself, fracturing the pure Light into a rigid dualism: the subjective "I" that knows, and the objective "Not-I" (the factical world) that is known.

For Fichte, this is the exact generation of the "word" and "concept" (_śabda_ and _vikalpa_). Ordinary existence is nothing more than the mind linguistically and conceptually projecting its own internal boundaries outward, creating a "mixed" (_saṃkīrṇā_) reality where pure Life is paralyzed and represented as dead, empirical facts. The mind deliberates over shadows, mistaking the sign/concept for the living Absolute.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **The Anstoß (Check):** The initial boundary that disrupts the pure transparency of the Absolute Light, forcing the Knowing to turn backwards, creating the Subject/Object split.
- k2. **Factical Discursion (Śabda/Vikalpa):** The linguistic and discursive intelligence that results from the Check. It mediates reality through rigid conceptual markers ("dead facts").
- k3. **The Loss of Life (Saṃkīrṇā):** The pure Living _Esse_ is contaminated. Knowing is mixed with Being; what was purely active is now misrecognized as static, objective meaning (_artha_).
- k4. **Finite Dualism:** This state maps exactly to _savitarkā_, the deliberative trap where the philosopher exists in opposition to the world, arguing with words rather than apprehending the Light.
