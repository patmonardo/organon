## The Bhasya: The Principle of the Anstoß

Sūtra I.42 describes the fall from pure transparency into the mixed, deliberative state of _savitarkā_, caused by the contamination of linguistic and conceptual definitions (_śabda_ and _vikalpa_). Johann Gottlieb Fichte’s _Science of Knowing_ provides the deeper philosophical _Bhāṣya_ for this precise mechanism: the principle of the _Anstoß_ (the Check).

If the Absolute Light (the crystal of I.41) is pure, infinite activity, why does it ever take on a specific coloration or fall into deliberation? Fichte answers that the infinite, outward-going activity of the "I" encounters an absolute _Anstoß_—a boundary, a check, a resistance. This check forces the active Light to rebound upon itself. The infinite activity is suddenly paralyzed, arrested, and forced to relate to a limit.

This rebounding creates the fundamental architecture of ordinary empirical consciousness. The mind, feeling checked, assumes there is a "Not-I" (an independent, factical object) standing outside it causing the check. To handle this shock, the mind generates language and concepts (the exact equivalent of Patañjali's _śabda-artha-jñāna_ triad). It labels the resistance. It traps the living reality within dead, empirical facts.

Thus, the pure transparency of Knowing is _saṃkīrṇā_ (mixed). It is mixed with the assumption of external Being. The philosopher or yogi in this state is not meditating on the Absolute; they are locked in a discursive debate with their own conceptual projections. They mistake the meaning (_artha_) generated by their own intellect (_jñāna_) for the Living _Esse_. The dualism of the Anstoß generates the trap of finite, deliberative existence.
