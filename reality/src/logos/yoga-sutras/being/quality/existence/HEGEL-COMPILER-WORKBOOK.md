### Entry heg-i-042 — The Derivative Comment: Existence as Determinate Being

Span:

- sourceFile: `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex` (Lines 278-291)
- reference: Hegel's Doctrine of Being (Derivative Commentary on Savitarkā)

Summary:
Acting as the derivative comment on Sūtra I.42, Hegel structures the transition from Pure Being into Existence (Dasein, Determinate Being). Patañjali notes that the mind falls from pure transparency into a "mixed" (_saṃkīrṇā_) or restricted state due to conceptual parsing. The source text perfectly defines this structural collapse: "Existence is determinate being; its determinateness is existent determinateness, quality."

To exist as a specific 'something' requires being opposed to something else. The text explicitly states: "Through its quality, something is opposed to an other; it is alterable and finite, negatively determined not only towards an other, but absolutely within it." This is the exact logical mechanism of _śabda-artha-jñāna-vikalpa_. The very act of naming (word/concept) creates a boundary, a negation, a negative determination. The transparent unity of the crystal is broken, and consciousness is now trapped in the finite, alterable, oppositional logic of Dasein (the deliberative state of savitarkā).

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Determinate Being / Existence:** Pure Being collapses into a recognizable state only by acquiring a specific boundary or determinateness.
- k2. **Opposition to an Other:** The core of existence is that a thing possesses its quality only by standing in contrast to an "other." This mirrors the discursive mechanism where Subject opposes Object.
- k3. **Alterable and Finite (Savitarkā):** Because determination relies on negative boundaries, existence is intensely fragile and finite. This captures the agitated, deliberative fluctuation of the savitarkā state.
- k4. **Negative Determination (Vikalpa):** The conceptual construction (_vikalpa_) is exposed as a purely negative act—defining what a thing is by bounding it off against what it is not.
