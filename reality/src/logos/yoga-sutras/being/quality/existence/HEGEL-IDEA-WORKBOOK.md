## The Varttika: The Deep Demonstration of Dasein

While Fichte supplies the governing philosophical principle (the Anstoß/Check breaking the pure expansion of the Absolute Light), Hegel supplies the _Varttika_—the microscopic transcendental proof of _how_ this check translates into the logical architecture of Existence.

Patañjali describes the mind falling into _savitarkā_ because it becomes mixed up (_saṃkīrṇā_) with the conceptual triad of word, object, and knowledge. The transparent crystal is stained by definitions. Hegel's _Science of Logic_ (from the extracted text) proves why this stain is structurally inevitable the moment Being tries to sustain itself.

Pure Being (I.41) fell into Becoming because it was indistinguishable from Nothing. When Becoming finally settles into "quiescent simplicity," it emerges not as Pure Being, but as **Existence** (_Dasein_ / Determinate Being). Hegel defines Existence with devastating precision: "its determinateness is existent determinateness, quality."

What exactly is a "quality" logically? It is a boundary. Hegel writes: "Through its quality, something is opposed to an other; it is alterable and finite, negatively determined not only towards an other, but absolutely within it."

This is the exact logical anatomy of Patañjali's _vikalpa_ (conceptual construction). To use a word (_śabda_) to label an object (_artha_) is fundamentally an act of **negative determination**. To conceptually declare "This is a tree" is to implicitly draw a boundary against everything that is _not_ a tree. The mind operates by negating. Therefore, the object constructed by language is not the infinite, self-identical Living _Esse_; it is a highly fragile, alterable, and finite "something" leaning heavily against an "other" merely to exist.

This proves that the discursive state of _savitarkā_ is finite existence. The yogi trapped in this state is not apprehending the absolute; they are merely ping-ponging across the negative boundaries they themselves constructed via language. The _Varttika_ shows that to exist conceptually is to be trapped in opposition.
