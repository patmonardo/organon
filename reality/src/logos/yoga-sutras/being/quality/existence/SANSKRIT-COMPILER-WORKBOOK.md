### Entry san-i-042 — Savitarkā Samāpatti, The Deliberative State

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_42.ts`
- reference: Yoga Sūtra I.42

Summary:
Sūtra I.42 introduces the first qualification or restriction on pure cognitive absorption (samāpatti). Patañjali says: _tatra śabda-artha-jñāna-vikalpaiḥ saṃkīrṇā savitarkā samāpattiḥ_. ("There [in the mind], the absorption that is mixed with the conceptualization of the word, the meaning, and the knowledge is the deliberative state [savitarkā].") The immediate, pristine transparency of the crystal (I.41) is now compromised or "mixed" (_saṃkīrṇā_). It is polluted by _vikalpa_ (conceptualization), which artificially divides cognition into three distinct pillars: the word (_śabda_), the actual object/meaning (_artha_), and the subjective knowledge (_jñāna_). Due to this linguistic and conceptual contamination, the absorption is no longer immediate; it is _savitarkā_—deliberative, analytical, mediated, and finite.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Saṃkīrṇā (Mixed/Contaminated):** The pure being of the mind is no longer self-identical. It is mixed with components that stand over and against the simple transparency of the crystal.
- k2. **Śabda-Artha-Jñāna (Word, Object, Idea):** The unitive state is fractured into a triadic structure of signification. The mind no longer immediately "is" the object; it mediates the object through language and representation.
- k3. **Vikalpa (Conceptual Construction):** The active engine of the mixture. It is the false attribution of word to meaning, creating a discursive, intellectualized layer over immediate reality.
- k4. **Savitarkā (Deliberative):** The cognitive state defined by this mixture. It is finite, argumentative, heavily mediated, and constitutes the mind's restricted existence.
