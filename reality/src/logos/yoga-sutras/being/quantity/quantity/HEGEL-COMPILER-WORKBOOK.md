### Entry heg-i-044 — The Derivative Comment: Pure Quantity and Continuity

Span:

- sourceFile: `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex` (Lines 377-412)
- reference: Hegel's Doctrine of Being (Derivative Commentary on Sūkṣma-Viṣayā)

Summary:
Acting as the derivative comment on Sūtra I.44, Hegel explicitly defines the transition out of Quality into the realm of the "subtle" (Quantity). Patañjali notes that the exact same mechanics previously mapped to gross qualitative objects are now applied to "subtle objects" (_sūkṣma-viṣayā_). The source text perfectly defines this structural shift from qualitative boundaries to subtle continuums: "Quantity is the determinateness that has become indifferent to being; a limit which is just as much no limit."

Hegel explains that the singular, intense focus of Being-for-itself (the One) inevitably shattered (repulsion). However, this repulsion immediately sublated itself because all the "Ones" are absolutely identical. The result is pure, indifferent continuity: "the repulsion of the many ones which is immediate non-repulsion, their continuity." Because the elements are indifferent and continuously connected without qualitative breaks, the mind has entered the domain of Pure Quantity (Space and Time): "a compact, infinite unity which continues itself into itself." This is the logical derivation of the subtle object—an expanse lacking rigid empirical borders.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Indifference to Being:** Quantity does not care "what" something is (its quality or gross nature). It cares only about magnitude. It is the underlying, mathematically indifferent structure of reality.
- k2. **A Limit Which is No Limit:** Because quantity is continuous (like space), any boundary you draw in it is arbitrary. You can always draw another line. This reflects the sprawling, un-delineated nature of subtle objects.
- k3. **Continuity (The Non-Repulsion of the Many):** The logical mechanism generating the continuum. The shattered qualitative pieces coalesce into a single, homogeneous, subtle expanse.
- k4. **Pure Quantity:** "Real being-for-itself turned back into itself, with as yet no determinateness in it." Before establishing a specific quantum (a degree), the mind rests in pure, empty, continuous extension.
