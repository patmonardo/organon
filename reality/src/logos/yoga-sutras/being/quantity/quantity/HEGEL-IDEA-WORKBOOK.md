## The Varttika: The Deep Demonstration of the Continuum

While Fichte supplies the governing philosophical principle (the outward sweep of the Continuous Productive Imagination after the boundary is dissolved), Hegel supplies the _Varttika_—the exact logical derivation of _why_ the mind is forced to contemplate subtle objects (Quantity).

Sūtra I.44 states that the mechanics of the mind are now transferred to _sūkṣma-viṣayā_ (subtle objects). Patañjali does not derive this transition out of nowhere. It is the necessary collapse of Sūtra I.43. In I.43, the mind achieved absolute singularity ("The One"). But as Hegel's _Science of Logic_ (from the extracted text in `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex`) definitively proved, "The One" is intensely unstable. By asserting its absolute exclusion, it shattered into a multiplicity of "Ones."

But what happens when you have millions of exact, identical "Ones"? They lose their qualitative uniqueness. Because they are perfectly identical, they conceptually collapse back into each other. Hegel writes: "the repulsion of the many ones... is immediate non-repulsion, their continuity."

The pieces fuse into a single, indifferent mass. A "limit which is just as much no limit." This is the monumental shift from Quality into **Pure Quantity**. The yogi, having gazed so intensely at the macroscopic object that it shattered into infinite identical particles (the atomic/subtle), must now contemplate the resulting vast, continuous expanse.

Because this expanse is perfectly continuous, it is indifferent to being—it does not care "what" it is. A mathematical continuum of space is identical whether it holds a mountain or empty air. Sūtra I.44 therefore marks the _Varttika's_ proof that qualitative intensity invariably destroys the object, replacing it with the mathematical, sprawling void of pure, continuous, subtle Quantity.
