### Entry san-i-044 — The Expansion to Subtle Objects

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_44.ts`
- reference: Yoga Sūtra I.44

Summary:
Sūtra I.44 leverages the precise cognitive structures developed in the Quality triad (I.41-I.43) to expand the mind's domain. Patañjali says: _etayaiva savicārā nirvicārā ca sūkṣma-viṣayā vyākhyātā_. ("By this [what has been said about savitarkā and nirvitarkā], the reflective [savicārā] and non-reflective [nirvicārā] states regarding subtle objects [sūkṣma-viṣayā] are also explained.") Having mastered massive, macroscopic objects (Quality), the mind now turns its attention to _sūkṣma-viṣayā_—subtle matters. The "subtle" in philosophical terms represents that which has lost its immediate, distinct, qualitative boundary. It is the underlying continuum (space, time, atoms) that supports the gross object. Thus, the mind enters the realm of pure extension and magnitude, transitioning from Quality into Quantity.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Etayaiva (By this very mechanism):** The transition relies strictly on the previous logic. The mixed/deliberative (_savitarkā_) and purified/singular (_nirvitarkā_) mechanics are transferred wholesale to a new domain.
- k2. **Sūkṣma-viṣayā (Subtle Objects):** The new domain of cognition. The gross, qualitative boundaries of objects have been bypassed in favor of their subtle, underlying continuums (magnitude/extension).
- k3. **Savicārā (Reflective / Mixed Subtle):** Just like I.42, the mind meditating on subtle continuums can be "mixed" with temporal and spatial conceptualizations (deliberating across the continuum).
- k4. **Nirvicārā (Non-reflective / Pure Subtle):** Just like I.43, the mind can purify itself of these temporal/spatial coordinates, apprehending the subtle continuum as an immediate, infinite expanse without qualitative disruption.
