## Narrative Synthesis: The Descent into the Subtle Continuum

The Doctrine of Being achieved a fragile victory in Sūtra I.43. The mind purified its linguistic memory and merged perfectly with the macroscopic object, achieving the singularity of _nirvitarkā_ (non-deliberative absorption). However, this singularity was inherently unstable. In Sūtra I.44, Patañjali initiates a massive structural pivot: _etayaiva savicārā nirvicārā ca sūkṣma-viṣayā vyākhyātā_.

Patañjali states that the exact same dualistic mechanics of "mixed/deliberative" (I.42) and "purified/singular" (I.43) are now to be applied to a completely different class of objects: _sūkṣma-viṣayā_ (subtle matters / subtle objects).

Why does the mind shift from the macroscopic to the subtle? Because the intense, unshakeable focus of _nirvitarkā_ effectively burns right through the surface qualitative boundaries of the gross object. For example, if the yogi achieves absolute absorption on a clay pot, the "potness" (Quality) dissolves under the intensity of the gaze, revealing the underlying, indifferent dust or atoms (the subtle).

This represents the transition from Quality to **Quantity**. Qualitative objects have hard, defining boundaries (a pot is not a table). Subtle objects—space, time, atoms, pure extension—are continuums. They are magnitudes. They lack immediate qualitative distinctness. Sūtra I.44 dictates that the mind must now learn to navigate this sprawling mathematical continuum. It can do so falteringly, mixing its awareness with spatial/temporal coordinates (_savicārā_), or perfectly, apprehending the entire subtle expanse as a single, immediate infinity (_nirvicārā_).
