### Entry fic-i-045 — The Abstraction of the Degree

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_45.ts`
- reference: Fichte's Doctrine of Being (Higher Commentary on Aliṅga)

Summary:
Acting as the higher comment on Sūtra I.45, Fichte's _Science of Knowing_ (1804) maps _aliṅga_ (the unmarked boundary) to the active philosophical positing of the Abstract Limit or Degree. In I.44, the Productive Imagination swept forward as pure continuity. But Knowing cannot rest as an infinite, empty sprawling activity; to know itself, it must arbitrarily arrest that activity.

According to Fichte, the "I" voluntarily halts its own infinite production. It posits a boundary. Since the qualitative object has already been destroyed, this boundary cannot be a new physical "thing." It is purely quantitative. It is an abstract, mathematical "thus far and no further." By arresting its own continuity, the "I" creates a specific "Degree" or Quantum. _Aliṅga_ is precisely this: an unmarked, entirely formal boundary constructed by the absolute Knowing to give scale and structure to its own subtle continuum.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Arrest of the Imagination (Paryavasānam):** The Productive Imagination, having operated as an endless continuum, is actively and arbitrarily halted by the knowing subject.
- k2. **The Positing of the Boundary:** To prevent consciousness from evaporating into pure empty extension, the "I" acts to draw a line in the sand.
- k3. **The Empty/Markless Nature (Aliṅga):** The boundary is purely formal (a mathematical degree). It possesses no qualitative content; it is literally "unmarked" by empirical sensation.
- k4. **Quantum Determination:** Pure expanding activity (Quantity) is now bounded and measured, giving rise to specific Quantitative existence (Quantum).
