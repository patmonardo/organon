### Entry heg-i-045 — The Derivative Comment: Quantum and the Indifferent Limit

Span:

- sourceFile: `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex` (Lines 413-422)
- reference: Hegel's Doctrine of Being (Derivative Commentary on Aliṅga)

Summary:
Acting as the derivative comment on Sūtra I.45, Hegel explicitly documents the transition from Pure Quantity to Quantum (Determinate Quantity). Patañjali notes that the sprawling continuum halts at the _aliṅga_ (the unmanifest / un-marked terminus). The source text beautifully captures this abstract arrest: "Quantity proceeds to determinateness, and this is posited in it as a determinateness that at the same time is none, is only external."

This entirely abstract, formal boundary is the definition of a Quantum. It halts the continuum not by introducing a new physical quality, but merely by drawing an external line: "Quantity becomes quantum. Quantum is indifferent determinateness." Hegel indicates that this border (_aliṅga_) is infinitely porous; because the boundary is indifferent, it is "one that transcends itself, negates itself; as this otherness of otherness, it lapses into infinite progress."

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Quantum (Determinate Quantity):** The transition from sprawling, empty space into a specific mathematical degree or measurement.
- k2. **Determinateness that is None:** This is the exact definition of _aliṅga_ (the unmanifest / unmarked). The boundary exists, but it possesses absolutely no qualitative content or "mark." It is an indifferent mathematical limit.
- k3. **Indifferent Determinateness:** The limit does not fundamentally alter the nature of what is being limited. 10 inches of space is structurally identical to 100 inches of space; the boundary is totally external to the Being itself.
- k4. **Infinite Progress:** Because the limit is indifferent and unmarked, there is logically nothing stopping consciousness from pushing past it. This structural flaw sets up the transition to the Spurious Infinite Ratio of I.46.
