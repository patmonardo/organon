## The Varttika: The Deep Demonstration of the Null Boundary

While Fichte supplies the governing philosophical principle (the "I" arbitrarily arresting the Productive Imagination to avoid losing itself in pure extension), Hegel supplies the _Varttika_—the exact logical derivation of the profound frailty of this boundary.

Patañjali states that the exploration of subtle objects possesses a definitive terminus: the _aliṅga_ (the unmarked / unmanifest ground). This functions as the ultimate baseline limit for the yogi's subtle magnitude.

Hegel's _Science of Logic_ (extracted from `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex`) models exactly why the continuum arrives at this abstract "Quantum" wall, and why it is philosophically terrifying. Pure Quantity (Sūtra I.44) inherently "proceeds to determinateness." It must acquire a boundary to exist as a discrete Quantum. But what kind of boundary can you draw across pure, sprawling space?

Hegel writes: "this is posited in it as a determinateness that at the same time is none, is only external... Quantum is indifferent determinateness."

This is the transcendental proof of _aliṅga_. You can draw a line across empty space to say "this is one degree" (a Quantum), but that line has absolutely zero physical or qualitative reality. The space on the left is perfectly identical to the space on the right. The "mark" is entirely an illusion posited by the Intelligence.

But the _Varttika_ does not stop there; it reveals the fatal defect of the _aliṅga_. If the boundary is completely void of content (indifferent), it cannot actually hold up. The mind inherently realizes that it can just draw another boundary further out, and another beyond that. Thus, Hegel concludes this passage with a dark warning for the yogi attempting to rest at the _aliṅga_: because the limit is indifferent, it constantly "transcends itself, negates itself... it lapses into infinite progress."

Sūtra I.45 therefore sets the stage for disaster. The mind has established an absolute border (_aliṅga_) to secure its meditations, but because this border is quantitatively indifferent, it mathematically invites the restless, infinite traversal of Sūtra I.46.
