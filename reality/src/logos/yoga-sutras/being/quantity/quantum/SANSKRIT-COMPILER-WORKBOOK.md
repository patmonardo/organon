### Entry san-i-045 — The Unmanifest Boundary

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_45.ts`
- reference: Yoga Sūtra I.45

Summary:
Sūtra I.45 establishes the structural limit of the subtle continuum. Patañjali specifies: _sūkṣma-viṣayatvaṃ cāliṅga-paryavasānam_. ("And the state of having subtle objects ends at the unmanifest/indissoluble (_aliṅga_).") As the mind traverses the continuous spectrum of subtle objects (Quantity/space/time/atoms), it must eventually arrive at a logical terminus. Sūtra I.45 declares this terminus to be _aliṅga_. _Liṅga_ means "mark" or "manifest characteristic." Thus, _aliṅga_ is the un-marked, the un-manifest, the absolute primordial substrate (Pradhāna or Prakṛti). It operates not as a new gross object, but as a purely quantitative boundary—an abstract "degree" where the subtle continuum perfectly curtails itself. It defines the absolute edge of quantitative exploration.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Sūkṣma-Viṣayatvaṃ (State of Subtle Objects):** The continuous meditation upon pure extension, magnitude, and space/time parameters described in I.44.
- k2. **Paryavasānam (Termination / Limit):** The continuum is not indefinitely sprawling for the yogic gaze; it possesses an absolute terminal limit where it mathematically halts.
- k3. **Aliṅga (The Unmanifest / Un-marked):** The boundary itself has no qualitative characteristics. It is not an object that can be "seen," but rather a logically necessary halt or "mark-less" substrate.
- k4. **Translation to Quantum:** A quantum is a specific quantity bounded by an indifferent limit (a degree). _Aliṅga_ acts precisely as this mathematical boundary condition for the subtle domain.
