## Narrative Synthesis: The Halt at the Unmarked

In Sūtra I.44, the yogic mind achieved mastery over the continuum. Having abandoned gross objects, it swept through pure magnitude and subtle extension (space, time, the infinitesimal atom/element). But pure, unbridled continuation is dizzying. Sūtra I.45 interjects to provide the structural anchor: _sūkṣma-viṣayatvaṃ cāliṅga-paryavasānam_.

Patañjali states that the traversal of the subtle domain does not fade infinitely into nothingness forever. It has an absolute, terminal end (_paryavasānam_). This terminus is the _aliṅga_.

What is the _aliṅga_? In the Sāṃkhya-Yoga system, reality generates from a primordial substrate called Prakṛti (nature or matter). As Prakṛti evolves, it generates "marked" (_liṅga_) forms—characteristics, attributes, and gross physical boundaries. Therefore, to travel backward down the continuum of the subtle is to move from the highly "marked" to the less and less marked.

Eventually, the mind must hit the absolute bedrock: the _aliṅga_, the completely "unmarked" or unmanifest. It is the logical limit of objective reality. The mind cannot pass beyond it because there is nothing "object-like" left to perceive.

By establishing the _aliṅga_, I.45 provides the necessary mathematical border for the subtle world. It takes the endless, sprawling continuity of pure Quantity and slams a wall down upon it. The continuum is bounded. The mind now has a specific, defined "degree" or magnitude (Quantum) to hold onto: the exact span stretching between the gross marker and the unmanifest ground.
