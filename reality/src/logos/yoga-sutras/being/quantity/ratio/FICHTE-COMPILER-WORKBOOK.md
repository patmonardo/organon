### Entry fic-i-046 — The Hiatus Irrationalis and the Bad Infinity

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_46.ts`
- reference: Fichte's Doctrine of Being (Higher Commentary on Sabīja Samādhi)

Summary:
Acting as the higher comment on Sūtra I.46, Fichte's _Science of Knowing_ (1804) maps _sabījaḥ_ (with seed) to the devastating structural flaw of the formal Quantitative limit: the generating of the "Bad Infinity" via the _hiatus irrationalis_. In I.45, the "I" posited an abstract limit (a degree) to anchor its continuous activity. But Fichte warns that because this limit is purely formal, it acts identically to the original rigid object (the Not-I). It sets up an absolute _hiatus irrationalis_—an unbridgeable gap between the Knowing subject and the mathematical limit it posited.

Because the limit is unbridgeable and indifferent, the "I" constantly pushes past it to posit another limit, and then another limit, in a relentless, futile chain. Fichte derides this mathematical progression as the spurious or "bad infinity." It never reaches the Living _Esse_; it just perpetually generates more abstract "seeds" of otherness. This perfectly translates Patañjali's warning: all meditation dependent upon a limit is "with seed" and fundamentally un-free.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **The Hiatus Irrationalis (The Seed):** The unbridgeable, irrational gap generated when Knowing artificially sets up a limit against itself. The dualism of Knower and Limit strictly prevents liberation.
- k2. **The Bad Infinity (Infinite Progress):** Because the mathematical limit has no true qualitative power to hold the Intelligence, Knowing constantly oversteps it, establishing a relentless, spurious progress of limits.
- k3. **Formal/Quantitative Ratio:** Knowing is trapped defining itself solely in relation to the limit it just produced. This unending numerical ratio replaces True Infinite self-reference.
- k4. **Translation of Sabījaḥ:** The formal boundary acts precisely as a "seed" (an enduring taint of dualism) driving the endless cyclical generation of the discursive intellect.
