## The Bhasya: The Principle of the Spurious Infinity

Sūtra I.46 lands like a hammer blow on the yogic progression: _tā eva sabījaḥ samādhiḥ_. Everything achieved so far—even the mastery of the subtle continuum up to the unmanifest limit (_aliṅga_)—is "with seed." It is not liberation; it is bondage. Johann Gottlieb Fichte’s _Science of Knowing_ provides the brilliant philosophical _Bhāṣya_ for this devastating conclusion, exposing it as the trap of the Spurious (or Bad) Infinity.

In Sūtra I.45, the philosopher posited an abstract mathematical limit (a degree) to stop Knowing from evaporating into pure empty extension. This seemed like a victory. But Fichte locates a fatal flaw: the "I" and the Limit are now locked in an abstract dualism. Fichte calls this the _hiatus irrationalis_—the irrational gap.

Because the limit is just a formal number or degree, there is absolutely nothing stopping the mind from extending past it. So the mind leaps the boundary and posits a new limit at +1. Then it leaps that boundary and posits a boundary at +2.

The mind mistakes this endless outward expansion for True Infinity. But Fichte laughs at this mathematical progression. It is a spurious, bad infinity. You are not arriving at the Living _Esse_; you are simply running on a treadmill, generating an endless succession of finite numbers. The philosopher is trapped in a Ratio between their own reaching activity and the empty boundary they keep erecting.

This mathematical, formal boundary is the exact definition of Patañjali’s "seed" (_bīja_). It is the lingering structural necessity of needing an "other" (a limit) to hold consciousness steady. As long as Knowing requires a numerical degree or spatial limit to define itself, it remains "with seed," endlessly cycling through the bad infinity of Quantitative Ratio.
