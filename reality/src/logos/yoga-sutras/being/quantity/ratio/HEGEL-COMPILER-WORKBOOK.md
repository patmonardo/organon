### Entry heg-i-046 — The Derivative Comment: Quantitative Ratio and Infinite Progress

Span:

- sourceFile: `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex` (Lines 423-441)
- reference: Hegel's Doctrine of Being (Derivative Commentary on Sabīja Samādhi)

Summary:
Acting as the derivative comment on Sūtra I.46, Hegel's text explicitly documents the fatal flaw of the Quantum and its collapse into Quantitative Ratio. Patañjali states that all preceding meditations are "with seed" (_sabīja_), meaning they are structurally dependent on an external limit that prevents total liberation. Hegel models this exactly in the transition from Quantum to Ratio. Because the Quantum (the limit drawn in I.45) is indifferent to the Being it limits, it inherently "transcends itself into its otherness." Hegel notes: "this otherness in which it has its determination is at the same time posited, is another quantum."

Because a Quantum only has meaning relative to another Quantum, the mind is trapped in a _Ratio_. Hegel states: "At the foundation of this relation there still lies the externality of quantum... it is indifferent quanta that relate themselves to each other... in this being-outside-itself." The Ratio is the dialectical equivalent of the "_bīja_" or seed—the structural necessity to remain "outside itself" in an external relation. Hegel explicitly warns that this leads to "infinite progress" (the spurious infinity) and concludes that the ratio is "only a formal unity of quality and quantity" demanding a transition into "their absolute unity, in measure."

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Infinite Progress:** Because the limit is indifferent, it constantly negates itself, plunging the logic into an endless, futile outward progression.
- k2. **Quantitative Ratio:** The realization that a single quantum cannot exist alone; it derives its determination exclusively by relating to "another quantum." This is the core logic of the _sabīja_ dependency.
- k3. **Externality/Being-Outside-Itself:** The ratio proves that the mind has externalized itself. It is relying on a connection in "being-outside-itself" (externality) rather than pure self-identical Being.
- k4. **Dialectic of the Formal Unity:** Because the ratio is only "formal," it sets the stage for the dialectic to tear it down and sublate it into "absolute unity" (Measure / I.47).
