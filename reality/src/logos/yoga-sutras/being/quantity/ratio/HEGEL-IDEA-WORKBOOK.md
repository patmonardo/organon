## The Varttika: The Deep Demonstration of the Ratio (The Seed)

While Fichte supplies the governing philosophical principle (the philosopher trapped on the treadmill of the Spurious Infinity), Hegel supplies the _Varttika_—the microscopic transcendental proof of _how_ the limit logically forces the mind into a state of structural dependency/bondage (the Seed).

Patañjali concludes his analysis of gross and subtle meditations with a dire warning: _tā eva sabījaḥ samādhiḥ_ ("All these are absorptions with seed"). Even reaching the absolute, unmanifest boundary of the universe (_aliṅga_ in I.45) leaves the mind chained.

Hegel’s _Science of Logic_ (from the extracted text in `prajna-greater-bhasya.tex`) proves exactly why the limit (_aliṅga_ / Quantum) operates as a chain. Hegel writes that because the Quantum is merely an indifferent limit, it inherently "transcends itself into its otherness, in such a way that this otherness in which it has its determination is at the same time posited, is another quantum."

Think of a number. The number 5 has no meaning as a pure, isolated singularity. It only has "determination" (meaning) if the number 6 and the number 4 are simultaneously posited. A Quantum inherently demands a **Ratio**. Hegel states that Quantum "refers to itself as into its otherness."

This is the exact logical mechanism of the "Seed" (_bīja_). The mind, trying to rest on the unmanifest boundary, finds that the boundary conceptually slips. The boundary points to the space _beyond_ the boundary. The mind must posit another quantum, then another, ad infinitum.

Hegel writes: "At the foundation of this relation there still lies the externality... being-outside-itself." The yogi is not free. By utilizing a "limit"—whether gross or subtle—the mind has externalized itself into a Ratio. It requires an "other" to maintain its focus, and thus remains trapped "with seed." The _Varttika_ proves that Quantitative meditation is a spurious dead-end; only the dialectical leap into Measure (the sublation of both Quality and Quantity in I.47) can break the curse of the Seed.
