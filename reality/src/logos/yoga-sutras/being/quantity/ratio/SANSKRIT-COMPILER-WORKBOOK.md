### Entry san-i-046 — Sabīja Samādhi (Seed-Bearing Absorption)

Span:

- sourceFile: `ys_i_46.ts`
- reference: Yoga Sūtra I.46

Summary:
Sūtra I.46 issues a profound, concluding warning regarding the entire meditative sequence accomplished thus far across Quality and Quantity. Patañjali states: _tā eva sabījaḥ samādhiḥ_. ("All these constitute cognitive absorption with seed [sabījaḥ].") Whether meditating on gross objects (I.41-43) or subtle continuums/boundaries (I.44-45), the mind has not achieved total liberation. It is still operating with a "seed" (_bīja_). The seed represents latent dependency. Because the mind is still referencing an object—even an abstract, unmanifest mathematical limit (_aliṅga_)—it is caught in an endless relational loop. The object is posited, the mind grasps it, but the structural distinction remains. This state is mathematically boundless but spiritually finite, as it endlessly relies on a seed of "otherness" to sustain the absorption.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. **Tā Eva (All These):** This refers collectively to the preceding four modes of absorption: savitarkā, nirvitarkā (gross quality), and savicārā, nirvicārā (subtle quantity).
- k2. **Bīja (The Seed):** The latent impression or relational dependency. The cognitive process still structurally relies on something "other" than pure spirit (an object, a mark, or a boundary) to maintain its focus.
- k3. **Sabījaḥ Samādhiḥ (Absorption with Seed):** The state is profound and intensely concentrated, yet fundamentally un-free because it is bound to the external ratio of "ascriber" and "ascribed."
- k4. **Translation to Ratio:** The "seed" perfectly matches the mathematical definition of a formal Ratio: the mind constantly leaping out to a posited Quantum (boundary), defining itself only in relation to its otherness.
