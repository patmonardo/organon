## Narrative Synthesis: The Warning of the Seed

Sūtras I.41 through I.45 document a magnificent, staggering expansion of human consciousness. The yogi moved from the immediate, transparent identity with gross objects into a total mastery over the subtle continuums of space, time, and atomic matter, bringing everything to rest at the abstract, unmanifest boundary of reality (_aliṅga_).

Yet, in Sūtra I.46, Patañjali drops a severe, uncompromising qualification: _tā eva sabījaḥ samādhiḥ_. "All these are absorptions with seed."

This is the ultimate philosophical warning. No matter how rarefied, how subtle, or how abstract the object of meditation has become, the mind has not yet broken free. Why? Because the structure of the meditation still relies on a "seed" (_bīja_).

A seed is a latent potential, something not fully actualized that relies on external conditions to exist. In this context, the seed is the lingering trace of objectification. The mind still needs _something_—even if it is just an unmarked boundary (_aliṅga_)—to relate to. The mind posits the boundary, reaches it, and holds it.

This creates a vicious infinite loop. Because the mind defines its absorption perfectly in relation to the object, it is trapped in a Ratio to that object. The Yogi is not resting in the spontaneous, free luminosity of pure consciousness; they are mathematically tethered to the limit they themselves drew. If the object shifts, the mind must shift. If the mind pushes past the boundary, it must just draw another mathematical boundary further out. The "seed" prevents ultimate liberation, turning the staggering achievements of Quality and Quantity into an endless, un-free mathematical treadmill.
