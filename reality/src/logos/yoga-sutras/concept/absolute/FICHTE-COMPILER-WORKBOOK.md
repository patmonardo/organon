- k2. Recognizing the "sequence" maps to discerning the primordial description of this immanent projection—the "We" is constituted by pure ideal seeing.
- k3. The Concept is a product of genesis, not a given premise.

### Entry fic-iii-054 — The Four Terms and the Manifesting Principle

Span:

- sourceFile: `src/logos/ys/ys_iii_54.ts` (Lecture 26 cont.)

Summary:
Yoga’s recognition of identity by removing delimiting markers (_jāti_, _lakṣaṇa_, _deśa_) corresponds to Fichte’s deduction of objectivity as strictly identical with genesis. Fichte identifies four fundamental terms (seeing as externality, being as self-enclosure, their twofold unity, and the drive). Once the drive (which projects external delimitations) is annulled, only the pure living synthesis remains recognized as the identical whole.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. Objectivity and genesis are entirely one; genesis is the inner externalization.
- k2. The absence of external delimitation mirrors Fichte's realization that any projected limitation (the "drive") must be struck down in appearance to find the truth.
- k3. Being is sought only in absolute seeing (a single manifesting principle).

### Entry fic-iii-055 — The Self-Positing Insight of Absolute Reason

Span:

- sourceFile: `src/logos/ys/ys_iii_55.ts` (Lecture 27)

Summary:
Patañjali’s _Tāraka_—the non-sequential, absolutely total insight—maps exactly to Fichte’s "Absolute Insight of Reason" (Lecture 27). Fichte demonstrates that reason makes itself; it is intrinsically genetic, necessary, and acts with an immediate totality (non-sequential/_akrama_). It encompasses all possible objectivity because existence is strictly identical to reason speaking about seeing (_sarva-viṣaya_).

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. The "seeing posited as living" necessitates immediate actuality. The inference goes from posited essence to immediate existence.
- k2. Absolute reason is absolute effect: it is unmediated (non-sequential) and permeates itself entirely.
- k3. The Doctrine of Reason asserts the totality of this self-determination—it is "all objects in all respects" because there is no reality left outside its genesis.

### Entry fic-iii-056 — The End of Dual Ambiguity and Closure of Science

Span:

- sourceFile: `src/logos/ys/ys_iii_56.ts` (Lecture 28)

Summary:
Patañjali’s definition of _Kaivalya_ as the exact equivalence of purity between _Sattva_ and _Puruṣa_ corresponds perfectly to Fichte’s climactic resolution of the "dual occurrence of reason" in Lecture 28. Fichte eliminates the ambiguity between Subjective reason and Objective reason. When the midpoint (pure thinking) is no longer split or objectified, the single primordial effect is grasped. The "equality of purity" is the sublation of the subject/object disjunction. Fichte declares the Science closed entirely upon this identity.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. Reason appears ambiguously twice: in us (subject) and outside (object).
- k2. The _śuddhi-sāmya_ (equality of purity) is the recognition that the "making of being" and the "making of making" flow from the very same identical point. Pure intellectual activity fills and negates the subject/object gap.
- k3. If this midpoint is abstracted from its objectification, the absolute "I" / "We" remains in unbroken equality.
- k4. For Fichte, failure to hold this equality throws the system down into the fivefoldness/25 forms (Prakṛti/Samkhya taxonomy). Maintaining it is _Kaivalya_ (the closing of the Science of Knowing).
