### Entry heg-iii-054 — Absolute Identity Through Sublation of the Understanding

Span:

- reference: The Hegelian logic of Identity and the Concept.

Summary:
Identity in Yoga (sameness via no delimitation of _jāti_, _lakṣaṇa_, _deśa_) correlates precisely to the Concept negating the external categories of the Understanding (_Verstand_). The Understanding tries to define things by spatial separation and taxonomic class. True speculative reason recognizes that if these external limits are sublated, what remains is the absolute identity of the Concept with itself.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. _Jāti_, _lakṣaṇa_, and _deśa_ are predicates of formal Understanding (abstract Universality and external location).
- k2. Negating them yields Speculative Identity: the recognition of the concrete Universal.

### Entry heg-iii-055 — Tāraka as the Systemic Encyclopedia

Span:

- reference: Hegel's mature system (Logic, Nature, Spirit) as totality.

Summary:
_Tāraka_ embodies the supreme characteristic of the Hegelian totality. Because it knows all objects in all respects without sequence (_akramam_), it mirrors the "Absolute Idea" as the systemic whole. It establishes the bridge between Logic, Nature, and Spirit seamlessly, acting as the finalized Encyclopedia of philosophical sciences.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. _Sarva-viṣaya_ and _sarvathā-viṣaya_ correlate to Hegel's insistence on the absolute systematicity of truth (the Truth is the Whole).
- k2. _Akramam_ (non-sequentiality) reflects that in the Absolute Idea, logical deduction is circular, simultaneous, and eternal—not chronologically stepwise.

### Entry heg-iii-056 — The Absolute Idea and the End of Logic

Span:

- reference: Hegel's Absolute Idea (The final chapter of the Science of Logic).

Summary:
_Kaivalya_ via the equal purity of _Sattva_ and _Puruṣa_ represents the absolute zenith of Hegel's dialectic: the Absolute Idea. In the Absolute Idea, the Subject (the cognitive method / _sattva_) and the Object (the logical content / _puruṣa_) are completely identical. The "mirror" of the Concept adds nothing to the Truth; the Truth recognizes itself perfectly. This identity ends the _Science of Logic_, leaving Spirit entirely free and independent.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. _Sattva_ equates to the achieved speculative dialectic (the perfect Concept).
- k2. _Puruṣa_ equates to the absolute logical Truth.
- k3. _Śuddhi-sāmye_ (equality of purity) is the Hegelian identity of Subject and Object—the Method is universally one with the Content.
- k4. _Kaivalya_ maps to the consequence of the Absolute Idea: the Logic closes its circle, and the Idea freely releases itself into Nature and Spirit.
