### Entry ys-iii-053 — Discrimination of Moments and Sequence

Span:

- reference: YS III.53 (kṣaṇa-tat-kramayoḥ saṃyamād viveka-jaṃ jñānam)

Summary:
By performing _saṃyama_ on the ultimate atomic unit of time (_kṣaṇa_ / the discrete moment) and its sequential nexus (_krama_), the yogi attains knowledge born of absolute discrimination (_viveka-jaṃ jñānam_). This represents the closure of the "middle" or pure conditioned path in the _Vibhūti-pāda_, securing formal certainty governed by transition.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. _Kṣaṇa_ is the instantaneous atomic quantum of time.
- k2. _Krama_ is the sequential flow or associative chain connecting these moments.
- k3. _Viveka-jaṃ jñānam_ is the clear, non-finitizing discernment that emerges, grasping reality not as a blurred mass but via precise, discrete articulation.

### Entry ys-iii-054 — Recognition of Sameness via Non-Delimitation

Span:

- reference: YS III.54 (jāti-lakṣaṇa-deśair anyatānavacchedāt tulyayos tataḥ pratipattiḥ)

Summary:
Sūtra III.54 establishes a strict logical criterion for identity/sameness: When two instances cannot be delimited (_anavacchedāt_) or differentiated by their general class (_jāti_), specific characteristic (_lakṣaṇa_), or spatial locus (_deśa_), they are recognized (_pratipattiḥ_) as identical or the same (_tulyayoḥ_). This applies the temporal discrimination of III.53 to establish perfect identity across the flow of time.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. Identity is defined negatively via the absence of delimiting predicates (_jāti_, _lakṣaṇa_, _deśa_).
- k2. When external/categorical differentiation fails, pure continuity and sameness are recognized.

### Entry ys-iii-055 — Tāraka: Omniscient Transcendental Insight

Span:

- reference: YS III.55 (tārakaṃ sarva-viṣayaṃ sarvathā-viṣayam akramaṃ ceti viveka-jaṃ jñānam)

Summary:
Discriminative knowledge reaches its epistemic peak as _Tāraka_ (the deliverative or "leading-across" insight). It is defined by three absolute magnitudes: it encompasses all objects without exception (_sarva-viṣayaṃ_), in every possible mode or respect (_sarvathā-viṣayam_), and it grasps this totality simultaneously, without sequence or sequential logic (_akramam_).

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. _Tāraka_ designates the transcendental bridge or crossing-over function of insight.
- k2. _Sarva-viṣayam_ means it possesses total objective scope; nothing is mathematically left out.
- k3. _Sarvathā-viṣayam_ means it holds these objects in all their infinite respects (all _dharmas_).
- k4. _Akramam_ means the knowing is whole-given, immediate, and non-stepwise. It subverts linear temporal reasoning.

### Entry ys-iii-056 — Kaivalya via Equality of Purity

Span:

- reference: YS III.56 (sattva-puruṣayoḥ śuddhi-sāmye kaivalyam iti)

Summary:
The ultimate closure of the _Vibhūti-pāda_ and the absolute terminus of the systemic epistemology. When the luminosity/clarity of the cognitive instrument (_sattva_ / _buddhi_) achieves exact equality of purity (_śuddhi-sāmye_) with the absolute non-active witness (_puruṣa_), the resulting state is _kaivalya_ (liberation/aloneness).

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. _Sattva_ represents the highest, purest manifestation of _Prakṛti_ (Nature), functioning as the cognitive mirror.
- k2. _Śuddhi-sāmye_ dictates that the mirror must add absolutely no remainder or distortion; it must be isomorphic to the Seer.
- k3. _Kaivalya_ is the ontological isolation that results when the instrument is perfectly transparent, causing the un-mixing of Spirit and Nature.
