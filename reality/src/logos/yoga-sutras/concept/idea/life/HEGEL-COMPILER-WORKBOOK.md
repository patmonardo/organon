# YS III.16: Hegel Mapping - Life

## 1. Hegel's Doctrine of the Concept: The Idea -> Life

In Hegel's _Science of Logic_, Objective Logic (Mechanism, Chemism, Teleology) resolves into **The Idea**. The Idea is the absolute unity of Concept and Objectivity. The first immediate form of the Idea is **Life**.

**YS III.16:** "By performing Samyama on the three transformations, arises the knowledge of the past and the future."

### 1.1 The Sublation of Objectivity into the Living Subject

Hegel defines Life as "the concept which, distinct from its objectivity, simple in itself, permeates that objectivity and... has its means within it." In Teleology (YS III.15), purpose was still somewhat external to the mechanical world it manipulated. In Life, the Concept fully animates objectivity.

Patañjali describes this animation structurally. The Yogin performs _Samyama_ on the _pariṇāma-traya_ (the three objective transformations of YS III.13). The Yogin does not just command them objectively; the Yogin absorbs them. The mechanical world is digested into the living intellect.

### 1.2 The Mastery of the Timeline

Hegel writes that the life-process consists in "sublating its presupposition, of positing as negative the objectivity indifferent to it, and of actualizing itself as the power and negative unity of this objectivity."

The indifferent objectivity is time itself—the blind progression of past (_atīta_) and future (_anāgata_). By unifying the three transformations in _Samyama_, the Yogin actualizes as the negative power over time. The Yogin holds the entirety of the objective timeline within a single, living pulse of _jñānam_ (knowledge). The timeline ceases to be an external sequence determining the subject; it becomes the subject's own living biological-conceptual rhythm. The Concept has become fully animated.
