# Concept/Idea/Life - HEGEL IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Demonstration (_Varttika_): The Animation of Objectivity

Fichte's principle demonstrates how the ego absorbs time back into its own living synthesis. Hegel’s _Science of Logic_ provides the transcendental demonstration of how Objective Logic (Mechanism, Chemism, Teleology) necessarily completes itself and passes over into The Idea, specifically its first immediate form: **Life**.

**1. The Defeat of External Purpose**
In Teleology (YS III.15), the Subjective Concept asserted its Purpose over the mechanical sequence of the objective world. But it did so externally; it used the world as a tool. Hegel argues this separation cannot hold. If the objective world is perfectly pliable to the Subject's Purpose, it is because there is no fundamental difference between them. The Subjective Concept must permeate the Objective world entirely.

"The concept is as a soul that has fully animated its objective reality."

**2. Life as the Internalization of the Timeline**
This full animation is Life. Hegel defines Life as the concept having its means within it; it is the realized purpose identical with itself.

Patañjali describes this animation practically. By performing _Samyama_ on the three objective transformations (_pariṇāma-traya_), the Yogin achieves _jñānam_ (immediate knowledge) of the past and future. The objective timeline (which, in Mechanism, marched on indifferently) is consumed by the living subject.

Hegel writes that the life-process is "sublating its presupposition, of positing as negative the objectivity indifferent to it." The timeline is the ultimate "indifferent objectivity." It flows on regardless of us. But through _Samyama_, the Yogin negates this indifference. The Yogin pulls the vast, external expanse of the past and future into the singular, living, subjective present of the Idea.

**3. The Totality of the Living Subject**
The Yogin is no longer operating _on_ the world as a detached Teleological Will; the Yogin is _living_ the entire span of the world's transformations within their own intellect. The external world has been digested. The physical elements are no longer foreign matter; their entire temporal trajectory (_atīta_ to _anāgata_) is grasped as the immediate organic life of the Absolute Idea recognizing itself.
