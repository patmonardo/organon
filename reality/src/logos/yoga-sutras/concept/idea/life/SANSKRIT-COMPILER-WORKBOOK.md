# YS III.16: The Living Timeline (Life)

## 1. Sanskrit Text & Transliteration

**Sutra III.16**
परिणामत्रयसंयमाद् अतीतानागतज्ञानम्॥ ३.१६॥
_pariṇāma-traya-saṃyamād atītānāgata-jñānam_

## 2. Philological Breakdown

- **pariṇāma (परिणाम):** transformation; modification; change.
- **traya (त्रय):** three; threefold (referring to the transformations of form, time, and condition from YS III.13).
- **saṃyamād (संयमात्):** perfectly integrated concentration (from applying Samyama).
- **atīta (अतीत):** past; gone by.
- **anāgata (अनागत):** future; not yet come.
- **jñānam (ज्ञानम्):** knowledge; direct cognition.

## 3. Direct Translation

"By performing Samyama on the three transformations (of form, time, and condition), [arises] the knowledge of the past and the future."

## 4. Systemic Analysis

This sutra marks the gateway to the _siddhis_ (supernatural powers) and corresponds structurally to the transition from Objective Logic to The Idea in German Idealism. Specifically, it maps to the first stage of The Idea: **Life**.

### The Threefold Transformation as Objectivity

In the previous triad (YS III.13-15), the Yogin deconstructed the objective world into its mechanical transformations (_pariṇāma-traya_), its chemical substance (_dharmī_), and its teleological sequence (_krama_). Objectivity was completely exhausted as an independent realm.

### The Return to the Living Subject

In YS III.16, the Yogin turns the laser of _Samyama_ upon these very transformations. Instead of being determined by time, the Yogin internalizes time. By integrating the three transformations into a single cognitive act, the absolute timeline (past and future) is brought into the immediate present of the living subject.

This is the definition of **Life**: the Concept permeating objectivity, sublating its alien externality, and treating the entirety of the objective timeline as its own internal organ of knowledge. The Yogin is no longer in time; time is in the Yogin. The whole mechanical and chemical process of the universe is vitalized into immediate, living knowledge (_jñānam_).
