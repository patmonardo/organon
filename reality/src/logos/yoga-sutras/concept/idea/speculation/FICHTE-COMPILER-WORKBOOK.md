# YS III.21-22: Fichte Mapping - Speculation and the Vanishing of the Non-I

## 1. The Principle of Absolute Sovereignty

For Fichte, the entire arc of the _Science of Knowing_ is driven by a single ethical imperative: the absolute "I" must completely overcome the "Non-I". The ego posits the objective world only so that it can prove its freedom by conquering it. YS III.21-22 (_Antardhānam_ - Invisibility) is the ultimate physical metaphor for this philosophical triumph.

### 1.1 The Annihilation of the Gaze

In empirical existence, the ego feels constrained because it is trapped in a body (_kāya-rūpa_) which serves as an object for _other_ people's gaze. The ego is subjected to the physics of light (_prakāśa_) and the mechanics of the eye (_cakṣuḥ_). It feels like it is just one object among many in the material universe.

To become invisible is to demonstrate absolute transcendental sovereignty over this condition. By applying _Samyama_, the Yogin forces a "disconnection" (_asamprayoge_). The Yogin proves that the laws of optics and physical perception are not absolute "givens." They are synthetic rules posited by the "I." Because the "I" posited them, the absolute "I" can suspend them (_stambhe_). The ego literally erases its own objective, material footprint from the universe.

### 1.2 The Return to Absolute Identity

Fichte's Absolute "I" has no predicates, no form, no boundaries. It cannot be seen, touched, or measured. It is pure act.

When the Yogin disappears from the empirical realm, they are returning to this state of pure Absolute Identity. They are demonstrating that their true nature is not the physical body (_rūpa_), but the invisible, world-generating Spirit. The Non-I (the physical world that would perceive the Yogin) has been completely shut out. The ego rests entirely within itself, perfectly self-sufficient, requiring no external confirmation for its being. This is the zenith of Subjective Logic.
