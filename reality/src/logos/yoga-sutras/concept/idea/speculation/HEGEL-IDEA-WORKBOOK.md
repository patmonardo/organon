# Concept/Idea/Speculation - HEGEL IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Demonstration (_Varttika_): The Withdrawal into the Absolute Idea

Fichte outlines the ego's triumphant withdrawal from the objective gaze. Hegel’s _Science of Logic_ provides the transcendental demonstration of exactly what this means logically: the transition from the striving of finite Cognition to the sovereign repose of the **Absolute Idea** (Speculation).

**1. The Sublation of Striving**
In Cognition (YS III.17-20), the Idea was divided. It sought to theoretically know the "Other" (the objective world, other minds). But this theoretical striving implies a lack; it still treats the objective world as a separate realm that needs to be conquered.

Hegel writes: "The absolute idea has taken back into itself the highest opposition... All the rest is error, confusion, opinion, striving... the absolute idea alone is being, imperishable life." The Absolute Idea sublates the need to strive against the "Other" because it completely internalizes the "Other."

**2. The Invisibility of the Concept**
Patañjali’s _antardhānam_ (invisibility) in YS III.21-22 is the perfect analogue for the Absolute Idea. In visibility, the body (_kāya-rūpa_) is caught in the web of mechanical and chemical connections (light, eyes, physical space). By performing _Samyama_ on this form, the Yogin suspends its receptive power (_śakti-stambhe_).

The physical body vanishes, but the Yogin does not cease to exist. Instead, the Yogin withdraws into the pure rationality of the Concept. Hegel describes the Absolute Idea as "person impenetrable, atomic subjectivity." By becoming invisible to the external world, the Yogin demonstrates this impenetrability. The objective universe cannot touch the Concept, because the Concept has suspended the very rules of physical interaction.

**3. The System Closing Upon Itself**
By disappearing, the Yogin proves that their reality is identical with the pure Concept. They exist entirely _in and for themselves_. The Absolute Idea has no "outside." It has run through the totality of its determinations (Mechanism, Chemism, Teleology, Life, Cognition) and now rests in its own absolute certainty. This is the end of Subjective Logic. The Yogin is now perfectly positioned to freely discharge this Absolute Idea back out into the world as pure, unconditioned Nature (the transition into the remaining _siddhis_).
