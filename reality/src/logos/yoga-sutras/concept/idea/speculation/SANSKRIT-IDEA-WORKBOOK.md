# Concept/Idea/Speculation - SANSKRIT IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Synthesis: The Vanishing Point of Absolute Subjectivity

With Yoga Sutras III.21-22, Patañjali concludes the strict philosophical progression of Subjective Logic. Having interiorized objective time (Life) and objective meaning (Cognition), the Yogin performs the ultimate act of subjective sovereignty: the erasure of the objective body (_antardhānam_, invisibility). This perfectly mirrors the culmination of German Idealism in Speculation, or the Absolute Idea.

We can synthesize these dialects as follows:

**1. The Absolute Identity of Theory and Practice**
In Hegelian terms, the Absolute Idea is the perfect coincidence of Theoretical Reason (knowing the world) and Practical Reason (willing the world).

Patañjali demonstrates this coincidence literally. The Yogin applies _Samyama_ (theoretical knowing) to the physical mechanism of sight—understanding how the form of the body (_kāya-rūpa_) interacts with the light of the eye (_cakṣuḥ-prakāśa_). But this theoretical knowing instantly translates into practical mastery: the Yogin suspends the power of that form (_stambhe_). The knowledge of physics is identical to the power to rewrite physics.

**2. Reclaiming the Body from the Object Sphere**
For Fichte, the empirical body is a massive philosophical liability—it is the one piece of the "I" that feels completely subordinate to the dead, mechanical laws of the "Non-I".

By achieving invisibility (YS III.21) and extending this power to silence and the other senses (YS III.22), the Yogin reclaims the body. The Yogin unplugs the body from the mechanical network of the universe (_asamprayoge_). The physical form is no longer an objective given; it is a manifestation that exists entirely at the pleasure of the Yogin's sovereign Will.

**3. The Threshold of Free Outgoing (_Siddhis_)**
When the Yogin vanishes, the Yogin becomes the Absolute Idea. Hegel says the Absolute Idea is impenetrable, pure subjectivity that rests in absolute certainty. Having withdrawn completely from the mechanical world, the Yogin is now a pure, invisible engine of Concept.

Because the Yogin has completely severed their dependence on the external sequence of reality, they are now free to step back _into_ reality on their own absolute terms. The subsequent dozens of sutras detailing various _siddhis_ (knowing the cosmos, mastering the elements, traveling through space) are no longer acts of a struggling subject trying to control the world. They are the playful, absolute outpourings of the Absolute Idea freely determining the world as its own Nature. The Logic is complete.
