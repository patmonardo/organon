# YS III.14: Fichte Mapping - Chemism and the Transcendental Substratum

## 1. The Principle of Inherent Continuity

If Mechanism (YS III.13) represents the ego looking outward at the raw, seemingly chaotic transformations of the physical world, Chemism (YS III.14) represents the ego's recognition that this external world is held together by the same transcendental glue that holds the ego together.

Patañjali describes the _dharmī_ (the substance or property-bearer) as that which continuously threads through the past, present, and future forms. For Fichte, this describes the very structure of the ego discovering its mirror in the objective world.

### 1.1 The Ego and the Dharmī

In Fichte's system, the "I" is the ultimate substance that persists through its endlessly changing representations. It is the permanent _dharmī_ underlying the shifting _dharma_ of thoughts and sensations.

When the transcendental philosopher looks at external reality, they realize that objectivity cannot be a disjointed, mechanical series of flashes. If it were, knowledge would be impossible. The "givenness" of the world must have a substantial continuity (an affinity, a "chemical" bond). Patañjali's _dharmī_ is the objective equivalent of the absolute "I". The ego recognizes that the external object maintains an identical internal core underneath its temporal changes, proving that the object is structured precisely like theoretical Reason itself.

### 1.2 Sublating Empty Time

Without the _dharmī_, time is a terrifying void that instantly annihilates everything in the present (_śānta_) and replaces it with the unknown future (_avyapadeśya_). Fichte fought relentlessly against this despair.

YS III.14 completely defeats this void. By proving the existence of an underlying substance (_dharmī_) that "closely follows and is common to" (_anupātī_) the three phases of time, Patañjali creates an indestructible continuum. The past, present, and future are bonded together essentially. The external world is not crumbling into dust at every moment; it is a unified substance dynamically changing its predicates, just as the eternal Spirit dynamically engages in knowing itself.
