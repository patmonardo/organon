# Concept/Object/Chemism - FICHTE IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Principle (_Bhasya_): The Immanence of the Transcendental Substratum

In YS III.14, Patañjali declares that beneath the changing phases of time (past, present, and future) lies a continuous property-bearer (_dharmī_). For Fichte, this marks the crucial moment where the transcendental philosopher, having just realized that the objective world operates mechanically (YS III.13), now discovers the inner _affinity_ of that world. The world is not just a machine; it has a substance.

**1. Surviving the Flux of Time**
Without a _dharmī_, the timeline is a nightmare of annihilation. Fichte observed that if reality is just a sequence of mechanical forms (_dharmas_), then when a form passes away (_śānta_), reality vanishes, only to be magically replaced by a new form (_udita_). Knowledge would be impossible because there would be no continuity connecting the two disparate moments.

To establish the Science of Knowing, the ego must deduce an immovable substratum. The ego knows that _it_ persists through its changing thoughts; therefore, when it looks at the objective world, it must posit that the object, too, persists through its changing phenomenal appearances.

**2. The Rejection of Indifference**
In Mechanism, objects bump into one another indifferently. But Fichte argues that the ego's pure act of positing cannot result in pure indifference. There must be an underlying unity. This translates logically into Chemical affinity. The object is drawn through its past, present, and future states precisely because those states "closely follow" (_anupātī_) and belong to its singular substance.

**3. The Mirror of the "I"**
Patañjali’s _dharmī_ is the objective mirror of the transcendental "I". The ego realizes that just as it is the unbroken substance underlying its own subjective history, every object in the mechanical universe possesses a _dharmī_ that binds its temporal sequence together.

The external world is therefore chemically bonded by the exact same logic that bonds the subject. This deepens the Yogin's mastery over nature. The Yogin no longer merely manipulates external, indifferent variables (Mechanism); they now have access to the underlying substantial thread (Chemism) that unites an object's future potential (_avyapadeśya_) with its present manifestation (_udita_).
