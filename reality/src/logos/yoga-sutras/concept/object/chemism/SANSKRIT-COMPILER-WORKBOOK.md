# YS III.14: The Underlying Substance of Time (Chemism)

## 1. Sanskrit Text & Transliteration

**Sutra III.14**
शान्तोदिताव्यपदेश्यधर्मानुपाती धर्मी ॥ ३.१४॥
_śāntoditāvyapadeśya-dharmānupātī dharmī_

## 2. Philological Breakdown

- **śānta (शान्त):** subsided; calmed down; passed away (referring to the past).
- **udita (उदित):** arisen; manifested; currently active (referring to the present).
- **avyapadeśya (अव्यपदेश्य):** unnamable; latent; unmanifest; not yet designatable (referring to the future).
- **dharma (धर्म):** characteristics; properties; forms.
- **anupātī (अनुपाती):** closely following; conforming to; running through; common to.
- **dharmī (धर्मी):** the property-bearer; the substratum; the substance.

## 3. Direct Translation

"The substratum (_dharmī_) is that which closely follows and is common to the characteristics (_dharma_) that have subsided, that are manifested, and that are unnamable (yet to be manifested)."

## 4. Systemic Analysis

Following the mechanical description of the universe (YS III.13), Patañjali immediately deepens the logical model. If objects were purely mechanical, they would be utterly disconnected from the past and the future—a series of isolated flashes without continuity. Here, Patañjali introduces the _dharmī_ (the substance).

### The Substratum (_Dharmī_) versus The Form (_Dharma_)

Patañjali makes a strict ontological distinction between an object's shifting phenomenal properties (_dharma_) and its underlying continuous self-identity (_dharmī_). The _dharma_ constantly changes mathematically:

1.  **Śānta:** The past forms have subsided.
2.  **Udita:** The present forms are currently active.
3.  **Avyapadeśya:** The future forms exist in latency.

### The Continuity Across Time

The _dharmī_ is the thread (_anupātī_) that runs through all of them. It is the underlying substance that prevents reality from disintegrating into a meaningless chaos of disconnected moments.

### The Shift Toward Chemism

In the stage of Mechanism (YS III.13), objects were indifferent. By introducing the _dharmī_, Patañjali demonstrates that objects have an _affinity_. They are deeply connected to their own past states and future potentials. The external world possesses an internal binding force. This is the transition from external mechanism to internal, substantial connection, setting up the affinity inherent in philosophical Chemism.
