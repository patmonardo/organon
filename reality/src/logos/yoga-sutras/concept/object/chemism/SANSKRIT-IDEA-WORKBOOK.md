# Concept/Object/Chemism - SANSKRIT IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Synthesis: The Architecture of Immanent Affinity

In YS III.14, Patañjali takes a critical step inward regarding the physics of the objective world. After outlining the mechanical transformations of objects (YS III.13), he introduces the anchor: the _Dharmī_ (the property-bearer). This aligns effortlessly with the transition in German Idealism from external Mechanism to immanent Chemism.

We can synthesize these dialects as follows:

**1. Moving Beyond the Dead Machine**
If we stopped at YS III.13, the universe would be a dead machine. Objects would just bump into each other, changing their forms and aging, with no underlying connection. Patañjali prevents this nihilistic descent by introducing the _Dharmī_. He defines it as the substrate that "runs through" (_anupātī_) all temporal phases.

Fichte recognized that without this substrate, time would constantly annihilate the present moment, plunging us into radical skepticism. The _Dharmī_ guarantees that the "I" is looking at a rationally cohesive universe.

**2. The Sublation of Indifferent Time (_Śānta, Udita, Avyapadeśya_)**
Hegel argues that Mechanism fails because objects cannot truly be isolated and indifferent if they are interacting. There must be a shared, immanent law—an affinity. This is Chemism.

Patañjali’s formulation is a perfect temporal descriptor of chemical affinity. The _dharmī_ is pulled across the timeline. It holds the past (_śānta_) as a foundation, acts in the present (_udita_), and reaches for the latent, unnamable future (_avyapadeśya_). The objective world is not a sequence of disconnected flashes; it is a chemically bonded continuum. The properties (_dharmas_) change, but the substance (_dharmī_) provides the necessary friction and continuity.

**3. The Preparation for Teleology**
Why is this important for the Yogin mastering _Samyama_? Because to perform a _siddhi_ (a supernatural perfection), the Yogin cannot just command dead matter. They must tap into the latent timeline of the object.

Because the _dharmī_ already contains the _avyapadeśya_ (the unnamable future) within its affinity, the Yogin can access that future. By understanding the chemical tension of the object—how it binds its present to its potential—the Yogin is rationally prepared for the final stage of Objective Logic: Teleology (YS III.15), where they can actively alter the sequence of time itself.
