# Concept/Object/Mechanism - FICHTE IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Principle (_Bhasya_): The Transparency of the Given

Patañjali’s transition into YS III.13 is abrupt but profound: "By this (the internal sequence of restriction, absorption, and one-pointedness), the transformations of form, time, and state in the external elements are explained." For Fichte's _Science of Knowing_, this single sentence acts as the culmination of transcendental deduction: the complete transparent realization of the Physical World.

**1. The Illusion of the "An-sich" (The Thing-in-Itself)**
Before the philosophical inward turn (_Nirodha_, YS III.9), the natural consciousness looks at the world of physical elements (_bhūtas_) and assumes they exist "in themselves" (the Kantian _Ding an sich_). They seem to obey mechanical laws that are totally alien to human thought. The ego feels small, trapped inside a vast, unfeeling machine undergoing endless transformations of decay and change (_pariṇāma_).

**2. The Reversal of the Vector (Etena)**
Fichte's revolutionary principle is that the "Given" is actually the "Posited." The objective world is not an alien machine; it is the necessary outward projection of the absolute "I". The word _etena_ ("by this") perfectly captures this. Patañjali does not give us a separate physics textbook. He simply says: look at the logic you just built in your own mind (_Samyama_). That exact same logic is what is running the universe outside you. The laws of thought are the laws of nature.

**3. The Mechanism Subjugated**
Because the ego (through _Samyama_) has learned to master the arising and subsiding of its own internal states, it now looks at the external world differently. When it watches a physical object change form (_dharma_), age in time (_lakṣaṇa_), and deteriorate in condition (_avasthā_), it no longer sees an alien process. It sees the exact same dialectical machine it learned how to operate in the Subjective Logic.

The objectivity is still mechanical—it is still a realm of objects acting upon one another externally—but the _terror_ of the mechanism is gone. It is transparent. It is nothing more than the ego's own rational Syllogism projected outwards into space and time. This theoretical mastery is the absolute prerequisite for the practical mastery that follows: the _siddhis_ (supernatural powers), which are merely the ego modifying the external mechanism because it knows it is the author of its laws.
