# Concept/Object/Mechanism - HEGEL IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Demonstration (_Varttika_): The Concept Steps into Immediacy

Fichte’s principle establishes that the objective world is transparent to the subjective mind because they share the exact same structural logic (_etena... vyākhyātāḥ_). Hegel’s _Science of Logic_ provides the rigorous transcendental demonstration of exactly how the Subjective Concept (the Syllogism of YS III.12) structurally converts itself into an Objective, Mechanical world (YS III.13).

**1. The Breaking of the Syllogism**
In the Syllogism (YS III.12), the extremes were perfectly united by the Middle Term. Time was sublated into a pure, identical present (_tulya-pratyayau_). The Concept achieved subjective totality.

Hegel demonstrates that because this totality is utterly complete within itself, it relates to itself as an immediate. It stepping out of the inwardness of subjectivity, it becomes a "thereness." The Concept posits itself as an object. This is Objectivity. It is the Concept, but the Concept taking the form of independent, mutually external entities.

**2. The Indifference of the Elements (_Bhūtas_)**
Hegel defines Mechanism precisely as this first stage of Objectivity. The objects (the physical elements or _bhūtas_) are totalities in themselves, but they are indifferent to one another. They interact, they push and pull, but their connections are entirely external.

When Patañjali describes the _pariṇāma_ (transformations) of the _bhūtas_ and _indriyas_, he focuses on three mechanical vectors: _dharma_ (form/property), _lakṣaṇa_ (time/phase), and _avasthā_ (condition/state). In Hegel's terms, this is the mechanical process: external alteration, moving objects through time and space, changing their state without altering their inner, indifferent core.

**3. The Inner Unity of the Machine**
But the crucial Hegelian point—mirrored precisely by Patañjali's _etena_ ("by this")—is that this mechanism is _not_ devoid of Reason. Hegel writes: "In so far as it has the concept immanent in it, the difference of the concept is present in it." The entire mechanical universe is saturated with the Logic of the Syllogism. The planets orbit, molecules vibrate, and bodies age not by random chance, but according to the strict, rational dialectic of the Concept.

The Yogin, having unified the Concept inwardly via _Samyama_, now looks at the mechanical world and recognizes it not as an opaque boundary, but as the inverted image of his own rational mind. The mechanism of nature is recognized as the externalized architecture of Freedom.
