# YS III.15: Fichte Mapping - Teleology and the Supremacy of the Ego

## 1. The Principle of Subjective Causation

In the final stage of Objective Logic, the transcendental philosopher must resolve the lingering threat of nature. If nature (Mechanism and Chemism) operates according to an unbroken temporal sequence, the ego might still be secretly determined by it. YS III.15 asserts the ultimate Fichtean victory: the ego is the author of the sequence.

Patañjali states that the variation in sequence (_krama_) is the cause (_hetu_) of all objective transformation. In Fichtean terms, this identifies that time itself is not heavily objective; it is a mechanism subordinate to the practical, purposive will of the absolute "I".

### 1.1 The Eradication of Blind Determinism

Naive realism assumes that physical transformations happen blindly, pushing the subject along with them. Fichte's entire _Science of Knowing_ aimed at proving that the "I" posits the non-I precisely in order to overcome it.

YS III.15 provides the exact functional lever for this overcoming: the _krama_ (sequence). If the sequence is what produces the transformation, then whoever controls the sequence controls reality. The ego realizes that the sequence is merely the order in which its own rational categories are deployed into objectivity.

### 1.2 The Will as _Hetu_

Fichte insists that the ultimate truth of the theoretical sphere must become the ground for the practical sphere (the Will). The _hetu_ (cause) discussed by Patañjali here is the Teleological Purpose. The ego realizes that it can alter the sequencing of physical events (_kramānyatvaṃ_) because the physical elements have no fixed independence; they are entirely transparent to the ego's logical structure.

This sutra marks the absolute limit of Objective Science. It proves that Objectivity is fully pliable to Subjective Purpose. The practitioner is now theoretically equipped to exercise total control over nature, opening the floodgates to the subsequent sutras focusing on _Vibhūtis_ (supreme perfections).
