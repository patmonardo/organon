# Concept/Object/Teleology - FICHTE IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Principle (_Bhasya_): The Sovereignty of the Will

In YS III.15, Patañjali provides the ultimate formula for objective mastery: "The variation in the sequence (_krama_) is the cause (_hetu_) of the variation in the transformation (_pariṇāma_)." For Fichte's _Science of Knowing_, this single sentence proves the absolute supremacy of Practical Reason (the Will) over Theoretical Reason (Nature).

**1. The Illusion of Objective Time**
In Mechanism and Chemism (YS III.13-14), the ego recognized its own logical structure reflected in the laws of the physical universe. However, as long as the temporal sequence (_krama_) unfolds automatically, the ego remains fundamentally passive in relation to the world. It is a spectator of nature's transformations (_pariṇāma_).

Fichte argues that to accept this passivity is to betray the absolute freedom of the "I". If the sequence of time is merely a necessary category by which the ego posits the non-I, then the ego must possess the power to alter that category.

**2. The Reversal of Causality (_Hetu_)**
Patañjali’s sutra perfectly articulates Fichte's practical turn. Nature does not dictate the sequence; the sequence dictates nature. And who controls the sequence? The absolute "I". The ego realizes that _krama_ is not an iron cage, but a lever.

By utilizing the intense focus cultivated in _Samyama_, the Yogin forces a variation in the sequence (_kramānyatvaṃ_). Because the physical transformation (_pariṇāma_) is nothing more than the objective shadow of this subjective sequence, altering the sequence _causes_ the physical world to change. The Will acts as the _hetu_ (first cause).

**3. The Teleological Engine**
This is the essence of Teleological Purpose. The Yogin is not breaking the laws of physics; the Yogin is realizing that the laws of physics are just the default sequence of a machine. Because the Yogin created the machine (via the absolute synthesis of Subject and Object), the Yogin can reprogram the sequence.

This theoretical validation is the absolute prerequisite for the _Vibhūti-pāda_. Every _siddhi_ (supernatural power) that follows from Sutra III.16 onward relies entirely on this principle: the world is pliable because the sequence of its manifestation is subordinate to the sovereign Purpose of the realized Will.
