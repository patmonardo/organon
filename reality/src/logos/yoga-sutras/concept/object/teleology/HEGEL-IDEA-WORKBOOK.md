# Concept/Object/Teleology - HEGEL IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Demonstration (_Varttika_): The Subordination of Mechanism to Purpose

Fichte establishes that the Will has the sovereign right to alter the sequence of time. Hegel’s _Science of Logic_ provides the rigorous transcendental demonstration of exactly how this occurs: how Mechanism and Chemism functionally break down under the weight of **Teleology** (Self-determining Purpose) and become mere tools for the Concept's realization (YS III.15).

**1. The Incompleteness of the Chemical Object**
In Chemism (YS III.14), the objective world was bonded by an inner affinity (the _dharmī_ or substrate). Yet, this object was still blind. It was driven through its past, present, and future forms (_śānta, udita, avyapadeśya_) by an internal tension, but without a conscious goal.

Hegel demonstrates that this blind, chemical unfolding is a contradiction. The object is attempting to realize its Concept, but it cannot do so purely objectively. It requires Subjectivity to return to it. It requires Purpose.

**2. Purpose as the Final Cause (_Hetu_)**
Patañjali states that the variation of the sequence (_krama_) is the cause (_hetu_) of the transformation (_pariṇāma_). Hegel translates this brilliantly: "Purpose is the concept posited as within it referring to objectivity, and through itself sublating its defect of being subjective."

Purpose is the ultimate _hetu_. The internal subjective Concept (the mind of the Yogin) looks at the objective world (the _pariṇāmas_) not as an indifferent machine (Mechanism) or a blind process (Chemism), but as a _means_. The Concept realizes that the mechanical sequence is indifferent to how it is ordered. Therefore, the purposeful Subject can step in and provide the order.

**3. The Machination of Reason (List der Vernunft)**
This is exactly how Yoga works practically. The Yogin does not abolish physical laws; the Yogin utilizes them as a subordinate sequence. By altering the sequence (_kramānyatvaṃ_), the Yogin forces a new structural outcome. Hegel calls this the "Cunning of Reason" (_List der Vernunft_). Reason allows the mechanical and chemical processes to act upon each other according to their nature, but it arranges their _sequence_ such that they blindly execute the Subject's internal Purpose.

Teleology is thus the absolute truth of Objectivity. The objective world proves itself to be nothing but the infinitely pliable material for the self-realization of the Absolute Idea. Nature is conquered by Purpose, opening the gateway to the infinite knowledge of the _siddhis_.
