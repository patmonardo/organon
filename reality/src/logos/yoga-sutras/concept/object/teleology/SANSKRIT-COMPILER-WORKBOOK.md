# YS III.15: The Purpose Governing the Sequence (Teleology)

## 1. Sanskrit Text & Transliteration

**Sutra III.15**
क्रमान्यत्वं परिणामान्यत्वे हेतुः ॥ ३.१५॥
_kramānyatvaṃ pariṇāmānyatve hetuḥ_

## 2. Philological Breakdown

- **krama (क्रम):** sequence; succession; order.
- **anyatvaṃ (अन्यत्वं):** otherness; difference; variation; alteration.
- **pariṇāma (परिणाम):** transformation; modification; change.
- **anyatve (अन्यत्वे):** in the otherness; in the variation.
- **hetuḥ (हेतुः):** cause; reason; underlying motive.

## 3. Direct Translation

"The variation in the sequence (_krama_) is the cause (_hetu_) for the variation in the transformation (_pariṇāma_)."

## 4. Systemic Analysis

This sutra represents the final mastery over the objective world before Patañjali lists the _siddhis_ (supernatural powers). Having understood the mechanical surface of objects (YS III.13) and their underlying chemical substance (YS III.14), the Yogin now seizes the engine of reality itself: Purpose.

### The Sequence (_Krama_)

An object is not just a block in space; it is a rapid sequence of overlapping temporal instances. Normally, this sequence unfolds blindly according to natural laws. Dust becomes clay, clay becomes a pot, a pot becomes shards.

### The Causal Lever (_Hetu_)

Patañjali identifies that the ultimate _cause_ of any transformation is the specific _order_ of this sequence. The sequence is the lever. If you alter the sequence (_kramānyatvaṃ_), you alter the transformation (_pariṇāmānyatve_).

This introduces the concept of Teleology—Self-Determining Purpose. The Yogin is no longer a passive observer watching the _dharmī_ (substance) chemically bind its past, present, and future. Through the highest application of _Samyama_, the Yogin can purposively reach into the sequence and alter its order, thereby causing the object to transform according to the Yogin's sovereign will, rather than blind natural necessity. The external world's sequence becomes subordinated to the Yogin's internal Purpose.
