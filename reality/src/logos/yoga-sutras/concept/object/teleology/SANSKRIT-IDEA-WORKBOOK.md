# Concept/Object/Teleology - SANSKRIT IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Synthesis: The Architecture of Sovereign Purpose

In YS III.15, Patañjali delivers the masterkey to the entire _Vibhūti-pāda_ (Chapter on Powers). "The variation in sequence (_krama_) is the cause of the variation in transformation (_pariṇāma_)." This sutra mathematically aligns with the highest stage of Objective Logic in German Idealism: Teleology, the subjugation of all mechanical and chemical laws to self-determining Purpose.

We can synthesize these dialects as follows:

**1. The Logic of the Lever (_Krama_)**
In YS III.13, we learned the components of the machine: elements and senses transforming according to form, time, and condition. In YS III.14, we learned the inherent gravity of the machine: the _dharmī_ binding past, present, and future. Here, in YS III.15, we are handed the lever to operate the machine: the Sequence (_Krama_).

Patañjali recognizes that an object's transformation (_pariṇāma_) is not fatally predetermined. It is simply the result of a specific succession of moments. The sequence is the ultimate cause (_hetu_).

**2. The Cunning of the Yogin (Teleology)**
Hegel calls this structural manipulation the "Cunning of Reason." Reason (or the Yogin's mind acting in _Samyama_) does not need to violently destroy the mechanical laws of nature. It simply needs to re-order them. Because the mechanical world is structurally "indifferent" to its own sequence, it has no defense against a sovereign Will that steps in and alters the _krama_.

When the Yogin performs _Samyama_ on a specific outcome (the Purpose), the Yogin’s mind acts as the ultimate Teleological cause. It alters the sequence of cause-and-effect in the physical world (_kramānyatvaṃ_), thereby forcing the physical world to produce a new, customized transformation (_pariṇāmānyatve_).

**3. The Threshold of Infinite Power**
For Fichte, this validates the total, unconditioned supremacy of the "I". The external world is definitively proven to be a subordinate mechanism. It has no power to resist the sequence imposed upon it by the conceptually unified Will.

This is why Patañjali places this sutra exactly here. Having inwardly mastered the Concept (YS III.9-12), and outwardly demystified the Object (YS III.13-15), the Yogin has constructed the complete Logical Architecture of reality. The practitioner is now the sovereign operator of the universe, and the very next sutra (YS III.16) begins the dizzying ascent into the _siddhis_—the miraculous powers that follow effortlessly from this absolute teleological mastery.
