# Concept/Subject/Concept - HEGEL IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Demonstration (_Varttika_): The Genesis of the Universal Concept

Fichte’s principle of the "artificial intuition" commands the mind to turn inward and restrict its outward flow (_Nirodha-pariṇāma_, YS III.9). Hegel’s _Science of Logic_ provides the rigorous transcendental demonstration of why this restriction must occur and how it structurally produces the Concept as such, transitioning from the Universal to the Particular and the Singular.

**1. The Genesis out of Essence**
As Hegel details in the opening of Subjective Logic (The Concept), the Concept is not an arbitrary starting point. It comes to be out of Being and Essence. In the realm of Essence, being was a "reflective shine" or a "positedness." But the Concept is the negation of this negation; it is "the mutual penetration of these moments." Patañjali defines _Nirodha_ not merely as stopping, but as an active intersection (_anvaya_): the subduing of emergence and the rising of restriction. Here, Hegel shows that the backward turn of Essence into its own reflection sublates itself, restoring a non-posited, original being. The Concept is absolute self-identity precisely because it is the infinite unity of negativity with itself.

**2. The Pure Universal (Praśānta-vāhitā)**
When the mind holds this moment of restriction (_nirodha-kṣaṇa_), it achieves a peaceful flow (_praśānta-vāhitā_). Hegel explicitly maps this to the Universal Concept. The Universal, Hegel says, is "free power" and "boundless blessedness." Why? Because it relates to that which is distinct from it as to itself. In it, the Concept has returned to itself. Just as the yogic mind flows peacefully because it is entirely determined by its own restrictive _saṁskāra_, the Hegelian Universal is "the soul of the concrete which it inhabits, unhindered and equal to itself in its manifoldness." It is not swept away in the becoming of objects (emergence); it persists undisturbed through them.

**3. The Dialectic Breakdown into Particularity**
However, the Yoga Sutras describe this state as still governed by _saṁskāras_ (impressions); it is not the final absolute void, but a highly formal structure. Hegel rigorously demonstrates that this Universal isn't a dead abstraction. Because it is absolute negativity, it already contains determinateness in itself. The Universal must differentiate itself exactly because the differentiating is one with the universality. It posits differences that are themselves universals.

Thus, the Universal immediately determines itself as the Particular. It is this very indeterminateness (the restriction) that makes its determinateness. As Hegel notes, the Universal sets itself on one side as the immediate, indeterminate universality, which makes it a particular against another side. It partitions itself internally. This logical slide from the pure Universal into the Particular leads directly into determining Singularity—where the Concept becomes a "this", stepping into actuality and precipitating the necessity of a formal Judgment (YS III.11).

Patañjali's _Nirodha_ prepares the ground; Hegel demonstrates that this restricted ground must, by its own structural necessity, particularize itself and generate the subjective architecture of truth.
