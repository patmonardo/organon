# Concept/Subject/Judgment - FICHTE IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Principle (_Bhasya_): The Immanent Construction of Truth

Patañjali describes the _Samādhi-pariṇāma_ (YS III.11) as the mind intentionally destroying its own scattered, multi-pointed nature (_sarvārthatā_) in order to elevate and sustain a singular point of focus (_ekāgratā_). In Fichte's _Science of Knowing_, this describes the active, constructive genesis of logical judgment—the mechanism by which the pure absolute light breaks itself open to look at itself.

**1. The Incompleteness of the Empty Universal**
In the previous stage (YS III.9-10), the philosopher successfully performed the inward turn, achieving the artificial intuition of the pure Concept (the _praśānta-vāhitā_). However, Fichte emphasizes that if we stay in this pure Light, we are effectively blind. It possesses no contrast, no determination, no form. To generate the "Science of Knowing," this pure Universal must divide itself. It must posit a specific thought-object against the background of the absolute. This is the transcendental imperative behind the _Samādhi-pariṇāma_.

**2. The Execution of the "I" (Ahamkāra)**
The _Samādhi_ transformation is an _act_. The mind actively judges. The given world appears to us as a chaotic array of independent objects (what Patañjali calls _sarvārthatā_ or all-pointedness). The philosophical "I" realizes this is an illusion of naive realism. Through a rigorous deduction, the ego explicitly destroys (_kṣaya_) the assumed independence of the empirical manifold, actively raising up (_udaya_) the singular structural principle (_ekāgratā_) that unites it.

**3. The Transcendental Copula**
This state is not yet the final, effortless absorption. It is the _pariṇāma_—the transformation _into_ absorption. The mind is operating as a transcendental copula. In a formal judgment ("A is B"), the mind holds two distinct terms together while remaining aware of their difference. Here, the ego is operating at full capacity to construct the formal linkage between the universal concept and the specific determination. The philosopher is explicitly constructing the world out of the Light, demonstrating that the manifold forms are merely predicate expressions of the single Subject.

This dynamic, constructive act is the necessary bridge. The ego must hold the "one point" steady against the chaotic pressure of the "many points," determining the exact shape of Truth before it can finally collapse the gap entirely in the Syllogism (YS III.12).
