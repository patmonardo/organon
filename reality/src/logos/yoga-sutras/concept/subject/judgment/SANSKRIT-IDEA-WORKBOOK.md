# Concept/Subject/Judgment - SANSKRIT IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Synthesis: The Architecture of the Original Division

The second transformation of the _Samyama_ gateway is the _Samādhi-pariṇāma_ (YS III.11). Here, the Yoga Sutras describe the precise mechanism by which the mind actively focuses its universal capability onto a singular truth. This seamlessly integrates with the German Idealist transition from the pure Universal Concept into formal Judgment.

We can synthesize these dialects as follows:

**1. The Necessity of the "Original Division" (_Urteil_)**
If the first transformation (_Nirodha_, YS III.9) restricts the outward flow to create a peaceful universal platform, the second transformation (_Samādhi_, YS III.11) begins to formally structure that platform. A mind entirely without an object is an empty abstraction. The Light is blind without something to illuminate. Therefore, the mind executes the _Samādhi-pariṇāma_. It breaks the universal open. In German, Judgment is _Ur-teil_ (the original division). The mind divides its pure capability into a positing subject and a focused predicate.

**2. The Dialectic of Destruction and Rising**
Patañjali describes this transformation as the destruction (_kṣaya_) of all-pointedness (_sarvārthatā_) and the rise (_udaya_) of one-pointedness (_ekāgratā_). This is not a passive event. As Fichte emphasizes, it is an explicit, constructive act of the _Ahamkāra_ (the ego or "I"). The mind encounters the scattered abstraction of immediate existence (the many points) and actively negates it, positing a specific, determinate focus. Hegel demonstrates that this is the essence of logic: the Concept determines itself by negating the abstract manifold and raising up the formal predicate.

**3. The Active Process of the Copula**
It is critical to understand that _Samādhi-pariṇāma_ is the _process_ of absorption, not the final resting point. The mind is actively judging. It is acting as the transcendental copula ("A is B"). It binds the universal focus (the one point) against the background of the fading distractions. Because it involves _kṣaya-udayau_ (a simultaneous fading and rising), there is still a temporal tension. The mind is actively forcing the connection.

This state represents the transitional stage of Subjective Logic. The mind is actively engaged in the process of converging on unity, but the extremes (the past distraction, the future focus) are still posited against each other. This dynamic tension sets the stage for the final resolution in YS III.12, where the gap is closed and absolute unity is restored.
