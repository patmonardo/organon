# Concept/Subject/Syllogism - HEGEL IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Demonstration (_Varttika_): The Restored Unity of the Middle Term

Fichte’s principle of absolute synthesis removes the temporal gap between the positing subject and the posited object. Patañjali maps this as the mathematically identical transition of fading and rising thoughts (_tulya-pratyayau_). Hegel’s _Science of Logic_ provides the rigorous transcendental demonstration of how this _Ekāgratā-pariṇāma_ structurally necessitates the resolution of the Judgment into the Syllogism (_Schluss_), restoring the Concept to its full, concrete totality.

**1. The Resolution of the Extremes**
Hegel defines the Syllogism as the result of the restoration of the concept in the judgment. In the judgment (YS III.11), the moments were posited as self-subsisting extremes: the chaotic manifold on one side, the formal focal point on the other. They were held together forcibly by the "is" (the copula). In the syllogism, their determinate unity is posited.

Patañjali describes this sublation of extremes explicitly: the "extremes" in the cognitive sequence are the cognition that is passing into the past (_śānta_) and the cognition moving into the future (_udita_). In ordinary cognition, these two extremes differ. But here, the extremes are made perfectly identical.

**2. Buddhi as the Perfect Middle Term**
Hegel's syllogism connects the immediate extremes via a mediating middle term. Because the arising thought and the subsiding thought are strictly identical, the mind's focus acts as the perfect Hegelian Middle Term.

It mediates the transition of Time itself without introducing any alterity. The contradictory relation that occurred in the formal sequence sublates itself. There is no longer a struggle between Subject and Predicate because the Middle Term perfectly unites them. The mind operates organically as Reason (_Buddhi_).

**3. The Stepping Forth into Objectivity**
Hegel notes that when this mediatedness is reflected into itself (the Syllogism of Necessity), it determines the extremes as totalities. The Syllogism is not merely a subjective trick; it is the absolute truth of reality. The concept attains its truth and is structurally ready to step forth out of subjectivity into objectivity.

For Patañjali, this identifies exactly why the perfection of _Samyama_ immediately leads to the _siddhis_ (perfections or powers). The mind has synthesized its subjective architecture so completely that it is no longer separate from the objective law of nature. The Syllogism is the final internal gate before Objective Spirit.
