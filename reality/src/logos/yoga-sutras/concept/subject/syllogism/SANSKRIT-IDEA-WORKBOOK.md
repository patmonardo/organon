# Concept/Subject/Syllogism - SANSKRIT IDEA WORKBOOK

## The Synthesis: The Architecture of Complete Unification

The final transformation of the _Samyama_ triad is the _Ekāgratā-pariṇāma_ (YS III.12). Here, the internal sequence of meditative absorption ceases relative development and achieves absolute structural integrity. This aligns precisely with the synthesis of the Syllogism in German Idealism, where the Subjective Concept heals the rupture of formal Judgment and prepares to engage Objective Reality.

We can synthesize these dialects as follows:

**1. Closing the Hiatum (The Temporal Gap)**
The previous stage (_Samādhi-pariṇāma_, YS III.11 / Judgment) was the _process_ of converging heavily on a singular point while fighting off distraction. It was a rigorous constructive act, but it structurally required a gap: the mind was actively holding together an old, fading state and a new, rising state.

Patañjali’s _Ekāgratā-pariṇāma_ closes this gap entirely. It specifies exactly how: the subsiding cognition (_śānta_) and the arising cognition (_udita_) must become mathematically identical (_tulya-pratyayau_). Fichte identifies this exact metric as the proof of absolute philosophical synthesis. When the Subject's act of knowing precisely mirrors the Object known across time, the "hiatum" of naive realism collapses. Time is sublated into spatialized, eternal unity.

**2. The Restored Unity (_Schluss_)**
Hegel demonstrates that this is the formal definition of the Syllogism. The extremes (the Subject and the Predicate, or the Past and the Future) are brought into perfect unity by an identical Middle Term. The _Ahamkāra_ (the dividing ego) passes the baton to _Buddhi_ (Reason). The mind is structurally "one-pointed" without having to exert force to maintain it. The struggle is over, and the Concept is restored to its full, concrete totality.

**3. The Completion of Subjective Logic**
This implies that _Samyama_ (the simultaneous integration of _Dhāraṇā_, _Dhyāna_, and _Samādhi_) is the complete mastery of Subjective Logic. The mind has systematically moved from grasping the empty Universal (YS III.9-10), to dividing it via Particular Judgment (YS III.11), to restoring it into the unbroken Singular Syllogism (YS III.12).

Having fully formed the inner architecture of Reason, the yogin is now equipped to turn this unassailable syllogistic framework onto any object in the external world—leading directly to the objective super-knowledges (_siddhis_) that comprise the remainder of the _Vibhūti-pāda_.
