# Actuality/Absolute - SANSKRIT IDEA WORKBOOK

## Purpose

Establishing the baseline Sanskrit philology and systemic philosophical analysis of Yoga Sūtras IV.22 through IV.26. This sequence drives the practitioner from the boundaries of Appearance into Actuality—unifying the invariant Seer and the form-taking mind in a single act—before triggering the cessation of the ego (_ātma-bhāva_) and tilting the mind irrevocably toward Absolute Freedom (_kaivalya_). Furthermore, it incorporates a _Vārtika_ (expansive commentary) integrating the Fichte/Hegel synthetic logic into the traditional reading.

## Sūtra IV.22: Actuality as Unity-in-Act

### I. Text and Translation

**Devanāgarī:**
चितेरप्रतिसङ्क्रमायास्तदाकारापत्तौ स्वबुद्धिसंवेदनम् ॥ ४.२२॥

**Transliteration:**
citer apratisaṅkramāyās tad-ākārāpattau sva-buddhi-saṁvedanam || 4.22 ||

**Translation:**
When, due to the absolute non-transference of consciousness (_citer apratisaṅkramāyās_), the mind takes on the form of that object (_tad-ākārāpattau_), there occurs the self-cognition of one's own mind (_sva-buddhi-saṁvedanam_).

### II. Systemic Analysis: The Physics of Awareness

If the mind is not self-luminous (IV.19) and cannot look at itself simultaneously (IV.20), how is self-awareness physically possible? Patañjali answers with the mechanics of Actuality.

Awareness is a "Unity-in-Act" comprising two strictly separated roles. First, the pure Light (_citi_ / _Puruṣa_) remains utterly immobile. It exhibits _apratisaṅkrama_ (non-transference)—it never migrates, leaks, or bleeds into the objects it illuminates. Second, the _buddhi_ (the reflective intellect) physically assumes the shape (_ākārāpatti_) of the object to be known.

Self-cognition (_sva-buddhi-saṁvedanam_) is the spark that happens _between_ these two elements. The _buddhi_ takes the form of the object, and the motionless Light illuminates that form. The mind becomes "self-aware" only because it catches the reflection of the Light in the very act of holding an object. The roles never collapse: the Light never becomes an object, and the mind never becomes the true Light.

### III. Vārtika: The Idealist Synthesis

In Fichte's system, the order from the Absolute downward is rigorously one-sided. The Absolute Light never becomes the dirty reality of the object; yet, to avoid two disconnected universes, they must cohere. Patañjali secures this coherence exactly where Fichte does: the Light never migrates (_apratisaṅkrama_), but the mind physically shapes itself to the object (_ākārāpatti_), producing a "Unity-in-Act." Hegel formally codifies this as _Actuality_ (_Wirklichkeit_) and _The Exposition of the Absolute_. In Actuality, the hidden inner Essence and the external Appearance fuse perfectly into an accomplished act. The self-cognition described by Patañjali is this Exposition—it is not an external reflection, but the Absolute's _own_ exposition through the specific form-determination of the intellect. The mind functions as the "Absolute Attribute"—a specific relative-absolute that comprehends the entire content without the pure Witness losing its transcendental stance.

## Sūtra IV.24: The Mind as Instrument (Being-For-Another)

### I. Text and Translation

**Devanāgarī:**
तदसङ्ख्येयवासनाभिश्चित्रमपि परार्थं संहत्यकारित्वात् ॥ ४.२४॥

**Transliteration:**
tad asaṁkhyeya-vāsanābhiś citram api parārthaṁ saṁhatya-kāritvāt || 4.24 ||

**Translation:**
Even though the mind is highly variegated by countless latent impressions (_asaṁkhyeya-vāsanābhiś citram api_), it exists strictly for another (_parārtham_), because it functions as a composite aggregate (_saṁhatya-kāritvāt_).

### II. Systemic Analysis: Teleological Demotion

Having explained how the mind operates (IV.22), Patañjali now explains _why_ it operates. The human mind appears wildly complex, driven by infinite historical software patterns (_asaṁkhyeya-vāsanābhiḥ_). It feels like it must be the center of the universe.

Patañjali demotes it based on physics. The mind operates by _saṁhati_ (conjunction/aggregation). It has moving parts. In Yoga logic, anything composed of parts cannot be the Absolute Subject; it must have been assembled to serve a higher, non-composite master. Therefore, the mind is absolutely _parārtham_—"for another." It is an instrument, a lens, a projector. The mind does not exist for itself; it exists solely to provide experiences to the Seer.

### III. Vārtika: The Idealist Synthesis

Fichte uses the concept of the hypothetical "Should" (_Sollen_) to describe this dynamic: the empirical mind operates functionally to connect conditions, but it is not the Absolute end itself. Derived knowing must recognize its subordinate instrumentality. Hegel expands this into _Being-For-Another_ (_Für-Anderes_) and _The Mode of the Absolute_. Whenever an object derives its meaning relationally as a moving, composite aggregate (_saṁhatya-kāritvāt_), it cannot be "For-Itself." The complexity of the _citta_ is merely "The Mode"—the Absolute's self-externality. It is entirely _parārtham_ (teleologically subordinate), an instrument utilized by the higher, non-composite Concept. The mind must abandon its delusions of grandeur and surrender to its role as the absolute projector for the pure Witness.

## Sūtra IV.25: The Cessation of Selfing (De-Appropriation)

### I. Text and Translation

**Devanāgarī:**
विशेषदर्शिन आत्मभावभावनाविनिवृत्तिः ॥ ४.२५॥

**Transliteration:**
viśeṣa-darśina ātma-bhāva-bhāvanā-vinivṛttiḥ || 4.25 ||

**Translation:**
For the one who sees the structural distinction (_viśeṣa-darśina_), there is a total cessation of the cultivation of self-being (_ātma-bhāva-bhāvanā-vinivṛttiḥ_).

### II. Systemic Analysis: Epistemic Annihilation of the Ego

This sutra is the psychological consequence of the ontological proofs in IV.22 and IV.24. If the practitioner achieves _viśeṣa-darśana_—the discriminative vision that clearly sees the mind as a mere composite aggregate that borrows light "for another"—what happens to the ego?

The ego (_ātma-bhāva_) is exposed as a fabrication (_bhāvanā_). "Selfing" is the habit of the mind pretending to be the Seer. Once the mind realizes that it is merely the lens and not the Light, the habit of self-appropriation instantly ceases (_vinivṛtti_). This is not ascetic suppression; you do not fight the ego. You merely recognize the physical impossibility of the mind being the Absolute Subject, and the false cultivation drops away on its own.

### III. Vārtika: The Idealist Synthesis

How does Fichte terminate the infinite regress of proof? Proof terminates when immediate facticity perfectly coincides with possibility—when you stop trying to ground the Absolute in something external and simply stand in it. The anxiety of Cartesian hypotheticalness ends entirely upon recognizing the Categorical. Patañjali describes this psychological terminus. The search for a solid ego-self drops. Hegel characterizes this as the entry into _Formal Actuality/Contingency_. The mind's rebellious illusion of independent Subjectivity (the false "For-Itself") is sublated (_Aufhebung_). "Self-building" ceases, and the mind's true instrumental dignity is preserved and elevated into alignment with Absolute Knowing.

## Sūtra IV.26: The Gravitational Shift toward Liberation

### I. Text and Translation

**Devanāgarī:**
तदा विवेकनिम्नं कैवल्यप्राग्भारं चित्तम् ॥ ४.२६॥

**Transliteration:**
tadā viveka-nimnaṁ kaivalya-prāg-bhāraṁ cittam || 4.26 ||

**Translation:**
Then (_tadā_), the mind, inclined downward toward discrimination (_viveka-nimnam_), bears the heavy forward-weight toward total isolation/liberation (_kaivalya-prāg-bhāram_).

### II. Systemic Analysis: The Pre-Liberative Vector

What happens to a mind that has stopped "selfing" (IV.25)? Does it vanish? No, its gravitational polarity literally reverses.

Before this realization, the mind's gravity pulled it outward to appropriate objects as "mine." Now (_tadā_), the mind slopes downhill toward discrimination (_viveka-nimnam_). Insight is no longer an uphill battle against ignorance; it is the natural current of the system.

The mind bears the "forward-weight" (_prāg-bhāram_) toward _kaivalya_ (Absolute Freedom/Isolation). It is in a pre-liberative freefall. The mind continues to operate, but every operation is now teleologically locked on emancipating the Seer from the entanglement of the seen.

### III. Vārtika: The Idealist Synthesis

Once the philosopher negates hypotheticalness, Fichte declares "the Science proves itself." The categorical turn actualizes: knowing operates with absolute forward-moving necessity—a fixed compass vector. Patañjali formalizes this physics in IV.26 as _prāg-bhāram_ (forward-weight). The mind enters a pre-liberative freefall. Hegel maps this precisely as the transition out of the Doctrine of Essence into the Doctrine of the Concept—a monumental shift in logical gravity from blind, external Necessity to fully conscious Absolute Freedom. The path is no longer an arduous climb; it is an irreversible, mathematically necessary descent into _kaivalya_. The Concept commands the entire system.
