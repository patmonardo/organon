# Actuality/Actuality - HEGEL IDEA WORKBOOK

## Purpose

To provide an expansive transcendental demonstration (a _Varttika_) of the terminal sequences of the Yoga Sūtras (IV.27 through IV.28) established by Fichte in the IDEA workbook. We map the "from-of-a-from" chinks, the ineradicable factional habit, and the categorical compass of method onto Hegel's concepts of Contingency, Formal Possibility, and the sublation of Absolute Restlessness into Real Actuality.

---

## Sūtra IV.27: Contingency and Formal Possibility

### I. The Fichtean Principle (The _Bhasya_)

Fichte warns that philosophical elevation does not destroy the biological/psychological architecture of ordinary life. Appearance is generated by a self-intersecting disjunction. If the practitioner's concentration wavers, they slip into the "chinks" (_chidreṣu_) of this intersection, allowing factions of old habit (_saṁskāras_) to re-intrude.

### II. The Hegelian Demonstration (The _Varttika_)

#### The Limitless Manifoldness

In Hegel's Logic, the transition out of Necessity and into Freedom requires passing through "Contingency or Formal Actuality, Possibility, and Necessity." Even when the mind is tipped toward liberation, the sheer mechanical receptacle of possibility allows old, ungrounded contingencies to surface momentarily.

Hegel notes that within Formal Possibility, everything is possible that does not contradict itself; the realm is a "limitless manifoldness." The temporary arising of non-discriminative cognitions in the gaps of realization (_tad-chidreṣu pratyayāntarāṇi_) maps perfectly to this. The _pratyayāntarāṇi_ (other cognitions) are precisely these formal possibilities erupting out of the manifoldness of the karmic substrate (_saṁskārebhyaḥ_).

#### Absolute Restlessness

For Hegel, these contingent intrusions operate as an "absolute restlessness." They are simply immediate actuations that have reverted into mere possibility—they lack any true ground or absolute necessity. For both thinkers, these intrusions are not refutations of the Absolute Truth; they are merely the rattling of a dying engine. Contingency remains, but meaning has been irrevocably withdrawn from it.

## Sūtra IV.28: Sublating the Receptacle of Contingency

### I. The Fichtean Principle (The _Bhasya_)

When confronted by intrusions, Fichte forbids inventing a harsh, novel suppression. The method you used to prove the Absolute must become the very law of your operations. You rely categorically on the established architecture of the path.

### II. The Hegelian Demonstration (The _Varttika_)

#### Sublation into Real Actuality

Patañjali states in IV.28 that these late-stage intrusions are removed exactly the same way you removed the early afflictions (_hānam eṣāṁ kleśavad uktam_). This aligns perfectly with Hegel's logic of sublating Contingency into Real Actuality and Absolute Necessity.

Because the contingent gap-intrusion is simply immediate actuality that has reverted into mere possibility, it must be logically negated. The old method of negation (_kleśavad uktam_) handles it perfectly; it is the established logical operation of negating a false, groundless positedness.

By applying the _kleśa_ method to the gap-intrusions, the practitioner refuses to grant them the dignity of new, terrifying substance. They are treated as what they are: mere formal possibilities asserting a false validity. This logical operation drives the dialectic out of this restless contingency and forces the transition toward true Necessity. The practitioner surrenders personal arbitrariness and lets the proven Method steer the system toward the Absolute.
