### Entry heg-iv-029and030 — The Arrival at Real Actuality

Span:

- sourceFile: `src/logos/ys-logic/dharma-greater-varttika.tex`
- lineStart: 7216
- lineEnd: 7260

Summary:
The emergence of _dharma-megha samādhi_ (IV.29) and the resultant cessation of _kleśa/karma_ (IV.30) correspond to Hegel's "Relative Necessity or Real Actuality, Possibility, and Necessity." When the mind establishes the algebraic discriminator, formal possibility is sublated into Real Actuality. This actuality is no longer an empty receptacle of contingency but a manifold content containing form as an indifferent identity. The cessation of karmic afflictions demonstrates that the subject has preserved itself dynamically in the manifoldness, relating only to itself.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. _Dharma-megha_ establishes Real Actuality: an actuality whose unity is an indifferent identity containing the manifold forms.
- k2. The cessation of _kleśa_ and _karma_ acts as the manifestation of this Real Actuality, which no longer loses itself in chaotic appearance but relates only to itself internally.

### Entry heg-iv-034 — The Relation of Substantiality (Kaivalya)

Span:

- sourceFile: `src/logos/ys-logic/dharma-greater-varttika.tex`
- lineStart: 7769
- lineEnd: 7800

Summary:
The ultimate involution of the purposeless _guṇas_ (_pratiprasava_) and the establishment of the Seer in its own power (_svarūpa-pratiṣṭhā_) conclude the dialectic under Hegelian "Relation of Substantiality." Absolute necessity forms an absolute relation—not abstract being standing behind appearance, but the absolute mediation of itself with itself. The _guṇas_ return to unmanifest latency because they are mere "accidents" (accidentality) against the shining totality of Substance.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. _Kaivalya_ is the absolute relation of Substantiality.
- k2. The _Puruṣa_ (Seer) is Substance: the final unity of essence and being, completely reflected into itself as a subsisting that exists in and for itself.
- k3. The _guṇas_ are recognized as mere accidentality against the absolute shining of Substance; their teleological purpose exhausted, they vanish.
