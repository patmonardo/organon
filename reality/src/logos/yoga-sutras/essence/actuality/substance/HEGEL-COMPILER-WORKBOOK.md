### Entry heg-iv-034 — The Return to Substance / The Absolute Idea

Span:

- sourceFile: `src/logos/ys/ys_iv_34.ts`
- lineStart: 4
- lineEnd: 27

Summary:
The ultimate closure of _Kaivalya_ maps strictly to the Absolute Idea in Hegel's _Science of Logic_, where the dialectical process exhausts itself entirely and the logical Idea freely "releases" itself into Nature.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. The physical involution of the _guṇas_ (_pratiprasava_) corresponds to the exhaustion of "Substance." Substance was merely a phase for the Subject to work through. Once the Concept is fully realized, Substance loses its independent validity and collapses into the transparent medium of the Idea.
- k2. The Seer established in its own power (_svarūpa-pratiṣṭhā_) corresponds to the Absolute Idea, which requires no external material or "other" to define itself. It is absolute self-knowing.
- k3. Teleology (_puruṣārtha_) is finally resolved. For Hegel, teleology is the highest form of objectivity, but it still relies on an external means to achieve an end. In the Absolute Idea, the means and the end are perfectly identical.
