### Entry fic-iv-020 — The Organic Law of Non-Simultaneity

Span:

- sourceFile: `src/logos/ys/ys_iv_20.ts`
- lineStart: 31
- lineEnd: 44

Summary:
The ban on simultaneous double-determination (_eka-samaye ca-ubhaya-anavadhāraṇam_) tracks Fichte's Organic Law regarding higher knowing and the "Hiatum". Genuine knowing can never simultaneously grasp itself as object and subject without positing an enduring "Being" derived from negating absolute genesis.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. Fichte identifies a necessary "gap" (_hiatum_) in the continuity of genesis, breaking pure reason from empirical appearance.
- k2. Immanent knowing never appears as self-genesis; it appears only as the positive negation of genesis (an endured condition).
- k3. The Organic Law structurally forbids collapsing these dual aspects (Genesis vs. Endured Being) into a single illegitimate simultaneous act.

### Entry fic-iv-021 — The Hinge of Reason and Understanding

Span:

- sourceFile: `src/logos/ys/ys_iv_21.ts`
- lineStart: 157
- lineEnd: 175

Summary:
The rejection of mind-on-mind transparency corresponds to Fichte's hinge between Pure Reason (Witness/Light) and Understanding (Reconstruction/Appearance). The _hiatum_ forbids direct transparency between the components of appearance without mediation through the higher principle.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. The progression from Principle to Principled-Thing is strictly one-sided and non-reciprocal. Minds (Understanding) do not build upward or sideways into Pure Reason.
- k2. Pure Reason fundamentally negates Genesis as absolute.
- k3. The _hiatum_ separates Pure Reason's qualitative oneness from the relational web of Appearance, preventing the "memory fusion" or "regress" that would happen if the boundary collapsed.
