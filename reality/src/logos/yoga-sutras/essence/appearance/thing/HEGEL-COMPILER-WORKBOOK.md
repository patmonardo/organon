### Entry heg-iv-014 — The Thing and its Properties

Span:

- sourceFile: `src/logos/ys-logic/dharma-greater-varttika.tex`
- lineStart: 3847
- lineEnd: 3880

Summary:
Identity-across-transformation (_pariṇāma-ekatvād_) defines Hegelian "Thinghood" out of fluid concrete existence. Appearance stabilizes into a "Thing" (_vastu-tattvam_) strictly via the preservation of negative unity mediated across lawful change. Concrete existence is the immediate result, but "The Thing" is that concrete existence reflected back into itself as an isolated unity.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. The thing is distinct from its concrete existence; it is existence possessing the moment of mediation within it.
- k2. The truth of the Thing (_Thing in itself_) is nothing but the persistent Identity generated across the transformation (_pariṇāma_) holding the properties together.
