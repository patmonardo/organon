# Appearance/Thing - SANSKRIT IDEA WORKBOOK

## Purpose

Establishing the baseline Sanskrit philology and systemic philosophical analysis of Yoga Sūtras IV.11 through IV.14. This sequence exits the Ground phase and formally establishes the physics of Appearance—mapping dependent facticity, time as a geometric mode, the guṇic spectrum of manifestation, and finally, the derivation of a "Thing" (_vastu_) via identity-across-change. Furthermore, it incorporates a _Vārtika_ (expansive commentary) integrating the Fichte/Hegel synthetic logic into the traditional reading.

## Sūtra IV.11: The Facticity Rule (_Asparśa_)

### I. Text and Translation

**Devanāgarī:**
हेतुफलाश्रयालम्बनसंगृहीतत्वादेषामभावे तदभावः ॥ ४.११॥

**Transliteration:**
hetu-phala-āśraya-ālambana-saṅgṛhītatvāt eṣām abhāve tad-abhāvaḥ || 4.11 ||

**Translation:**
Because these appearances are structurally encompassed (_saṅgṛhītatvāt_) by a dependency complex comprising cause, fruition, substrate, and object-contact (_hetu-phala-āśraya-ālambana_), in the absence of any one of these dependencies (_eṣām abhāve_), there is absolute non-arising of the phenomenon (_tad-abhāvaḥ_).

### II. Systemic Analysis: Breaking the Circuit

This sūtra serves as the ultimate transitional lock. Before diving into the mechanics of objects and time, Patañjali secures the overarching logic governing manifestation: the Absolute Rule of Absence.

Appearances are not independently sustaining entities with inherent atomic mass. They act more like illuminated pixels constrained entirely by a strict dependency matrix (_cause, result, base, contact_). Rather than fighting the content of consciousness, the yogic logic attacks its structural coordinates. Disable the "contact" parameter (_ālambana_), and the logic circuit breaks. This is the mechanical foundation of non-contact (_asparśa_) yoga: freedom is found not in destroying the world, but in cleanly un-plugging the dependencies that compute the appearance.

### III. Vārtika: The Idealist Synthesis

By asserting the Absence Rule, Patañjali provides the perfect mechanical resolution to Fichte's _propositio facto contraria_. Fichte diagnoses that normal reflection constantly contradicts itself because it is trapped in its own conditions—saying one thing ("this is absolute truth") while doing another ("I am conditioned in stating this"). You cannot argue your way out of this trap. You must perform an act of absolute absence. Patañjali disables the dependency matrix, specifically _ālambana_ (contact), allowing saying and doing to perfectly align in Absolute Reason. Hegel formulates this as the _Procession of the Fact into Concrete Existence_: a Fact is identical with the totality of its conditions. Therefore, if the Yogi severs the target condition, they do not destroy a magical substance; they execute a logical halt command, preventing the Unconditioned from proceeding into Concrete Existence.

## Sūtra IV.12: Time as a Mode of Appearance

### I. Text and Translation

**Devanāgarī:**
अतीतानागतं स्वरूपतोऽस्त्यध्वभेदाद्धर्माणाम् ॥ ४.१२॥

**Transliteration:**
atīta-anāgataṁ svarūpato ’sty adhva-bhedād dharmāṇām || 4.12 ||

**Translation:**
Past and future (_atīta-anāgatam_) exist in/by their own-form (_svarūpato ’sti_); this occurs because the fundamental determinacies of appearance—the _dharmas_—differentiate across disparate temporal paths (_adhva-bhedāt_).

### II. Systemic Analysis: Time is Not a Container

This is arguably the most radical ontological claim regarding time in the classical corpus. Patañjali dismantles "Naive Presentism"—the instinctive human belief that only the "now" exists, while the past is utterly dead, and the future is an empty void.

Instead, Patañjali declares that past and future _exist_. But they exist strictly as _svarūpa_ ("existence-as-form"). Because the _dharmas_ traverse different temporal pathways, time itself generates as a geometric differentiation of form. Time is not a massive, empty container within which objects move; time is the internal path-difference of the appearance itself. The past is not gone; it is structurally located on a different _adhva_ (path) within the unitary matrix.

_(Note on Svarūpataḥ: Translators commonly read this as "in its own form." The radical deictic/reflexive reading asks: "Whose form?" Past and future exist not as autonomous things-out-there, but strictly as form-structures appearing directly to/in the witness.)_

### III. Vārtika: The Idealist Synthesis

Here, Patañjali’s geometry maps perfectly onto Fichte and Hegel's deduction of Time. Fichte argues that the manifold must arise genetically from the Light itself; the Light fractures its inner singularity into a spectrum. Time (_adhva-bheda_) is the primary geometric pathway of this fracturing. Hegel agrees, rejecting the notion of Time as an empty box. For Hegel, Space and Time are the immediate forms in which logical Conceptness externalizes itself. Past and Future are objectively real logical _forms_ (_svarūpa_). They plot the trajectory of the Ground’s necessary unfoldment because, ontologically, Essence _must_ appear.

## Sūtra IV.13: The Substrate of the Spectrum

### I. Text and Translation

**Devanāgarī:**
ते व्यक्तसूक्ष्मा गुणात्मानः ॥ ४.१३॥

**Transliteration:**
te vyakta-sūkṣmā guṇātmānaḥ || 4.13 ||

**Translation:**
Those [dharmas], whether occupying the manifest state (_vyakta_) or the subtle/latent state (_sūkṣmāḥ_), are fundamentally composed of the nature of the _guṇas_ (_guṇātmānaḥ_).

### II. Systemic Analysis: The Guṇic Processor

If IV.12 establishes time pathways, IV.13 provides the hardware. How do past forms translate into future forms? They move along a continuous state-spectrum ranging from sub-threshold latency (_sūkṣma_) to full presentation (_vyakta_).

This movement is entirely governed by the _guṇas_—the primary energetic vectors of nature (_sattva_, _rajas_, _tamas_). The _dharmas_ are not ghostly concepts; they are heavy with guṇic mechanics. A change in time/state is functionally equivalent to a shift in the underlying ratio of the _guṇas_. This preserves a mathematically tight ontology: nothing is created from nothing, nor destroyed into nothing. Forms simply slide up and down the manifest-subtle guṇic slider.

### III. Vārtika: The Idealist Synthesis

Fichte insists that philosophy must present Appearance "as such" genetically, rather than empirically pointing at sensible objects. The guṇas satisfy this demand. They provide the a priori physical infrastructure to manage the derivations of the Light, translating its qualitative singularity into quantitative ratios. Hegel similarly demands that whatever emerges into Concrete Existence requires underlying logical hardware to manage its states. Patañjali’s guṇic tripartite substrate allows Concrete Existence to retain its rational architecture through smooth shifts in quantitative intensity, ensuring continuity across the entire spectrum.

## Sūtra IV.14: The Genesis of the "Thing"

### I. Text and Translation

**Devanāgarī:**
परिणामैकत्वाद्वस्ततत्त्वम् ॥ ४.१४॥

**Transliteration:**
pariṇāma-ekatvād vastu-tattvam || 4.14 ||

**Translation:**
The truth/reality of a "Thing" (_vastu-tattvam_) derives from the unifying oneness (_ekatvād_) persisting across its processual transformations (_pariṇāma_).

### II. Systemic Analysis: Identity-Across-Change

What is a "Thing"? Normal consciousness sees solid nouns. Patañjali defines a Thing (_vastu_) rigorously as a verb masking as a noun.

A _vastu_ achieves reality strictly via _pariṇāma-ekatva_—the identity across its transformation. _Pariṇāma_ literally acts as a "change of name": the same underlying substance appears differently at Time 1 and Time 2 due to guṇic shifting (IV.13). The "oneness" that mathematically links State 1 to State 2 is what consciousness grabs hold of and calls an "object." Without the lawful process of transformation, there is no identity. The "Thing" is derived immanently from the rules of its own change, rejecting any need to posit a mysterious static substance hiding behind reality.

### III. Vārtika: The Idealist Synthesis

This elegant Sanskrit formula (_pariṇāma-ekatvād vastu-tattvam_) perfectly resolves the world-puzzle of Idealism. Fichte uses the Absolute "From" operator to explain this: the Light posits a "From" (processual transformation, _Pariṇāma_), and Reason sees that the Light maintains qualitative oneness across these splits. Dead intuition freezes this process and calls it an object. Hegel achieves one of his greatest triumphs precisely here by destroying the Kantian noumenal "Thing-in-Itself." The Thing (_Das Ding_) is not hiding behind appearance; it is derived _out of_ appearance as the unifying knot (Identity) across the changing properties (Transformation). Both Patañjali and Hegel agree: the "Thing" is nothing but the persistent Identity acting as the mathematical link across the lawful "From" of manifestation.
