### Entry heg-iv-016 — Dissolution of the Thing

Span:

- sourceFile: `src/logos/ys-logic/dharma-greater-varttika.tex`
- lineStart: 4434
- lineEnd: 4460

Summary:
The refutation of single-mind dependence formalizes Hegel's "Dissolution of the Thing." If an object were merely the quantitative combination of external matters dependent upon a single locus, it would be "absolutely alterable" and "absolutely dissoluble" whenever the binding condition lapsed. True objectivity prevents this catastrophic dissolution.

Key points: (KeyPoint)

- k1. Subjective idealism renders the Thing structurally porous and absolutely dissoluble.
- k2. The transition out of the Thing into Appearance prevents the object from collapsing into its discrete matters.

- sourceFile: `src/logos/ys-logic/dharma-greater-varttika.tex`

Summary:
The epistemic gating mechanism (_upārāga_) aligns with Hegel's "The Law of Appearance." The object is known or unknown through the specific overlapping mediation with the mind. Key points: (KeyPoint)
