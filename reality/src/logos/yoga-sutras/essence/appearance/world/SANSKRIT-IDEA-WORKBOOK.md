# Appearance/World - SANSKRIT IDEA WORKBOOK

## Purpose

Establishing the baseline Sanskrit philology and systemic philosophical analysis of Yoga Sūtras IV.15 through IV.18. This sequence exits the localized physics of the "Thing" and formalizes "Worldness"—how objects are secured across multiple minds, the refutation of single-mind dependence (solipsism), the epistemic condition of knownness, and the ultimate grounding in the invariant Witness (_Puruṣa_). Furthermore, it incorporates a _Vārtika_ (expansive commentary) integrating the Fichte/Hegel synthetic logic into the traditional reading.

---

## Sūtra IV.15: The Variants of Worldness

### I. Text and Translation

**Devanāgarī:**
वस्तुसाम्ये चित्तभेदात्तयोर्विभक्तः पन्थाः ॥ ४.१५॥

**Transliteration:**
vastu-sāmye citta-bhedāt tayor vibhaktaḥ panthāḥ || 4.15 ||

**Translation:**
Even while the object remains exactly the same (_vastu-sāmye_), because of the structural differences between subjective minds (_citta-bhedāt_), the pathways of appearance are divided (_tayoḥ vibhaktaḥ panthāḥ_).

### II. Systemic Analysis: Parameterizing the Object

In IV.14, Patañjali defined a "Thing" (_vastu_) as the invariant identity mathematically holding across transformation. Immediately, IV.15 stress-tests this definition against the problem of multiple observers.

Patañjali asserts two things simultaneously. First, he holds the object as a constant variable (_vastu-sāmye_). Second, he parameterizes the appearance pathway by _citta-bheda_ (the differences in mental software, karmic residue, and attunement). The result is a mathematically predicted divergence (_vibhaktaḥ panthāḥ_). One person looks at a pile of gold and feels joy; another feels anxiety; a third feels apathy.

Patañjali is outlining the Law of Worldness here. A "world" is not a uniform block of dead matter. A world is the intersection of stable, invariant nodal points (the _vastu_) crossed by wildly variant, subjective perceptual trajectories. The true yogin learns to separate the constant _vastu_ from the variant coloration painted onto it by their specific _citta_.

### III. Vārtika: The Idealist Synthesis

For Fichte, navigating this disjunction without losing the Absolute is critical. The Object (_vastu_) acts as the locus of qualitative oneness. The different minds (_cittas_) represent the disjunction within the Fichtean "From"—the myriad ways the Light refracts under empirical conditions. Recognizing divergent paths is the prerequisite to isolating their invariant root. Hegel mathematically upgrades this into the _Law of Appearance_. The World is not static substance; it is the dialectical holding-together of an invariant Law alongside massive subjective variance. In Objective Logic, this is "The Constitution of the Thing out of Matters." The myriad cognitive frames (_citta-bhedāt_) are the diverse properties/matters that safely interpenetrate and constitute the single abstract Object.

## Sūtra IV.16: Science and the Refutation of Solipsism

### I. Text and Translation

**Devanāgarī:**
न चैकचित्ततन्त्रं वस्तु तदप्रमाणकं तदा किं स्यात् ॥ ४.१६॥

**Transliteration:**
na ca-eka-citta-tantraṁ vastu tad-apramāṇakam tadā kim syāt || 4.16 ||

**Translation:**
Moreover, the object is absolutely not dependent on a single mind (_na ca-eka-citta-tantraṁ vastu_); if it were, when it remains unvalidated by that singular mind’s attention (_tad-apramāṇakam_), what would it be then (_tadā kim syāt_)?

### II. Systemic Analysis: Denying Mind-Creationism

If mental differences cause divergence in appearance (IV.15), doesn't that imply the mind _creates_ the object? Patañjali fiercely refutes this subjective idealism.

He uses a brilliant reductio ad absurdum. If an object is nothing more than the emanation of Mind A (_eka-citta-tantram_), what happens when Mind A gets distracted or falls asleep? The object would undergo an ontological collapse (_apramāṇakam_—"lacking validation"). But this directly contradicts IV.15, where we established that Mind B and Mind C can still interact with the same _vastu_ along their own divergent paths.

This sutra is the charter for objective science within Yoga. The world is an intersubjective architecture. While consciousness _filters_ the appearance, no single empirical ego _sustains_ the atomic structure of reality. The _vastu_ possesses an ontological independence from any single _citta_.

### III. Vārtika: The Idealist Synthesis

A brutal misreading of Idealism (and Yoga) suggests "the mind creates reality." Fichte directly refutes this subjective idealism: the absolute Light posits itself universally, not through the flimsy empirical ego (_eka-citta_). If the empirical "We" created the world, the universe would vanish when we step away. Hegel formalizes the impossibility of this subjective solipsism through the _Dissolution of the Thing_. If an object were merely dependent on a single subjective locus, it would be "absolutely alterable" and "absolutely dissoluble" whenever the binding condition lapsed (_tad-apramāṇakam_). By grounding Worldness intersubjectively, Patañjali escapes absolute dissolubility, ensuring objective coherence across multiple vantage points.

## Sūtra IV.17: Epistemic Gating (_Upārāga_)

### I. Text and Translation

**Devanāgarī:**
तदुपरागापेक्षित्वाच्चित्तस्य वस्तु ज्ञाताज्ञातम् ॥ ४.१७॥

**Transliteration:**
tad-upārāga-apekṣitvāt cittasya vastu jñāta-ajñātam || 4.17 ||

**Translation:**
For a specific mind (_cittasya_), the object is either known or unknown (_vastu jñāta-ajñātam_) depending entirely upon the conditional coloring/overlap between the mind and the object (_tad-upārāga-apekṣitvāt_).

### II. Systemic Analysis: Conditionality of Knowing

Patañjali must strike a fragile balance here. IV.16 proved the object exists independently of the mind. Does this mean the mind automatically mirrors all existing objects? No.

Patañjali introduces the epistemic condition of _upārāga_ (coloring, attunement, overlap). The mind operates functionally like a radio receiver. The radio waves (objects) always exist in the room (IV.16). But whether the music is "known or unknown" (_jñāta-ajñātam_) depends entirely on whether the receiver's frequency overlaps/attunes (_upārāga_) with the wave.

Here we see the crucial distinction between epistemology and ontology. "Unknown-for-me" does not equal "non-existent-in-reality." To know an object requires establishing a structural, conditional bridge.

### III. Vārtika: The Idealist Synthesis

How is the architecture of reality proven? Fichte points to the Performative Proof Nerve: we know Truth only by precisely organizing the conditions of our knowing. Patañjali’s _upārāga_ (coloring/overlap) is this exact conditionality. Knowing is an active overlapping, not a passive soaking. Hegel elevates this epistemology to _The Relation of Appearance_. For Hegel, Appearance is the concrete existent mediated through its negation to return into itself. A thing is only known (_jñāta_) when the form of the subjective mind structurally aligns with the rational form of the objective thing. The World operates independently of ignorance; to know it, you must execute the conditional mathematical relation (_upārāga_) that couples subject to object.

## Sūtra IV.18: The Apex Invariant (_Puruṣa_)

### I. Text and Translation

**Devanāgarī:**
सदा ज्ञाताश्चित्तवृत्तयस्तत्प्रभोः पुरुषस्यापरिणामित्वात् ॥ ४.१८॥

**Transliteration:**
sadā jñātāḥ citta-vṛttayaḥ tat-prabhoḥ puruṣasya apariṇāmitvāt || 4.18 ||

**Translation:**
The fluctuating modifications of the mind (_citta-vṛttayaḥ_) are always known (_sadā jñātāḥ_) because their Lord/Master—the Puruṣa—is absolutely unchanging/invariant (_tat-prabhoḥ puruṣasya apariṇāmitvāt_).

### II. Systemic Analysis: The Absolute Stance

IV.17 established that the empirical mind (_citta_) is violently conditional; it knows things, then forgets things, toggling on and off based on attunement. But if everything is toggling, how is continuous experience even mathematically possible? Who is watching the toggling?

Appearance absolutely requires an invariant pole against which variance can be measured. Patañjali identifies this transcendental ground as the _Puruṣa_. Because the _Puruṣa_ is structurally invariant (_apariṇāmī_—not subject to transformation or "change of name"), it does not suffer from conditional _upārāga_.

The _vṛttis_ (mental modifications) dance and fluctuate. But relative to the _Puruṣa_, they are _sadā jñātāḥ_ (always known). The _Puruṣa_ acts as the non-agentive Light, the ultimate backdrop that makes the entire cinematic projection of the World mathematically coherent. It is the "Lord" (_prabhu_) not because it dictates action, but because it effortlessly illuminates the totality of the mind's operations without ever flickering itself.

### III. Vārtika: The Idealist Synthesis

At the summit of the Appearance triad, all derived logic and subjective toggling must anchor in an absolute absolute. Fichte describes this as abandoning deduction for Qualitative Oneness (_LogoLogia_). You do not deduce the Light; you stand immersed in it. The _Puruṣa_ is this self-validating, non-agentive Light rendering the machinery visible without catching fire itself. Hegel locates this precisely at the close of Appearance as _The World-In-Itself_. If the empirical mind (_citta_) is a restless kingdom of flux, the _Puruṣa_ is the tranquil Kingdom of Laws standing as the enduring substrate behind the changes of the appearance-world. It is the Hegelian World-in-Itself actualized as Pure Knowing: presiding over particular moments while retaining absolute identity.
