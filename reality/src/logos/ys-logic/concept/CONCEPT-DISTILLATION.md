### §1. The Concept Cannot Be Presupposed as a Dead Beginning

**Lines:** 8–61

**Distillation:**
The concept cannot be introduced like an underived axiom. If it were merely immediate, it would still be an abstraction and therefore something mediated whose truth would lie elsewhere. The concept is indeed foundation, but only because it has made itself into foundation by sublating its own mediation. For that reason it is the truth of Being and Essence: those earlier spheres are not independent regions standing alongside it, but the genetic exposition of the concept's own becoming.

**Structural Moves:**

- **No abstract first:** The concept is not a bare starting-point.
- **Self-grounding immediacy:** It is immediate only as mediation overcome.
- **Truth of prior logic:** Being and Essence are preserved as its becoming.

### §2. The Concept Emerges from the Consummation of Substance

**Lines:** 63–234

**Distillation:**
Substance is the immediate presupposition of the concept because it is real essence stepped into actuality. But substance is only implicitly what the concept is explicitly. Through the movement of active and passive substance, cause and effect, and finally reciprocal action, substance reveals that its own truth is not blind necessity but self-returning unity. The decisive result is that being-in-and-for-itself is such only by equally being positedness. When this infinite immanent reflection becomes explicit, substance consummates itself into something higher than substance: the concept as subject.

**Structural Moves:**

- **From substance to concept:** Substance is the immediate presupposition, not the final truth.
- **Reciprocity as turning point:** Each side becomes itself only in its opposite.
- **Consummation:** Blind substantial necessity becomes self-relating subjectivity.

---

### §3. Freedom Is the Truth of Necessity

**Lines:** 81–101, 324–362

**Distillation:**
What substance contains as inner necessity, the concept displays as freedom. Freedom does not abolish necessity from outside; it is necessity become transparent to itself. In the concept, positedness is no longer alien to being-in-and-for-itself but identical with it. That is why the kingdom of freedom opens here: the obscure causal relation of substances is released into a self-referential identity that knows itself in its own positing.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Necessity transformed:** Freedom is necessity brought to self-clarity.
- **Identity of being and positedness:** The concept is self-relating mediation.
- **Kingdom of freedom:** Substance is let go into transparent self-determination.

### §4. Spinoza Is Overcome Only Immanently

**Lines:** 236–323

**Distillation:**
The standpoint of substance is necessary and therefore true, but not highest. For that reason Spinozism cannot be refuted by external objections grounded in assumptions foreign to it. The only genuine refutation is immanent: to show that the substantial relation, on its own strength, passes over into the concept. The transition from substance to subject is therefore not a rejection of Spinoza's truth but its completion.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Necessary standpoint:** Substance is not error in the trivial sense.
- **No external refutation:** Higher truth cannot merely negate from outside.
- **Immanent elevation:** Substance unveils itself as concept.

### §5. The Concept Is Concrete Self-Relation, Not an Empty Universal

**Lines:** 364–581

**Distillation:**
The concept is absolute determinateness referring only to itself. As such it is universality, but this universality already contains determinateness within itself and therefore is equally negativity and singularity. Each side contains the other; their opposition is only the reflective shine of one self-completing movement. This same structure appears concretely in the I. The I is not a thing that merely has concepts as properties; it is the pure concept in existent form, the unity of universality and singularity in self-consciousness.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Concrete universality:** Universality contains determinateness within itself.
- **Universality and singularity:** Each immediately implies the other.
- **No inert abstraction:** The concept is living self-differentiating unity.
- **The I as concept:** Self-consciousness gives concrete existence to this structure.

### §6. The Ordinary View of Thought and Kant Both Stop Short

**Lines:** 583–888

**Distillation:**
Ordinary thinking treats the concept as an empty form that abstracts from a richer sensible given. Kant advances beyond this by recognizing in apperception the original synthetic unity that constitutes objectivity. Yet he still leaves the concept dependent on intuition, treats it as merely subjective, and reduces reason to a merely regulative use. The result is a contradiction: the concept is recognized as the source of objectivity and truth, but denied the power to reach reality as it is in and for itself.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Ordinary error:** Sensuous immediacy is treated as the true real.
- **Kant's advance:** Objectivity is grounded in conceptual unity.
- **Kant's limit:** Concept and reason are still kept short of full truth.

### §7. The Concept Must Generate Its Own Reality

**Lines:** 890–1150

**Distillation:**
If truth is the agreement of concept and thing, then the concept cannot remain a merely formal abstraction facing an alien reality. Its reality must arise from itself. The incompleteness of the concept is therefore not that it lacks sensible material, but that it has yet to give itself an existence adequate to its own form. Logic, for this reason, is not empty formalism. It is the science of the absolute form whose content is generated by that form itself and is already truth in its own element.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Truth criterion:** Concept and object must agree.
- **No fallback to givenness:** Reality cannot be borrowed from intuition.
- **Logic as real form:** Absolute form generates adequate content from itself.

---

### §8. Traditional Logic Is Too Formal Because It Does Not Critique Its Own Forms

**Lines:** 1152–1236

**Distillation:**
Traditional logic registers forms of judgment and inference without asking whether they are themselves true forms of truth. A proposition such as the positive judgment is accepted as valid in itself, while truth is made to depend only on content. But if subject and predicate fail to agree conceptually, the form itself is already defective. Aristotle's merit lies in first describing these forms. The further task is to determine their systematic connection and speculative value.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Against formal cataloging:** Logical forms must be criticized, not merely listed.
- **Form matters to truth:** Judgment-form can itself be dialectically inadequate.
- **Further task:** The system and value of forms must be exhibited.

---

### §9. The Whole Doctrine Divides into Subjectivity, Objectivity, and Idea

**Lines:** 1238–1376

**Distillation:**
The concept is the restored unity of Being and Essence, but at first only as concept. It therefore begins as subjectivity or the formal concept, where its determinations still appear as immediate and externally related. Through the dialectic of this formality, the concept gives itself objective existence as objectivity. Finally, when concept and objectivity are united in fully realized freedom, the adequate concept is the Idea. The whole doctrine is therefore the self-explication of the concept from inward form, through objective existence, into truth that knows itself.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Subjectivity:** The concept first appears in formal inwardness.
- **Objectivity:** The concept gives itself free existence.
- **Idea:** The concept recognizes itself in its objectivity as truth.
