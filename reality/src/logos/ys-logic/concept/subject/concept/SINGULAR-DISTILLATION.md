### §1. Singularity Is the Concept's Return Out of Determinateness into Itself

**Lines:** 4–41

**Distillation:**
Singularity first appears as the reflection of the concept out of its determinateness back into itself. It is the concept's self-mediation, restoring itself as self-equal through absolute negativity. This return is the true alternative to empty abstraction. Abstraction rises to ever higher genera and thereby becomes progressively more vacuous, whereas singularity is the depth in which the concept grasps itself and is truly posited as concept.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Self-return:** The concept recollects itself out of its difference.
- **Absolute negativity:** Singularity is active self-mediation.
- **Against abstraction:** True depth lies in singularity, not higher empty genera.

---

### §2. Abstract Universality Cannot Grasp Life, Spirit, or the Concept Itself

**Lines:** 43–73

**Distillation:**
Universality and particularity do not pass over into an external other in singularity; what is posited there is what they already are in and for themselves. Abstract universality fails precisely because its negativity remains external to it. For that reason, it cannot grasp life, spirit, God, or the pure concept, all of which require singularity as the principle of individuality and personality. Abstraction therefore yields only lifeless universalities devoid of spirit and content.

**Structural Moves:**

- **No external transition:** Universality and particularity are fulfilled within singularity.
- **Failure of abstraction:** Externalized negativity leaves universality void.
- **Need for singularity:** True conceptual content requires individuality.

### §3. Even Abstraction's Products Reveal the Inescapability of Singularity

**Lines:** 75–101

**Distillation:**
Although abstraction claims to drop singularity, its own products are themselves singulars. In raising the concrete to universality, abstraction always picks out some determinate content and therefore produces a unity of singular content and abstract universal form. What abstraction calls universality is already infected by singularity. Its result is therefore concrete, the opposite of what abstraction intends.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Abstraction's contradiction:** It cannot eliminate singularity from its own results.
- **Determinate content remains:** Abstract universality always carries selected singular content.
- **Concrete outcome:** The product of abstraction is itself singularly concrete.

### §4. Singularity Posits the Inseparability of Universal, Particular, and Singular

**Lines:** 103–177

**Distillation:**
Because the singular is a determinate universal and the particular is likewise singular, the three moments of the concept cannot be rigidly separated. In singularity, the true relation of the concept's determinations is posited: each distinct determination is the totality. Singularity is not a third thing merely placed next to universality and particularity. It is the negation of negation in which their opposition is grounded and reconciled. The concept's return into itself means that each determination is, in its very determinateness, the whole concept.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Mutual implication:** Universal, particular, and singular imply one another.
- **Negation of negation:** Singularity grounds their unity.
- **Each as totality:** Determinate moments are no longer separable fragments.

---

### §5. Singularity Is Also the Immediate Loss of the Concept into Actuality

**Lines:** 179–227

**Distillation:**
Singularity is not only the return of the concept into itself but also the immediate loss of that inwardness. Through singularity the concept steps into actuality. As self-referring negativity, the singular is a one, a qualitative this, excluding others and standing for itself. If the universal is then taken merely as what is common to many singulars, the concept is reduced to an external relation. This is the lowest view of universality, because it starts from indifferent singulars rather than from the concept's own self-determination.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Step into actuality:** The concept becomes an immediate this.
- **Exclusion:** The singular stands as one against others.
- **Critique of commonality:** Universality cannot be reduced to what many singulars share externally.

---

### §6. The Conceptual Singular Is Not the Merely Pointed-Out This

**Lines:** 229–248

**Distillation:**
The singular in the conceptual sphere differs from the this of ordinary immediacy. The merely immediate this is what it is only because it is pointed out from outside. Its mediation lies outside it. The conceptual singular, by contrast, is an immediacy that has mediation within itself. It is posited abstraction and repelling separation, yet precisely in that separation it remains a positive connection. The conceptual singular is therefore not a brute given, but immediacy generated by self-mediation.

**Structural Moves:**

- **External this:** Immediate singularity depends on being pointed at.
- **Conceptual singular:** Mediation is internal to the immediacy.
- **Positive connection:** Separation and inward unity coexist.

---

### §7. Singularity Posits Self-Subsistent Differences and Thereby Becomes Judgment

**Lines:** 250–282

**Distillation:**
As immanent reflection, singularity first posits differences as self-subsistent. Yet these differences are immediately in essential relation, because they are differences of the concept rather than external pluralities. In positing them as self-subsistent, the concept loses its immediately posited unity: its determinations no longer appear as moments but as terms existing in and for themselves. This is the absolute, originative partition of the concept. As singularity, the concept is therefore posited as judgment.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Positing of differences:** Singularity gives the determinations independent standing.
- **Essential relation remains:** The differences are still conceptual, not merely external.
- **Transition:** The concept's self-partition in singularity is judgment.
