### §1. Immediate Formal Syllogism and the Task of Mediation

**Lines:** 2–36

**Distillation:**
The syllogism of existence begins as the strictly formal syllogism. Its terms are the concept's moments in immediate abstraction: singularity and universality stand as extremes, while particularity occupies the middle. But this middle is only a simple determinateness, not yet the explicitly posited mediation that truly makes a syllogism a syllogism. The entire movement of this section therefore has one task: to force the merely immediate form to exhibit the mediating moments that it implicitly contains.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Immediate form:** The first syllogism stops at abstract form.
- **Particularity as first middle:** The middle unites singularity and universality only immediately.
- **Program of the section:** What is implicit as mediation must become explicit.

### §2. The First Figure (S-P-U) and Its Superficial Objectivity

**Lines:** 40–115

**Distillation:**
The first figure, $S-P-U$, is the general schema of the determinate syllogism. Singularity rises to universality only through particularity, and universality lowers itself to singularity only through that same middle. This gives the first objective meaning of the syllogism: the singular emerges from inwardness into external universality and returns from externality into itself. Yet this objectivity is still superficial, because the unity of the terms is not yet posited as their own concrete essence. Hegel's critique of Aristotle here is precise: inherence alone does not capture the determinateness of the terms in relation to each other. The further figures are therefore not side-by-side species but necessary transformations of the first form.

**Structural Moves:**

- **S-P-U as basic schema:** The extremes are joined only through the middle.
- **Objective meaning:** The singular both externalizes and returns through mediation.
- **Critique of inherence:** Mere inclusion of one term in another misses the real term-relation.
- **Necessity of development:** The later figures are generated by the inadequacy of the first.

### §3. The "Therefore" Is Grounded in the Thing, Not in Subjective Reflection

**Lines:** 117–210

**Distillation:**
The conclusion is not a merely subjective add-on tacked onto two premises by an observing mind. The "therefore" belongs to the nature of the extremes themselves, because their true connection is posited in the middle term. To treat the syllogism as three separate judgments is thus a formalist distortion: the living unity is broken into an artificial sequence of statements. Hegel's boredom with the textbook example is methodological. The rational is not a verbal procedure that reason performs when direct insight fails; rather, the syllogism is the truth of judgment itself, the objective element in which determinations are already united in one essence. "All things are a syllogism" means: reality is mediation, not a pile of isolated propositions.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Internal therefore:** Mediation is objective, not a subjective trick.
- **Critique of the three-judgment model:** The usual textbook arrangement falsifies the living relation.
- **Truth of judgment:** Syllogism is the objective completion of judgment.
- **Ontological claim:** Things themselves are syllogistic unities.

### §4. The Qualitative Syllogism and the Arbitrariness of the Middle Term

**Lines:** 212–347

**Distillation:**
Once the immediate syllogism is taken from the side of content, it becomes the qualitative syllogism. The singular subject is an immediate concrete with an indeterminate multiplicity of properties, relations, and determinacies. Any one of these may be selected as middle term, and each selection opens onto a different universal. The same subject can therefore be led into incompatible predicates, and the same formal correctness can justify opposite conclusions. Hegel's examples make the point sharply: the wall may be inferred blue or yellow, the human being morally neutral or spiritual, a political order communal or dissolving, depending on the abstract middle term chosen. The defect is not accidental to the content; it is rooted in the form itself, because the form reduces the concrete to a one-sided quality.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Content enters the form:** The syllogism is now tested as qualitative inference.
- **Infinite manifold of properties:** The singular offers many possible middles.
- **Contradictory correctness:** Opposed conclusions can be equally well inferred.
- **Form is to blame:** Abstract form forces one-sided content.

### §5. The Premises Demand Proof and Generate a Bad Infinite Regress

**Lines:** 348–431

**Distillation:**
The formal syllogism claims mediation, but its premises remain immediate judgments. This contradicts the concept of the syllogism, since the unity of the determinations is supposed to be posited, not merely assumed. Once one demands that the premises themselves be proved, each premise requires a further syllogism, which in turn requires more premises, and so on in a geometric progression. The result is the bad infinity already familiar from Being: the same contradiction is repeated without resolution. The truth of this regress is not more repetition, but the sublation of the deficient form that generates it.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Contradiction in the premises:** Immediate judgments cannot ground mediation.
- **Proof-demand:** Each premise becomes a conclusion needing its own syllogism.
- **Bad infinity:** Formal mediation endlessly reproduces its own deficiency.
- **True result:** The first figure must be transformed, not repeated.

### §6. The Rotation of the Figures and the Emergence of Singularity as Middle

**Lines:** 432–483

**Distillation:**
Because the same pattern cannot mediate its own premises, mediation must take a different shape. The connection of $P-U$ is reconfigured as $P-S-U$, and the connection of $S-P$ as $S-U-P$. This is not an arbitrary rearrangement. According to the concept, the immediate singular is what underlies the contingent annexation of subject to predicate, and the conclusion of the first figure has already posited singularity as universal and particular together. Singularity therefore emerges as the true middle: the place where the previously separate determinations are united, even if only in a still defective form.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Need for re-formation:** The same mediation form cannot solve its own defect.
- **Figure-rotation:** New syllogistic figures are generated from the breakdown of the first.
- **Singularity promoted:** The singular now carries universal and particular within it.
- **Conceptual gain:** The middle becomes richer than simple particularity.

### §7. The Second Figure (P-S-U): Contingency Posited as Singularity

**Lines:** 486–540

**Distillation:**
The truth of the first qualitative syllogism is that a subject is not united to a universal quality in and for itself, but only through contingency, that is, through singularity. The second figure makes this explicit. Singularity is now the middle, and with it mediation carries a negative moment: the connection is no longer immediate but mediated through something that sublates immediacy. The universal appears as one species among others, mediated by singularity, while the particular is raised toward universality only through that negative mediation. The second figure therefore exposes what the first figure concealed: the link between subject and predicate is still externally grounded.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Truth of the first figure:** Qualitative connection is contingent, not essential.
- **Singular middle:** Contingency itself becomes the mediating center.
- **Negative unity:** Mediation now includes an internal negation of immediacy.
- **External species-relation:** The universal is still only one species through singular mediation.

### §8. The Second Figure's Defect and the Pointing Beyond Itself

**Lines:** 542–670

**Distillation:**
Despite the gain in form, the second figure still leaves the terms as immediate contents, externally shifted into new positions. As a subjective syllogism it fails to conform to the original schema, and if its conclusion is accepted, that correctness belongs to the conclusion on its own rather than to the inferential form. The result is at best a particular conclusion, hence one that is as much positive as negative and therefore logically weak. By positing contingency explicitly, however, the second figure also sublates itself. Its singular middle is infinitely manifold and external; what it tries to join must therefore already be united more immediately than this figure can show. This points beyond singular immediacy toward universality reflected into itself.

**Structural Moves:**

- **External form-shift:** The terms exchange places without yet becoming concrete.
- **Weak conclusion:** The figure yields only a particular, indeterminate result.
- **Contingency exposed:** The singular middle shows itself to be self-external.
- **Beyond singularity:** The figure points to abstract universality as the next middle.

---

### §9. The Third Figure (S-U-P): Reciprocal Presupposition and the Truth of Formalism

**Lines:** 672–783

**Distillation:**
The third figure completes the formal syllogism by making reciprocal presupposition explicit. It no longer has a single immediate premise: one connection is mediated by the first figure, the other by the second, and each figure presupposes the others. This brings formal mediation to completion, but only by revealing its inner defect. The universal as middle unites the extremes only abstractly, not according to their essential determinateness. The form thereby confesses its own emptiness: what was supposed to mediate is precisely what is not concretely contained in the middle. The negative conclusion and the indifference of term-positions then open the way to the so-called fourth figure.

**Structural Moves:**

- **No isolated premise remains:** Each figure leans on the others.
- **Completion of formal mediation:** The system of figures becomes explicit.
- **Universal as abstract middle:** Unity is achieved only through abstraction.
- **Preparation for collapse:** Indifference of term-positions empties the figure further.

### §10. The Fourth Figure (U-U-U) and the Emptiness of the Mathematical Syllogism

**Lines:** 785–831

**Distillation:**
The fourth figure, the mathematical syllogism, strips away qualitative determinateness altogether: if two things are equal to a third, they are equal to each other. Here the mediating third has no distinctive determination over against the extremes, and any term can serve as middle depending on external circumstances. Its celebrated self-evidence is thus purchased at the cost of conceptual poverty. Mathematics here enjoys certainty only because it abstracts from the conceptual differences that matter for speculative logic. The axiom is immediate only because thought has emptied it of qualitative and conceptual content.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Equality replaces inherence:** The relation of subsumption disappears.
- **Interchangeable middle:** Any term can serve as the mediating third.
- **Axiom by abstraction:** Self-evidence rests on formal poverty.
- **No conceptual comprehension:** Determinations of the concept have been excluded.

### §11. The Positive Result: Concrete Mediation and the Transition to Reflection

**Lines:** 833–895

**Distillation:**
The result of the syllogism of existence is not merely the collapse of quality into empty quantity. Its positive truth is that every abstract determinateness has posited its other within itself and has therefore become concrete. The figures of existence mutually presuppose one another, and this reciprocal presupposition closes into a circle. Mediation is no longer based on a given immediacy, but on mediation itself: a self-referring mediation. Each form-determination has occupied the place of middle and of extreme, showing that no single abstract determinateness can serve as the true middle. The genuine middle is therefore the concrete identity of the determinacies in their reflection into one another. At this point the immediate syllogism of existence passes over into the syllogism of reflection.

**Structural Moves:**

- **Not mere collapse:** The negative result has a positive speculative side.
- **Circle of presupposing:** The figures include one another within a totality.
- **Mediation of mediation:** Reflection replaces appeal to given immediacy.
- **Concrete middle:** The true middle is the reflected identity of the determinations.
- **Transition:** Existence yields to reflection.
